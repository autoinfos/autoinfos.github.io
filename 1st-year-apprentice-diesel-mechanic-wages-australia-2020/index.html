1St Year Apprentice Diesel Mechanic Wages Australia 2020 All You Need To Know

Are you considering a career as a diesel mechanic in Australia? As a first year apprentice, you may have questions about your wages and what to expect. In this post, we'll cover everything you need to know about 1st year apprentice diesel mechanic wages in Australia for 2020.

As a first year apprentice diesel mechanic, you may be worried about your wages being too low. It's true that apprenticeships generally pay less than fully qualified positions, but it's important to remember that this is a training period. Your wages will increase as you gain more experience and skills.

The target of 1st year apprentice diesel mechanic wages in Australia for 2020 is to provide a fair wage for apprentices while keeping in mind the costs for employers. The Fair Work Commission sets national minimum wages for apprentices, which are currently set at $12.18 per hour or $462.40 per week. However, some employers may pay more than this minimum wage.

In summary, as a first year apprentice diesel mechanic in Australia, you can expect to earn at least $12.18 per hour or $462.40 per week. Your wages may increase as you gain more experience and skills, and some employers may pay more than the minimum wage.

What to Expect as a First Year Apprentice Diesel Mechanic

As a first year apprentice diesel mechanic, you can expect to spend most of your time learning on the job. You'll work alongside experienced mechanics and attend TAFE or other training institutions to learn the theory behind the practical work you'll be doing.

During your apprenticeship, you'll learn how to diagnose and repair problems with diesel engines, as well as how to maintain and service them. You'll also learn how to use and maintain tools and equipment, and how to follow safety procedures.

The Benefits of Being a First Year Apprentice Diesel Mechanic

While the wages for first year apprentice diesel mechanics may be lower than fully qualified positions, there are many benefits to being an apprentice. You'll gain valuable skills and experience, which will set you up for a successful career in the industry. You'll also have the opportunity to work alongside experienced mechanics, who can provide mentorship and guidance.

How to Increase Your Wages as a First Year Apprentice Diesel Mechanic

While your wages as a first year apprentice diesel mechanic may be set, there are ways to increase your earnings. One way is to work overtime or take on additional shifts. Another way is to seek out employers who pay more than the minimum wage. You can also work hard to develop your skills and knowledge, which can lead to promotions and pay raises in the future.

How to Find Employers Who Pay More Than the Minimum Wage

If you're looking for an employer who pays more than the minimum wage for first year apprentice diesel mechanics, there are a few things you can do. First, do your research. Look for companies in your area that specialize in diesel engines, and check their websites for job openings or apprenticeship opportunities. You can also ask around in your network or attend job fairs to learn about potential employers.

FAQs about 1st Year Apprentice Diesel Mechanic Wages Australia 2020

Q: Can I negotiate my wages as a first year apprentice diesel mechanic?

A: While you may not be able to negotiate your wages as a first year apprentice diesel mechanic, you can seek out employers who pay more than the minimum wage.

Q: Do I need to pay for my own tools as a first year apprentice diesel mechanic?

A: It depends on the employer. Some employers may provide tools for their apprentices to use, while others may require apprentices to purchase their own tools.

Q: How long does a diesel mechanic apprenticeship last?

A: A diesel mechanic apprenticeship typically lasts for four years.

Q: Can I work while attending TAFE or other training institutions as a first year apprentice diesel mechanic?

A: It depends on the employer and the training institution. Some employers may allow apprentices to work part-time while attending classes, while others may require full-time attendance at TAFE or other training institutions.

Conclusion of 1st Year Apprentice Diesel Mechanic Wages Australia 2020

As a first year apprentice diesel mechanic in Australia, you can expect to earn at least the national minimum wage of $12.18 per hour or $462.40 per week. While this may be lower than fully qualified positions, being an apprentice provides valuable skills and experience that will set you up for a successful career in the industry.
