Aircraft Mechanic Helper Job Description What You Need To Know

Written by Oliver Nov 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in working in the aviation industry but don't have the technical skills to become a full-fledged aircraft mechanic? Look no further than the aircraft mechanic helper position!

As an aircraft mechanic helper, you'll be responsible for assisting experienced mechanics with maintenance, repairs, and inspections of aircraft. While this job can be physically demanding and requires attention to detail, it's also a great way to gain hands-on experience in the field and potentially work your way up to a full-time mechanic position.

The ideal candidate for an aircraft mechanic helper position is someone who is mechanically inclined, able to work in a fast-paced environment, and willing to learn. You'll need to be comfortable working with your hands and have a good understanding of basic tools and equipment.

What Does an Aircraft Mechanic Helper Do?

As previously mentioned, an aircraft mechanic helper is responsible for assisting experienced mechanics with maintenance, repairs, and inspections of aircraft. This can include tasks such as:

  • Assisting with engine repairs and inspections
  • Replacing components such as brakes, wheels, and tires
  • Inspecting and repairing hydraulic and pneumatic systems
  • Assisting with electrical system maintenance and repair
  • Performing routine inspections and preventative maintenance

Overall, the goal of an aircraft mechanic helper is to support the experienced mechanics in completing their tasks safely and efficiently.

My Experience as an Aircraft Mechanic Helper

During my time as an aircraft mechanic helper, I was able to gain valuable experience in the aviation industry. While the work was challenging at times, I enjoyed being able to work with my hands and learn from experienced mechanics.

One of the most memorable experiences I had was assisting with an engine repair. I was able to help remove and replace components, and even got to witness the engine being started up again after the repairs were completed. It was a great feeling knowing that I played a small role in helping to keep an aircraft in top condition.

Qualifications for an Aircraft Mechanic Helper

While there are no specific educational requirements for an aircraft mechanic helper, most employers prefer candidates who have a high school diploma or equivalent. Additionally, having some prior experience with mechanical work or knowledge of aviation systems can be beneficial.

Some key qualifications for an aircraft mechanic helper include:

  • Strong attention to detail
  • Ability to work in a fast-paced environment
  • Good communication skills
  • Ability to lift heavy objects and work in tight spaces
  • Basic knowledge of tools and equipment

Advancement Opportunities

One of the great things about working as an aircraft mechanic helper is that it can be a stepping stone to a full-time mechanic position. With experience and additional training, you may be able to take on more advanced tasks and eventually become a certified aircraft mechanic.

Tips for Success as an Aircraft Mechanic Helper

If you're interested in pursuing a career as an aircraft mechanic helper, here are some tips to help you succeed:

  • Ask questions and take notes to learn as much as possible from experienced mechanics
  • Be willing to work hard and take on additional responsibilities
  • Take advantage of any training or certification programs offered by your employer
  • Stay up-to-date on industry trends and developments

FAQs About Aircraft Mechanic Helper Job Description

What is the salary for an aircraft mechanic helper?

The salary for an aircraft mechanic helper can vary depending on location and employer. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for aircraft mechanics and service technicians was $64,090 as of May 2020.

What type of training is required to become an aircraft mechanic helper?

While there are no specific educational requirements, having a high school diploma or equivalent is preferred. Additionally, some prior mechanical experience or knowledge of aviation systems can be helpful.

What are the working conditions like for an aircraft mechanic helper?

Working as an aircraft mechanic helper can be physically demanding and may require working in tight spaces or at heights. Additionally, the work environment can be loud and require the use of protective gear such as earplugs or safety glasses.

What are some common tasks performed by an aircraft mechanic helper?

Some common tasks performed by an aircraft mechanic helper include assisting with engine repairs and inspections, replacing components such as brakes and tires, and performing routine inspections and preventative maintenance.

Conclusion of Aircraft Mechanic Helper Job Description

The aircraft mechanic helper position is a great way to gain hands-on experience in the aviation industry and potentially work your way up to a full-time mechanic position. While the work can be physically demanding, it's also rewarding to know that you're playing a role in keeping aircraft in top condition. With a willingness to learn and a strong work ethic, the aircraft mechanic helper position can be a great career choice for those interested in the aviation industry.
