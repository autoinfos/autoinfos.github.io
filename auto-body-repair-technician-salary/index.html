Auto Body Repair Technician Salary What You Need To Know

Written by Bardi Jun 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever considered becoming an auto body repair technician? It can be a rewarding career, but like any job, you need to know what you can expect to earn. In this post, we'll dive into the world of auto body repair technician salary and give you the information you need to make an informed decision.

One of the biggest pain points for those considering a career in auto body repair is the uncertainty around salary. Many people worry that they won't make enough money to support themselves or their families. Others may wonder if the job is even worth pursuing if the pay is too low. These concerns are valid, but with the right information, you can make an informed decision about your future.

So, what can you expect to earn as an auto body repair technician? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive body and glass repairers was $44,550 as of May 2020. This means that half of all workers in the field earned more than this amount, while the other half earned less.

Overall, auto body repair technician salary can vary depending on a number of factors, including experience, location, and the type of employer. However, there are some general trends that can help you understand what to expect.

The Importance of Experience

One thing that can have a big impact on auto body repair technician salary is experience. Like many other jobs, the more experience you have, the more money you can expect to earn. This is because experienced technicians are often able to work more efficiently and effectively, which can lead to higher wages.

For example, if you're just starting out in the field, you might earn around $30,000 per year. However, if you have several years of experience under your belt, you could earn closer to $60,000 per year. Of course, this is just a rough estimate, and your earnings will depend on a variety of factors.

Location Matters

Another factor that can impact auto body repair technician salary is location. In general, areas with higher costs of living and higher demand for auto body repair services will offer higher wages. For example, if you live in a major city with a lot of auto body repair shops, you might earn more than someone who lives in a small town with only a few shops.

However, it's important to note that this isn't always the case. Sometimes, even in areas with high demand, employers may offer lower wages due to competition or other factors. That's why it's important to do your research and understand the market in your area.

The Bottom Line on Auto Body Repair Technician Salary

Overall, auto body repair technician salary can vary widely depending on a number of factors. However, with the right experience, location, and employer, you can earn a comfortable living in this field. If you're passionate about cars and enjoy working with your hands, it may be worth considering a career in auto body repair.

Tips for Maximizing Your Earnings

If you're already working in the field or plan to pursue a career in auto body repair, there are a few things you can do to maximize your earnings:

  • Seek out additional certifications or training to become a more valuable employee
  • Consider working at a dealership or other high-paying employer
  • Explore opportunities to work on high-end or specialty vehicles

Questions and Answers

Q: What is the average starting salary for an auto body repair technician?

A: The average starting salary for an auto body repair technician is around $30,000 per year.

Q: How much can I expect to earn as an experienced auto body repair technician?

A: Experienced auto body repair technicians can earn anywhere from $40,000 to $60,000 per year, depending on their location and employer.

Q: Are there any certifications or training programs that can help me increase my earnings?

A: Yes, there are a variety of certifications and training programs available that can help you become a more valuable employee and earn a higher salary.

Q: Is auto body repair technician salary affected by the type of vehicles I work on?

A: Yes, auto body repair technicians who work on high-end or specialty vehicles may be able to earn more than those who work on more common vehicles.

Conclusion of Auto Body Repair Technician Salary

Auto body repair technician salary can vary depending on a number of factors, but with the right experience, location, and employer, you can earn a comfortable living in this field. Whether you're just starting out or you're a seasoned pro, there are always opportunities to increase your earnings and take your career to the next level.
