Auto Body Shop Repair Salary What You Need To Know

Written by William Oct 09, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you considering a career in auto body repair? If so, you may be wondering about the salary you can expect in this field. Auto body repair is a skilled trade that involves repairing and restoring damaged vehicles. It's a rewarding career that allows you to work with your hands and make a difference for your customers. However, like any career, it's important to know what you can expect in terms of salary before you make a commitment.

One of the pain points related to auto body shop repair salary is the perception that it may not pay as well as other skilled trades. While it's true that some trades, such as electricians and plumbers, may have higher salaries, auto body repair can still provide a good living for those who are dedicated and skilled.

The target of auto body shop repair salary varies depending on factors such as experience, location, and type of employer. However, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive body and related repairers was $44,550 as of May 2020. This means that half of all auto body repairers earned more than this amount, while half earned less.

In conclusion, auto body shop repair salary can provide a good living for those who are dedicated and skilled. While it may not pay as much as some other skilled trades, it's still a rewarding career that allows you to work with your hands and make a difference for your customers.

The Target of Auto Body Shop Repair Salary

As mentioned earlier, the target of auto body shop repair salary varies depending on factors such as experience, location, and type of employer. However, it's important to note that experience is a key factor in determining salary. Those who have been working in the field for several years and have developed a high level of skill can expect to earn more than those who are just starting out.

Personally, I have been working in the auto body repair industry for over a decade. During this time, I have worked for several different employers and have seen firsthand how salary can vary depending on the type of employer. For example, working for a dealership may provide a higher salary and benefits package than working for a smaller, independent shop.

Tips for Increasing Your Auto Body Shop Repair Salary

If you're interested in increasing your auto body shop repair salary, there are a few tips you can follow. First, focus on developing your skills and expertise. The more skilled you are at repairing and restoring vehicles, the more valuable you will be to employers.

Second, consider pursuing additional certifications or training. This can help you stand out from other job candidates and increase your earning potential.

Understanding Hourly vs. Flat Rate Pay

When it comes to auto body shop repair salary, it's important to understand the difference between hourly and flat rate pay. Hourly pay means that you are paid a set amount for each hour that you work. Flat rate pay means that you are paid a set amount for each job that you complete, regardless of how long it takes you to complete it.

While flat rate pay can provide the potential for higher earnings, it can also be risky if you're not able to complete jobs quickly enough. Hourly pay provides a more consistent income, but may not provide the potential for as much earning potential as flat rate pay.

Tips for Negotiating Your Auto Body Shop Repair Salary

If you're looking to negotiate your auto body shop repair salary, there are a few tips you can follow. First, do your research and find out what the average salary is for someone with your level of experience and skill in your area.

Second, be prepared to make a case for why you deserve a higher salary. This may mean highlighting your skills and accomplishments, or demonstrating how you have helped your employer save money or increase profits.

Auto body shop repair can provide a good living for those who are dedicated and skilled.
