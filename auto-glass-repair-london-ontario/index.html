How to Prevent Auto Glass Damage in London, Ontario?

While auto glass damage can happen unexpectedly, there are some preventive measures you can take to reduce the risk and protect your windshield and windows. Here are some tips:

  • Drive at a safe distance from other vehicles, especially trucks and buses that can kick up debris.
  • Avoid parking your car under trees or near construction sites that can drop branches or debris.
  • Use a windshield cover or sunshade to protect your windshield from the sun and extreme temperatures.
  • Regularly clean your windshield and windows from dirt, dust, and debris that can scratch or damage them.

Question and Answer

Q: Is auto glass repair covered by my insurance?

A: It depends on your insurance policy and the type of coverage you have. Comprehensive coverage usually covers auto glass repair, but you may need to pay a deductible. Check with your insurance company to see if you're covered.

Q: Can I drive my car immediately after auto glass repair?

A: It depends on the type of repair or replacement and the type of adhesive used. Some adhesives require a curing time of several hours or days, while others can be used immediately. Your auto glass repair company will give you specific instructions on when you can drive your car.

Q: Can I wash my car after auto glass repair?

A: It's recommended to wait at least 24 hours before washing your car after auto glass repair, especially if it involves a windshield replacement. Water and soap can affect the adhesive and compromise the bonding process.

Q: Can I claim auto glass repair on my taxes?

A: In some cases, you may be able to claim auto glass repair on your taxes, especially if it's related to your work or business. Check with your accountant or tax professional to see if you're eligible.
