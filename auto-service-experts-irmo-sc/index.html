Question and Answer

Q: How often should I have my oil changed?

A: It's generally recommended that you have your oil changed every 3,000 miles or every three months, whichever comes first. However, this can vary depending on your car's make and model, so it's best to check your owner's manual for specific recommendations.

Q: Do I really need to rotate my tires?

A: Yes! Regular tire rotations can help extend the life of your tires and improve your car's performance. We recommend having your tires rotated every 5,000 to 7,500 miles.

Q: How do I know when my brakes need to be replaced?

A: If you hear a grinding or squeaking noise when you apply your brakes, or if your car takes longer than usual to come to a stop, it's time to have your brakes checked. We recommend having your brakes inspected at least once a year.

Q: How can I tell if my car needs a tune-up?

A: If your car is running rough, idling poorly, or experiencing a decrease in gas mileage, it may be time for a tune-up. We recommend having your car inspected by a professional mechanic at least once a year to ensure that it's running smoothly.
