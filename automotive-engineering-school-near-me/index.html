Question and Answer

Q: What kind of job can I get with a degree in automotive engineering?

A: Graduates with a degree in automotive engineering can go on to work in a variety of fields, including design, research and development, testing, and manufacturing.

Q: How long does it take to complete a degree in automotive engineering?

A: The length of time it takes to complete a degree in automotive engineering can vary depending on the program and degree level. Typically, a bachelor's degree takes four years to complete, while a master's degree can take an additional two years.

Q: Is it difficult to get accepted into an automotive engineering program?

A: Admission requirements for automotive engineering programs can vary depending on the school and program. However, most programs require a strong academic record and a passion for the field.

Q: What kind of salary can I expect with a degree in automotive engineering?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for mechanical engineers, which includes automotive engineers, was $88,430 as of May 2019.
