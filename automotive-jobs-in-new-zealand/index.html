Discovering Automotive Jobs In New Zealand A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Oct 27, 2023 · 5 min read

If you're looking for a career in the automotive industry, New Zealand is an excellent place to start. The country's bustling economy and diverse job market make it an attractive destination for job seekers from all over the world. However, finding the right job in a new country can be overwhelming, especially if you're not familiar with the local job market. In this post, we'll take a closer look at the automotive jobs available in New Zealand and offer tips on how to find your dream job.

Looking for a job in any industry can be challenging, and the automotive industry is no exception. Some of the common pain points that job seekers face include a lack of relevant qualifications, limited job opportunities, and stiff competition for available jobs. In addition, some job seekers may struggle to adapt to the local work culture or face language barriers, making it harder to land a job.

The target of automotive jobs in New Zealand is to provide employment opportunities for people with different skill sets and experience levels. If you have a passion for cars and enjoy working with your hands or solving complex problems, there are several roles you could consider. These include mechanic, automotive engineer, salesperson, or customer service representative. Depending on your experience and qualifications, you could work for a dealership, a repair shop, or a manufacturing plant.

To summarise, the automotive industry in New Zealand offers diverse job opportunities for people with different skill sets and experience levels. Although finding a job can be challenging, it's not impossible with the right approach. In the next sections, we'll take a closer look at some of the roles available in the automotive industry and how you can increase your chances of landing your dream job.

Exploring the Different Roles in the Automotive Industry

One of the benefits of working in the automotive industry is that there are many roles to choose from. Whether you're interested in design, engineering, sales, or customer service, there's a role that could suit your interests and skills.

Personally, I started my career as a mechanic in a small repair shop. Although it was challenging at first, I enjoyed the hands-on work and the sense of accomplishment that came with fixing cars. Over time, I developed my skills and gained experience, which allowed me to progress to more senior roles, such as a service manager and a technical trainer.

If you're interested in becoming a mechanic, you'll need to complete a relevant qualification, such as a Certificate in Automotive Engineering (Level 3) or a National Certificate in Motor Industry (Automotive Electrical and Mechanical Engineering) (Level 4). Once you've completed your training, you could work as a general mechanic, a specialist mechanic (e.g., working on specific brands or models), or a diagnostic technician.

How to Increase Your Chances of Landing a Job in the Automotive Industry

Although finding a job in the automotive industry can be challenging, there are several things you can do to increase your chances of success. Here are some tips:

1. Tailor your CV to the job you're applying for

When applying for a job, make sure you read the job description carefully and tailor your CV and cover letter to match the requirements. Highlight your relevant skills and experience and explain how they make you a good fit for the role.

2. Network with industry professionals

Attending industry events, joining online groups, or reaching out to industry professionals on LinkedIn can help you expand your network and learn more about the local job market. You could also consider doing some voluntary work or an internship to gain experience and make connections.

3. Be prepared for the interview

Research the company and the role before the interview and prepare some questions to ask the interviewer. Dress appropriately and arrive on time. During the interview, demonstrate your enthusiasm for the role and share examples of how you've used your skills and experience in the past.

4. Consider upskilling or reskilling

If you're struggling to find a job in the automotive industry, it could be worth considering upskilling or reskilling. Look for courses or qualifications that could help you develop new skills or improve your existing ones. For example, you could consider a Certificate in Automotive Refinishing (Level 3) or a National Certificate in Automotive Sales (Level 4).

Question and Answer

Q: What qualifications do I need to become an automotive engineer in New Zealand?

A: To become an automotive engineer in New Zealand, you'll need to have a relevant qualification, such as a Bachelor of Engineering (Mechanical) or a Bachelor of Engineering (Mechatronics). You'll also need to be registered with the Engineering New Zealand and have a minimum of two years' work experience.

Q: What is the average salary for a mechanic in New Zealand?

A: According to Trade Me Jobs, the average salary for a mechanic in New Zealand is around $55,000 per annum. However, this can vary depending on your experience, qualifications, and location.

Q: What are some of the challenges of working in the automotive industry in New Zealand?

A: Some of the challenges of working in the automotive industry in New Zealand include a shortage of skilled workers, increasing competition from overseas markets, and the need to keep up to date with new technologies and environmental regulations.

Q: Is it possible to work in the automotive industry in New Zealand without a qualification?

A: While having a relevant qualification can increase your chances of finding a job in the automotive industry in New Zealand, it's not always essential. Some employers may be willing to provide on-the-job training or offer apprenticeships to people with a passion for cars and a willingness to learn.

Conclusion of Automotive Jobs in New Zealand

Overall, the automotive industry in New Zealand offers diverse job opportunities and a chance to pursue a career in a field that you're passionate about. By following the tips and advice in this guide, you can increase your chances of finding your dream job and building a successful career in the industry.
