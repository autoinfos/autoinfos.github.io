Get Your Automotive Mechanic Certification In Ontario

Written by Robby Jun 22, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you an automotive enthusiast looking to turn your passion into a career? Or maybe you're already a mechanic but want to take your skills to the next level. Regardless of your situation, obtaining an automotive mechanic certification in Ontario can open doors to new opportunities and higher earning potential.

Without certification, you may struggle to find employment or receive fair compensation for your work. Employers often prioritize candidates who have demonstrated their knowledge and skills through certification. Additionally, certification can give you the confidence and credibility to start your own business or specialize in a specific area of automotive repair.

The Ontario Ministry of Training, Colleges and Universities oversees the certification process for automotive mechanics in the province. To become certified, you must complete a training program at a registered vocational school or college and pass a certification exam. The exam covers a range of topics, including engine repair, brakes, suspension, and steering systems.

In addition to completing a formal training program, you must also have a certain amount of work experience to be eligible for certification. The exact requirements vary depending on the type of certification you are seeking. However, expect to have to work in the field for at least a few years before applying for certification.

Why Choose Automotive Mechanic Certification in Ontario?

Personally, I found that obtaining my automotive mechanic certification in Ontario gave me the tools and confidence I needed to excel in the industry. Before certification, I struggled to find steady employment and often felt like I wasn't taken seriously as a mechanic. However, after completing the certification process, I found that doors opened up and more opportunities were available to me.

Furthermore, becoming certified allowed me to specialize in certain areas of automotive repair, such as electrical systems, and charge higher rates for my services. This gave me the financial stability I needed to start my own business and become my own boss.

What Types of Automotive Mechanic Certification are Available in Ontario?

The Ontario Ministry of Training, Colleges and Universities offers several types of automotive mechanic certifications, including:

Automotive Service Technician Certification

This certification is for those who want to specialize in repairing and maintaining cars and light trucks. To become certified, you must complete a three-year apprenticeship or equivalent training program and pass a certification exam.

Truck and Coach Technician Certification

This certification is for those who want to specialize in repairing and maintaining heavy trucks and buses. To become certified, you must complete a four-year apprenticeship or equivalent training program and pass a certification exam.

How to Prepare for the Automotive Mechanic Certification Exam

Preparing for the automotive mechanic certification exam can be challenging, but it is essential for success. Here are some tips for preparing:

1. Review the exam content outline to understand what topics will be covered.

2. Enroll in a certification exam preparation course or program.

3. Take practice exams to get a feel for the format and types of questions.

4. Study consistently and thoroughly, focusing on areas where you need improvement.

FAQs About Automotive Mechanic Certification in Ontario

Q: How long does it take to become certified?

A: The time it takes to become certified varies depending on the type of certification you are seeking. However, expect to spend at least a few years working in the field before applying for certification.

Q: Is certification required to work as an automotive mechanic in Ontario?

A: Certification is not required by law, but it is highly recommended. Many employers prefer to hire certified mechanics, and certification can lead to higher earning potential.

Q: How much does it cost to become certified?

A: The cost of becoming certified varies depending on the training program and certification exam fees. Expect to spend several thousand dollars to complete the certification process.

Q: What is the passing score for the certification exam?

A: The passing score for the certification exam is 70%. If you do not pass on your first attempt, you can retake the exam after a waiting period.

Conclusion of Automotive Mechanic Certification in Ontario

In conclusion, obtaining an automotive mechanic certification in Ontario can be a game-changer for your career in the automotive industry. It can open up new opportunities, increase your earning potential, and give you the confidence and credibility to excel in your field. While the certification process may be challenging, the benefits are well worth the effort.
