Automotive Mechanic Salary Australia What You Need To Know

Written by Oliver Nov 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an automotive mechanic in Australia? Do you want to know what kind of salary you can expect in this field? Look no further, because we have all the information you need right here.

As with any profession, there are some pain points that come with being an automotive mechanic in Australia. For one, the job can be physically demanding and requires a lot of standing, bending, and reaching. Additionally, the work can be dirty and greasy, which can be a turn-off for some people.

However, if you have a passion for cars and enjoy working with your hands, then a career as an automotive mechanic might be right up your alley. Not only can it be a fulfilling job, but it also pays well.

So, let's get down to the nitty-gritty. What kind of salary can you expect as an automotive mechanic in Australia? The answer, of course, depends on a variety of factors, such as your level of experience, location, and the type of employer you work for.

The Average Automotive Mechanic Salary in Australia

According to data from PayScale, the average salary for an automotive mechanic in Australia is AUD $55,102 per year. This figure can range from as low as AUD $41,000 to as high as AUD $91,000 per year, depending on the factors mentioned above.

In my personal experience, I have found that the salary for an automotive mechanic can vary greatly depending on the type of employer you work for. For example, if you work for a large dealership, you may be paid a higher salary than if you work for a small independent repair shop.

How to Increase Your Automotive Mechanic Salary in Australia

If you are looking to increase your salary as an automotive mechanic in Australia, there are a few things you can do. Firstly, you can gain additional certifications or qualifications to make yourself more valuable to employers. This can include obtaining a Certificate III in Light Vehicle Mechanical Technology, or a Certificate IV in Automotive Mechanical Diagnosis.

Additionally, you can look for job opportunities in areas where demand for automotive mechanics is high, such as in remote or regional areas. Employers in these areas may offer higher salaries to attract top talent.

The Benefits of Pursuing a Career as an Automotive Mechanic in Australia

Aside from the competitive salary, there are many other benefits to pursuing a career as an automotive mechanic in Australia. For one, there is a high demand for skilled mechanics, so job security is relatively good. Additionally, there is room for growth and advancement within the field.

How to Become an Automotive Mechanic in Australia

If you are interested in becoming an automotive mechanic in Australia, there are a few steps you need to take. Firstly, you will need to complete a Certificate III in Light Vehicle Mechanical Technology, which is a nationally-recognised qualification. You may also need to complete an apprenticeship, which typically takes four years to complete.

Once you have completed your qualifications, you can start looking for work as an automotive mechanic. It's important to keep in mind that the job market for automotive mechanics can be competitive, so it's a good idea to gain as much experience and education as possible to make yourself stand out to potential employers.

Conclusion of Automotive Mechanic Salary Australia

Overall, a career as an automotive mechanic in Australia can be both financially rewarding and fulfilling. With a competitive salary and room for growth, it's a great career choice for those who have a passion for cars and enjoy working with their hands.

Question and Answer

A: The demand for skilled automotive mechanics in Australia is high, so job security is relatively good.

Q: How long does it take to become an automotive mechanic in Australia?

A: It typically takes four years to complete an apprenticeship and gain the necessary qualifications to become an automotive mechanic in Australia.

Q: What kind of salary can I expect as an entry-level automotive mechanic in Australia?

A: Entry-level automotive mechanics in Australia can expect to earn an average salary of around AUD $45,000 to $50,000 per year.
