Why Automotive Service Technician Career Is In Demand In Canada

Written by Eveline Sep 17, 2023 · 4 min read

Canada has one of the largest automotive industries in the world with millions of registered vehicles. As the automotive industry in Canada continues to grow, the demand for automotive service technicians is also increasing.

Many car owners experience difficulties with their vehicles and require the services of an automotive service technician. These difficulties can range from simple routine maintenance to complex engine repairs.

Automotive service technicians in Canada are responsible for inspecting, diagnosing, repairing, and maintaining vehicles. They work on different types of cars and trucks, from gasoline-powered vehicles to hybrid and electric vehicles.

The automotive industry in Canada is evolving, and so are the skills required to work in this field. Automotive service technicians must be knowledgeable about the latest automotive technologies, including computerized diagnostic equipment, high-tech electronics, and hybrid and electric vehicles.

What is Automotive Service Technician in Canada?

Automotive Service Technicians are skilled professionals who work on different types of vehicles, from cars to trucks. They are responsible for inspecting, diagnosing, repairing, and maintaining vehicles. Automotive service technicians work in a variety of settings, including car dealerships, repair shops, and government agencies.

As an automotive service technician in Canada, I have had the pleasure of working on different types of vehicles. One of the most exciting parts of this job is diagnosing a problem and finding a solution. It is a rewarding feeling to know that I have helped a customer get back on the road safely.

What are the Requirements to Become an Automotive Service Technician in Canada?

Automotive service technicians in Canada are required to complete a high school diploma and an apprenticeship program. The apprenticeship program typically lasts for four years and includes both on-the-job training and classroom instruction.

After completing the apprenticeship program, automotive service technicians must obtain a certificate of qualification, which is issued by the provincial or territorial government. This certificate is required to work as an automotive service technician in Canada.

Types of Certifications for Automotive Service Technicians in Canada

There are different types of certifications available for automotive service technicians in Canada. The most common are:

  • Automotive Service Technician Certificate (AST)
  • Automotive Service Technician - Truck and Transport Certificate (ASTT)
  • Automotive Service Technician - Collision Repair and Refinishing Certificate (ASTC)

How to Maintain Your Vehicle to Avoid Frequent Repairs?

Maintaining your vehicle is essential to avoid frequent repairs. Here are some tips to help you maintain your vehicle:

  • Regularly check your oil levels and change your oil every 5,000 to 7,500 kilometers.
  • Check your tire pressure regularly and rotate your tires every 10,000 kilometers.
  • Replace your air filter every 12,000 to 24,000 kilometers.
  • Replace your spark plugs every 40,000 to 80,000 kilometers.
  • Inspect your brakes every 50,000 kilometers.

FAQs about Automotive Service Technician in Canada

Q. How much does an automotive service technician make in Canada?

A. The average salary for an automotive service technician in Canada is around $50,000 to $60,000 per year.

Q. What are the work hours for an automotive service technician in Canada?

A. Automotive service technicians in Canada typically work 40 hours per week, Monday to Friday. However, some technicians may work evenings and weekends.

Q. Is there a demand for automotive service technicians in Canada?

A. Yes, there is a high demand for automotive service technicians in Canada, and it is expected to grow as the automotive industry continues to evolve.

Q. What skills are required to be an automotive service technician in Canada?

A. Automotive service technicians in Canada must have excellent problem-solving skills, be detail-oriented, and have good communication skills.

Conclusion of Automotive Service Technician in Canada

The automotive industry in Canada is growing, and so is the demand for automotive service technicians. If you are passionate about cars and have an interest in mechanics, a career as an automotive service technician in Canada may be a good fit for you. With the right training and skills, you can have a rewarding career in this exciting field.
