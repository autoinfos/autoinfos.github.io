Question and Answer Section

Q: How often should I change my oil?

A: It is recommended to change your oil every 5,000 to 7,500 miles, depending on your driving habits and the type of oil you use.

Q: What is the difference between ABS and EBD?

A: ABS (anti-lock braking system) prevents your wheels from locking up during hard braking, while EBD (electronic brakeforce distribution) helps to distribute braking force evenly between the front and rear wheels.

Q: How can I tell if my brakes need servicing?

A: Signs that your brakes may need servicing include squeaking or grinding noises, a spongy or soft brake pedal, or a vibrating steering wheel.

Q: How can I improve my car's performance?

A: Some ways to improve your car's performance include upgrading your air intake and exhaust systems, installing a performance chip, and using higher octane fuel.
