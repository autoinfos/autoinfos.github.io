The Truth About Average Mechanical Engineer Salary In New Zealand

Written by Oliver Oct 31, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you a mechanical engineer looking to work in New Zealand? Or are you already working as a mechanical engineer in New Zealand but unsure about your salary? If yes, then you're not alone. Many mechanical engineers in New Zealand are concerned about their earning potential.

As a mechanical engineer, you have invested a lot of time and money in your education, and you deserve to be paid well for your skills and knowledge. However, the reality is that the average mechanical engineer salary in New Zealand is not as high as it is in some other countries.

So, what is the average mechanical engineer salary in New Zealand? According to the latest data from the New Zealand Government, the average salary for a mechanical engineer is NZD 71,000 per year. This is lower than the average salary for all occupations in New Zealand, which is NZD 83,000 per year.

In this article, we'll take a closer look at the average mechanical engineer salary in New Zealand, and provide you with some tips on how to improve your earning potential.

The Average Mechanical Engineer Salary in New Zealand

As we mentioned earlier, the average salary for a mechanical engineer in New Zealand is NZD 71,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on a number of factors, such as:

- Your level of experience

- Your location in New Zealand

- Your industry

- Your employer

For example, mechanical engineers working in Auckland or Wellington generally earn higher salaries than those working in smaller cities or rural areas. Similarly, mechanical engineers working in the manufacturing industry usually earn more than those working in the construction industry.

My Personal Experience

As a mechanical engineer who has worked in New Zealand for several years, I can tell you that the average salary is not a true reflection of what you can earn. With the right skills and experience, you can earn significantly more than the average salary.

When I first started working as a mechanical engineer in New Zealand, my salary was around NZD 60,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and moved into more senior roles, my salary increased to NZD 100,000 per year.

So, my advice to you is to not be too worried about the average salary. Instead, focus on developing your skills and gaining experience, and the money will follow.

How to Improve Your Mechanical Engineer Salary in New Zealand

If you're looking to improve your mechanical engineer salary in New Zealand, here are some tips:

- Gain more experience: The more experience you have, the more valuable you become to employers.

- Upskill: Keep up-to-date with the latest technologies and techniques in your field.

- Network: Attend industry events and conferences to meet new people and learn about new job opportunities.

- Consider moving to a different region or industry: As we mentioned earlier, your location and industry can have a big impact on your salary.

What is the Future Outlook for Mechanical Engineer Salaries in New Zealand?

The future outlook for mechanical engineer salaries in New Zealand is positive. According to the New Zealand Government, there is a high demand for skilled mechanical engineers, especially in the manufacturing and construction industries. This means that salaries are likely to increase in the future as employers compete for the best talent.

How to Negotiate Your Mechanical Engineer Salary in New Zealand?

If you're offered a mechanical engineer job in New Zealand, it's important to negotiate your salary. Here are some tips:

- Do your research: Find out what other mechanical engineers are earning in your industry and location.

- Highlight your skills and experience: Explain to your employer why you're worth more than the average salary.

- Be prepared to negotiate: Don't be afraid to ask for more money, but be willing to compromise.

- Consider other factors: Salary is important, but it's not the only factor to consider. Think about other benefits, such as flexible working arrangements, training opportunities, and career progression.

Conclusion of Average Mechanical Engineer Salary in New Zealand

The average mechanical engineer salary in New Zealand may not be as high as it is in some other countries, but this doesn't mean that you can't earn a good salary as a mechanical engineer in New Zealand. By developing your skills and gaining experience, you can improve your earning potential and achieve your career goals.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average starting salary for a mechanical engineer in New Zealand?

A: The average starting salary for a mechanical engineer in New Zealand is around NZD 55,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on your location and industry.

Q: How much can a senior mechanical engineer earn in New Zealand?

A: A senior mechanical engineer in New Zealand can earn anywhere from NZD 80,000 to NZD 150,000 per year, depending on their skills, experience, and industry.

Q: What industries pay the highest salaries for mechanical engineers in New Zealand?

A: The manufacturing and construction industries generally pay the highest salaries for mechanical engineers in New Zealand.

Q: Is it possible to earn more than the average mechanical engineer salary in New Zealand?

A: Yes, it is possible to earn significantly more than the average mechanical engineer salary in New Zealand. By developing your skills and gaining experience, you can improve your earning potential and achieve your career goals.
