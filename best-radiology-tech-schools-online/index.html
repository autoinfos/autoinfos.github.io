The Top Online Radiology Tech Schools

1. University of Cincinnati

The University of Cincinnati offers an online Bachelor of Science in Radiation Science Technology program, which is accredited by the Joint Review Committee on Education in Radiologic Technology (JRCERT). This program covers all aspects of radiologic technology, including imaging techniques, radiation safety, and patient care.

2. Weber State University

Weber State University's online Bachelor of Science in Radiologic Sciences program is also accredited by the JRCERT. This program includes coursework in anatomy, medical terminology, and radiologic physics, as well as hands-on clinical experience at a local healthcare facility.

3. Indiana University

Indiana University's online Bachelor of Science in Radiologic Sciences program is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission and the JRCERT. In addition to coursework in radiologic technology, this program also includes a focus on leadership and management skills, preparing students for higher-level positions in the field.

FAQs About Online Radiology Tech Schools

Q: Are online radiology tech programs accredited?

A: Yes, reputable online radiology tech programs should be accredited by the Joint Review Committee on Education in Radiologic Technology (JRCERT) or another recognized accrediting body.

Q: Can I complete clinical requirements for an online radiology tech program in my local area?

A: Yes, many online radiology tech programs require students to complete clinical requirements at a local healthcare facility. This allows students to gain hands-on experience and apply their knowledge in a real-world setting.

Q: How long does it take to complete an online radiology tech program?

A: The length of an online radiology tech program can vary depending on the school and program. However, most programs take around two to four years to complete.

Q: What careers can I pursue with a degree in radiologic technology?

A: Graduates of radiology tech programs can pursue a variety of careers, including radiologic technologist, MRI technologist, CT technologist, and radiation therapist.
