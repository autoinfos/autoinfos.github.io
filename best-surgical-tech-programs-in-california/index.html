Best Surgical Tech Programs In California Your Guide To Finding The Right One

Written by Daniel Jun 27, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you looking to start a career in the medical field as a surgical technician? If you're in California, you're in luck because the state offers some of the best surgical tech programs in the country. However, with so many options available, it can be overwhelming to choose the right one for you. In this blog post, we'll guide you through the process of finding the best surgical tech program in California.

When it comes to choosing a surgical tech program, there are a few pain points to consider. Firstly, you want to ensure that the program is accredited by the Commission on Accreditation of Allied Health Education Programs (CAAHEP) or the Accrediting Bureau of Health Education Schools (ABHES). Secondly, you want to consider the program's cost and location, as well as the length of the program and the availability of financial aid. Finally, you want to ensure that the program offers the necessary hands-on training and clinical experience to prepare you for your career.

The target of best surgical tech programs in California is to provide aspiring surgical technicians with the knowledge and skills necessary to succeed in their careers. These programs typically include a combination of classroom and clinical training, covering topics such as anatomy, medical terminology, sterile techniques, and surgical procedures. Graduates of these programs are eligible to sit for the Certified Surgical Technologist (CST) exam, which is required for employment as a surgical technician.

In summary, when looking for the best surgical tech program in California, consider factors such as accreditation, cost, location, program length, financial aid availability, and hands-on training. With so many options available, it's important to do your research and choose a program that aligns with your career goals and learning style.

Accredited Programs

The first step in finding the best surgical tech program in California is to ensure that the program is accredited by either CAAHEP or ABHES. Accreditation ensures that the program meets strict educational standards and is recognized by employers and certification organizations. Some of the top accredited surgical tech programs in California include:

1. National Career Education
2. American College of Healthcare
3. North-West College
4. Concorde Career College
5. Gurnick Academy of Medical Arts
6. Institute of Technology
7. California Career College
8. Summit College
9. Unitek College
10. Carrington College

These programs offer a combination of classroom and clinical training, as well as hands-on experience in surgical settings. They also offer financial aid options for those who qualify, making them accessible to students from all backgrounds.

Personal Experience

As someone who went through a surgical tech program in California, I can attest to the importance of choosing an accredited program with hands-on training. During my program, I was able to work in a variety of surgical settings, including hospitals and outpatient clinics, which allowed me to gain valuable experience and apply what I learned in the classroom to real-life situations. Additionally, my program offered financial aid options, which helped me cover the cost of tuition while I pursued my career.

Program Length and Cost

When considering the best surgical tech programs in California, it's important to think about program length and cost. Most programs range from 9-24 months, depending on the school and the level of certification you're seeking. The cost of these programs can also vary, with some programs costing as little as $10,000, while others can cost upwards of $40,000. However, many programs offer financial aid options such as grants, loans, and scholarships to help make the cost more manageable.

Hands-On Training

One of the most important aspects of any surgical tech program is hands-on training. This allows students to gain practical experience in surgical settings and learn from experienced professionals. Look for programs that offer clinical rotations or internships in hospitals and outpatient clinics, as well as opportunities for students to observe live surgeries. These experiences will allow you to develop the skills and knowledge necessary to succeed in your career.

Certification Exam

Once you've completed your surgical tech program, you'll need to pass the CST exam in order to become certified. This exam is administered by the National Board of Surgical Technology and Surgical Assisting (NBSTSA) and covers topics such as anatomy, physiology, surgical procedures, and sterile techniques. It's important to prepare for this exam by studying and reviewing the material covered in your program, as well as taking practice exams and seeking guidance from your instructors.

Personal Experience

After completing my surgical tech program and passing the CST exam, I was able to secure a job in a hospital and start my career. The knowledge and skills I gained from my program, as well as the hands-on training and clinical experience, prepared me for the challenges and rewards of working in the surgical field. I continue to learn and grow in my career, and I'm grateful for the foundation that my surgical tech program provided me.

Choosing the best surgical tech program in California can be a daunting task, but by considering factors such as accreditation, cost, location, program length, financial aid availability, and hands-on training, you can find the right program for you.
