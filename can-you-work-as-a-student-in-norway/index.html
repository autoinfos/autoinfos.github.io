Can You Work As A Student In Norway Everything You Need To Know

Written by Oliver Sep 08, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering studying in Norway but worried about the possibility of working as a student? Look no further, as we have all the information you need to know about working as a student in Norway.

Many international students are curious about the possibility of working in Norway, whether it be to support themselves financially or gain work experience in their field of study. However, navigating the Norwegian job market as a student can be daunting, especially if you are not familiar with the country's laws and regulations.

The short answer is yes, you can work as a student in Norway. However, there are certain restrictions and requirements you must meet before you can start working.

To work as a student in Norway, you must be enrolled in a full-time study program that lasts for at least one academic year. You must also have a valid student residence permit and a Norwegian tax card.

My Experience Working as a Student in Norway

As an international student studying in Norway, I was curious about the possibility of working part-time to support myself financially. After doing some research and consulting with my university's career center, I was able to secure a part-time job as a research assistant in my field of study.

Working as a student in Norway was a great experience for me, as it allowed me to gain valuable work experience while also earning some extra income. However, I did have to balance my studies with work, which could be challenging at times.

Types of Jobs Available for Students in Norway

As a student in Norway, you are eligible to work part-time for up to 20 hours per week during the academic year and full-time during the summer months. There are several types of jobs available for students, including:

  • Retail and customer service
  • Tutoring and teaching
  • Research and administrative assistant positions
  • Freelance and remote work

How to Find a Job as a Student in Norway

There are several resources available to help you find a job as a student in Norway. Your university's career center is a great place to start, as they may have job listings and resources specifically for students. You can also search for jobs on online job boards and websites such as Finn.no and Nav.no.

Tips for Working as a Student in Norway

If you are considering working as a student in Norway, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Make sure you have a valid student residence permit and Norwegian tax card
  • Balance your studies with work to avoid burnout
  • Be aware of your employment rights and responsibilities
  • Network and make connections in your field of study to increase your chances of finding work

FAQs: Can You Work as a Student in Norway?

Q: How many hours can I work as a student in Norway?

A: You can work up to 20 hours per week during the academic year and full-time during the summer months.

Q: Do I need a work permit to work as a student in Norway?

A: No, you do not need a separate work permit to work as a student in Norway. However, you must have a valid student residence permit and a Norwegian tax card.

Q: Can I work as a student in Norway without speaking Norwegian?

A: Yes, many jobs in Norway do not require fluency in Norwegian, especially in the tech and hospitality industries. However, knowing Norwegian can be an advantage when searching for jobs.

Q: Can I work as a student in Norway after I graduate?

A: Yes, if you have a job offer in Norway after you graduate, you can apply for a work permit and extend your residence permit to work in Norway.

Conclusion of Can You Work as a Student in Norway

Working as a student in Norway can be a great way to gain work experience, support yourself financially, and network in your field of study. However, it is important to be aware of the restrictions and requirements before you start working. Make sure you have a valid student residence permit and Norwegian tax card, balance your studies with work, and network to increase your chances of finding work. With the right preparation and mindset, you can successfully work as a student in Norway.
