Car Dealership Detailer Job Description What You Need To Know

Written by Bardi Jul 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

When it comes to car dealerships, there are a variety of roles that keep the business running smoothly. One of those roles is the car dealership detailer. If you're considering a career in this field, it's important to know what the job entails and what you can expect on a day-to-day basis.

As a car dealership detailer, you'll be responsible for ensuring that vehicles are cleaned and prepared for sale or delivery to customers. This can be a physically demanding job, as you'll be required to spend a lot of time on your feet and may need to lift heavy equipment or supplies. Additionally, you'll need to be comfortable working with a variety of cleaning products and tools.

The target of the car dealership detailer job description is anyone who is interested in pursuing a career in the automotive industry. Whether you're just starting out or looking for a new challenge, this role can be a great way to get your foot in the door and gain valuable experience.

In summary, the car dealership detailer job description involves cleaning and preparing vehicles for sale or delivery to customers. It can be physically demanding and requires comfort with a variety of cleaning products and tools.

What Does a Car Dealership Detailer Do?

As mentioned earlier, the primary responsibility of a car dealership detailer is to ensure that vehicles are clean and ready for sale or delivery. This involves a variety of tasks, including:

  • Washing and drying vehicles
  • Vacuuming and shampooing carpets and upholstery
  • Cleaning and polishing windows and mirrors
  • Detailing the engine compartment

One personal experience I had as a car dealership detailer was when I was tasked with cleaning a particularly dirty vehicle that had been traded in. The interior was covered in dirt and debris, and it took several hours to get it looking presentable. However, seeing the transformation from a dirty, neglected car to a clean, shiny one was incredibly satisfying.

What Skills Do You Need to Be a Car Dealership Detailer?

In order to be successful as a car dealership detailer, there are several key skills you'll need to have. These include:

  • Attention to detail: You'll need to be meticulous in your work, ensuring that every inch of the vehicle is clean and polished.
  • Physical stamina: This job can be physically demanding, so you'll need to be in good physical shape and able to stand for long periods of time.
  • Effective time management: Detailing a vehicle can take several hours, so you'll need to be able to manage your time effectively and work efficiently to get the job done.
  • Strong communication skills: You'll need to be able to communicate effectively with your colleagues and customers, particularly when it comes to explaining the work you've done on a vehicle.

How Much Does a Car Dealership Detailer Earn?

The salary for a car dealership detailer can vary depending on a number of factors, including the dealership's location, the employee's experience level, and whether or not they receive any commission or bonuses. According to Glassdoor, the national average salary for a car dealership detailer is $25,000 per year.

How Can You Become a Car Dealership Detailer?

If you're interested in becoming a car dealership detailer, there are a few steps you can take to get started. First, you'll need to have a high school diploma or GED. From there, you can look for entry-level positions at local car dealerships or auto detailing shops. You may also want to consider getting certified through a detailing program, which can help you stand out from other job applicants.

Conclusion of Car Dealership Detailer Job Description

Overall, the car dealership detailer job description involves cleaning and preparing vehicles for sale or delivery to customers. It can be a physically demanding job, but can also be incredibly rewarding for those who are passionate about cars and have an eye for detail. If you're interested in pursuing a career in this field, it's important to have the necessary skills and qualifications, as well as a willingness to work hard and learn on the job.

Question and Answer

Q: What kind of training do you need to become a car dealership detailer?
A: While a high school diploma or GED is typically required, there are no specific educational requirements for this job. However, getting certified through a detailing program can help you stand out from other job applicants.

Q: Is the car dealership detailer job description the same as an auto detailer?
A: Yes, the two terms are generally interchangeable, though the specific job duties may vary slightly depending on the employer.

Q: What are some challenges you might face as a car dealership detailer?
A: Some challenges can include working in hot or cold weather, dealing with difficult or demanding customers, and lifting heavy equipment or supplies.

Q: What kind of career advancement opportunities are there for car dealership detailers?
A: Depending on the dealership, there may be opportunities to advance to a management or supervisory role, or to specialize in a particular area such as detailing high-end luxury vehicles.
