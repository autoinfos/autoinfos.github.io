Careers After Automotive Technician Exploring Your Options

Written by Christine Oct 19, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you an automotive technician looking for a change? Maybe you've been in the industry for years and are ready for a new challenge. Or perhaps you're just starting out and wondering what your career path might look like. Whatever your situation, there are plenty of exciting opportunities out there for those with automotive experience.

One common pain point for automotive technicians is the physical demands of the job. Lifting heavy parts and working in awkward positions can take a toll on your body over time. Additionally, the pay scale for technicians can be limited, which can make it difficult to support a family or save for the future.

If you're ready for a change, there are many career paths you can consider. The skills you've developed as an automotive technician are highly transferable and can open doors in a variety of industries. Whether you're interested in sales, management, or technical writing, there are opportunities out there that could be a great fit for you.

In this article, we'll explore some of the most popular career paths for automotive technicians, as well as some lesser-known options. We'll also share some tips for making the transition to a new field and finding success in your new role.

1. Sales

If you're a people person with a knack for persuasion, a career in sales might be right up your alley. Many automotive technicians find success in sales roles, as they have a deep understanding of the products they're selling and can speak to their features and benefits with authority. Whether you're selling cars, parts, or services, there are opportunities to earn a good income in sales.

My personal experience with this was when I transitioned to selling cars. It was a natural fit for me, as I already knew a lot about the technical aspects of the vehicles. I was able to earn a good income and enjoyed the challenge of meeting sales goals.

2. Management

If you're a natural leader with excellent communication skills, a career in management might be a good fit for you. Many automotive technicians transition into management roles, where they oversee a team of technicians and are responsible for scheduling, training, and quality control. This can be a rewarding path for those who enjoy mentoring others and seeing their team succeed.

My personal experience with this was when I became a service manager at a dealership. It was a challenging role, but I enjoyed being able to help my team grow and develop their skills.

3. Technical Writing

If you have a talent for writing and a deep understanding of automotive systems, a career in technical writing might be a good fit for you. Technical writers are responsible for creating manuals, guides, and other instructional materials that help people understand complex systems. This can be a great fit for those who enjoy working independently and have a passion for sharing their knowledge with others.

4. Entrepreneurship

If you have an entrepreneurial spirit and a passion for the automotive industry, starting your own business could be a great option. Whether you want to open your own repair shop or start a parts distribution business, there are plenty of opportunities for those with the skills and drive to succeed.

One important thing to keep in mind when starting your own business is that it can be a risky proposition. You'll need to be prepared to invest time and money into your venture and be willing to take some calculated risks.

Question and Answer

Q: What skills do I need to transition into a new career?

A: The skills you've developed as an automotive technician are highly transferable. You'll need to focus on developing your communication, leadership, and problem-solving skills to succeed in a new field.

Q: How can I make the transition to a new career?

A: Networking is key when transitioning to a new career. Reach out to people in your desired field and ask for informational interviews. Attend industry events and conferences to learn more about the opportunities available.

Q: What are some common misconceptions about transitioning out of the automotive industry?

A: One common misconception is that your skills are only valuable in the automotive industry. In reality, the skills you've developed as an automotive technician can be highly transferable and can open doors in a variety of fields.

Q: How do I know if a new career is right for me?

A: It's important to do your research and talk to people in your desired field to get a sense of what the work is really like. You may also want to consider doing some volunteer work or taking a part-time job in your desired field to see if it's a good fit.

Conclusion of Careers after Automotive Technician

Whether you're ready for a change or just curious about your options, there are plenty of exciting careers out there for automotive technicians. From sales and management to technical writing and entrepreneurship, the skills you've developed as an automotive technician are highly transferable and can open doors in a variety of industries. So don't be afraid to explore your options and take the leap into a new and exciting career!
