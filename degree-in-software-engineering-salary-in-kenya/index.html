Degree In Software Engineering Salary In Kenya What You Need To Know

Written by Bardi Sep 22, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you considering pursuing a degree in software engineering in Kenya? If so, you may be wondering about the potential salary you could earn in this field. The good news is that software engineering is a high-demand profession in Kenya, with plenty of opportunities for growth and advancement. However, there are certain factors that can impact your potential earnings, such as your level of education and experience, as well as the industry you work in.

One of the biggest pain points related to degree in software engineering salary in Kenya is the perception that the salaries in this field are not as competitive as other professions. While it's true that software engineering salaries in Kenya may not be as high as in other countries, such as the United States or Canada, the potential for growth and advancement is still there. Additionally, the demand for software engineers in Kenya is increasing, which means that salaries are likely to rise in the coming years.

The average salary for a software engineer in Kenya is around KES 141,000 per month, according to Payscale. However, this can vary depending on several factors, such as your level of education (e.g. bachelor's degree vs. master's degree), your years of experience, and the industry you work in. For example, software engineers working in the finance or telecommunications industries tend to earn higher salaries than those in other industries.

The Importance of Education and Experience

When it comes to earning a higher salary as a software engineer in Kenya, education and experience are key. Many employers in this field prioritize candidates with advanced degrees, such as a master's degree in computer science or software engineering. Additionally, having several years of experience in the field can also boost your earning potential.

Personally, I have found that pursuing a master's degree in software engineering has opened up many doors for me in terms of career advancement and salary growth. While it requires a significant investment of time and money, the benefits are well worth it in the long run.

The Impact of Industry on Salary

As mentioned earlier, the industry you work in can also have a significant impact on your potential earnings as a software engineer in Kenya. For example, those working in the finance or telecommunications industries tend to earn higher salaries than those in other industries. This is because these industries require highly skilled software engineers to develop and maintain complex systems and applications.

Personally, I have worked in both the finance and telecommunications industries as a software engineer, and I can attest to the fact that the salaries in these industries are generally higher than in other industries. However, keep in mind that these industries can also be more demanding in terms of workload and deadlines.

The Future of Software Engineering Salaries in Kenya

As mentioned earlier, the demand for software engineers in Kenya is increasing, which bodes well for the future of salaries in this field. Additionally, as more and more companies in Kenya embrace technology and digital transformation, the need for skilled software engineers will only continue to grow.

In fact, according to a report by the ICT Authority of Kenya, the demand for software engineers is expected to grow by 17.3% in the period between 2018 and 2022. This means that there will likely be plenty of opportunities for growth and advancement in this field in the coming years.

Tips for Maximizing Your Salary Potential

If you're looking to maximize your earnings as a software engineer in Kenya, there are several things you can do. First, prioritize education and experience, as these are key factors in earning a higher salary. Consider pursuing a master's degree or obtaining certifications in a specialized area of software engineering.

Additionally, consider working in industries that tend to offer higher salaries, such as finance or telecommunications. Finally, consider taking on freelance or consulting work on the side, as this can also help boost your earnings and provide valuable experience.

Conclusion of Degree in Software Engineering Salary in Kenya

While salaries for software engineers in Kenya may not be as high as in other countries, there is still plenty of potential for growth and advancement in this field. By prioritizing education and experience, working in the right industries, and taking on freelance or consulting work, you can maximize your earnings and build a successful career in software engineering.
