Diesel Mechanic Salary 2020 All You Need To Know

Written by Luffy Oct 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering becoming a diesel mechanic, but unsure about the salary prospects in 2020? Look no further, as we break down everything you need to know about diesel mechanic salaries in 2020.

It's no secret that the economy has taken a hit in 2020, and many industries have been affected, including the automotive industry. With that being said, diesel mechanics are still in high demand and continue to earn competitive salaries.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for diesel service technicians and mechanics was $48,500 in May 2019. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $32,980, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $72,180. These salaries may vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and industry.

In summary, diesel mechanics can still expect to earn competitive salaries in 2020. While the pandemic has affected the economy, the demand for diesel mechanics remains strong.

What is the Median Salary for Diesel Mechanics?

As mentioned earlier, the median annual wage for diesel service technicians and mechanics was $48,500 in May 2019. However, this number can vary depending on several factors. For example, diesel mechanics working in the government sector can earn a median annual wage of $54,550, while those working in the automotive repair and maintenance industry can earn a median annual wage of $46,360. Experience also plays a role, as diesel mechanics with more years of experience typically earn higher salaries.

My Personal Experience as a Diesel Mechanic

As a diesel mechanic with over a decade of experience, I can attest to the competitive salaries in the industry. While starting out may not yield the highest salary, hard work and dedication can lead to higher wages and even opportunities for career advancement.

How to Become a Diesel Mechanic

If you're interested in becoming a diesel mechanic, there are several steps you can take. Firstly, obtaining a high school diploma or equivalent is necessary. From there, you can pursue postsecondary education in diesel technology, which can include certificate programs, associate's degrees, and even bachelor's degrees. Gaining hands-on experience through internships or apprenticeships can also be beneficial in gaining employment and advancing in the field.

The Importance of Certification

Becoming certified as a diesel mechanic can also lead to higher salaries and more job opportunities. The National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) offers certification for diesel mechanics, which can demonstrate a high level of skill and knowledge in the field.

Negotiating Your Salary as a Diesel Mechanic

Once you've gained experience and certification as a diesel mechanic, it's important to negotiate your salary to ensure you're being fairly compensated for your skills and expertise. Researching industry standards and knowing your worth can help in negotiating a higher salary.

FAQs about Diesel Mechanic Salary 2020

Q: Can diesel mechanics earn overtime pay?

A: Yes, diesel mechanics can earn overtime pay, especially if they work for companies that require 24/7 maintenance on their diesel-powered vehicles and equipment.

Q: Do diesel mechanics receive benefits?

A: Yes, many employers offer benefits packages to diesel mechanics, which can include health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off.

Q: Can diesel mechanics work remotely?

A: It's rare for diesel mechanics to work remotely, as the nature of the job requires hands-on work with diesel-powered vehicles and equipment.

Q: Can diesel mechanics advance in their careers?

A: Yes, diesel mechanics can advance in their careers by gaining experience and certifications, which can lead to higher salaries and opportunities for promotions into management positions.

Conclusion of Diesel Mechanic Salary 2020

Overall, diesel mechanics can still expect to earn competitive salaries in 2020. While the pandemic has affected the economy, the demand for diesel mechanics remains strong, and with the right education, experience, and certifications, diesel mechanics can advance in their careers and earn even higher salaries.
