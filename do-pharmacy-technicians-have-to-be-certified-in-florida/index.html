Do Pharmacy Technicians Have To Be Certified In Florida

Written by Robby Aug 27, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you interested in becoming a pharmacy technician in Florida? If so, you may be wondering if certification is required. This can be a confusing topic, as the requirements can vary depending on your location and the specific job you are applying for.

Many employers prefer to hire pharmacy technicians who are certified, as this demonstrates a certain level of knowledge and dedication to the profession. However, certification is not always required by law.

In Florida, pharmacy technicians are not required to be certified in order to work in a pharmacy. However, there are some important considerations to keep in mind.

First, certification can improve your job prospects and potentially increase your salary. Additionally, some employers may require certification as a condition of employment.

The Benefits of Certification

While certification is not mandatory in Florida, there are several reasons why you may want to consider pursuing it.

First, becoming certified can demonstrate your commitment to the profession and your desire to provide the highest level of care to patients. It can also show potential employers that you have a certain level of knowledge and expertise in the field.

Second, certification can potentially increase your salary. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, certified pharmacy technicians earn a higher median wage than those who are not certified.

My Personal Experience

As a certified pharmacy technician myself, I can attest to the benefits of certification. Not only did it improve my job prospects and earning potential, but it also helped me feel more confident and knowledgeable in my role. I highly recommend pursuing certification if you are considering a career as a pharmacy technician.

How to Become Certified

If you are interested in becoming a certified pharmacy technician in Florida, there are several steps you will need to take.

First, you will need to complete a training program that is accredited by the Accreditation Council for Pharmacy Education (ACPE) or the American Society of Health-System Pharmacists (ASHP).

Once you have completed your training, you will need to pass the Pharmacy Technician Certification Exam (PTCE) or the Exam for the Certification of Pharmacy Technicians (ExCPT). These exams test your knowledge of pharmacy law, medication safety, and other important topics.

The Importance of Continuing Education

Even after you become certified, it is important to continue your education and stay up-to-date on the latest developments in the field. This can help you provide the best possible care to patients and advance your career.

There are many options for continuing education, including online courses, conferences, and workshops. You may also want to consider pursuing additional certifications, such as specialized certifications in areas like sterile compounding or chemotherapy.

Conclusion

In summary, pharmacy technicians in Florida are not required to be certified, but certification can offer many benefits. If you are considering a career as a pharmacy technician, I highly recommend pursuing certification through an accredited training program and passing the certification exam. This can help you stand out to potential employers and potentially increase your salary. And even after you become certified, continuing education can help you stay at the top of your game and provide the best possible care to patients.

Question and Answer

Q: Is certification required to work as a pharmacy technician in Florida?

A: No, certification is not required by law, but many employers may prefer to hire certified technicians.

Q: How can certification improve my job prospects and salary?

A: Certification can show potential employers that you have a certain level of knowledge and expertise in the field, which can make you a more attractive candidate. Additionally, certified pharmacy technicians tend to earn a higher median wage than those who are not certified.

Q: What steps do I need to take to become a certified pharmacy technician in Florida?

A: You will need to complete an accredited training program and pass the Pharmacy Technician Certification Exam (PTCE) or the Exam for the Certification of Pharmacy Technicians (ExCPT).

Q: Why is continuing education important for pharmacy technicians?

A: Continuing education can help pharmacy technicians stay up-to-date on the latest developments in the field and provide the best possible care to patients. It can also help them advance their careers and pursue specialized certifications in areas like sterile compounding or chemotherapy.

Conclusion of Do Pharmacy Technicians Have to be Certified in Florida

While certification is not required by law for pharmacy technicians in Florida, it can offer many benefits. Pursuing certification through an accredited training program and passing the certification exam can help you stand out to potential employers and potentially increase your salary. And even after you become certified, continuing education is important to stay at the top of your game and provide the best possible care to patients.
