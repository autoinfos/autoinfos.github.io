Does Autozone Sell Brake Pads

Written by Aprilia Aug 04, 2023 · 4 min read

When it comes to maintaining your car's brake system, finding the right brake pad replacement can be a daunting task. With so many brands and types available in the market, it can be challenging to choose the right one for your car. However, the biggest question that most car owners have is whether AutoZone sells brake pads.

Replacing brake pads is a necessary evil in maintaining your car's safety and performance. It's a task that should be done regularly to avoid accidents on the road. However, finding the right brake pads for your vehicle can be a challenge, especially if you're not sure where to look.

AutoZone is one of the largest auto parts retailers in the United States, and they offer a wide variety of car parts and accessories, including brake pads. Whether you're looking for ceramic or metallic brake pads, AutoZone has got you covered.

In summary, yes, AutoZone sells brake pads. They offer a wide range of brake pads that are suitable for different types of vehicles, ensuring that you can easily find the right brake pads for your car.

Types of Brake Pads Available at AutoZone

AutoZone offers a wide range of brake pads to suit different types of vehicles, driving styles, and budgets. Some of the brake pads available at AutoZone include ceramic, semi-metallic, and organic brake pads.

Ceramic brake pads are the most popular type of brake pads for most cars. They are made from ceramic fibers, nonferrous filler materials, and bonding agents. Ceramic brake pads are known for their durability, low dust production, and low noise levels.

Semi-metallic brake pads, on the other hand, are made from a mixture of metal and other materials. They are more durable than ceramic brake pads and are ideal for high-performance vehicles that require more stopping power.

Organic brake pads are made from non-metallic materials, including rubber, glass, and Kevlar fibers. They are softer and quieter than other types of brake pads, but they don't last as long.

How to Choose the Right Brake Pads for Your Car

Choosing the right brake pads for your car can be overwhelming, but it's essential to ensure your safety on the road. When choosing brake pads, consider the type of driving you do, your budget, and the type of vehicle you own.

If you're looking for brake pads that produce less dust and noise, ceramic brake pads are an excellent choice. However, if you drive a high-performance vehicle, semi-metallic brake pads are the best option as they offer better stopping power.

How to Replace Your Brake Pads

If you're replacing your brake pads for the first time, it's essential to seek the help of a professional mechanic. However, if you're a DIY enthusiast, you can replace your brake pads using the following steps:

  • Loosen the lug nuts on the wheel
  • Jack up the car and remove the tire
  • Remove the brake caliper
  • Remove the old brake pads and install new ones
  • Reinstall the brake caliper and tire
  • Lower the car and tighten the lug nuts

Question and Answer

Q: What brand of brake pads does AutoZone sell?

A: AutoZone sells a wide range of brake pad brands, including Duralast, Bosch, and Wagner.

Q: Can I return brake pads to AutoZone?

A: Yes, you can return brake pads to AutoZone within 90 days of purchase, provided the pads are in their original packaging and unused.

Q: How much do brake pads cost at AutoZone?

A: The cost of brake pads at AutoZone varies depending on the brand, type, and size. On average, you can expect to pay between $20 and $100 per set of brake pads.

Q: Does AutoZone offer installation services for brake pads?

A: No, AutoZone does not offer installation services for brake pads. However, they have a wide range of how-to guides and videos on their website that you can use to install the brake pads yourself.

Conclusion of Does AutoZone Sell Brake Pads

AutoZone is a one-stop-shop for all your car part needs, including brake pads. They offer a wide range of brake pads that are suitable for different types of vehicles, driving styles, and budgets. When choosing brake pads, consider the type of driving you do, your budget, and the type of vehicle you own.
