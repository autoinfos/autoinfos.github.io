Entry Level Mechanic Hourly Pay What You Need To Know

Written by Aprilia Sep 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an entry level mechanic? Do you want to know more about the hourly pay for this profession? If so, keep reading.

Working as an entry level mechanic can be a rewarding career choice for those who enjoy working with their hands and have a passion for cars. However, one of the biggest challenges for those starting out in this field is the hourly pay.

The average hourly pay for entry level mechanics is around $15 per hour, according to data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics. However, this can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and the type of employer.

In this article, we will explore the topic of entry level mechanic hourly pay in more detail, including the factors that can affect it and what you can do to increase your earning potential.

What Affects Entry Level Mechanic Hourly Pay?

Several factors can affect the hourly pay of entry level mechanics. One of the main factors is experience. Those who have been working in the field for several years and have gained additional certifications and training may be able to command a higher hourly rate.

Location can also play a role in entry level mechanic hourly pay. For example, mechanics working in urban areas may earn more than those working in rural areas due to higher demand and cost of living.

My Personal Experience with Entry Level Mechanic Hourly Pay

When I started out as an entry level mechanic, I was making around $14 per hour. However, as I gained more experience and certifications, my hourly pay increased to around $18 per hour. I also found that working for a larger dealership offered higher pay and better benefits compared to working for a smaller independent shop.

How Can You Increase Your Entry Level Mechanic Hourly Pay?

If you want to increase your earning potential as an entry level mechanic, there are several steps you can take. One of the most important is to gain additional certifications and training. This can demonstrate your expertise and make you a more valuable employee.

You can also look for opportunities to work for larger dealerships or automotive manufacturers, which often offer higher pay and better benefits. Networking with other mechanics and industry professionals can also help you learn about job opportunities and negotiate for higher pay.

The Benefits of Being an Entry Level Mechanic

While the hourly pay for entry level mechanics may not be the highest, there are many benefits to this career. For one, it can be a very fulfilling job for those who enjoy working with their hands and solving problems. Additionally, there is often a high demand for mechanics, which means that job security can be strong.

How to Get Started as an Entry Level Mechanic

If you want to become an entry level mechanic, there are several steps you can take. First, you will need to complete a high school education or equivalent. From there, you can pursue additional training and certifications through vocational schools, community colleges, or apprenticeship programs.

Conclusion of Entry Level Mechanic Hourly Pay

While entry level mechanic hourly pay may not be the highest, there are many opportunities for growth and advancement in this field. By gaining additional certifications and training, networking with other professionals, and seeking out job opportunities with larger employers, you can increase your earning potential and build a rewarding career in the automotive industry.
