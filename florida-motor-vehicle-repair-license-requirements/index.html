Florida Motor Vehicle Repair License Requirements

Written by Daniel Jun 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering starting a motor vehicle repair business in Florida, but unsure about the license requirements? Look no further! In this blog post, we will break down the necessary steps to obtain a motor vehicle repair license in Florida, and provide helpful tips along the way.

Dealing with license requirements can be a headache, especially if you are new to the industry or have never gone through the process before. It can be overwhelming trying to navigate the various rules and regulations, and ensuring that you are meeting all of the necessary requirements.

The first step towards obtaining a motor vehicle repair license in Florida is to apply for an Occupational License, which is issued by the Florida Department of Business and Professional Regulation (DBPR). This license is required for any individual or business that engages in motor vehicle repair, including painting, bodywork, and mechanical repairs.

Once you have obtained your Occupational License, you will need to apply for a Motor Vehicle Repair Registration, which is also issued by the DBPR. This registration requires proof of liability insurance, worker's compensation insurance (if you have employees), and a surety bond (if you are repairing vehicles that weigh over 5,000 pounds).

Insurance Requirements

Having proper insurance coverage is a crucial part of obtaining a motor vehicle repair license in Florida. The minimum liability insurance coverage required is $25,000 for injury or death of one person, $50,000 for injury or death of two or more people, and $10,000 for property damage.

It's important to note that you will also need to provide proof of insurance coverage to the DBPR when you apply for your Motor Vehicle Repair Registration. Keep in mind that your insurance coverage must be maintained throughout the duration of your license.

Experience Requirements

While there are no specific experience requirements to obtain a motor vehicle repair license in Florida, it is highly recommended that you have some experience in the industry before starting your own business. Having a solid foundation of knowledge and experience will help to ensure that you are providing high-quality repairs and services to your customers.

Training Requirements

Although there are no formal training requirements to obtain a motor vehicle repair license in Florida, there are several educational programs available that can help you to gain the necessary knowledge and skills. These programs range from short courses and workshops, to full-length degree programs.

Renewal Requirements

Once you have obtained your motor vehicle repair license and registration in Florida, you will need to renew them on an annual basis. This renewal process requires that you submit a renewal application, along with any necessary fees and documentation.

Conclusion of Florida Motor Vehicle Repair License Requirements

Obtaining a motor vehicle repair license in Florida may seem overwhelming at first, but by following the necessary steps and requirements, you can ensure that you are on the right path to starting a successful business. Remember to keep up-to-date with any changes to the regulations, and always strive to provide high-quality repairs and services to your customers.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the difference between an Occupational License and a Motor Vehicle Repair Registration?

A: An Occupational License is required for any individual or business that engages in motor vehicle repair, while a Motor Vehicle Repair Registration is a specific registration that is required in addition to the Occupational License.

Q: Do I need to provide proof of insurance coverage when applying for my Occupational License?

A: No, proof of insurance is only required when applying for your Motor Vehicle Repair Registration.

Q: Can I obtain a motor vehicle repair license if I have a criminal record?

A: It depends on the nature of the offense. The DBPR will review each application on a case-by-case basis and determine whether the individual is eligible for a license.

Q: How long does it take to obtain a motor vehicle repair license in Florida?

A: The length of time it takes to obtain a license can vary, depending on the individual's qualifications and the completeness of their application. It's recommended to start the process early and allow plenty of time for processing.
