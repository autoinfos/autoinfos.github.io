Exploring Graduate Engineer Salary In Kenya What To Expect

Written by Petter Nov 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you a recent graduate engineer in Kenya? Are you wondering what your salary expectations should be? The truth is, the engineering field is vast and salaries can vary depending on your specialty, experience, and location. Keep reading to learn more about graduate engineer salary in Kenya and what you can expect.

As a graduate engineer in Kenya, you may face some challenges when it comes to salary expectations. Some companies may offer lower salaries than what you were hoping for, while others may require years of experience before offering higher salaries. It can be frustrating, but it's important to keep in mind that salary negotiation is always an option.

So, what can you expect in terms of graduate engineer salary in Kenya? According to payscale.com, the average salary for a graduate engineer in Kenya is KSh 686,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on your field of engineering. For example, a graduate civil engineer can expect an average salary of KSh 753,000 per year, while a graduate software engineer can expect an average salary of KSh 707,000 per year.

In summary, graduate engineer salaries in Kenya can vary depending on your field of engineering, experience, and location. It's important to research and negotiate your salary to ensure that you are being paid fairly.

What to Consider When Negotiating Your Salary

When negotiating your salary as a graduate engineer in Kenya, there are several factors to consider. Firstly, your level of experience will play a big role in determining your salary. If you have completed internships or have some work experience, you may be able to negotiate a higher salary than a recent graduate.

Secondly, your field of engineering can also affect your salary negotiation. Some fields, such as software engineering or data science, are in high demand and can command higher salaries. On the other hand, some fields may not be as in-demand and can result in lower salaries.

My Personal Experience with Graduate Engineer Salary in Kenya

As a recent graduate civil engineer in Kenya, I was initially offered a salary that was lower than what I was expecting. However, after negotiating with my employer, I was able to secure a higher salary that was more in line with my expectations and qualifications.

It's important to remember that salary negotiation is always an option, and it's worth doing your research and preparing before entering into negotiations with your employer.

Factors that Affect Graduate Engineer Salary in Kenya

Several factors can affect graduate engineer salary in Kenya. These include:

  • Field of engineering
  • Level of experience
  • Location of job
  • Type of employer (government vs. private sector)

Tips for Negotiating Your Graduate Engineer Salary in Kenya

When negotiating your graduate engineer salary in Kenya, it's important to:

  • Do your research on salary expectations in your field of engineering
  • Prepare a list of your qualifications and achievements to present to your employer
  • Consider alternative forms of compensation, such as benefits or flexible work arrangements
  • Be confident and assertive in your negotiations
