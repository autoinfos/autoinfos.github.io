According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for diesel service technicians and mechanics, which includes heavy duty mechanics, was $49,150 as of May 2020. This means that half of all workers in this field earned more than this amount, while half earned less. The top 10% of earners made more than $73,000 per year.

In conclusion, working as a heavy duty mechanic can be a lucrative career choice, with the potential to earn a solid income even in your first year on the job. Keep in mind that your salary may vary based on a number of factors, but with experience and dedication, you can work your way up to higher paying positions.

Factors That Can Impact Heavy Duty Mechanic Yearly Salary

While the median annual salary for heavy duty mechanics is around $49,150, there are many factors that can impact your earnings. For example, your location can play a big role in how much you can make. In areas where heavy duty mechanics are in high demand, you may be able to command a higher salary.

Your years of experience can also impact your salary. Generally speaking, the more experience you have, the more you can expect to make. Additionally, the type of employer you work for can play a role. Working for a large company may offer more stability and potentially higher pay, while working for a smaller business may offer more flexibility and room for growth.

Location and Heavy Duty Mechanic Yearly Salary

Location is one of the biggest factors that can impact your salary as a heavy duty mechanic. For example, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the states with the highest average salaries for diesel service technicians and mechanics (which includes heavy duty mechanics) as of May 2020 were Alaska, Hawaii, and the District of Columbia.

Of course, this doesn't mean that you can't earn a good salary in other areas. It simply means that your location can play a role in how much you can make, and it's worth considering when choosing where to work.

Experience and Heavy Duty Mechanic Yearly Salary

Your years of experience as a heavy duty mechanic can also impact your salary. Generally speaking, the more experience you have, the more you can expect to earn. For example, according to Payscale.com, heavy duty mechanics with 1-4 years of experience earn an average of $20.22 per hour, while those with 5-9 years of experience earn an average of $23.48 per hour.

Keep in mind that these are just averages, and your specific salary will depend on a number of factors. However, it's worth noting that experience can play a big role in how much you can earn as a heavy duty mechanic.
