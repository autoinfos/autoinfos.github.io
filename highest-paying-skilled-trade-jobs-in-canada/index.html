Highest Paying Skilled Trade Jobs In Canada

Written by Christine Jul 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending years in college and accumulating a mountain of debt, only to find out that the job market is oversaturated? If you're looking for a career that pays well and doesn't require a university degree, then skilled trades might be the answer you've been searching for. In Canada, there is a high demand for skilled tradespeople, and the salaries can be quite lucrative.

Many people are unaware of the opportunities available in the skilled trades, and assume that they are low-paying jobs with little room for growth. However, this couldn't be further from the truth. Skilled tradespeople are in high demand across Canada, and some of the highest paying jobs fall under this category.

If you're interested in pursuing a career in the skilled trades, there are several routes you can take. You can attend a trade school, complete an apprenticeship, or obtain certification through a professional association. The path you choose will depend on your personal preferences and the specific trade you're interested in.

In this article, we'll explore some of the highest paying skilled trade jobs in Canada, and provide you with information on how to get started in these careers.

Electrician

As an electrician, you'll be responsible for installing and maintaining electrical systems in homes, businesses, and industrial settings. The average salary for an electrician in Canada is $65,000 per year, with the potential to earn even more with experience and additional certifications.

Personally, I know an electrician who started as an apprentice and worked his way up to owning his own electrical contracting business. He earns a six-figure salary and has the freedom to work on his own terms.

Plumber

Plumbers are responsible for installing and maintaining plumbing systems in residential, commercial, and industrial settings. The average salary for a plumber in Canada is $70,000 per year, with the potential to earn more with experience and additional certifications.

A friend of mine became a plumber after completing an apprenticeship. He now owns his own plumbing business and earns upwards of $100,000 per year.

Millwright

Millwrights are responsible for installing, maintaining, and repairing heavy machinery in industrial settings. The average salary for a millwright in Canada is $75,000 per year, with the potential to earn more with experience and additional certifications.

I have a family member who is a millwright and he enjoys the challenge of working with complex machinery. He has also been able to earn a comfortable living for himself and his family.

Welder

Welders are responsible for using heat to join metal pieces together. They work in a variety of settings, including construction, manufacturing, and repair work. The average salary for a welder in Canada is $50,000 per year, with the potential to earn more with experience and additional certifications.

A neighbor of mine became a welder after attending a trade school. He now works for a manufacturing company and earns a salary that allows him to provide for his family and pursue his interests outside of work.

Question and Answer

Q: Do I need a university degree to work in the skilled trades?

A: No, you don't need a university degree to work in the skilled trades. Many trades require completion of an apprenticeship or certification through a professional association.

Q: What are some other high paying skilled trade jobs in Canada?

A: Other high paying skilled trade jobs in Canada include HVAC technician, carpenter, and heavy equipment operator.

Q: How do I get started in the skilled trades?

A: To get started in the skilled trades, you can attend a trade school, complete an apprenticeship, or obtain certification through a professional association. Research the specific trade you're interested in to determine the best path for you.

Q: Is there a high demand for skilled tradespeople in Canada?

A: Yes, there is a high demand for skilled tradespeople in Canada. As the population grows and infrastructure ages, there is a need for skilled tradespeople to build and maintain homes, businesses, and public infrastructure.

Conclusion of Highest Paying Skilled Trade Jobs in Canada

If you're looking for a career that pays well and doesn't require a university degree, then the skilled trades may be the perfect fit for you. Electricians, plumbers, millwrights, and welders are just a few of the highest paying skilled trade jobs in Canada. With the right training and experience, you can earn a comfortable living and enjoy a fulfilling career in the skilled trades.
