How Can A Foreigner Get A Job In New Zealand

Written by Bardi Nov 11, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you a foreigner looking to work in New Zealand? The thought of moving to a new country and finding a job can be daunting, but with the right information and approach, it is definitely achievable. In this blog post, we will discuss the steps you can take to increase your chances of finding employment in New Zealand.

One of the biggest pain points for foreigners looking to work in New Zealand is the visa process. Without a valid work visa, it can be challenging to secure a job. Additionally, New Zealand employers tend to prefer candidates who are already in the country and have a work visa, as it eliminates the need for them to sponsor a visa.

The first step in obtaining a job in New Zealand as a foreigner is to apply for a work visa. You can do this through Immigration New Zealand, and the process will depend on your individual circumstances. It is essential to research the different types of visas available and choose the one that best suits your needs.

In addition to applying for a work visa, networking and building connections in your industry can increase your chances of finding employment. Many jobs in New Zealand are not advertised, and instead, employers rely on recommendations and referrals from their networks. Attending industry events and reaching out to people in your field can help you build these vital connections.

In summary, to increase your chances of finding employment in New Zealand as a foreigner, you should first apply for a work visa through Immigration New Zealand. Additionally, networking and building connections in your industry can be beneficial in finding employment opportunities.

Understanding the Work Visa Options

When it comes to obtaining a work visa in New Zealand, there are several options available. The most common is the Essential Skills work visa, which requires a job offer from a New Zealand employer. This visa is valid for up to five years and can be renewed.

Another option is the Skilled Migrant Category visa, which is for individuals who possess skills that are in demand in New Zealand. This visa requires an application process that involves submitting an Expression of Interest and meeting specific criteria.

My personal experience with obtaining a work visa in New Zealand was through the Essential Skills work visa. I secured a job offer from a New Zealand employer, and they sponsored my visa application. It was a relatively smooth process, but it did take some time to gather all the necessary documentation.

Building Connections in Your Industry

Networking and building connections in your industry is crucial when it comes to finding employment opportunities in New Zealand. Attending industry events, joining professional associations, and reaching out to people in your field can help you build these connections.

When I first moved to New Zealand, I attended several industry events and met a lot of people in my field. One of these connections led to a job offer that was not advertised, and I was able to secure employment without going through a lengthy application process.

Researching Companies and Job Opportunities

Researching companies and job opportunities is also essential when it comes to finding employment in New Zealand. Many jobs are not advertised, and instead, employers rely on recommendations and referrals from their networks. It is essential to do your research and target companies that align with your skills and experience.

When I was looking for employment in New Zealand, I researched companies in my field and identified ones that aligned with my experience. I then reached out to them directly and inquired about any job opportunities. This approach led to several interviews and ultimately, a job offer.

Preparing Your Resume and Cover Letter

Preparing your resume and cover letter is critical when it comes to finding employment in New Zealand. New Zealand employers tend to prefer candidates with local experience, so it is essential to tailor your application to the New Zealand job market.

When I was applying for jobs in New Zealand, I made sure to highlight my relevant experience and skills in my resume and cover letter. I also researched the company and the job requirements and made sure to address them in my application.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I apply for a work visa before securing a job offer in New Zealand?

A: Yes, it is possible to apply for a work visa without a job offer. However, you will need to meet certain criteria, such as having a certain amount of funds and demonstrating that you are actively seeking employment in New Zealand.

Q: How long does the visa application process take?

A: The visa application process can take several weeks to several months, depending on the type of visa you are applying for and your individual circumstances.

Q: Do I need to have local experience to obtain a job in New Zealand?

A: While having local experience can be beneficial, it is not always necessary. Employers in New Zealand are often looking for individuals with the right skills and experience, regardless of where they gained it.

Q: Are there any industries in New Zealand that are particularly in demand?

A: Yes, there are several industries in New Zealand that are experiencing growth and are in demand, such as healthcare, IT, and construction.

Conclusion

Obtaining a job in New Zealand as a foreigner can be challenging, but with the right approach, it is achievable. Applying for a work visa, networking and building connections, researching companies and job opportunities, and preparing your application materials are all essential steps in finding employment in New Zealand. By following these steps, you can increase your chances of securing a job in this beautiful country.
