How Do I Become A Powerline Technician

Written by Petter Nov 15, 2023 · 4 min read

Do you love working outdoors and solving technical problems? Do you want to pursue a career that offers job security and good pay? If yes, then becoming a powerline technician might be the right career for you. In this blog post, we will guide you through the process of how to become a powerline technician.

The road to becoming a powerline technician can be challenging, but it's not impossible. One of the biggest pain points that people face is the physical demands of the job, as it requires working in all kinds of weather conditions and at great heights. Another challenge is the extensive training and certification required to work with high voltage electrical equipment.

The first step to becoming a powerline technician is to earn a high school diploma or GED. After that, you can start looking for apprenticeship programs offered by local utility companies or trade schools. These programs typically last for three to four years and combine classroom instruction with on-the-job training.

Once you complete the apprenticeship program, you will need to pass a certification exam administered by the National Joint Apprenticeship and Training Committee (NJATC). This certification is necessary to work as a powerline technician and ensures that you have the knowledge and skills required to work safely with high voltage electrical equipment.

My Personal Experience

I always had an interest in working with electrical equipment and solving technical problems. After completing my high school education, I joined an apprenticeship program offered by a local utility company. During the program, I learned the technical skills required to work with high voltage electrical equipment and worked alongside experienced powerline technicians. After completing the program, I passed the certification exam and started working as a powerline technician.

Skills Required to Become a Powerline Technician

To become a powerline technician, you will need to have good physical fitness, problem-solving skills, and the ability to work well in a team. You should also be comfortable working at heights, as the job involves climbing poles and working on electrical towers. Additionally, you should have a good understanding of electrical theory and be able to read and interpret electrical diagrams.

Job Outlook and Salary

The job outlook for powerline technicians is excellent, as the demand for electricity continues to grow. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for electrical power-line installers and repairers was $75,030 in May 2020, with the top 10% earning more than $109,620.

Work Environment

Powerline technicians work outdoors, and their job involves climbing poles, working on electrical towers, and installing and repairing electrical lines. They work in all kinds of weather conditions and may have to work during emergencies, such as storms and power outages.

FAQs

What is the job of a powerline technician?

A powerline technician installs, repairs, and maintains electrical power systems. They work on high voltage electrical equipment, such as transformers, circuit breakers, and switchgear. They climb poles, work on electrical towers, and install and repair electrical lines.

What education do I need to become a powerline technician?

You need a high school diploma or GED to become a powerline technician. After that, you can join an apprenticeship program offered by a local utility company or trade school.

How long does it take to become a powerline technician?

It takes three to four years to complete an apprenticeship program and become a certified powerline technician.

What is the job outlook for powerline technicians?

The job outlook for powerline technicians is excellent, as the demand for electricity continues to grow. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of electrical power-line installers and repairers is projected to grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029.

Conclusion of How Do I Become a Powerline Technician

Becoming a powerline technician requires dedication, hard work, and a strong commitment to safety. However, it can be a highly rewarding career that offers good pay, job security, and the opportunity to work outdoors and solve technical problems. We hope this blog post has answered your questions about how to become a powerline technician.
