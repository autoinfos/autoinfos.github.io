How To Get Sponsored To Work In Australia

Written by Oliver May 24, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you dreaming of working in Australia but unsure of how to secure sponsorship? It can be a confusing and overwhelming process, but with the right information and guidance, it can become a reality.

Many people face the same pain points when it comes to getting sponsored to work in Australia. One of the biggest challenges is finding an employer who is willing to sponsor you. Employers are required to prove that they have tried to fill the position with an Australian citizen or permanent resident before sponsoring a foreign worker. Additionally, navigating the visa application process can be complicated and time-consuming.

The first step to getting sponsored to work in Australia is finding a job that is eligible for sponsorship. The Australian government has a list of eligible skilled occupations that are in high demand. Once you have found a job in your occupation, the employer will need to sponsor you by nominating you for a visa. You will then need to apply for the visa and meet the eligibility requirements.

In summary, to get sponsored to work in Australia, you need to:

- Find a job in an eligible skilled occupation

- Have your employer nominate you for a visa

- Apply for the visa and meet the eligibility requirements

My Personal Experience

When I decided to move to Australia, I knew that getting sponsored would be the best way for me to stay long-term. I started by researching eligible skilled occupations and found a job in my field. I then applied and was offered the job, and my employer nominated me for a visa. The visa application process was daunting, but with the help of a migration agent, I was able to submit a successful application and start my new job in Australia.

Using a Migration Agent

If you are feeling overwhelmed by the visa application process or unsure of where to start, using a migration agent can be a great option. These professionals are trained in Australian immigration law and can help guide you through the process. They can also help ensure that your application is complete and meets all the requirements.

Eligibility Requirements

When applying for a sponsored visa, there are several eligibility requirements that you must meet. These include:

- Having the required skills and qualifications for your nominated occupation

- Meeting English language requirements

- Meeting health and character requirements

Visa Options

There are several visa options available for those seeking sponsorship to work in Australia. These include:

- Temporary Skill Shortage visa (subclass 482)

- Employer Nomination Scheme visa (subclass 186)

- Regional Sponsored Migration Scheme visa (subclass 187)

Networking and Job Search

It's important to start networking and job searching early on in your sponsorship journey. Attend job fairs and networking events in your field, and consider reaching out to employers directly. Additionally, there are many job search websites and recruitment agencies that specialize in finding sponsorship opportunities.

Question and Answer

Q: How long does it take to get sponsored to work in Australia?

A: The process can take anywhere from a few months to over a year, depending on the visa and individual circumstances.

Q: Can I change jobs while on a sponsored visa?

A: It is possible, but you will need to find a new employer who is willing to sponsor you and apply for a new visa.

Q: How much does it cost to apply for a sponsored visa?

A: The cost varies depending on the visa type and individual circumstances. It can range from a few thousand dollars to over ten thousand dollars.

Q: Can I bring my family with me on a sponsored visa?

A: Yes, many sponsored visas allow you to bring your family members with you. However, you will need to meet additional eligibility requirements and apply for their visas separately.

Conclusion of How to Get Sponsored to Work in Australia

Getting sponsored to work in Australia can seem like a daunting task, but with the right information and support, it can become a reality. By finding an eligible skilled occupation, having your employer nominate you, and meeting the eligibility requirements, you can start your new life and career in Australia.
