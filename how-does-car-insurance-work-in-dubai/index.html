How Does Car Insurance Work In Dubai A Guide For Expats

Written by Aprilia Aug 18, 2023 · 4 min read

Driving in Dubai can be an exciting experience for expats, but it comes with a lot of responsibilities too. Car insurance is one such responsibility that every driver in Dubai must bear in mind. In this blog post, we will guide you through the basics of how does car insurance work in Dubai and why it is important for expats.

As an expat, you may have a lot of questions related to car insurance in Dubai. Some of the common pain points include:

  • What type of car insurance do I need?
  • How much does it cost?
  • What does it cover?
  • What are the legal requirements?
  • How can I find the best car insurance provider?

Let's answer these questions one by one.

What Type of Car Insurance Do I Need?

In Dubai, there are two types of car insurance – third-party liability and comprehensive insurance. While third-party liability insurance is mandatory by law, comprehensive insurance is optional. Third-party liability insurance covers damages caused to third-party property or person, while comprehensive insurance covers damages to your own vehicle as well.

As an expat, it is recommended to opt for comprehensive insurance to ensure maximum coverage and protection for your vehicle.

How Much Does It Cost?

The cost of car insurance in Dubai depends on several factors such as the type of insurance, make and model of the car, driver's age and driving history, and more. On average, comprehensive insurance can cost anywhere between AED 1500 to AED 7000 per year.

It is important to note that the cheapest car insurance may not always be the best. It is advisable to compare quotes from multiple providers and choose the one that offers the best coverage and value for money.

What Does It Cover?

Comprehensive car insurance in Dubai typically covers accidental damage, theft or fire, third-party liability, medical expenses, and roadside assistance. Some insurance providers may also offer additional benefits such as agency repairs, personal accident cover, and more.

What Are the Legal Requirements?

As per UAE law, every vehicle on the road must have third-party liability insurance. The minimum coverage limit for third-party liability insurance is AED 250,000 for property damage and AED 200,000 for bodily injury or death.

It is important to note that driving without valid insurance can lead to severe penalties such as fines, impounding of the vehicle, and even imprisonment in some cases.

How Can I Find the Best Car Insurance Provider?

There are several insurance providers in Dubai that offer car insurance policies. It is recommended to research and compare quotes from multiple providers to find the best coverage and value for money. You can also consult with an insurance broker or agent who can guide you through the process and help you find the right insurance policy for your needs.

Conclusion

Car insurance is a crucial aspect of driving in Dubai, and as an expat, it is important to understand how does car insurance work in Dubai. By opting for comprehensive insurance, comparing quotes from multiple providers, and ensuring that you comply with legal requirements, you can ensure maximum protection and peace of mind while driving on the roads of Dubai.
