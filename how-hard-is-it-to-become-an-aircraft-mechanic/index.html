How Hard Is It To Become An Aircraft Mechanic

Written by Oliver Jun 03, 2023 · 5 min read

Have you ever wondered how hard is it to become an aircraft mechanic? If you're considering a career in aviation, becoming an aircraft mechanic is a great option. However, it's not an easy path. It requires a lot of hard work, dedication, and commitment to become a licensed aircraft mechanic.

One of the biggest pain points of becoming an aircraft mechanic is the extensive training and education required. You'll need to complete a certified aviation maintenance technician program, which can take up to two years. Additionally, you'll need to complete at least 18 months of on-the-job training under the supervision of a licensed mechanic.

But, is it worth it? Absolutely. Becoming an aircraft mechanic is a rewarding career, both personally and financially. You'll have the satisfaction of knowing that you're responsible for ensuring the safety of the aircraft and the passengers on board. And, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for aircraft mechanics and service technicians was $65,230 in May 2020.

In summary, becoming an aircraft mechanic is not an easy task, but it's definitely worth the effort. It requires extensive training, education, and on-the-job experience. However, the rewards are great, both personally and financially.

Why Is Becoming an Aircraft Mechanic So Hard?

When I decided to become an aircraft mechanic, I had no idea how challenging the process would be. The first hurdle was finding a certified aviation maintenance technician program. There are only a few schools that offer this program, and they're highly competitive. Once I was accepted into the program, I had to commit to two years of intense coursework and hands-on training.

After completing the program, I still had 18 months of on-the-job training to complete. This was a challenging time, as I was learning new skills and techniques every day. However, I had the support of my colleagues and supervisor, who were always willing to answer my questions and provide guidance.

What Are the Requirements to Become an Aircraft Mechanic?

To become an aircraft mechanic, you'll need to complete a certified aviation maintenance technician program. These programs are offered by a few schools across the country and can take up to two years to complete. Once you've completed the program, you'll need to pass a series of exams to become a licensed aircraft mechanic. Additionally, you'll need to complete at least 18 months of on-the-job training under the supervision of a licensed mechanic.

How Can I Prepare for a Career in Aviation Maintenance?

If you're interested in a career in aviation maintenance, there are a few things you can do to prepare. First, focus on your math and science skills in high school. These skills will be essential in your aviation maintenance training. Additionally, consider taking a few courses in aviation maintenance or mechanics at a local community college or vocational school. This will give you a head start on your training and help you decide if this is the right career path for you.

What Are Some of the Challenges of Working as an Aircraft Mechanic?

Working as an aircraft mechanic can be challenging, both physically and mentally. You'll need to be able to work in tight spaces and at heights. Additionally, you'll need to be able to handle the pressure of ensuring the safety of the aircraft and passengers. However, if you're passionate about aviation and enjoy working with your hands, these challenges can be overcome.

My Personal Experience as an Aircraft Mechanic

When I first decided to become an aircraft mechanic, I had no idea how challenging the process would be. However, I was determined to pursue my passion for aviation maintenance. I completed a certified aviation maintenance technician program and 18 months of on-the-job training. Today, I'm proud to be a licensed aircraft mechanic and have a rewarding career in aviation maintenance.

A: To become an aircraft mechanic, you'll need to have strong math and science skills, good hand-eye coordination, and a strong attention to detail.

Conclusion of How Hard Is It to Become an Aircraft Mechanic

In conclusion, becoming an aircraft mechanic is a challenging career path, but it's worth the effort. It requires extensive training, education, and on-the-job experience, but the rewards are great. If you're passionate about aviation and enjoy working with your hands, becoming an aircraft mechanic could be the right career path for you.
