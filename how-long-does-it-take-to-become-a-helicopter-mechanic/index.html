How Long Does It Take To Become A Helicopter Mechanic

Written by Luffy Aug 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a helicopter mechanic but wondering how long it will take to become one? Becoming a helicopter mechanic can be a rewarding and exciting career, but it does require time and dedication to achieve. In this article, we will explore the process of becoming a helicopter mechanic and provide insight into how long it takes to achieve this goal.

Before we dive into the specifics of how long it takes to become a helicopter mechanic, let's first discuss some of the challenges that aspiring mechanics may face. One of the main challenges is the amount of time and money that it takes to complete the necessary training and certification. Additionally, the job can be physically demanding and requires a high level of attention to detail to ensure the safety of the aircraft.

So, how long does it take to become a helicopter mechanic? The answer to this question depends on a few factors such as the level of education you have completed, your work experience, and the type of certification you are seeking. Generally, it takes anywhere from 18 months to 2 years to complete the necessary training and certification to become a helicopter mechanic.

In summary, becoming a helicopter mechanic requires time, dedication, and a significant investment in education and training. However, for those who are willing to put in the effort, it can be a rewarding and fulfilling career.

Types of Certification

There are several types of certifications available for those interested in becoming a helicopter mechanic. The most common are the Airframe and Powerplant (A&P) certification and the Inspection Authorization (IA) certification.

Personally, I obtained my A&P certification after completing a 2-year program at a technical school. The program covered a wide range of topics such as aircraft systems, physics, and maintenance practices. After completing the program, I had to pass a series of exams to obtain my certification.

On-the-Job Experience

While completing an education program is an important step in becoming a helicopter mechanic, on-the-job experience is also crucial. Throughout my career, I have gained valuable experience by working with experienced mechanics and learning from them. This has helped me to develop a deeper understanding of aircraft maintenance and has enabled me to troubleshoot and solve problems more effectively.

Continuing Education

Even after obtaining certification and gaining on-the-job experience, it's important to continue learning and staying up-to-date with new technologies and practices. This can be achieved through attending seminars, workshops, and other training opportunities.

How to Get Started

If you're interested in becoming a helicopter mechanic, the first step is to research education and training programs in your area. Look for programs that are accredited by organizations such as the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) and the Accrediting Commission of Career Schools and Colleges (ACCSC).

Question and Answer

Q: How much does it cost to become a helicopter mechanic?
A: The cost of becoming a helicopter mechanic can vary depending on the program and level of certification you are seeking. On average, it can cost anywhere from $20,000 to $40,000.

Q: Is there a high demand for helicopter mechanics?
A: Yes, there is a growing demand for helicopter mechanics as the aviation industry continues to expand.

Q: What skills are needed to become a helicopter mechanic?
A: Some of the skills that are important for helicopter mechanics include attention to detail, problem-solving, and good communication skills.

Q: What is the job outlook for helicopter mechanics?
A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the job outlook for aircraft and avionics equipment mechanics and technicians is projected to grow 5 percent from 2019 to 2029, which is faster than the average for all occupations.

Conclusion of How Long Does it Take to Become a Helicopter Mechanic

In conclusion, becoming a helicopter mechanic can be a rewarding and exciting career path. However, it requires time, dedication, and a significant investment in education and training. By completing an accredited program, gaining on-the-job experience, and continuing to learn and stay up-to-date with new technologies and practices, you can achieve your goal of becoming a helicopter mechanic.
