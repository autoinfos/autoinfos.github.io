How Long Is School For Aircraft Mechanic

Written by Bardi Jun 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering becoming an aircraft mechanic? It can be an exciting and rewarding career, but the process of getting there can seem daunting. One of the biggest questions on your mind may be how long is school for aircraft mechanic? The answer isn't straightforward, but read on to learn more.

When considering how long is school for aircraft mechanic, it's important to note that there are a few different paths you can take. The most common route is attending an FAA-approved aviation maintenance technician school. These schools typically take 18-24 months to complete, and upon graduation, you'll have your FAA Airframe and Powerplant (A&P) certification.

If attending a school full-time for 18-24 months isn't feasible for you, there are other options. You can also complete an apprenticeship program, which can take up to four years. This program involves working under the supervision of a certified aircraft mechanic while also taking classes to learn the necessary skills. Another option is enlisting in the military and receiving training through their program.

In summary, how long is school for aircraft mechanic? It largely depends on the path you choose to take. While attending an FAA-approved school is the most common route, it's not the only one. An apprenticeship program or military training can also lead to a career as an aircraft mechanic.

How Long is School for Aircraft Mechanic: My Personal Experience

When I decided to pursue a career as an aircraft mechanic, I knew I wanted to attend an FAA-approved school. I enrolled in a two-year program and was able to graduate with my A&P certification. It was a challenging program, but the skills I learned and the connections I made were invaluable.

How Long is School for Aircraft Mechanic: The Pros and Cons

Attending an FAA-approved school for 18-24 months may seem like a lot of time to invest in your education, but there are many benefits. Firstly, you'll receive a comprehensive education that covers all aspects of aircraft maintenance. Additionally, having an A&P certification can make you a more attractive job candidate and can lead to higher pay.

On the other hand, attending school full-time for two years may not be feasible for everyone. It can also be expensive, with tuition costs ranging from $20,000-$50,000. However, there are financial aid options available, such as scholarships and grants.

How Long is School for Aircraft Mechanic: Explained in Detail

As previously mentioned, attending an FAA-approved school for 18-24 months is the most common way to become an aircraft mechanic. These schools offer a comprehensive education that covers both airframe and powerplant maintenance. The curriculum includes both classroom instruction and hands-on training.

If you choose to complete an apprenticeship program, you'll work under the supervision of a certified aircraft mechanic while also taking classes to learn the necessary skills. These programs can take up to four years to complete and often require a high school diploma or GED.

If you're interested in receiving military training to become an aircraft mechanic, you can enlist in the Air Force or Army. The training programs vary in length, but typically take 12-18 months to complete. Upon graduation, you'll receive your A&P certification and be qualified to work on military aircraft.

How Long is School for Aircraft Mechanic: Tips for Success

If you're considering attending an FAA-approved school to become an aircraft mechanic, here are a few tips to help you succeed:

  1. Stay organized and manage your time effectively.
  2. Take advantage of any hands-on training opportunities.
  3. Build relationships with your instructors and classmates.
  4. Stay up-to-date on industry news and developments.

When it comes to how long is school for aircraft mechanic, there are a few different paths you can take.
