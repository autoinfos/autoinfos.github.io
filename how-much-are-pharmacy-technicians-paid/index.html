How Much Are Pharmacy Technicians Paid A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a pharmacy technician? If so, you may be wondering about the salary potential for this profession. In this article, we'll explore the average pay for pharmacy technicians and provide some insight into what factors can impact your earning potential.

As with any profession, there are certain pain points to consider when it comes to pharmacy technician salaries. For one, there can be a wide range of pay depending on location and experience level. Additionally, some pharmacy technicians may feel that their compensation does not accurately reflect the level of responsibility they hold within their job.

So, how much are pharmacy technicians paid on average? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for pharmacy technicians in the United States is $34,020. This breaks down to an hourly wage of $16.35. However, it's important to note that this is just a median and some pharmacy technicians may earn more or less depending on certain factors.

In terms of what can impact your earning potential as a pharmacy technician, there are a few key factors to consider. For one, your level of experience can play a big role in how much you are paid. Generally speaking, more experienced pharmacy technicians will earn a higher salary. Additionally, location can also impact your pay. For example, pharmacy technicians working in urban areas may earn more than those working in rural areas.

How Much Are Pharmacy Technicians Paid? My Personal Experience

As a pharmacy technician with five years of experience, I can say that my salary has increased significantly since I first started in this profession. When I first began, I was making around $13 per hour. However, as I gained more experience and proved my worth to my employer, my salary increased to around $20 per hour.

One thing that I have noticed is that location can play a big role in how much pharmacy technicians are paid. I currently work in a large city and I know that pharmacy technicians working in more rural areas may not earn as much as those in urban areas.

Factors That Impact How Much Pharmacy Technicians Are Paid

Aside from experience and location, there are a few other factors that can impact how much pharmacy technicians are paid. For example, the type of employer you work for can have an impact on your salary. Pharmacy technicians working for hospitals or other healthcare facilities may earn more than those working in retail settings.

Additionally, certification can also impact your earning potential as a pharmacy technician. While it is not required by law, becoming certified as a pharmacy technician can help boost your salary and make you a more competitive job candidate.

How Certification Affects Pharmacy Technician Pay

If you're looking to boost your earning potential as a pharmacy technician, becoming certified may be a good option for you. According to the National Healthcareer Association, pharmacy technicians who hold a certification typically earn more than those who are not certified. Additionally, certification can also help you stand out from other job candidates and may lead to more job opportunities.

How to Become Certified as a Pharmacy Technician

If you're interested in becoming certified as a pharmacy technician, there are a few steps you'll need to take. First, you'll need to complete a training program that is accredited by the Accreditation Council for Pharmacy Education (ACPE). Once you've completed your training, you'll need to pass an exam administered by the Pharmacy Technician Certification Board (PTCB) or the National Healthcareer Association (NHA).

Answering Your Questions About How Much Pharmacy Technicians Are Paid

Q: Is it possible to earn a higher salary as a pharmacy technician?

A: Yes, it is possible to earn a higher salary as a pharmacy technician. Factors like location and experience can impact your earning potential, so it's important to keep this in mind when considering this profession.

Q: Do pharmacy technicians typically receive benefits like health insurance?

A: Many pharmacy technicians do receive benefits like health insurance as part of their employment package. However, this can vary depending on the employer.

Q: How does the pay for pharmacy technicians compare to other healthcare professions?

A: The pay for pharmacy technicians is typically lower than other healthcare professions like nurses or pharmacists. However, it is still a viable career option with potential for growth and advancement.

Q: Is becoming certified as a pharmacy technician worth the investment?

A: Becoming certified as a pharmacy technician can be worth the investment, as it can lead to higher pay and more job opportunities. However, it's important to weigh the costs of certification against the potential benefits before making a decision.

Conclusion of How Much Are Pharmacy Technicians Paid

Overall, how much pharmacy technicians are paid can vary depending on a number of factors. However, with a median annual wage of over $34,000, this profession can be a good option for those looking for a career in healthcare. By considering factors like location and experience, pharmacy technicians can work to maximize their earning potential and build a successful career in this field.
