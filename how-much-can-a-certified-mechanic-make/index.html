How Much Can A Certified Mechanic Make

Written by Robby Jun 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a certified mechanic? If so, you might be wondering how much you can expect to make in this field. The truth is that there are several factors that can impact your earning potential as a mechanic, including your level of experience, your location, and the type of work that you do.

One of the biggest pain points for those considering a career as a mechanic is uncertainty about their earning potential. After all, nobody wants to invest time and money into a career that won't provide financial stability over the long term.

So, how much can a certified mechanic make? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 as of May 2020. However, the highest 10 percent of earners in this field made more than $70,000 per year.

In order to maximize your earning potential as a certified mechanic, it's important to build up your skills and experience over time. This might involve pursuing additional certifications or specializations, seeking out opportunities to work on more complex or high-end vehicles, or even starting your own repair business.

Personal Experience with Earning Potential as a Mechanic

As someone who has worked as a certified mechanic for several years, I can attest to the fact that there is a lot of variability when it comes to earnings in this field. When I first started out, I was making around $25,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and built up a loyal customer base, I was able to increase my rates and make closer to $50,000 per year.

Of course, your earnings as a mechanic will depend largely on your location and the types of jobs that are available to you. In some areas, there may be a lot of competition among mechanics, which can drive down rates. In other areas, there may be a shortage of qualified mechanics, which can drive up rates.

Factors That Impact Earning Potential as a Certified Mechanic

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can impact how much you can expect to make as a certified mechanic. Some of the most important factors include:

Experience and Training

The more experience and training you have as a mechanic, the more valuable you will be to potential employers or clients. This can translate into higher rates or salaries.

Type of Work

The type of work you do as a mechanic can also impact your earning potential. For example, working on high-end vehicles or specialized equipment may command higher rates than working on standard passenger cars.

How to Increase Your Earning Potential as a Certified Mechanic

If you're looking to maximize your earning potential as a certified mechanic, there are several steps you can take:

Pursue Additional Certifications or Specializations

By pursuing additional certifications or specializations, you can demonstrate your expertise in specific areas of auto repair. This can make you more valuable to potential clients or employers.

Seek Out Higher-Paying Jobs

If you're not satisfied with your current earnings as a mechanic, it may be time to start looking for higher-paying jobs. This might involve networking with other mechanics in your area, researching job postings online, or even starting your own repair business.

Conclusion of How Much Can a Certified Mechanic Make

While there is no one-size-fits-all answer to the question of how much a certified mechanic can make, it's clear that there is significant earning potential in this field for those who are willing to put in the work. Whether you're just starting out or looking to take your career to the next level, there are many steps you can take to increase your earning potential and build a successful career in auto repair.
