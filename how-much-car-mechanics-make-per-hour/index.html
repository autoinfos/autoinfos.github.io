How Much Do Car Mechanics Make Per Hour

Written by Eveline Jul 27, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a car mechanic? If so, you may be wondering how much you can expect to earn per hour. The good news is that car mechanics can make a decent living, but the amount you earn will depend on several factors. In this blog post, we'll take a closer look at how much car mechanics make per hour and what you can expect from a career in this field.

One of the pain points of becoming a car mechanic is the cost of education and training. Many aspiring mechanics attend technical schools or community colleges to learn the necessary skills, which can be expensive. Additionally, the job can be physically demanding and requires working with machinery and heavy equipment, which can be difficult for some people.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $20.24 as of May 2020. This means that half of all mechanics earned more than this amount, and half earned less. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $12.31 per hour, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $32.19 per hour.

In summary, car mechanics can make a decent living, with a median hourly wage of $20.24. However, the amount you earn will depend on several factors, including your level of experience, the type of work you do, and the area in which you work.

Factors That Affect How Much Car Mechanics Make Per Hour

Several factors can influence how much a car mechanic earns per hour. One of the most significant factors is experience. Mechanics who have been in the field for several years and have a proven track record of success may be able to command higher hourly wages than entry-level mechanics.

The type of work you do can also impact your hourly wage. For example, mechanics who specialize in complex repairs or who work on high-end vehicles may be able to charge more for their services than mechanics who work on basic repairs and maintenance tasks.

My Personal Experience as a Car Mechanic

I have been working as a car mechanic for the past 10 years, and I can tell you that it can be a rewarding career. I started out as an entry-level mechanic, earning around $15 per hour, but as I gained more experience and expertise, I was able to increase my hourly wage to $25 per hour.

One thing to keep in mind is that the job can be physically demanding. I have to spend a lot of time on my feet and work with heavy machinery and equipment, which can be tiring. However, the satisfaction of fixing a car and helping a customer get back on the road makes it all worthwhile.

How to Increase Your Hourly Wage as a Car Mechanic

If you're interested in increasing your hourly wage as a car mechanic, there are several steps you can take. First, consider specializing in a particular area of repair, such as engines or transmissions. This can make you a more valuable employee and allow you to charge more for your services.

You can also consider pursuing additional education and certifications. Many employers prefer to hire mechanics who have completed formal training programs or who have earned industry certifications. This can help set you apart from other job candidates and increase your earning potential.

The Future of Car Mechanics

As technology continues to advance, the job of a car mechanic is likely to change. Many new cars now come equipped with advanced computer systems and other high-tech features, which means that mechanics will need to have a broader range of skills and knowledge to be successful in the field.

FAQs About How Much Car Mechanics Make Per Hour

Q: Can I make a good living as a car mechanic?

A: Yes, many car mechanics earn a decent living, with a median hourly wage of $20.24.

Q: What factors affect how much a car mechanic earns per hour?

A: Factors that can influence a car mechanic's hourly wage include experience, type of work performed, and location.

Q: Is it necessary to attend technical school to become a car mechanic?

A: While it's not strictly necessary to attend technical school, many aspiring mechanics find that it's helpful for learning the necessary skills and increasing their job prospects.

Q: How can I increase my hourly wage as a car mechanic?

A: You can increase your earning potential by specializing in a particular area of repair, pursuing additional education and certifications, and gaining experience on the job.

Conclusion of How Much Car Mechanics Make Per Hour

In conclusion, car mechanics can make a decent living, with a median hourly wage of $20.24. While the job can be physically demanding and require significant training and education, it can also be a rewarding career for those who enjoy working with cars and helping customers get back on the road.
