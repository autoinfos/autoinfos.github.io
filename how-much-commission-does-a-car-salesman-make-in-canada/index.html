How Much Commission Does A Car Salesman Make In Canada

Written by Aprilia Nov 17, 2023 · 5 min read

Buying a car can be an exciting experience, but for many people, the process of negotiating with a car salesman can be overwhelming. One of the biggest concerns that people have when buying a car is how much commission the salesperson will make on the sale. This can lead to questions like, "Am I paying too much for this car?" and "Is the salesperson trying to take advantage of me?"

When it comes to buying a car, the commission that a salesperson makes can vary widely. This is because car dealerships have different commission structures, and some salespeople are paid hourly or receive a salary instead of a commission. However, on average, a car salesman in Canada can expect to make between 10% and 20% commission on the sale of a new or used vehicle.

So, for example, if you purchase a car for $30,000, the salesperson could make between $3,000 and $6,000 in commission. This may seem like a significant amount of money, but it's important to keep in mind that the salesperson has likely put in a lot of time and effort to make the sale. They have likely spent hours with you answering questions, providing test drives, and negotiating the price.

Overall, it's important to remember that the commission that a car salesman makes is just one factor to consider when buying a car. You should also take into account the overall cost of the vehicle, any financing options that are available to you, and the reputation of the dealership and salesperson.

Personal Experience with Car Salesman Commission

When I purchased my last car, I was initially hesitant about working with a salesperson because I was worried about being taken advantage of. However, I ultimately decided to work with a salesperson who came highly recommended by a friend. While I was nervous about negotiating the price, the salesperson was understanding and patient throughout the process. In the end, I felt comfortable with the price we agreed upon, and I was happy to know that the salesperson was being fairly compensated for their work.

How Commission Affects the Sales Process

The commission that a car salesman makes can have a significant impact on the sales process. Salespeople who are paid purely on commission may be more aggressive in their sales tactics, as they have a direct financial incentive to close the sale. On the other hand, salespeople who are paid a salary or hourly wage may be less aggressive, as they are not as directly motivated by the sale.

The Role of Negotiation

Negotiation is an important part of the car buying process, and it can have a significant impact on the commission that a salesperson makes. If you are able to negotiate a lower price for the vehicle, the salesperson's commission will be lower as well. However, it's important to remember that the salesperson still deserves to be fairly compensated for their time and effort.

How to Navigate the Sales Process

If you're feeling overwhelmed by the sales process, there are a few things you can do to make it easier. First, do your research ahead of time so you have a good idea of what kind of car you want and what a fair price would be. Second, be upfront with the salesperson about your budget and what you're looking for. Finally, don't be afraid to walk away if you're not comfortable with the price or the salesperson's tactics.

Common Questions About Car Salesman Commission

Q: Can I negotiate the commission that a salesperson makes?
A: No, the commission that a salesperson makes is typically set by the dealership, and it's not negotiable.

Q: Do salespeople make more commission on new or used cars?
A: It depends on the dealership's commission structure. Some dealerships may offer higher commission rates on new cars, while others may offer higher commission rates on used cars.

Q: What happens if a salesperson doesn't make a sale?
A: If a salesperson doesn't make a sale, they typically don't receive any commission for their time and effort.

Q: Is it better to work with a salesperson who is paid a salary or one who is paid on commission?
A: It depends on your personal preference. Salespeople who are paid on commission may be more motivated to make a sale, but they may also be more aggressive in their sales tactics. Salespeople who are paid a salary may be less motivated to make a sale, but they may also be more willing to work with you to find a car that fits your needs and budget.

Conclusion of How Much Commission Does a Car Salesman Make in Canada

The commission that a car salesman makes can vary widely based on the dealership's commission structure and the type of car being sold. While it's important to be aware of the commission that a salesperson makes, it's also important to take into account the overall cost of the vehicle, any financing options that are available to you, and the reputation of the dealership and salesperson. By doing your research ahead of time and being upfront with the salesperson about your needs and budget, you can make the car buying process a smooth and stress-free experience.
