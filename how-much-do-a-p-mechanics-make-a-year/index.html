How Much Do A P Mechanics Make A Year

Written by Petter Aug 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an A&P mechanic? One of the most common questions people have when considering a career in aviation maintenance is how much money they can expect to make. In this post, we will discuss the average annual salary for A&P mechanics and factors that can affect your earnings.

For many A&P mechanics, salary is a pain point. The aviation industry is notorious for its low pay and long hours. However, the earning potential for A&P mechanics can vary depending on a variety of factors such as experience, location, and employer.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), the median annual wage for aircraft mechanics and service technicians, including A&P mechanics, was $64,090 as of May 2020. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $38,210, and the highest 10 percent earned more than $98,720.

In summary, the average annual salary for A&P mechanics is $64,090, but this can vary depending on several factors such as experience, location, and employer.

Factors that Affect an A&P Mechanic's Earnings

Experience is one of the biggest factors that can affect an A&P mechanic's earnings. The more experience you have, the more valuable you are to an employer, and the more you can expect to earn.

Location is another factor that can affect an A&P mechanic's earnings. Generally, mechanics who work in metropolitan areas or near major airports can expect to earn more than those who work in rural areas.

My Experience with A&P Mechanics Salaries

As someone who has worked in the aviation industry for over 10 years, I can attest that A&P mechanics' salaries can vary greatly depending on the factors mentioned above. In my experience, mechanics who work for major airlines or repair stations tend to earn more than those who work for smaller operators or general aviation facilities.

How to Increase Your Earnings as an A&P Mechanic

If you are looking to increase your earnings as an A&P mechanic, there are a few things you can do. First, consider getting additional certifications or training, such as avionics or composites. These specializations can make you more valuable to an employer and increase your earning potential.

Second, consider working for a major airline or repair station. These employers tend to offer better pay and benefits than smaller operators or general aviation facilities.

The Future of A&P Mechanic Salaries

The future of A&P mechanic salaries is difficult to predict. However, the demand for qualified mechanics is expected to increase in the coming years as more and more aircraft enter service. This could lead to increased competition for qualified mechanics and potentially higher salaries.

Tips for Negotiating Your A&P Mechanic Salary

If you are considering a job as an A&P mechanic, it is important to negotiate your salary. Here are a few tips to help you get the best possible salary:

  • Research salaries for similar positions in your area
  • Highlight your experience and certifications during the interview process
  • Be prepared to negotiate and have a salary range in mind

Conclusion of How Much Do A&P Mechanics Make a Year

The average annual salary for A&P mechanics is $64,090, but this can vary depending on several factors such as experience, location, and employer. While the aviation industry is known for its low pay, there are opportunities for A&P mechanics to increase their earnings through additional certifications, working for major airlines or repair stations, and negotiating their salary during the hiring process.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average hourly wage for A&P mechanics?

A: According to the BLS, the average hourly wage for A&P mechanics is $30.82 as of May 2020.

Q: What is the job outlook for A&P mechanics?

A: The BLS projects that employment of aircraft mechanics and service technicians, including A&P mechanics, will grow 5 percent from 2019 to 2029, about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Q: Can A&P mechanics earn overtime pay?

A: Yes, many A&P mechanics are eligible for overtime pay, which can significantly increase their earnings.

Q: Are there any other benefits that A&P mechanics can receive besides salary?

A: Yes, many employers offer benefits such as healthcare, retirement plans, and paid time off to their A&P mechanics.
