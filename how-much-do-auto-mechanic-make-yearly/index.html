How Much Do Auto Mechanics Make Yearly

Written by Daniel Oct 19, 2023 · 3 min read

Do you love working with cars and have a knack for problem-solving? Becoming an auto mechanic may be the perfect career for you. But one question that may be on your mind is, how much do auto mechanics make yearly?

It's no secret that finances can be a major concern when choosing a career path. Many people may worry about not earning enough to support themselves or their families. This is where knowing the average salary for a specific job can come in handy.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 as of May 2020. Keep in mind that this is just a median, meaning half of automotive service technicians and mechanics earn more than this amount, while the other half earn less.

In addition to experience, several factors can affect an auto mechanic's salary, such as location, industry, and certifications. Mechanics working in metropolitan areas or at car dealerships may earn more than those in rural areas or working at independent repair shops.

My Personal Experience with Auto Mechanic Salary

As someone who has worked in the automotive industry for several years, I can attest to the fact that auto mechanics can make a decent living. When I started as a mechanic, my starting wage was around $20 an hour. However, as I gained experience and certifications, my salary increased to around $30 an hour.

How to Increase Your Auto Mechanic Salary

If you're interested in becoming an auto mechanic and want to increase your earning potential, there are several steps you can take. First and foremost, gaining experience is crucial. The more years of experience you have, the more you can potentially earn.

Another way to increase your salary is by obtaining certifications. Certifications show employers that you have a certain level of expertise and can perform specific tasks. This can make you more valuable in the eyes of your employer and potentially earn you a higher salary.

Industry-Specific Salaries for Auto Mechanics

While the above information is a general overview of auto mechanic salaries, it's important to note that there are variations in salary depending on the specific industry you work in. For example, auto mechanics who work for the federal government may earn a higher salary than those who work in the private sector.

Tips for Negotiating Your Auto Mechanic Salary

If you're offered a job as an auto mechanic but feel the salary is too low, don't be afraid to negotiate. Researching the average salary for auto mechanics in your area and industry can help you make a more informed decision.

Conclusion of How Much Do Auto Mechanics Make Yearly

Overall, auto mechanics can make a decent living, with a median annual salary of $44,050. However, factors such as location, industry, and certifications can affect an auto mechanic's salary. Gaining experience and obtaining certifications can also increase your earning potential.

Question and Answer

Q: Can auto mechanics make more than the median annual wage?

A: Yes, auto mechanics with more experience, certifications, and working in certain industries or locations can potentially earn more than the median annual wage.

Q: What certifications can help increase an auto mechanic's salary?

A: Certifications such as ASE (Automotive Service Excellence) and manufacturer-specific certifications can help increase an auto mechanic's salary.

Q: Are auto mechanics in high demand?

A: Yes, the demand for auto mechanics is expected to grow by 4% from 2019 to 2029, which is about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Q: What is the average hourly wage for auto mechanics?

A: As of May 2020, the average hourly wage for auto mechanics was $21.17.
