How Much Do Auto Mechanics Make At A Dealership

Written by William Nov 09, 2023 · 4 min read

Do you love cars and are considering a career as a mechanic? Or are you already working as one and wondering whether you're being paid fairly? If so, you may be wondering, "How much do auto mechanics make at a dealership?"

When it comes to the salary of an auto mechanic working at a dealership, there are a few factors that come into play. These include the location of the dealership, the mechanic's level of experience and education, and the specific type of work they perform.

On average, the salary of an auto mechanic at a dealership in the United States ranges from around $35,000 to $60,000 per year. However, this can vary widely depending on the factors mentioned above.

To give you a better idea of what you can expect as a mechanic at a dealership, let's take a closer look at some of the factors that affect your earning potential.

The Location Factor

One of the biggest factors that can affect how much you make as a mechanic at a dealership is where you're located. For example, mechanics who work in larger cities or in areas with a high cost of living may be paid more than those who work in smaller towns or areas with a lower cost of living.

Additionally, some states have a higher demand for mechanics than others. This can also affect your salary as a mechanic.

The Experience and Education Factor

Another important factor that affects how much you can earn as a mechanic at a dealership is your experience and education. Generally, the more experience and education you have, the higher your earning potential.

For example, someone who has been working as a mechanic for 10 years and has a degree in automotive technology may be paid more than someone who is just starting out in the field with no formal education or experience.

The Type of Work Factor

The type of work you perform as a mechanic at a dealership can also play a role in how much you get paid. For example, specialized work such as engine rebuilding or transmission repair may pay more than routine maintenance work like oil changes and brake jobs.

The Commission Factor

Finally, some dealerships may offer their mechanics commission-based pay. This means that the mechanic earns a percentage of each sale they make, in addition to their base salary.

Personal Experience

I have been working as a mechanic at a dealership for five years now. I started out with no formal education in the field, but I quickly learned on the job and was able to progress to more specialized work like engine repair.

Overall, I feel that I am fairly compensated for my work. I make around $50,000 per year, but I know that this varies depending on where you're located and what type of work you're doing.

Advancement Opportunities

One of the great things about working as a mechanic at a dealership is that there are often opportunities for advancement. For example, you may be able to move up to a management position or specialize in a certain type of repair work, which can lead to a higher salary.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I make more money as a mechanic working at an independent shop?

A: It's possible, but it depends on a variety of factors such as the location of the shop, the type of work you perform, and the demand for mechanics in the area.

Q: How much can I expect to make as a mechanic with no formal education or experience?

A: It varies widely, but you may start out making around $30,000 per year and can work your way up as you gain more experience and education.

Q: Are there any certifications or licenses that can help me earn more as a mechanic?

A: Yes, there are several certifications that can help improve your earning potential such as the ASE certification or the EPA 609 certification for working with air conditioning systems.

Q: Is it possible to negotiate my salary as a mechanic at a dealership?

A: Yes, it's always worth asking if there is any room for negotiation when it comes to your salary. However, keep in mind that the dealership may have a set pay scale based on your experience and education.

Conclusion of How Much Do Auto Mechanics Make at a Dealership

Overall, if you're considering a career as a mechanic at a dealership, you can expect to make a decent salary with opportunities for advancement. However, keep in mind that your earning potential will depend on factors such as your location, experience, education, and the type of work you perform.
