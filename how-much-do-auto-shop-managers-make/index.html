How Much Do Auto Shop Managers Make

Written by Bardi Nov 14, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you wondering how much auto shop managers earn? If you are planning to pursue a career in this field, it's important to know the earning potential. In this blog post, we will explore the average salary of auto shop managers, factors that affect their pay, and tips for maximizing your earnings in this role.

As a manager in an auto shop, you may be responsible for overseeing a team of mechanics, ordering and managing inventory, handling customer complaints, and ensuring that the shop runs smoothly. While it can be a rewarding career, it can also be challenging at times. One of the biggest concerns for those considering this role is the salary.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics, including shop managers, was $44,050 as of May 2020. However, this can vary widely based on several factors, including location, experience, and the size of the shop.

So, how can you maximize your earning potential as an auto shop manager? One way is to gain experience and expertise in the field. The more knowledge and skills you have, the more valuable you will be to your employer. Additionally, consider pursuing additional certifications or training to specialize in a particular area of auto repair.

Personal Experience

As an experienced auto shop manager, I can attest that earning potential varies widely based on a variety of factors. In my experience, location plays a major role in determining salary. In areas with a high cost of living, such as major cities, salaries tend to be higher.

Factors That Affect Earnings

Aside from location and experience, there are several other factors that can impact the earning potential of auto shop managers. One of the biggest factors is the size of the shop. Managers in larger shops tend to earn more, as they are responsible for overseeing a larger team and managing a larger inventory.

Another factor is the type of vehicles serviced by the shop. Shops that specialize in high-end or luxury vehicles tend to pay their managers more, as the repairs and maintenance on these vehicles can be more complex and require specialized knowledge.

Location Matters

If you're looking to maximize your earning potential as an auto shop manager, it's important to consider the location of the shop. As mentioned earlier, shops located in major cities tend to offer higher salaries due to the higher cost of living. Additionally, shops located in areas with a high demand for auto repairs and maintenance may also offer higher salaries.

Specialize to Earn More

To earn a higher salary as an auto shop manager, consider specializing in a particular area of auto repair. This could include electric or hybrid vehicles, diesel engines, or high-end luxury vehicles. By developing expertise in a particular area, you can become a valuable asset to your employer and potentially earn a higher salary.

Personal Experience

Early in my career as an auto shop manager, I realized that specializing in high-end luxury vehicles was a great way to increase my earning potential. By gaining expertise in this area, I was able to perform more complex repairs and maintenance, which led to higher-paying jobs and increased job security.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it possible to negotiate a higher salary as an auto shop manager?

A: Yes, it is possible to negotiate a higher salary as an auto shop manager. However, it's important to come prepared with data to support your request. Research the average salary for the position in your area and be prepared to discuss your experience and qualifications.

Q: Are there any additional benefits or perks that come with being an auto shop manager?

A: Yes, many auto shops offer additional benefits and perks to their managers, such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off. Some shops may also offer bonuses or commission for meeting certain performance goals.

Q: How much experience do I need to become an auto shop manager?

A: While there is no set amount of experience required to become an auto shop manager, most shops prefer candidates with at least five years of experience in the field. Additionally, having a degree or certification in automotive technology can be beneficial.

Q: What is the job outlook for auto shop managers?

A: The job outlook for auto shop managers is positive, with a projected growth rate of 6% from 2019-2029, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. As the demand for auto repairs and maintenance continues to grow, so too will the need for skilled managers to oversee these operations.

Conclusion of How Much Do Auto Shop Managers Make

While the salary for auto shop managers can vary widely based on several factors, there are ways to maximize your earning potential in this role. By gaining experience and expertise in the field, specializing in a particular area of auto repair, and considering the location and size of the shop, you can increase your chances of earning a higher salary. Negotiating your salary and taking advantage of additional benefits and perks can also help to boost your earnings as an auto shop manager.
