How Much Do Automotive Electricians Make

Written by Oliver May 31, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you have a passion for cars and a talent for electrical work? If so, becoming an automotive electrician may be the perfect career choice for you. However, before embarking on this career path, it's essential to understand how much automotive electricians typically make.

One of the main pain points related to becoming an automotive electrician is the fear of not making enough money. Many people worry that this career choice may not provide them with the income they need to support themselves and their families.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics, including electricians, was $44,050 as of May 2020. However, this figure can vary depending on several factors, including experience, location, and specialization.

In summary, automotive electricians can make a decent living in the United States. However, several factors can affect their income, including experience, location, and specialization.

How Much Do Entry-Level Automotive Electricians Make?

When starting as an automotive electrician, you can expect to earn an entry-level salary. According to Payscale, the average entry-level salary for an automotive electrician is $15.42 per hour. However, as you gain more experience and skills, your salary can increase.

How Much Do Specialized Automotive Electricians Make?

If you specialize in a particular area, such as hybrid or electric vehicles, you may be able to earn a higher salary. For instance, according to the BLS, electric motor, power tool, and related repairers earned a median annual wage of $45,820 as of May 2020. Specialized automotive electricians are in high demand, and there is usually a shortage of skilled workers in this area.

Factors That Affect Automotive Electricians' Salaries

Several factors can affect how much automotive electricians make, including:

  • Experience: The more experience you have, the more you can earn.
  • Location: Salaries can vary depending on where you live and work.
  • Specialization: Specialized automotive electricians can earn higher salaries.
  • Industry: Electricians who work in the automotive manufacturing industry may earn more than those who work in repair shops.

Tips for Increasing Your Salary as an Automotive Electrician

If you want to increase your salary as an automotive electrician, consider taking the following steps:

  • Get certified: Obtaining industry certifications can demonstrate your expertise and increase your earning potential.
  • Specialize: Consider specializing in a specific area, such as hybrid or electric vehicles.
  • Gain experience: The more experience you have, the more you can earn.
  • Network: Building relationships with other professionals in the industry can help you find higher-paying job opportunities.

Question and Answer

Q: Is becoming an automotive electrician a good career choice?

A: Yes, becoming an automotive electrician can be an excellent career choice if you have a passion for cars and electrical work. There is a demand for skilled workers in this field, and the earning potential can be high.

Q: How long does it take to become an automotive electrician?

A: It can take anywhere from six months to two years to complete the necessary training to become an automotive electrician.

Q: Can I make a good living as an automotive electrician?

A: Yes, you can make a good living as an automotive electrician. The median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics, including electricians, was $44,050 as of May 2020.

Q: How much does location affect an automotive electrician's salary?

A: Location can significantly affect an automotive electrician's salary. Salaries can vary depending on where you live and work, with higher salaries typically found in more populated areas with higher costs of living.

Conclusion of How Much Do Automotive Electricians Make

Overall, becoming an automotive electrician can be a lucrative career choice. While entry-level salaries may be lower, experienced and specialized automotive electricians can earn a good living. Factors that can affect an automotive electrician's salary include experience, location, specialization, and industry. By taking steps to gain experience, specialize, and network, you can increase your earning potential as an automotive electrician.
