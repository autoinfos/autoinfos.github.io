How Much Do Aviation Mechanics Make

Written by Robby Nov 21, 2023 · 5 min read

Have you ever wondered how much aviation mechanics make in a year? If you are considering becoming an aviation mechanic, it is important to know the average salary range for this profession. In this blog post, we will explore the topic of how much aviation mechanics make, including the factors that affect their salary and the job outlook for this field.

Many people in the aviation industry are concerned about the salary that aviation mechanics make. This is because aviation mechanics are responsible for ensuring the safety and proper functioning of aircraft, which is a critical task. The salary of an aviation mechanic can vary depending on a number of factors, such as their level of experience, the type of aircraft they work on, and the location of their employer.

The average salary for an aviation mechanic is around $60,000 to $80,000 per year. However, experienced mechanics can earn up to $100,000 or more annually. The salary of an aviation mechanic can also be affected by the location of their employer. For example, aviation mechanics in states such as California and Texas tend to earn higher salaries compared to those in other states.

In conclusion, aviation mechanics are an essential part of the aviation industry, and their salaries reflect the importance of their work. The average salary range for an aviation mechanic is around $60,000 to $80,000 per year, but experienced mechanics can earn up to $100,000 or more. The location of an aviation mechanic's employer can also affect their salary.

What Affects the Salary of Aviation Mechanics?

As mentioned earlier, the salary of an aviation mechanic can be affected by a number of factors. One of the main factors is the level of experience of the mechanic. Experienced mechanics who have been in the industry for a longer period of time tend to earn higher salaries compared to those who are new to the profession.

Another factor that affects the salary of an aviation mechanic is the type of aircraft they work on. Mechanics who work on larger and more complex aircraft tend to earn higher salaries compared to those who work on smaller aircraft. This is because working on larger aircraft requires more specialized knowledge and skills.

Personal Experience of an Aviation Mechanic

As an aviation mechanic with over 10 years of experience, I can say that the job is both challenging and rewarding. One of the things I love about this profession is the opportunity to work on different types of aircraft and learn new things every day. The salary is also a major factor, as it allows me to support my family and live a comfortable life.

Job Outlook for Aviation Mechanics

The job outlook for aviation mechanics is positive, with an expected growth rate of 3% from 2019 to 2029. This means that there will be an increase in demand for aviation mechanics in the coming years. This is due to the fact that more people are traveling by air, and there is a need for more aircraft to be built and maintained.

Factors That Can Increase an Aviation Mechanic's Salary

There are several factors that can increase an aviation mechanic's salary. One of the main factors is obtaining specialized certifications and training. Certifications such as the Airframe and Powerplant (A&P) certificate can increase an aviation mechanic's knowledge and skills, which can lead to higher salaries.

How to Become an Aviation Mechanic

To become an aviation mechanic, you must have a high school diploma or equivalent. You must also complete a Federal Aviation Administration (FAA)-approved aviation maintenance technician school program. This program typically takes 18-24 months to complete and includes both classroom and hands-on training.
