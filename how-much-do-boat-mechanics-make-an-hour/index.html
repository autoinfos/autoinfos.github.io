How Much Do Boat Mechanics Make An Hour

Written by Aprilia Jun 20, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you thinking about becoming a boat mechanic, but wondering how much you can expect to make per hour? Or are you a boat owner who wants to know how much to budget for repairs? Whatever your situation, understanding boat mechanic salaries is essential.

Boat mechanics are responsible for maintaining and repairing boats, motors, and other marine equipment. They often work in marinas, boatyards, or repair shops. Unfortunately, there is no easy answer to the question of how much boat mechanics make per hour. Salaries can vary widely depending on a number of factors, including location, experience, and type of employer.

That being said, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for boat mechanics and service technicians was $20.16 in May 2020. This means that half of all boat mechanics earned more than this amount, while the other half earned less.

In addition to hourly wages, boat mechanics may also earn commission or receive bonuses for completing jobs on time or under budget. Some may be self-employed, which can provide more flexibility but also requires more business savvy and marketing skills.

What Factors Affect Boat Mechanic Salaries?

As mentioned, boat mechanic salaries can vary widely depending on a number of factors. Here are some of the most important:

Location:

Like many professions, boat mechanic salaries can vary depending on where you live. For example, mechanics in coastal areas or major boating destinations may earn more than those in landlocked or less popular areas. This is due to the higher demand for marine services and the higher cost of living in these areas.

Experience:

As with any profession, boat mechanics with more experience generally earn more than those who are just starting out. This is because experienced mechanics are more knowledgeable, efficient, and able to complete more complex jobs.

Type of Employer:

Boat mechanics may work for a variety of employers, including marinas, boatyards, repair shops, or manufacturers. The type of employer can affect salaries, as some may offer more competitive wages, benefits, or bonuses than others.

Education and Certification:

Formal education and certification can also impact boat mechanic salaries. Those with a degree or certification in marine technology or a related field may be able to command higher wages than those without.

Personal Experience:

When I was starting out as a boat mechanic, I worked for a small marina in a coastal area. I had some basic mechanical knowledge but not much experience with boats specifically. At first, I earned around $15 per hour, which was enough to pay my bills but not much more.

Over time, I gained more experience and knowledge, and my salary increased accordingly. I also started working on larger, more complex boats, which allowed me to earn more per job. Eventually, I was able to go into business for myself and set my own rates.

FAQs About Boat Mechanic Salaries:

Q: Is being a boat mechanic a good career?

A: Yes, being a boat mechanic can be a rewarding career for those who love working with their hands and have an interest in boats and the marine industry. It can be a challenging but fulfilling profession that offers a variety of opportunities for growth and advancement.

Q: How much do entry-level boat mechanics make?

A: Entry-level boat mechanics can expect to earn around $12-15 per hour on average. However, this can vary depending on location, employer, and other factors.

Q: Can boat mechanics make six figures?

A: While it is possible for boat mechanics to earn six figures, this is relatively rare. Most boat mechanics earn a median hourly wage of around $20 per hour, although this can vary depending on experience, location, and other factors.

Q: Do boat mechanics get benefits?

A: Yes, many boat mechanics receive benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off. However, this can vary depending on the employer.

Conclusion:

While boat mechanic salaries can vary widely depending on a number of factors, the median hourly wage is around $20 per hour. Factors like location, experience, type of employer, and education can all impact salaries. However, being a boat mechanic can be a rewarding career that offers a variety of opportunities for growth and advancement.
