How Much Do Car Dealer Mechanics Make

Written by Robby May 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you ever wonder how much car dealer mechanics make? Are you considering a career in the automotive industry but unsure about the potential earning? In this blog post, we will discuss the average salary of car dealer mechanics and what factors affect their pay.

As a car dealer mechanic, you may face some common pain points such as low pay or lack of job stability. These factors are important to consider when deciding whether to pursue a career in this field. However, there are also many benefits to becoming a car dealer mechanic, including the opportunity to work with your hands and the satisfaction of fixing complex issues.

The average salary for a car dealer mechanic is around $44,000 per year. However, this number can vary depending on factors such as experience, location, and type of dealership. For example, mechanics working in larger dealerships or in high-end car brands may earn more than those working in smaller dealerships or with lower-end brands.

In summary, car dealer mechanics make an average salary of around $44,000 per year. While this salary may not be as high as some other professions, there are many benefits to pursuing a career in the automotive industry.

Factors Affecting Car Dealer Mechanics' Salaries

Experience is one of the biggest factors affecting a car dealer mechanic's salary. Those with more years of experience are likely to earn a higher salary than those just starting out. Location is also a factor, as mechanics working in high-cost-of-living areas may earn more to compensate for the higher expenses.

Type of dealership is another factor affecting salaries. Mechanics working in luxury car dealerships or dealerships with higher-end brands may earn more due to the complexity of the cars they work on. Additionally, certifications and specializations can increase earning potential for car dealer mechanics.

Personal Experience: My Journey as a Car Dealer Mechanic

After completing a technical school program in automotive technology, I started my career as a car dealer mechanic at a small dealership. I quickly learned that the pay was not as high as I expected, but I enjoyed the work and the opportunity to learn about different cars.

Over time, I gained more experience and started working at a larger dealership with higher-end brands. This allowed me to earn a higher salary and work on more complex cars. I also obtained additional certifications which further increased my earning potential.

The Future of Car Dealer Mechanics' Salaries

The future of car dealer mechanics' salaries is promising, as the demand for skilled mechanics continues to grow. Additionally, advancements in technology and electric cars may create new opportunities for mechanics with specialized skills.

How to Increase Your Earning Potential as a Car Dealer Mechanic

To increase your earning potential as a car dealer mechanic, consider obtaining additional certifications or specializing in a specific area such as electric cars or hybrid technology. Additionally, gaining more experience and working at a larger dealership or with higher-end brands can also increase your earning potential.

The Importance of Negotiating Your Salary as a Car Dealer Mechanic

When starting a new job as a car dealer mechanic or negotiating a raise, it's important to be aware of the average salary for the position and the factors that may affect your earning potential. Don't be afraid to negotiate for a higher salary or ask for additional benefits such as health insurance or paid time off.

Question and Answer

Q: Is a career as a car dealer mechanic a good choice?

A: It depends on your interests and career goals. While the salary may not be as high as some other professions, the job stability and opportunity for hands-on work can be appealing.

Q: How much experience do I need to become a car dealer mechanic?

A: While formal education or certification is not always required, most employers prefer mechanics with at least some training and experience. Consider attending a technical school program or completing an apprenticeship to gain experience.

Q: Are there opportunities for advancement as a car dealer mechanic?

A: Yes, there are opportunities for advancement such as becoming a lead mechanic or service manager. Additionally, obtaining additional certifications or specializing in a specific area can increase your earning potential.

Q: Can I negotiate my salary as a car dealer mechanic?

A: Yes, it's important to be aware of the average salary for the position and the factors that may affect your earning potential. Don't be afraid to negotiate for a higher salary or ask for additional benefits such as health insurance or paid time off.

Conclusion of How Much Do Car Dealer Mechanics Make

Car dealer mechanics make an average salary of around $44,000 per year, with factors such as experience, location, and type of dealership affecting their earning potential. While the salary may not be as high as some other professions, opportunities for advancement and the satisfaction of hands-on work make a career as a car dealer mechanic a viable choice for many.
