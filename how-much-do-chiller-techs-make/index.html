How Much Do Chiller Techs Make

Written by Luffy Jun 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a chiller technician? If so, you may be wondering how much money you can expect to make in this field. The good news is that chiller technicians are in high demand, and the pay is typically quite competitive. In this blog post, we'll explore how much chiller techs make, as well as some factors that can affect their salaries.

The Pain Points of Chiller Tech Salaries

One of the biggest pain points for chiller technicians is that their salaries can vary widely depending on a number of factors. For example, chiller techs who work for larger companies or in more expensive areas may earn more money than those who work for smaller companies or in less expensive areas. Additionally, the level of experience and education of the chiller tech can also impact their salary.

Answering the Question: How Much Do Chiller Techs Make?

According to data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for heating, air conditioning, and refrigeration mechanics and installers (which includes chiller techs) was $48,730 as of May 2020. However, salaries can range from around $30,000 per year for entry-level positions to over $75,000 per year for more experienced chiller techs.

Factors That Affect Chiller Tech Salaries

As mentioned, there are several factors that can impact how much money a chiller technician can expect to make. Some of these factors include:

  • Level of experience and education
  • Location
  • Type of industry (e.g. commercial, industrial, residential)
  • Size of the company
  • Additional certifications or licenses

Personal Experience with Chiller Tech Salaries

As a chiller technician with over 10 years of experience, I can attest to the fact that salaries can vary widely in this industry. When I first started out, I was making around $35,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and moved up the ranks, I was able to earn closer to $60,000 per year. Additionally, I found that working for larger companies tended to come with higher salaries, as did obtaining additional certifications and licenses.

Tips for Maximizing Your Chiller Tech Salary

If you're interested in maximizing your salary as a chiller technician, there are a few things you can do:

  • Obtain additional certifications or licenses
  • Look for job opportunities in more expensive areas or with larger companies
  • Consider specializing in a certain type of chiller system or equipment
  • Stay up-to-date on the latest industry trends and developments

Exploring Chiller Tech Salaries in More Detail

While the median salary for chiller technicians may be around $48,730 per year, it's important to remember that this is just a starting point. Depending on your level of experience, education, and other factors, you could earn significantly more than this amount. Additionally, the demand for skilled chiller technicians is only expected to grow in the coming years, which could lead to even higher salaries in the future.

How to Negotiate Your Chiller Tech Salary

If you're considering a new job as a chiller technician, it's important to know how to negotiate your salary. Some tips for negotiating a higher salary include:

  • Researching the average salary for chiller techs in your area and industry
  • Highlighting your experience and education during the negotiation process
  • Showing enthusiasm for the job and the company
  • Being willing to negotiate on other aspects of the job (such as benefits or vacation time)

Conclusion of How Much Do Chiller Techs Make

Overall, chiller technicians are in a good position when it comes to salaries. While there can be some variability depending on experience, location, and other factors, there is generally strong demand for skilled chiller techs, which can lead to competitive salaries. By staying up-to-date on industry trends and maximizing their experience and education, chiller techs can position themselves for even higher salaries in the future.
