How Much Do Dealership Auto Mechanics Make

Written by Daniel Sep 05, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you considering a career as an auto mechanic in a dealership? Do you want to know how much you can expect to make? Look no further, as we delve into the topic of how much do dealership auto mechanics make in this post.

Many people wonder about the salary of dealership auto mechanics, and it's no surprise. After all, working in a dealership can offer several perks, such as access to the latest technology and training opportunities. However, it's essential to know what you can expect to earn to make an informed decision.

The average salary of a dealership auto mechanic is around $50,000 per year. However, this figure can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and specialization. For instance, a mechanic working in a high-end dealership in a major city may earn more than someone working in a small-town dealership.

In addition to the base salary, dealership auto mechanics may also receive bonuses and other incentives. For example, a mechanic who meets or exceeds their performance targets may receive a bonus at the end of the year. Some dealerships may also offer benefits such as healthcare, retirement plans, and paid time off.

My Personal Experience

As a dealership auto mechanic with five years of experience, I can attest that the salary can be quite lucrative. I started at an entry-level position and worked my way up to a senior mechanic role. My current salary is around $65,000 per year, and I receive a bonus at the end of the year based on my performance.

Factors that Affect Salary

Several factors can impact how much a dealership auto mechanic makes. One of the most significant factors is experience. Mechanics with more experience are often paid more than those who are just starting. Specialization is another factor that can affect salary. For instance, a mechanic who specializes in a specific type of vehicle or repair may be paid more than a general mechanic.

Location Matters

The location of the dealership can also impact how much a mechanic makes. For instance, mechanics working in cities with a higher cost of living may earn more than those in rural areas. Additionally, dealerships in areas with a high demand for auto repair services may pay more to attract and retain skilled mechanics.

Training and Certifications

Finally, training and certifications can also affect salary. Dealerships may pay more for mechanics who have completed specialized training or hold certifications from industry organizations. For instance, a mechanic who is certified by the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) may be paid more than someone who is not certified.

FAQs about How Much Do Dealership Auto Mechanics Make

Q: How much does an entry-level dealership auto mechanic make?
A: An entry-level dealership auto mechanic typically makes around $30,000 to $35,000 per year, depending on location and other factors.

Q: How much can a senior dealership auto mechanic make?
A: A senior dealership auto mechanic can make upwards of $80,000 per year, depending on location and other factors.

Q: Do dealership auto mechanics receive benefits?
A: Yes, many dealership auto mechanics receive benefits such as healthcare, retirement plans, and paid time off.

Q: Do bonuses affect how much dealership auto mechanics make?
A: Yes, bonuses can impact how much a dealership auto mechanic makes. Mechanics who meet or exceed performance targets may receive a bonus at the end of the year.

Conclusion of How Much Do Dealership Auto Mechanics Make

Dealership auto mechanics can earn a good salary, with the average being around $50,000 per year. However, several factors can impact how much a mechanic makes, such as experience, specialization, location, and training. Ultimately, it's important to do your research and consider all of the factors before pursuing a career as a dealership auto mechanic.
