How Much Do Dealership Mechanics Get Paid

Written by Eveline Aug 30, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you considering a career as a dealership mechanic? Do you want to know how much you can expect to earn in this field? Look no further, as we explore the salaries of dealership mechanics in this blog post.

Many individuals wonder about the compensation of dealership mechanics. After all, this is a physically demanding job that requires technical expertise, so it's only fair to expect a decent salary. Unfortunately, some mechanics feel that they are not paid enough for their hard work, which can lead to dissatisfaction and high turnover rates in the industry.

So, how much do dealership mechanics get paid? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics, including those working in dealerships, was $44,050 as of May 2020. However, the salary can vary based on various factors such as location, experience, and certifications.

In summary, dealership mechanics can expect to earn a median annual wage of $44,050, but this can vary based on location, experience, and certifications. Now, let's dive deeper into the topic and explore some personal experiences and additional information.

What Factors Affect the Salary of Dealership Mechanics?

As mentioned earlier, several factors can impact the salary of a dealership mechanic. Let's take a closer look at each of these factors.

Location

The location of the dealership can significantly affect the salary of a mechanic. For instance, mechanics working in metropolitan areas tend to earn more than those working in rural areas due to the high cost of living.

Experience

Experience is another significant factor that can impact the salary of dealership mechanics. Those with years of experience tend to earn more than entry-level mechanics, as they have developed a unique set of skills and knowledge over time.

Personal Experience of a Dealership Mechanic

As a former dealership mechanic, I can attest to the fact that the salary can vary significantly based on the factors mentioned above. When I started as a mechanic, I earned around $30,000 annually, but as I gained more experience and certifications, my salary increased to around $60,000.

How Can Dealership Mechanics Increase Their Salary?

Dealership mechanics can increase their salary by pursuing certifications and advancing their skills. For instance, mechanics with advanced certifications such as ASE Master Technician tend to earn more than those without certifications. Additionally, specializing in a specific area such as diesel engines or hybrid vehicles can also increase a mechanic's salary.

Question and Answer

Q: Is the salary of dealership mechanics negotiable?

A: Yes, the salary of dealership mechanics is negotiable, especially during the hiring process. However, it's essential to research the average salary for the position and take into account your experience and certifications before negotiating.

Q: Can dealership mechanics earn overtime pay?

A: Yes, dealership mechanics can earn overtime pay if they work more than 40 hours a week. However, this can vary based on the dealership's policy and state laws.

Q: Do dealership mechanics receive benefits?

A: Yes, many dealerships offer benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off to their mechanics.

Q: Can dealership mechanics work part-time?

A: Yes, some dealerships offer part-time positions for mechanics. However, part-time positions may not offer the same benefits as full-time positions.

Conclusion of How Much Do Dealership Mechanics Get Paid

In conclusion, dealership mechanics can expect to earn a median annual wage of $44,050, but this can vary based on location, experience, and certifications. However, by pursuing certifications and advancing their skills, mechanics can increase their salary and improve their career prospects. Ultimately, the compensation of dealership mechanics is negotiable, and it's essential to research the average salary for the position and take into account your experience and certifications before negotiating.
