What is a Diesel Fitter?

A diesel fitter is a skilled tradesperson who maintains and repairs diesel engines and heavy vehicles, such as trucks, buses, and earthmoving equipment. They are responsible for diagnosing faults, replacing parts, and carrying out regular maintenance to ensure that the vehicles are running safely and efficiently.

As a diesel fitter, you may work in a variety of industries, including mining, construction, transport, and agriculture. You may work for a company or be self-employed, and your work may involve travel to different locations.

My Experience as a Diesel Fitter

I have been working as a diesel fitter in Australia for over 10 years, and I can say that it is a challenging but rewarding career. I started out as an apprentice and worked my way up to become a senior diesel fitter, earning a good salary along the way.

One of the things I enjoy about being a diesel fitter is the variety of work. Every day is different, and I get to work on a wide range of vehicles and equipment. I also enjoy the problem-solving aspect of the job, as diagnosing faults and finding solutions can be challenging and satisfying.

How to Become a Diesel Fitter in Australia

If you're interested in becoming a diesel fitter in Australia, there are several steps you can take to get started. Firstly, you will need to complete a Certificate III in Heavy Commercial Vehicle Mechanical Technology or a similar qualification.

You may also need to obtain a National Police Certificate and a White Card, which is a mandatory requirement for working on construction sites. Additionally, you may need to obtain a Heavy Vehicle Driver's Licence, depending on the type of vehicles you will be working on.
