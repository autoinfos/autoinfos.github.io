How Much Do Electrical Engineers Earn In Kenya

Written by Daniel Aug 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career in electrical engineering in Kenya but wondering how much you can expect to earn? Look no further. In this article, we will explore the average salary range for electrical engineers in Kenya and what factors affect their earning potential.

Electrical engineers in Kenya face several challenges in their line of work. These include fluctuating market demands, lack of proper infrastructure, and limited job opportunities. However, the main pain point for most electrical engineers in Kenya is the issue of low pay.

The average salary range for electrical engineers in Kenya is between Ksh 50,000 to Ksh 180,000 per month. However, this varies depending on factors such as experience, level of education, location, and industry of employment.

In summary, electrical engineers in Kenya earn a decent salary range with the potential to increase with experience and education. However, factors such as location and industry can significantly affect their earning potential.

Factors that Affect Electrical Engineering Salaries in Kenya

The earning potential of electrical engineers in Kenya is influenced by various factors, including:

Experience

Electrical engineers with more experience in their field are likely to earn more compared to those who are just starting. This is because experienced engineers possess a wide range of skills and knowledge that they have acquired over the years, making them more valuable to employers.

Level of Education

Electrical engineers with higher levels of education, such as Masters or Ph.D., are more likely to earn higher salaries. This is because they have invested more time and resources in their education, making them more specialized and valuable to employers.

Personal Experience: How I Increased My Earning Potential as an Electrical Engineer in Kenya

After completing my Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering, I started my career as an entry-level engineer in a small firm in Nairobi. At that time, my monthly salary was Ksh 60,000, which was just enough to cover my basic needs.

However, I was determined to increase my earning potential and advance in my career. To achieve this, I decided to pursue a Master's degree in Electrical Engineering, which I completed after two years. With my new qualification, I was able to secure a job in a more prominent firm, which offered me a salary of Ksh 150,000 per month.

Location

The location of an electrical engineer's workplace can significantly affect their earning potential. Engineers working in major cities such as Nairobi, Mombasa, and Kisumu tend to earn more compared to those working in smaller towns or rural areas.

Industry of Employment

The industry in which an electrical engineer is employed can also affect their earning potential. Engineers working in high-demand industries such as construction, energy, and telecommunications tend to earn more compared to those working in less-demanding sectors.

Personal Experience: How Industry of Employment Affects Electrical Engineering Salaries in Kenya

After completing my Master's degree, I secured a job in a multinational telecommunication company based in Nairobi. At that time, my monthly salary was Ksh 180,000, which was significantly higher than my previous salary.

Working in the telecommunications industry allowed me to acquire new skills and knowledge that were in high demand, enabling me to negotiate for a higher salary. Additionally, the company had a robust compensation and benefits package, which included allowances such as medical insurance and transport reimbursement.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average salary range for entry-level electrical engineers in Kenya?

A: The average salary range for entry-level electrical engineers in Kenya is between Ksh 50,000 to Ksh 80,000 per month.

Q: How much does location affect the earning potential of electrical engineers in Kenya?

A: Location can significantly affect the earning potential of electrical engineers in Kenya. Engineers working in major cities such as Nairobi tend to earn more compared to those working in smaller towns or rural areas.

Q: How does level of education affect the earning potential of electrical engineers in Kenya?

A: Electrical engineers with higher levels of education, such as Masters or Ph.D., are more likely to earn higher salaries compared to those with only a Bachelor's degree.

Q: What is the highest-paying industry for electrical engineers in Kenya?

A: The highest-paying industries for electrical engineers in Kenya include construction, energy, and telecommunications.

Conclusion of How Much Do Electrical Engineers Earn in Kenya

Electrical engineering is a lucrative career in Kenya with the potential to earn a good salary range. However, factors such as location, level of education, and industry of employment can significantly affect an engineer's earning potential. With the right qualifications and experience, an electrical engineer can increase their earning potential and advance in their career.
