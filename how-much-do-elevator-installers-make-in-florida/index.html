How Much Do Elevator Installers Make In Florida

Written by Luffy Nov 04, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Did you know that elevators are a crucial part of commercial and residential buildings? They make it easier for people to go up and down floors, especially for those who have mobility issues. However, installing and maintaining elevators require specialized skills and knowledge, which is why elevator installers are in demand. But how much do elevator installers make in Florida?

If you're someone who's interested in becoming an elevator installer in Florida, you might be wondering about the potential salary you can earn. The salary is one of the most critical factors people consider when deciding on a career path. Knowing the potential income can help you plan your finances and set expectations for your future.

According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for elevator installers and repairers in Florida is $79,030 as of May 2020. The median wage is the point where half of the workers in the occupation earn more than that amount, and half earn less. The lowest 10 percent of elevator installers and repairers earned less than $45,580, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $107,250.

In summary, elevator installers and repairers in Florida can earn a median annual wage of $79,030, with the lowest earners making less than $45,580 and the highest earners making more than $107,250.

What Determines the Salary of Elevator Installers in Florida?

Several factors can influence the salary of elevator installers and repairers in Florida, such as experience, location, and industry. For example, those who have more experience or work in high-rise buildings may earn higher salaries than entry-level or low-rise elevator installers. Similarly, those who work in metropolitan areas may earn higher wages than those in rural areas.

Personal experience wise, I have a friend who worked as an elevator installer in Miami, Florida. He started as an apprentice and worked his way up to become a licensed elevator installer. He said that the salary was excellent, and he was able to support his family comfortably. However, he also mentioned that the job can be physically demanding and requires attention to detail. But overall, he enjoys the job and feels financially stable.

How to Become an Elevator Installer in Florida?

If you're interested in becoming an elevator installer in Florida, you need to have a high school diploma or equivalent and complete an apprenticeship program. The program typically lasts four years and includes both classroom instruction and hands-on training. You'll learn about electrical and mechanical systems, blueprint reading, safety procedures, and building codes.

After completing the apprenticeship program, you need to obtain a license from the Florida Department of Business and Professional Regulation. To qualify for a license, you need to have at least four years of experience as an elevator installer or repairer and pass an exam.

What Are the Job Prospects for Elevator Installers in Florida?

The job prospects for elevator installers and repairers in Florida are expected to be favorable. According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, the employment of elevator installers and repairers in Florida is projected to grow by 9.9 percent from 2019 to 2029, which is higher than the average for all occupations. The demand for new elevator installations and modernizations is expected to drive the job growth.

How to Increase Your Salary as an Elevator Installer in Florida?

If you want to increase your salary as an elevator installer in Florida, you can consider gaining more experience, obtaining additional certifications or licenses, or specializing in a particular area of elevator installation and repair. For example, you can specialize in installation and maintenance of high-speed elevators or elevators with advanced safety features. You can also consider working for larger companies or in metropolitan areas, which may offer higher salaries.

Conclusion of How Much Do Elevator Installers Make in Florida?

Being an elevator installer in Florida can be a rewarding and financially stable career. The median annual wage for elevator installers and repairers in Florida is $79,030, and the job prospects are expected to be favorable. However, becoming an elevator installer requires completing an apprenticeship program and obtaining a license from the Florida Department of Business and Professional Regulation. If you're interested in this career path, gaining more experience and specializing in a particular area can help you increase your salary.
