How Much Do Entry Level Aircraft Mechanics Make

Written by Frank Nov 05, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you considering a career as an aircraft mechanic? If so, you're likely wondering how much you can expect to earn. The good news is, the aviation industry is experiencing a rapid growth, which means there are plenty of job opportunities for entry-level aircraft mechanics. In this post, we'll be discussing the average salary of entry-level aircraft mechanics, as well as the factors that can impact their earnings.

One of the biggest pain points for entry-level aircraft mechanics is the fear of not making enough money to support themselves. It's no secret that the cost of living in many areas is high, and these new mechanics may be worried that their starting salary won't be enough to cover their expenses.

The average salary for an entry-level aircraft mechanic is around $40,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on a number of factors, such as location, experience, and education. Mechanics who work in larger cities or for major airlines can expect to earn more than those who work for smaller companies or in more rural areas.

In summary, entry-level aircraft mechanics can expect to earn around $40,000 per year, but this can vary depending on several factors. It's important for those considering a career in this field to research the job market in their area and take into account the cost of living.

How Location Impacts Earnings

When it comes to how much entry-level aircraft mechanics make, location can have a big impact. Mechanics who work in larger cities or for major airlines can expect to earn more than those who work for smaller companies or in more rural areas. For example, a mechanic working in New York City may earn around $50,000 per year, while one working in a smaller town in the Midwest may only earn around $35,000.

Personally, I started my career as an entry-level aircraft mechanic in a small town in the Midwest. While the cost of living was low, I quickly realized that my earnings were also lower than if I had been working in a larger city. However, I was able to gain valuable experience and eventually move on to a higher-paying position.

How Experience Impacts Earnings

Experience is another factor that can impact how much entry-level aircraft mechanics make. Those who have more experience in the field can expect to earn a higher salary than those who are just starting out. For example, a mechanic with five years of experience may earn around $50,000 per year, while one who is just starting out may only earn around $30,000.

When I first started out as an entry-level aircraft mechanic, I had very little experience in the field. As a result, my starting salary was on the lower end of the scale. However, as I gained more experience and became more skilled at my job, my earnings increased.

How Education Impacts Earnings

Education can also impact how much entry-level aircraft mechanics make. Those who have completed a formal training program or have a degree in aviation maintenance can expect to earn a higher salary than those who do not. For example, a mechanic with an Associate's degree in aviation maintenance may earn around $45,000 per year, while one without any formal education may only earn around $35,000.

Personally, I completed a formal training program before starting my career as an aircraft mechanic. While this did require an investment of time and money, I believe it has paid off in the long run by allowing me to earn a higher salary and advance in my career.

How Union Membership Impacts Earnings

Union membership can also impact how much entry-level aircraft mechanics make. Those who are members of a union may be able to negotiate higher salaries and better benefits than those who are not. For example, a mechanic who is a member of a union may earn around $50,000 per year, while one who is not a member may only earn around $40,000.

While I have never been a member of a union myself, I have worked with many mechanics who are. They have told me that being a union member has allowed them to earn higher salaries, receive better benefits, and have more job security.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it possible for entry-level aircraft mechanics to earn more than $40,000 per year?

A: Yes, it is possible. Mechanics who work for major airlines or in larger cities can earn more than $40,000 per year.

Q: Do entry-level aircraft mechanics receive benefits?

A: Yes, entry-level aircraft mechanics typically receive benefits such as healthcare, retirement plans, and paid time off.

Q: How long does it take to become an entry-level aircraft mechanic?

A: It typically takes around 18 months to complete a formal training program in aviation maintenance and become an entry-level aircraft mechanic.

Q: Is there room for advancement in the field of aircraft maintenance?

A: Yes, there is plenty of room for advancement in the field of aircraft maintenance. Mechanics can become supervisors, managers, or even move into other areas of aviation such as engineering or safety.

Conclusion

In conclusion, entry-level aircraft mechanics can expect to earn around $40,000 per year, but this can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, education, and union membership. It's important for those considering a career in this field to do their research and take into account the cost of living in their area. With the aviation industry experiencing growth, there are plenty of opportunities for entry-level mechanics to advance in their careers and earn higher salaries.
