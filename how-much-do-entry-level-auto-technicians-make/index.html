How Much Do Entry Level Auto Technicians Make

Written by Christine Sep 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an entry level auto technician but wondering how much you can expect to make? The answer may surprise you.

Many people assume that entry level auto technicians make low wages, but the reality is that the pay can vary depending on a variety of factors. For example, the location of the job, the type of employer, and the technician's level of experience can all impact their starting salary.

On average, entry level auto technicians make around $30,000 to $40,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on the factors mentioned above. Some technicians may start out making less than $30,000 per year, while others may make more than $40,000 per year.

In summary, entry level auto technicians can expect to make around $30,000 to $40,000 per year, but this can vary depending on their location, employer, and experience level.

What Factors Affect Entry Level Auto Technician Salaries?

The salary of an entry level auto technician can be influenced by several factors. One of the most significant factors is the technician's level of experience. Technicians with more experience may be able to command a higher salary than those who are just starting out.

The location of the job can also impact a technician's salary. In general, technicians who work in urban areas or in states with higher costs of living may make more than those who work in rural areas or in states with lower costs of living.

The type of employer can also impact a technician's salary. For example, technicians who work for dealerships or high-end repair shops may make more than those who work for independent repair shops or franchises.

My Personal Experience as an Entry Level Auto Technician

When I first started out as an entry level auto technician, I was making around $32,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and moved to a higher-end repair shop, my salary increased to around $40,000 per year.

One thing I learned is that it's important to negotiate your starting salary and to keep track of your accomplishments and experience as you gain more knowledge and skills. This can help you negotiate raises and promotions in the future.

Tips for Maximizing Your Salary as an Entry Level Auto Technician

If you're interested in maximizing your salary as an entry level auto technician, here are a few tips:

1. Gain Experience

The more experience you have, the more valuable you are to potential employers. Consider completing an apprenticeship or on-the-job training to gain valuable experience.

2. Look for High-End Repair Shops or Dealerships

Technicians who work for high-end repair shops or dealerships may be able to command higher salaries than those who work for independent repair shops or franchises.

Conclusion of How Much Do Entry Level Auto Technicians Make

Entry level auto technicians can expect to make around $30,000 to $40,000 per year, but this can vary depending on their location, employer, and experience level. To maximize your salary as an entry level auto technician, consider gaining experience and looking for high-end repair shops or dealerships.

A: Dealerships and high-end repair shops typically pay entry level auto technicians the most.
