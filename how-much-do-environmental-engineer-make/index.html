How Much Do Environmental Engineers Make

Are you considering a career as an environmental engineer and wondering how much you can expect to make? Environmental engineering is a rewarding field that allows you to make a positive impact on the environment and public health. However, it's important to understand the salary expectations before pursuing this career path.

Environmental engineering can be a lucrative career, but it also has its challenges. It's a highly specialized field that requires a strong foundation in science and engineering. Additionally, environmental engineers must stay up-to-date with regulations and policies that affect the environment and public health.

The median salary for environmental engineers in the United States is $88,860 per year, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. However, salaries can vary depending on factors such as experience, education, location, and industry. For example, environmental engineers in the oil and gas industry tend to earn higher salaries than those in government or non-profit organizations.

In summary, environmental engineering can be a rewarding and lucrative career choice. It requires a strong foundation in science and engineering, as well as a commitment to staying up-to-date with regulations and policies. Salaries can vary depending on factors such as experience, education, location, and industry.

Salary Expectations for Entry-Level Environmental Engineers

As an entry-level environmental engineer, you can expect to earn a starting salary of around $55,000 to $65,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on factors such as your location and the industry you work in. For example, environmental engineers working in metropolitan areas tend to earn higher salaries than those in rural areas.

When I started my career as an environmental engineer, I worked for a small consulting firm in a rural area. My starting salary was around $60,000 per year, which was lower than what I would have earned if I had worked for a larger firm in a metropolitan area. However, I enjoyed the work and gained valuable experience that helped me advance in my career.

Salary Expectations for Senior Environmental Engineers

Senior environmental engineers with several years of experience can expect to earn a higher salary than entry-level engineers. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the top 10 percent of environmental engineers earn more than $137,090 per year.

After several years of working as an environmental engineer, I was promoted to a senior position at my consulting firm. My salary increased to around $100,000 per year, and I was able to take on more challenging projects and responsibilities.

Factors that Affect Environmental Engineer Salaries

As mentioned earlier, several factors can affect the salary of an environmental engineer. These include:

  • Experience
  • Education
  • Location
  • Industry
  • Specialization

For example, an environmental engineer with a Ph.D. may earn a higher salary than one with a bachelor's degree. Similarly, an environmental engineer working in the oil and gas industry may earn a higher salary than one working for a non-profit organization.

How to Increase Your Salary as an Environmental Engineer

If you're interested in increasing your salary as an environmental engineer, there are several ways to do so. One way is to specialize in a particular area of environmental engineering, such as air quality or water resources. Specialization can make you a more valuable asset to your employer and increase your earning potential.

Another way to increase your salary is to pursue advanced education, such as a master's degree or Ph.D. This can qualify you for higher-level positions and increase your earning potential.
