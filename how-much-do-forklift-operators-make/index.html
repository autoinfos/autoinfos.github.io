How Much Do Forklift Operators Make

Written by Eveline Aug 18, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you considering a career as a forklift operator, but unsure of the salary? Or maybe you're already a forklift operator, but wondering if you're being paid fairly? Either way, understanding how much forklift operators make is crucial information to have.

The salary of a forklift operator can vary depending on a variety of factors, such as location, experience, and industry. However, the national average salary for a forklift operator in the United States is around $15 per hour, or $31,000 per year. This is just the average, and some forklift operators may make more or less than this amount.

In addition to hourly pay, some forklift operators may receive benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off. It's important to factor in these benefits when considering the overall compensation for a forklift operator position.

To summarize, the national average salary for a forklift operator is around $15 per hour or $31,000 per year, but may vary depending on location and other factors. It's important to consider additional benefits when evaluating compensation for a forklift operator position.

What Affects the Salary of Forklift Operators?

When it comes to determining the salary of a forklift operator, several factors come into play. One of the most significant factors is experience. Generally, the more experience a forklift operator has, the higher their salary will be. Another factor is location. Forklift operators in urban areas tend to make more than those in rural areas due to the higher cost of living. The industry in which a forklift operator works can also impact their salary. For example, forklift operators in the warehousing and storage industry tend to make more than those in the retail industry.

Personally, I have been a forklift operator for over a decade and have seen my salary increase as I gained more experience and moved up in the industry. Starting out, I made around $12 per hour but now make closer to $20 per hour. It's important to note that not all forklift operator positions offer opportunities for advancement, so it's important to do your research and find a company that values its employees and offers room for growth.

How Can Forklift Operators Increase Their Salary?

One way for forklift operators to increase their salary is to gain additional certifications or training. For example, becoming certified to operate a different type of forklift or learning how to operate other machinery can make a forklift operator more valuable to their employer and lead to higher pay. Another way to increase salary is to seek out job opportunities in industries that tend to pay more for forklift operators, such as the warehousing and storage industry.

What Skills Are Important for Forklift Operators?

Aside from experience and training, there are certain skills that are crucial for forklift operators to have. One of the most important skills is attention to detail. Forklift operators need to be able to safely maneuver their equipment around tight spaces and stack products without causing damage. Communication skills are also important, as forklift operators often work as part of a team and need to be able to communicate effectively with their co-workers. Finally, physical dexterity is important, as forklift operators need to be able to sit for extended periods of time and operate their equipment with precision.

What Are Some Common Misconceptions About Forklift Operators?

One common misconception about forklift operators is that it's an easy job that anyone can do. In reality, operating a forklift safely and efficiently requires a great deal of skill and attention to detail. Another misconception is that forklift operators don't need to be physically fit. While forklift operators may not need to be in top physical condition, they do need to be able to sit for extended periods of time and operate their equipment with precision, which requires a certain level of physical dexterity.

Conclusion of How Much Do Forklift Operators Make

In conclusion, the salary of a forklift operator can vary depending on several factors, including experience, location, and industry. The national average salary for a forklift operator is around $15 per hour or $31,000 per year, but this may vary depending on the specific position. To increase their salary, forklift operators can gain additional certifications or seek out job opportunities in higher-paying industries. It's important to remember that operating a forklift is a skilled job that requires attention to detail, communication skills, and physical dexterity.
