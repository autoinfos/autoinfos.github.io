How Much Do Forklift Technicians Make

Written by Daniel Nov 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a forklift technician? If so, you may be wondering how much money you can expect to make in this field. The answer is not as simple as a specific number, as there are several factors that can impact a forklift technician's salary.

One of the pain points of considering a career as a forklift technician is the fear of not making enough money to support oneself or one's family. Additionally, there may be concerns about the stability and growth potential of the position.

The average salary for a forklift technician in the United States is around $42,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on factors such as experience level, location, and type of employer. For example, a forklift technician with several years of experience working for a large company may make significantly more than a technician with only a year or two of experience working for a smaller business.

In summary, how much a forklift technician makes can vary depending on several factors. However, the average salary in the United States is around $42,000 per year.

How Experience Impacts Salary

One factor that can greatly impact a forklift technician's salary is their level of experience. Those with more experience, typically make more money than those who are just starting out in the field. For example, a forklift technician with 10 years of experience may make upwards of $60,000 per year, while someone with only a year or two of experience may make closer to $30,000 per year.

Location and Employer Type

Another factor that can impact a forklift technician's salary is their location and type of employer. Technicians who work in larger cities or for larger companies may make more money than those who work in smaller towns or for smaller businesses. Additionally, those who work for unionized companies may have higher salaries and better benefits than non-unionized technicians.

The Importance of Certification

Obtaining certification as a forklift technician can greatly impact one's salary potential. Certification demonstrates to potential employers that the technician is knowledgeable and skilled in their field, which can lead to higher salaries and more job opportunities. Additionally, certified technicians may be able to take on more complex and higher-paying jobs than those who are not certified.

Negotiating Salary

When it comes to negotiating salary as a forklift technician, it's important to do research on the average salaries in one's area and for one's level of experience. It's also important to highlight any certifications or specialized skills that one may have that could lead to a higher salary. Additionally, it's important to be open to negotiating other benefits such as vacation time, sick leave, and healthcare options.

Personal Experience

As a forklift technician with 5 years of experience, I have found that my salary has increased steadily over the years. I started out making around $35,000 per year, and now make closer to $50,000 per year. I believe that my certification and experience have helped me to negotiate higher salaries and take on more complex jobs.
