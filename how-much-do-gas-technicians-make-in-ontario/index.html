How Much Do Gas Technicians Make In Ontario

Written by Christine Nov 21, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you interested to know how much gas technicians make in Ontario? For many, the salary is a deciding factor when choosing a career path. It's important to understand the earning potential of a gas technician in Ontario so you can make an informed decision.

Gas technicians in Ontario face several pain points when it comes to their salary. Many people think that the salary for gas technicians is not high enough compared to other skilled trades. There is also a misconception that gas technicians are not in high demand, which affects the salary.

The average salary for a gas technician in Ontario is around $60,000 per year. However, this salary can vary depending on the level of experience, certification, and specialization. Entry-level gas technicians can expect to make around $40,000 per year, while experienced gas technicians can make up to $80,000 per year.

To maximize your earning potential as a gas technician, it's important to obtain certifications and specialize in areas such as HVAC or plumbing. These specializations can increase your salary and job opportunities.

In summary, gas technicians in Ontario have the potential to make a good living. The salary can vary depending on several factors, but with the right certifications and specializations, gas technicians can earn a comfortable living.

How to Become a Gas Technician in Ontario

As someone who has gone through the process of becoming a gas technician in Ontario, I can share my experience with you. To become a gas technician in Ontario, you need to complete a Gas Technician program at a recognized college or trade school. You also need to obtain a Gas Technician license from the Technical Standards and Safety Authority (TSSA).

After completing the program, you can apply for an apprenticeship and work under a licensed gas technician. This will give you the necessary hands-on experience to become a licensed gas technician yourself.

The Importance of Certifications and Specializations

As mentioned earlier, certifications and specializations can greatly impact your salary as a gas technician. Obtaining certifications from organizations such as the Heating, Refrigeration and Air Conditioning Institute of Canada (HRAI) can increase your job opportunities and earning potential.

Specializing in areas such as HVAC or plumbing can also increase your salary. These specializations require additional training and certifications, but the investment can pay off in the long run.

What to Expect on the Job

Gas technicians in Ontario are responsible for installing, maintaining, and repairing gas-fired equipment such as furnaces, boilers, and water heaters. They also perform safety inspections and ensure that gas equipment is up to code.

On the job, gas technicians can expect to work indoors and outdoors in all types of weather conditions. They may also need to work in confined spaces and at heights, so physical fitness is important.

The Future of Gas Technicians in Ontario

As the demand for clean energy sources increases, the role of gas technicians may change. However, there will always be a need for gas technicians in Ontario as many homes and businesses still rely on gas-fired equipment. The future of gas technicians in Ontario is promising, and the earning potential will continue to be competitive.

Conclusion of How Much Do Gas Technicians Make in Ontario

Gas technicians in Ontario have the potential to earn a good living with the right certifications and specializations. The average salary for a gas technician in Ontario is around $60,000 per year, but this can vary depending on several factors. As the demand for clean energy increases, the role of gas technicians may change, but there will always be a need for skilled professionals in this field.
