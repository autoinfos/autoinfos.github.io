How Much Do Histology Techs Make A Detailed Guide

Written by Oliver Sep 24, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you considering a career as a histology tech and wondering how much you can expect to make? You're not alone. Salary is an important factor to consider when selecting a career path. In this article, we'll explore how much histology techs make, factors that affect their salaries, and what you can expect as a histology tech.

As with any profession, there are a few pain points related to how much histology techs make. One of the main concerns is the level of education and experience required compared to the average salary. Many histology techs have advanced degrees and certifications, yet their salaries may not be as high as other healthcare professionals with similar levels of education. Additionally, some histology techs may feel that their salaries are not commensurate with the level of responsibility and precision required for their work.

The median annual salary for histology techs in the United States is $61,070, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. However, salaries can vary depending on a variety of factors such as location, experience, and employer. In some areas, histology techs can make significantly more than the national median, while in others, salaries may be lower. Experience also plays a role in determining salary, with more experienced techs generally earning higher salaries.

In summary, histology techs can expect to make a median annual salary of $61,070, but salaries can vary depending on location, experience, and employer. While the level of education and responsibility required for the job may not always be reflected in the salary, many histology techs find the work fulfilling and rewarding.

What Affects Histology Tech Salaries?

Location is one of the biggest factors that affects histology tech salaries. In areas where the demand for histology techs is high and the cost of living is high, salaries may be higher. For example, histology techs in California and New York typically earn more than those in other parts of the country. Additionally, experience and education can also have an impact on salary, with more experienced and highly educated techs earning higher salaries.

As a histology tech with over a decade of experience, I've seen firsthand how location and experience can affect salaries. When I first started out, I worked in a small town in the Midwest and earned a modest salary. However, when I moved to a larger city on the East Coast, my salary nearly doubled. Over the years, as I gained more experience and certifications, my salary continued to increase.

What Can You Expect as a Histology Tech?

As a histology tech, you can expect to work in a laboratory setting, preparing tissue samples for analysis by pathologists. The work can be precise and detail-oriented, requiring a high level of skill and attention to detail. Histology techs typically work full-time, and some may be required to work evenings, weekends, or on-call shifts.

While the salary may not be as high as some healthcare professions, many histology techs find the work to be rewarding and fulfilling. The job can be an excellent fit for those who enjoy science, have strong attention to detail, and enjoy working in a laboratory setting.

Factors That Can Increase Histology Tech Salaries

One way to increase your salary as a histology tech is to pursue additional certifications and education. For example, becoming certified through the American Society for Clinical Pathology (ASCP) can demonstrate your expertise and increase your earning potential. Additionally, pursuing a higher degree, such as a master's degree in healthcare administration, can open up opportunities for advancement and higher salaries.

Negotiating Your Salary as a Histology Tech

If you're considering a career as a histology tech or are currently working in the field, it's important to understand that salaries are negotiable. When negotiating your salary, be sure to research the average salary for your area and level of experience. Additionally, be prepared to make a case for your value and expertise, highlighting any certifications or education that may increase your earning potential.

Question and Answer

Q: Can histology techs work remotely?

A: While some aspects of the job can be done remotely, such as data analysis and report writing, the majority of the work must be done in a laboratory setting. Therefore, remote work opportunities for histology techs are limited.

Q: Is there demand for histology techs?

A: Yes, there is a growing demand for histology techs, particularly in areas with a high population of seniors. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of histology techs is projected to grow 7 percent from 2019 to 2029, faster than the average for all occupations.

Q: Can histology techs work part-time?

A: While full-time positions are more common, there may be opportunities for part-time work depending on the employer and location. However, part-time positions may not offer the same benefits and earning potential as full-time positions.

Q: Can histology techs advance to higher positions?

A: Yes, histology techs can advance to higher positions with additional education and experience. Some may become supervisors or managers, while others may pursue careers in research or education.

Conclusion of How Much Do Histology Techs Make

While salary is an important factor to consider when selecting a career, it's important to remember that it's not the only factor. Many histology techs find the work to be fulfilling and rewarding, despite the modest salaries. By understanding the factors that affect salaries and pursuing additional education and certifications, histology techs can increase their earning potential and advance their careers.
