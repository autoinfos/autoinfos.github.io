How Much Do Hvac Techs Make Per Hour

Written by Eveline Jul 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career in the HVAC industry? Or maybe you're just curious about how much HVAC technicians make per hour? Either way, you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll dive into the topic of HVAC technician salaries and explore the factors that can impact how much they earn.

One of the biggest pain points for those considering a career in the HVAC industry is uncertainty about how much money they can expect to make. While HVAC technicians are generally considered to be well-paid professionals, the amount they earn can vary widely depending on a number of factors.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for HVAC technicians as of May 2020 was $24.87. This means that half of all HVAC technicians earned more than this amount, while the other half earned less.

So, what factors can impact how much an HVAC technician earns per hour? One of the biggest factors is experience. As with most professions, HVAC technicians who have been working in the industry for many years generally earn higher salaries than those who are just starting out. Other factors that can impact HVAC technician salaries include location, level of education, and any specialized skills or certifications they may have.

What Is the Average Salary for HVAC Technicians?

When it comes to HVAC technician salaries, the average can vary widely depending on a number of factors. Some HVAC technicians earn as little as $14 per hour, while others earn more than $40 per hour. On average, however, HVAC technicians in the United States earn around $50,000 per year.

One thing to keep in mind is that HVAC technicians who work for larger companies may have more opportunities for advancement and higher salaries than those who work for smaller companies or are self-employed. Additionally, HVAC technicians who specialize in areas such as commercial refrigeration or industrial HVAC systems may be able to command higher salaries than those who work in residential HVAC.

My Personal Experience with HVAC Technician Salaries

As someone who has worked in the HVAC industry for several years, I can attest to the fact that salaries can vary widely depending on a number of factors. When I first started out as an HVAC technician, I was earning around $18 per hour. However, as I gained more experience and specialized skills, my hourly rate increased to around $30 per hour.

One thing that I've found to be true in the HVAC industry is that hard work and dedication often pay off in the form of higher salaries and more opportunities for advancement. By continuing to learn and grow in your field, you can position yourself for higher-paying jobs and a successful career in HVAC.

How Can You Increase Your HVAC Technician Salary?

If you're an HVAC technician looking to increase your salary, there are a few things you can do to position yourself for success. First and foremost, focus on developing your skills and knowledge of the industry. This can include pursuing additional certifications or training, attending industry conferences and events, and staying up-to-date on the latest trends and technologies in HVAC.

Another way to increase your salary as an HVAC technician is to specialize in a certain area of the industry. For example, if you have experience working with commercial refrigeration systems, you may be able to command higher salaries than those who work primarily in residential HVAC.

Conclusion

So, how much do HVAC techs make per hour? The answer is that it depends on a number of factors, including experience, location, education, and specialized skills. However, on average, HVAC technicians in the United States earn around $24.87 per hour. By focusing on developing your skills and specializing in certain areas of the industry, you can position yourself for higher-paying jobs and a successful career in HVAC.

Question and Answer

Q: Can HVAC technicians earn more than $40 per hour?

A: Yes, some HVAC technicians earn more than $40 per hour, particularly those with many years of experience or specialized skills and certifications.

Q: How much do entry-level HVAC technicians typically earn?

A: Entry-level HVAC technicians may earn around $14-18 per hour, depending on the company they work for and their location.

Q: Is it possible to earn a six-figure salary as an HVAC technician?

A: While it's rare, some HVAC technicians who have many years of experience and specialized skills may be able to earn six-figure salaries.

Q: Do HVAC technicians receive benefits in addition to their hourly wages?

A: Yes, many HVAC technicians receive benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off in addition to their hourly wages.
