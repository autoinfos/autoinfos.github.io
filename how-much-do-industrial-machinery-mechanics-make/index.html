How Much Do Industrial Machinery Mechanics Make

Written by Christine Jul 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an industrial machinery mechanic but wondering about the salary? Industrial machinery mechanics play a vital role in the manufacturing industry, and their work involves repairing and maintaining complex machinery used in manufacturing. It's no surprise that many people are curious about how much they can earn in this field.

One of the main pain points for those considering a career as an industrial machinery mechanic is the fear of not earning enough to support themselves and their families. With the rising cost of living, people need to know that their hard work will be adequately compensated.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, industrial machinery mechanics earn a median annual salary of $53,590, which equates to a median hourly wage of $25.76. However, the salary range can vary depending on factors such as experience, location, and industry.

In summary, industrial machinery mechanics can make a decent living, with the median salary being around $54,000 per year. However, as with any profession, the salary can vary depending on various factors.

What Is the Average Salary of an Industrial Machinery Mechanic?

When I started as an industrial machinery mechanic, I was earning around $40,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and knowledge, my salary increased, and I now earn around $60,000 per year. The average salary for an industrial machinery mechanic ranges from $41,000 to $75,000 per year, depending on various factors.

How Much Can an Industrial Machinery Mechanic Earn in Different Industries?

The industry you work in can also affect your salary as an industrial machinery mechanic. For example, those working in aerospace and telecommunications tend to earn more than those in other industries. I've worked in various industries, and I've found that the food and beverage industry typically offers lower salaries than other industries.

Factors That Affect an Industrial Machinery Mechanic's Salary

Several factors can affect an industrial machinery mechanic's salary, including their experience, location, industry, and education level. Typically, those with more experience and education tend to earn higher salaries.

How to Increase Your Salary as an Industrial Machinery Mechanic

If you're an industrial machinery mechanic looking to increase your salary, there are several ways to do so. Firstly, gaining more experience and education can help you earn more. Additionally, consider specializing in a particular area of industrial machinery maintenance, such as hydraulics or electronics. Specializing can make you more valuable to employers, which can lead to higher salaries.

Is Overtime Common for Industrial Machinery Mechanics?

Yes, overtime is common for industrial machinery mechanics. Many manufacturing plants operate 24/7, so mechanics may need to work outside of regular business hours to ensure that machinery is maintained and repaired promptly. Overtime pay can increase an industrial machinery mechanic's salary significantly, making it a lucrative career choice.

Question and Answer

Q: Can industrial machinery mechanics earn bonuses?

A: Yes, some companies offer bonuses to industrial machinery mechanics who meet or exceed performance targets.

Q: Is a college degree necessary to become an industrial machinery mechanic?

A: No, a college degree is not required to become an industrial machinery mechanic. However, some employers may prefer candidates with an associate's degree in industrial maintenance or a related field.

Q: What are the job prospects like for industrial machinery mechanics?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the employment of industrial machinery mechanics is projected to grow 13 percent from 2019 to 2029, which is much faster than the average for all occupations.

Q: Are there any additional benefits to being an industrial machinery mechanic?

A: Yes, many companies offer benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off to their industrial machinery mechanics.

Conclusion of How Much Do Industrial Machinery Mechanics Make

Industrial machinery mechanics can make a decent living, with the median salary being around $54,000 per year. However, the salary can vary depending on various factors such as experience, location, and industry. By gaining more experience and education and specializing in a particular area of industrial machinery maintenance, you can increase your earning potential as an industrial machinery mechanic. With job prospects projected to grow, becoming an industrial machinery mechanic can be a lucrative and rewarding career choice.
