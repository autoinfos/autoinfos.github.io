How Much Do Mechanics Make An Hour

Written by Petter Jun 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in becoming a mechanic but don't know how much they make an hour? Perhaps you want to know if it's a financially viable career before making the leap? This article will explore the average hourly wage of mechanics, as well as factors that can affect their earnings.

It's no secret that mechanics work hard and sometimes in difficult conditions. They're responsible for diagnosing and repairing vehicles, which can be a stressful job. On top of that, they often work long hours and may need to work weekends or evenings. All of these factors can make it important for mechanics to earn a decent wage.

The average hourly wage for mechanics in the United States is $20.24, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. However, this can vary widely depending on the type of work they do and where they're located.

In general, mechanics who work for dealerships or repair shops tend to earn more than those who work for smaller businesses or independently. Additionally, mechanics who specialize in certain areas, such as diesel engines or transmissions, tend to earn more than those who work on general repairs.

My Personal Experience

As someone who has worked in the auto industry, I can speak to the fact that mechanics can earn a good wage. I've seen some earn as little as $15 an hour, while others can earn upwards of $30 an hour or more. It really depends on their skill level, experience, and where they work.

Factors That Affect Earnings

As mentioned earlier, where a mechanic works and what they specialize in can have a big impact on their earnings. However, there are other factors to consider as well.

For example, mechanics who are ASE certified tend to earn more than those who aren't. ASE (Automotive Service Excellence) is a professional certification that shows a mechanic is skilled in their trade. Additionally, mechanics who work in areas with a high cost of living, such as New York City or San Francisco, tend to earn more than those in rural areas.

Experience Matters

Another important factor to consider is experience. Mechanics who are just starting out in their careers may earn less than those who have been working for decades. However, as they gain experience and build their skills, their earnings can increase significantly.

Negotiating Pay

If you're considering becoming a mechanic, it's important to know that you may be able to negotiate your pay. Before accepting a job offer, do some research on what mechanics in your area typically earn. Then, when discussing pay with a potential employer, be prepared to explain why you believe you're worth a higher wage.

Conclusion

So, how much do mechanics make an hour? On average, it's around $20.24. However, this can vary widely depending on factors such as where they work, what they specialize in, and their experience. If you're interested in becoming a mechanic, it's important to do your research and negotiate your pay to ensure you earn a fair wage.

Question and Answer

Q: Can mechanics earn more than $30 an hour?
A: Yes, some mechanics who are highly skilled and work in specialized areas can earn more than $30 an hour.

Q: How important is ASE certification for mechanics?
A: ASE certification is important for mechanics who want to earn a higher wage and show that they're skilled in their trade.

Q: Do mechanics who work in rural areas earn less than those in cities?
A: Yes, mechanics who work in areas with a lower cost of living may earn less than those in cities with a higher cost of living.

Q: Can mechanics negotiate their pay?
A: Yes, mechanics can negotiate their pay before accepting a job offer. Researching what other mechanics in the area typically earn can help when negotiating.
