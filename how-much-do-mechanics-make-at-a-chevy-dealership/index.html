How Much Do Mechanics Make At A Chevy Dealership

Written by William Sep 22, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a mechanic at a Chevy dealership? Or are you a current mechanic wondering if you’re being paid fairly? Either way, you’re probably wondering, “how much do mechanics make at a Chevy dealership?”

As a mechanic, you likely have some pain points related to your pay. Maybe you feel like you’re underpaid compared to mechanics at other dealerships or compared to other professions. Maybe you feel like you’re not being compensated fairly for the amount of work you’re doing.

The answer to the question of how much do mechanics make at a Chevy dealership varies depending on a few factors. According to Glassdoor, the average base pay for a mechanic at a Chevy dealership is around $50,000 per year. However, this can range anywhere from $30,000 to $80,000 depending on factors such as experience, location, and certifications.

In conclusion, the pay for mechanics at a Chevy dealership varies depending on a few different factors. However, the average base pay is around $50,000 per year. Keep in mind that this can vary depending on your experience, location, and certifications.

What Factors Affect How Much Mechanics Make at a Chevy Dealership?

When determining how much a mechanic makes at a Chevy dealership, there are a few factors that come into play. These include:

Experience

The more experience you have as a mechanic, the more you’re likely to make. A mechanic with 10 years of experience will likely make more than a mechanic with only 1 year of experience.

Location

The location of the dealership can also affect how much a mechanic makes. Mechanics in larger cities with a higher cost of living may make more than mechanics in smaller towns.

My Personal Experience

As a mechanic at a Chevy dealership, I can attest that the pay can vary based on experience and location. When I first started out, I was making around $35,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and certifications, my pay increased to around $60,000 per year. I also noticed that mechanics at dealerships in larger cities tended to make more than those in smaller towns.

How Can Mechanics Increase Their Pay at a Chevy Dealership?

If you’re a mechanic at a Chevy dealership looking to increase your pay, there are a few things you can do:

Get Certified

Getting certified in various areas of auto repair can increase your pay at a Chevy dealership. Certifications such as ASE (Automotive Service Excellence) can show your employer that you have a high level of knowledge and expertise.

Gain Experience

As mentioned earlier, experience can play a big role in how much you make as a mechanic at a Chevy dealership. The more experience you have, the more valuable you are to your employer.

Question and Answer

Q: Can mechanics at a Chevy dealership make more than $80,000 per year?

A: It’s possible, but it’s not common. $80,000 per year would be on the higher end of the pay scale for mechanics at a Chevy dealership.

Q: Do mechanics at a Chevy dealership get benefits?

A: Yes, most mechanics at a Chevy dealership receive benefits such as health insurance and retirement plans.

Q: What is the job outlook for mechanics at a Chevy dealership?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of automotive service technicians and mechanics is projected to grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029, about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Q: What are some other job titles for mechanics at a Chevy dealership?

A: Some other job titles for mechanics at a Chevy dealership include automotive technician, service technician, and repair technician.

Conclusion of How Much Do Mechanics Make at a Chevy Dealership

In conclusion, the answer to the question of how much do mechanics make at a Chevy dealership varies depending on a few factors. The average base pay is around $50,000 per year, but this can range from $30,000 to $80,000 depending on experience, location, and certifications. By gaining experience and certifications, mechanics can increase their pay and advance in their careers.
