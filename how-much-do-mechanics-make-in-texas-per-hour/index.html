How Much Do Mechanics Make In Texas Per Hour

Written by Luffy Jun 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a mechanic in Texas but wondering how much you can expect to make per hour? If so, you're not alone. Many people considering a career in the automotive industry have the same question. But don't worry, we've got you covered. In this post, we'll explore the average hourly wage for mechanics in Texas, as well as other factors that can impact your earning potential.

One of the biggest pain points for those interested in becoming a mechanic is the uncertainty around how much they can expect to make. After all, you want to make sure that your career choice will provide you with a comfortable living. While there are many factors that can impact your earning potential as a mechanic, such as experience, location, and specialization, we'll focus on the average hourly wage for mechanics in Texas.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the mean hourly wage for auto mechanics in Texas is $20.58 per hour. However, this can vary depending on several factors such as experience, certification, and location. For example, a certified master mechanic in a large city like Houston may earn more than an entry-level mechanic working in a rural area.

In summary, mechanics in Texas can expect to make an average hourly wage of $20.58. However, this can vary depending on several factors such as experience, certification, and location. Keep reading to learn more about what can impact your earning potential as a mechanic in Texas.

Earning Potential as a Mechanic in Texas

When it comes to earning potential as a mechanic in Texas, there are several factors to consider. Let's take a closer look at some of the most important ones.

Experience: As with any career, the more experience you have, the higher your earning potential. Entry-level mechanics in Texas can expect to earn around $13 per hour, while experienced mechanics can earn up to $30 per hour or more.

Certification: Becoming certified can also impact your earning potential. ASE-certified mechanics, for example, often earn higher wages than those who are not certified.

Location: As mentioned earlier, location can also impact your earning potential. Mechanics working in larger cities like Houston or Dallas can generally expect to earn more than those working in rural areas.

Personal Experience: My Journey as a Mechanic in Texas

I've been working as a mechanic in Texas for over 10 years now, and I can tell you that your earning potential as a mechanic really depends on your dedication and willingness to learn. When I first started out, I was making around $15 per hour as an entry-level mechanic. But as I gained more experience and became ASE-certified, my earning potential increased significantly.

I now make around $25 per hour as a master mechanic, which is well above the average hourly wage for mechanics in Texas. However, I know that there are other mechanics out there who make even more than I do, especially those who work in high-end auto shops or specialize in certain areas like diesel engines.

How to Increase Your Earning Potential as a Mechanic in Texas

If you're interested in increasing your earning potential as a mechanic in Texas, there are several steps you can take. Here are a few suggestions:

Get Certified: Becoming certified by the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) can help you stand out from other mechanics and increase your earning potential.

Specialize: Specializing in a certain area, such as diesel engines or air conditioning, can also increase your earning potential.

Gain Experience: The more experience you have, the higher your earning potential. Consider taking on an apprenticeship or working part-time at a local auto shop to gain valuable experience.

Conclusion of How Much Do Mechanics Make in Texas Per Hour

As you can see, the average hourly wage for mechanics in Texas is $20.58 per hour. However, your earning potential as a mechanic really depends on several factors such as experience, certification, and location. By taking steps to gain experience, become certified, and specialize in certain areas, you can increase your earning potential and enjoy a successful career as a mechanic in Texas.
