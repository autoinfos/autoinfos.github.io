How Much Do Mechanics Make Monthly

Written by Eveline Jul 15, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career in mechanics but wondering how much you can expect to earn each month? It's a valid concern, and one that many aspiring mechanics have before entering the field. The good news is that mechanics can make a decent living, but the answer to how much they make isn't necessarily straightforward.

One of the biggest pain points related to how much mechanics make monthly is that there is a wide range of salaries within the field. Factors like experience, location, and type of employer can all impact how much a mechanic earns. Additionally, different types of mechanics (such as automotive versus aircraft) may have different earning potentials.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for automotive service technicians and mechanics in the United States is $44,050. This breaks down to a monthly salary of around $3,670. However, it's important to note that this is just a median, and many mechanics earn more or less than this amount.

In summary, how much mechanics make monthly can vary depending on a variety of factors. However, the median annual salary for automotive mechanics in the US is around $44,050, which translates to a monthly salary of approximately $3,670.

How to Increase Your Earning Potential as a Mechanic

If you're interested in becoming a mechanic and want to increase your earning potential, there are several steps you can take. First and foremost, gaining experience and building your skills is crucial. The more experience you have, the more valuable you'll be to employers, which can lead to higher salaries.

Additionally, obtaining certifications in specific areas (such as electrical systems or brake repair) can make you a more sought-after mechanic and increase your earning potential. Finally, exploring different types of mechanic jobs (such as working for a dealership versus a small repair shop) can help you find the highest-paying opportunities.

The Importance of Location in Earnings Potential

One factor that can significantly impact how much a mechanic makes is their location. Salaries for mechanics can vary widely depending on where they work. For example, mechanics working in urban areas may earn more than those in rural areas due to higher demand for their services and higher living costs.

Additionally, some states have a higher demand for mechanics than others, which can also impact salaries. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the top-paying states for automotive service technicians and mechanics are Alaska, California, and Hawaii.

Types of Mechanics and Their Earning Potential

While the median annual salary for automotive service technicians and mechanics is around $44,050, it's important to note that different types of mechanics may have different earning potentials. For example, aircraft mechanics and service technicians have a higher median annual salary of $64,090, while diesel service technicians and mechanics have a median annual salary of $48,500.

How to Negotiate a Higher Salary as a Mechanic

If you're already working as a mechanic but feel you're not earning enough, there are steps you can take to negotiate a higher salary. Start by researching the average salary for your position and experience level in your area. Use this information to make a case for why you deserve a higher salary.

Additionally, highlight any accomplishments or skills you've gained that make you a valuable employee. Finally, be prepared to negotiate and work with your employer to find a mutually agreeable salary.

Conclusion of How Much Do Mechanics Make Monthly

Overall, how much mechanics make monthly can vary widely depending on factors like experience, location, and type of employer. However, the median annual salary for automotive service technicians and mechanics in the US is around $44,050, which translates to a monthly salary of approximately $3,670. By building your skills, obtaining certifications, and exploring different types of mechanic jobs, you can increase your earning potential as a mechanic.
