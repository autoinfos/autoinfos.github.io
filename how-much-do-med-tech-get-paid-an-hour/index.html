How Much Do Med Tech Get Paid An Hour

Written by Luffy Jul 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a medical technologist or wondering whether you're being paid fairly as one? If so, you're not alone. Many medical techs have questions about their earning potential and whether their salaries are commensurate with the demands of their jobs.

Medical technologists are responsible for performing complex laboratory tests that help diagnose and treat diseases. They work in a variety of settings, including hospitals, clinics, and research laboratories. Given the importance of their work, it's not surprising that many medical techs wonder whether they're being fairly compensated.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for medical technologists is $27.88. This means that half of all medical techs earn more than this amount and half earn less. The top 10 percent of earners in this field make more than $40.36 per hour, while the bottom 10 percent earn less than $19.19 per hour.

In summary, medical technologists can expect to earn a median hourly wage of $27.88. However, this amount can vary depending on factors such as experience, location, and employer.

What Affects a Medical Technologist's Salary?

As mentioned above, several factors can influence a medical technologist's salary. One of the most significant is experience. Medical techs who have been in the field for many years and have extensive knowledge and skills tend to earn more than those who are just starting.

Location is another factor that affects salaries. Medical techs who work in high-cost cities or regions tend to earn more than those who work in lower-cost areas. The employer can also play a significant role in determining a medical technologist's salary. Hospitals, for example, tend to pay more than clinics or research labs.

My Experience as a Medical Technologist

As a medical technologist with ten years of experience, I can attest to the fact that experience is a significant factor in determining salaries. When I started my career, I earned around $22 per hour, but as I gained experience and took on more responsibilities, my salary increased to $35 per hour.

I also found that location played a role in my salary. I worked in a high-cost city, which meant that I earned more than some of my colleagues who worked in lower-cost areas. Additionally, I found that working for a hospital allowed me to earn more than I would have if I worked for a clinic or research laboratory.

Earning Potential for Medical Technologists

While the median hourly wage for medical technologists is $27.88, there are ways for medical techs to increase their earning potential. One is to continue their education and earn a higher degree or certification. Medical techs who hold a master's degree or certification in a specialized area can earn more than those who only have a bachelor's degree.

Another way to increase earning potential is to gain experience in a high-demand area such as molecular biology or genetics. Medical techs who have expertise in these areas are often in high demand and can command higher salaries.

How to Negotiate a Higher Salary

If you're a medical technologist who feels that you're not being paid fairly for your work, there are ways to negotiate a higher salary. One is to do your research and find out what other medical techs in your area with similar experience and qualifications are earning. This will give you a baseline for what you should be earning.

Another way to negotiate a higher salary is to demonstrate your value to your employer. Keep track of your achievements and be prepared to present them during salary negotiations. This can include examples of how you've saved the company money or improved processes.

Conclusion of How Much Do Med Tech Get Paid an Hour

In conclusion, medical technologists can expect to earn a median hourly wage of $27.88. However, this amount can vary depending on factors such as experience, location, and employer. Medical techs who want to increase their earning potential can do so by continuing their education, gaining experience in high-demand areas, and pursuing specialized certifications. By doing their research and demonstrating their value to their employers, medical techs can also negotiate higher salaries.
