How Much Do New Grad Engineers Make

Written by Eveline Sep 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you a recent engineering graduate wondering how much you can expect to earn in your first job? Look no further - we've got you covered. In this post, we'll break down the average salaries for new grad engineers, as well as factors that can affect your earning potential.

It can be frustrating to come out of college with a degree and not know what your salary expectations should be. You want to make sure you're being fairly compensated for your hard work, but you also don't want to price yourself out of the job market. This is where understanding average salaries and industry standards can be helpful.

According to the National Association of Colleges and Employers (NACE), the average starting salary for engineering graduates in the United States is $72,900. However, this can vary depending on your specific field of engineering, as well as the location and size of the company you work for.

In general, engineering fields that are in higher demand tend to have higher salaries. For example, software engineering and computer science are two fields that have seen significant growth in recent years, and therefore tend to have higher starting salaries. Additionally, engineering jobs in large cities with high costs of living may also pay more to compensate for the higher expenses.

Personal Experience:

As a recent mechanical engineering graduate, I was thrilled to receive a job offer for $75,000 per year. However, I also had friends in different engineering fields who were making anywhere from $60,000 to $90,000 per year. It really depends on the specific job, company, and location you're working in.

Factors That Affect Earnings:

Aside from your field of engineering and location, there are other factors that can impact your earning potential as a new grad engineer. One of these is your level of education - individuals with a master's or PhD may be able to command higher salaries than those with just a bachelor's degree.

Your level of experience also plays a role - while you may be starting in an entry-level position, if you have relevant internships or co-op experience, you may be able to negotiate a higher starting salary. Additionally, certain industries (such as oil and gas or aerospace) may pay more due to the specialized nature of the work.

Industry Standards:

While it's important to understand the factors that can impact your earnings, it's also useful to have a general idea of industry standards. NACE provides average salary data for a variety of engineering fields, including:

  • Computer Engineering: $76,639
  • Civil Engineering: $61,365
  • Electrical Engineering: $73,078
  • Mechanical Engineering: $69,244

Negotiating Your Salary:

Keep in mind that while these are average salaries, they are not set in stone. You may be able to negotiate a higher salary based on your qualifications, experience, or the company's budget. It's important to do your research and come prepared with data to support your salary request.

Personal Experience:

When I received my job offer, I did some research on industry standards and found that the average starting salary for mechanical engineers in my area was closer to $65,000. I used this information to negotiate a higher starting salary, and was ultimately able to secure $75,000.

Question and Answer:

Q: Do engineering salaries vary by region?

A: Yes, engineering salaries can vary significantly based on where you live and work. Cities with high costs of living (such as San Francisco or New York City) may pay more to compensate for the higher expenses.

Q: Is it common to negotiate your starting salary as a new grad engineer?

A: While it may not be common, it is definitely possible to negotiate your starting salary. It's important to do your research and come prepared with data to support your salary request.

Q: How much of a difference can a master's degree make in terms of salary?

A: In general, individuals with a master's degree can command higher salaries than those with just a bachelor's degree. According to NACE, the average starting salary for engineering graduates with a master's degree is $77,000.

Q: What is the highest-paying field of engineering?

A: According to NACE, the highest-paying field of engineering is petroleum engineering, with an average starting salary of $86,220.

Conclusion of How Much Do New Grad Engineers Make:

While the average starting salary for new grad engineers is $72,900, there are many factors that can impact your earning potential. Your field of engineering, location, level of education, and experience all play a role in determining your salary. However, with research and negotiation, it is possible to secure a higher starting salary than the average.
