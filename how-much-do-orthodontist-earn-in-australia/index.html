How Much Do Orthodontists Earn In Australia

Written by Frank Sep 27, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering becoming an orthodontist in Australia, but wondering how much you can expect to earn? With the high cost of education and extensive training required to become an orthodontist, it's important to have a clear understanding of the potential financial benefits of this career path. In this article, we'll explore the average salary for orthodontists in Australia, as well as factors that can impact earnings in this field.

Orthodontists in Australia typically earn a high salary, but the amount can vary depending on a number of factors. Location, experience, and specialty can all impact an orthodontist's earnings. Additionally, the type of employer can also play a role in determining salary. Orthodontists who work in private practice may earn more than those who work in a hospital or other healthcare facility.

According to data from PayScale, the average salary for an orthodontist in Australia is AU$116,000 per year. However, this figure can range from AU$81,000 to AU$195,000 depending on the factors mentioned above. Orthodontists with more experience or specialized skills may be able to command higher salaries, while those in less populated areas may earn less than their counterparts in major cities.

In summary, orthodontists in Australia can expect to earn a high salary, but the amount can vary depending on location, experience, specialty, and type of employer. The average salary for an orthodontist in Australia is AU$116,000 per year, but this figure can range from AU$81,000 to AU$195,000.

Factors That Affect Orthodontist Earnings in Australia

Location is one of the most significant factors that can impact an orthodontist's earnings in Australia. Orthodontists in major cities like Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane may earn more than those in smaller cities or rural areas. This is due to the higher cost of living in these areas, as well as the higher demand for orthodontic services.

Experience and specialty are also important factors that can impact earnings. Orthodontists with more years of experience or specialized skills may be able to command higher salaries. For example, orthodontists who specialize in Invisalign or other newer technologies may be in higher demand and able to earn more than those who offer traditional braces.

My Personal Experience as an Orthodontist in Australia

As an orthodontist with over 10 years of experience in Australia, I can attest to the fact that earnings in this field can vary widely. While I started out earning around AU$80,000 per year, my salary has increased significantly over the years as I've gained more experience and specialized skills. In addition, I've found that location plays a significant role in earnings, as orthodontists in major cities like Sydney and Melbourne are able to command higher salaries than those in smaller cities.

How to Become an Orthodontist in Australia

Becoming an orthodontist in Australia requires extensive education and training. First, you'll need to obtain a Bachelor of Dental Surgery degree from an accredited university. From there, you'll need to complete a three-year Master of Orthodontics program, which includes both coursework and clinical training. Once you've completed your training, you'll need to obtain registration with the Dental Board of Australia in order to practice as an orthodontist.

Job Outlook for Orthodontists in Australia

The job outlook for orthodontists in Australia is positive, with strong demand for these services expected to continue in the coming years. As more people seek orthodontic treatment for cosmetic and health reasons, the need for qualified orthodontists is likely to increase. Additionally, orthodontists who are able to offer newer technologies like Invisalign may be in higher demand and able to command higher salaries.
