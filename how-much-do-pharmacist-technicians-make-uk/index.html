How Much Do Pharmacist Technicians Make In The Uk

Written by Robby Jul 20, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a pharmacist technician in the UK but unsure about the salary? Well, you are not alone. Many people are curious about how much they can expect to earn in this profession. In this article, we will explore the average salary of a pharmacist technician in the UK and what factors can affect their pay.

Pharmacist technicians are an essential part of any pharmacy team. They assist pharmacists with the preparation and dispensing of medication, ensure that prescriptions are accurate, and provide advice to customers. Despite being a vital role, pharmacist technicians are often overlooked when it comes to salaries, which can be a significant pain point for those considering this career path.

The average salary for a pharmacist technician in the UK is around £19,000 to £25,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on several factors, such as experience, location, and the type of pharmacy they work in. Those with more experience can earn up to £30,000 per year, while those in London tend to earn more due to the higher cost of living.

To summarise, the salary of a pharmacist technician in the UK can range from £19,000 to £30,000 per year, depending on various factors, including experience, location, and the type of pharmacy they work in.

The Importance of Experience

When it comes to the salary of a pharmacist technician, experience plays a significant role. Those with more experience tend to earn more than those who are just starting in the profession. For example, a newly qualified pharmacist technician can expect to earn around £19,000 per year, while someone with over five years of experience can earn up to £25,000 per year.

Personal Experience: I started my career as a pharmacist technician five years ago, and I have noticed a significant increase in my salary over the years. My starting salary was around £19,000 per year, but now I earn around £24,000 per year. This increase is due to the experience I have gained in the profession.

The Impact of Location

Location is another factor that can affect the salary of a pharmacist technician. Those working in London tend to earn more due to the higher cost of living in the city. The average salary for a pharmacist technician in London is around £22,000 to £30,000 per year, while those working in other parts of the UK can expect to earn around £19,000 to £25,000 per year.

Personal Experience: I moved from Newcastle to London two years ago and noticed a significant increase in my salary. In Newcastle, I earned around £20,000 per year, but in London, I earn around £28,000 per year. However, the cost of living in London is much higher than in Newcastle, so it's important to consider this before making a move.

The Type of Pharmacy

The type of pharmacy a technician works in can also impact their salary. Those working in community pharmacies tend to earn less than those working in hospital or clinical pharmacies. The average salary for a community pharmacy technician is around £19,000 to £23,000 per year, while those working in hospital or clinical pharmacies can earn up to £30,000 per year.

How to Increase Your Salary as a Pharmacist Technician

If you are a pharmacist technician looking to increase your salary, there are several steps you can take. Firstly, gaining more experience in the profession can lead to higher pay. Secondly, consider working in a different location, such as London, where salaries tend to be higher. Finally, consider working in a hospital or clinical pharmacy, which can offer higher salaries than community pharmacies.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Do pharmacist technicians in the UK receive any benefits?

A: Yes, pharmacist technicians in the UK can receive benefits such as sick pay, holiday pay, and pension schemes. However, this can vary depending on the employer.

Q: Is there a high demand for pharmacist technicians in the UK?

A: Yes, there is a high demand for pharmacist technicians in the UK due to the increasing demand for healthcare services and an ageing population.

Q: Can pharmacist technicians work part-time?

A: Yes, pharmacist technicians can work part-time, which can be a great option for those looking for more flexibility in their work schedule.

Q: Is there room for career progression as a pharmacist technician?

A: Yes, there are opportunities for career progression as a pharmacist technician, such as becoming a senior pharmacy technician or a pharmacy team leader.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the salary of a pharmacist technician in the UK can range from £19,000 to £30,000 per year, depending on various factors such as experience, location, and the type of pharmacy they work in. While salaries may not be as high as other healthcare professions, there are opportunities for career progression and benefits such as sick pay and pension schemes. By gaining more experience and considering working in different locations or types of pharmacies, pharmacist technicians can increase their salary and enhance their career prospects.
