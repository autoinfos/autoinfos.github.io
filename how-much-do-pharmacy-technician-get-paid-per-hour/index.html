How Much Do Pharmacy Technicians Get Paid Per Hour

Written by Christine Jul 19, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a pharmacy technician? One of the most commonly asked questions about this profession is how much do pharmacy technicians get paid per hour? This is an important question to consider when deciding if this career path is the right choice for you.

The average hourly pay for a pharmacy technician in the United States is around $16.50. However, pay can vary depending on a variety of factors such as experience, education, and location. It's important to do your research and understand the different factors that can impact your potential earnings as a pharmacy technician.

In summary, pharmacy technicians can expect to make an average hourly pay of $16.50. However, this can vary based on several factors. Let's take a closer look at how these factors can impact your potential earnings as a pharmacy technician.

Education and Experience

One of the most significant factors that impact how much a pharmacy technician gets paid per hour is education and experience. Typically, the more education and experience you have, the higher your potential earnings will be. For example, a pharmacy technician with a certification or degree will typically make more than someone without these credentials.

Personally, I decided to pursue a certification as a pharmacy technician, which allowed me to earn a higher hourly rate than someone without certification. Additionally, I gained valuable experience through internships and on-the-job training, which helped me secure a higher-paying position.

Location

Another factor that can impact how much a pharmacy technician gets paid per hour is location. The cost of living and demand for pharmacy technicians can vary depending on where you live. For example, pharmacy technicians in urban areas may earn more than those in rural areas due to a higher demand for their services.

Personally, I have found that living in a larger city has allowed me to earn a higher hourly rate as a pharmacy technician. However, it's important to consider the cost of living in these areas, as it can be more expensive to live in urban areas compared to rural areas.

Job Setting

The type of job setting can also impact how much a pharmacy technician gets paid per hour. For example, pharmacy technicians who work in hospitals or other healthcare settings may earn more than those who work in retail pharmacies.

Personally, I have worked in both hospital and retail settings as a pharmacy technician. While I enjoyed working in a hospital setting more, I found that I earned more money working in a retail pharmacy.

Job Responsibilities

The type of job responsibilities can also impact how much a pharmacy technician gets paid per hour. For example, pharmacy technicians who have additional responsibilities such as managing inventory or supervising other technicians may earn more than those who simply fill prescriptions.

Personally, I have found that taking on additional responsibilities has allowed me to earn a higher hourly rate as a pharmacy technician. However, it's important to consider the added stress and workload that comes with these responsibilities.

Question and Answer

Q: Can you become a pharmacy technician without any experience?

A: Yes, it is possible to become a pharmacy technician without any experience. However, having experience or education in the field can help you earn a higher hourly rate.

Q: How long does it take to become a certified pharmacy technician?

A: The length of time it takes to become a certified pharmacy technician can vary. Some certification programs can be completed in as little as six months, while others may take up to two years.

Q: Do pharmacy technicians get benefits?

A: Yes, many pharmacy technicians receive benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off.

Q: Is there room for advancement as a pharmacy technician?

A: Yes, there is room for advancement as a pharmacy technician. Some technicians may choose to pursue additional education or certifications to become a pharmacy supervisor, pharmacy manager, or even a pharmacist.

Conclusion of How Much Do Pharmacy Technicians Get Paid Per Hour

Overall, pharmacy technicians can expect to make an average hourly pay of $16.50. However, pay can vary depending on factors such as education, experience, location, job setting, and job responsibilities. It's important to do your research and consider these factors when deciding if a career as a pharmacy technician is right for you.
