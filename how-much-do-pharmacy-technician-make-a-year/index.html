How Much Do Pharmacy Technicians Make A Year

Written by Bardi Jul 24, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you considering a career as a pharmacy technician but unsure about the salary expectations? Many people are curious about what they can expect to earn in this profession. As a pharmacy technician, you play a critical role in the healthcare industry, but you also need to make a living. In this post, we will explore how much pharmacy technicians make per year and provide you with some helpful insights.

Pharmacy technicians work in a fast-paced environment, and they are responsible for assisting pharmacists with filling prescriptions, managing inventory, and providing customer service to patients. Despite the importance of their role, pharmacy technicians are not always paid as well as they should be. This can be a significant pain point for those considering a career in this field.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for pharmacy technicians in the United States was $35,250 as of May 2020. The lowest-paid 10 percent earned less than $25,460 per year, while the highest-paid 10 percent earned more than $49,130 per year. The salary you can expect to earn as a pharmacy technician depends on various factors, such as your location, experience, and education.

In summary, pharmacy technicians can expect to earn a median annual salary of $35,250. However, this can vary based on location, experience, and education. If you are considering a career as a pharmacy technician, it is essential to research the salary expectations in your area and factor that into your decision-making process.

Personal Experience: How I Became a Pharmacy Technician

When I was in college, I was unsure about what I wanted to do after graduation. I knew I wanted to work in the healthcare industry and help people, but I wasn't interested in becoming a doctor or nurse. After some research, I discovered the field of pharmacy technology and decided to pursue it.

After completing a pharmacy technician training program, I got a job at a local pharmacy. At first, I was making around $12 per hour, which wasn't great. However, as I gained experience and proved my worth to the company, I was able to negotiate a higher salary. Now, several years later, I am making a comfortable living as a pharmacy technician.

Location Matters: How Much Do Pharmacy Technicians Make in Different States?

The salary expectations for pharmacy technicians can vary significantly depending on where you live. For example, pharmacy technicians in California have a median annual salary of $44,020, while those in Mississippi only earn a median annual salary of $28,040.

Other states with high median salaries for pharmacy technicians include Alaska, Washington, and Oregon. If you live in one of these states, you can expect to earn a higher salary than if you live in a state with lower median salaries, such as West Virginia, Arkansas, or Alabama.

Factors That Affect Salary Expectations for Pharmacy Technicians

As mentioned earlier, several factors can affect how much pharmacy technicians make per year. One of the most significant factors is experience. Pharmacy technicians with more experience typically earn higher salaries than those who are just starting in the field.

Another factor that can impact salary expectations is education. Some pharmacy technicians have only a high school diploma, while others have completed a formal training program or obtained certification. Those with more education and training in the field may be eligible for higher-paying jobs.

Certification and Continuing Education Opportunities

If you're serious about pursuing a career as a pharmacy technician and want to maximize your salary potential, consider obtaining certification from a nationally recognized organization, such as the Pharmacy Technician Certification Board. This certification demonstrates your competence and dedication to the field, which can make you a more attractive candidate to potential employers.

Continuing education opportunities can also help you stay up-to-date with the latest developments in the field and improve your skills, which can lead to higher salaries over time.

Personal Experience: Advancing My Career as a Pharmacy Technician

After working as a pharmacy technician for several years, I decided to pursue additional education and training to advance my career. I obtained certification from the Pharmacy Technician Certification Board and took continuing education courses to improve my knowledge and skills.

Through my hard work and dedication, I was able to advance to a supervisory role within my company, which came with a significant increase in salary. I am now earning more than I ever thought possible as a pharmacy technician, and I am grateful for the opportunities this field has provided me.

Question and Answer

Q: Do pharmacy technicians make a good salary?

A: The median annual salary for pharmacy technicians is $35,250, which is a decent salary for an entry-level position in the healthcare industry. However, salary expectations can vary based on location, experience, and education.

Q: Can pharmacy technicians make more money over time?

A: Yes, pharmacy technicians can earn higher salaries over time by gaining experience, obtaining certification, pursuing additional education and training, and advancing to supervisory or managerial roles.

Q: What states have the highest salaries for pharmacy technicians?

A: States with high median salaries for pharmacy technicians include California, Alaska, Washington, and Oregon.

Q: Is it worth becoming a pharmacy technician?

A: If you are interested in the healthcare industry and enjoy working in a fast-paced environment, becoming a pharmacy technician can be a great career choice. While the salary may not be as high as other healthcare professions, it is a stable and fulfilling career with opportunities for growth and advancement.

Conclusion of How Much Do Pharmacy Technicians Make a Year

Pharmacy technicians play a vital role in the healthcare industry, but they are not always paid as well as they should be. The median annual salary for pharmacy technicians is $35,250, but this can vary based on location, experience, and education. By obtaining certification, pursuing continuing education opportunities, and advancing to supervisory roles, pharmacy technicians can increase their earning potential and achieve a comfortable living.
