How Much Do Pharmacy Technicians Get Paid In Canada

Written by Robby Aug 08, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a pharmacy technician in Canada? One of the most important factors to consider is how much you can expect to earn. Let's take a closer look at the salary range for pharmacy technicians in Canada.

The salary of a pharmacy technician can vary depending on several factors, including the region they work in, their level of experience, and the type of pharmacy they work in. However, one thing is certain - pharmacy technicians play a critical role in the healthcare industry and their skills are in high demand.

According to data from the Government of Canada Job Bank, the median wage for pharmacy technicians in Canada is $24.00 per hour. This means that half of pharmacy technicians earn more than this amount and half earn less. The highest 10 percent of earners make over $31.00 per hour, while the lowest 10 percent make less than $17.00 per hour.

In addition to a competitive salary, many pharmacy technicians in Canada also receive benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off. Some employers also offer opportunities for advancement and additional training.

My Personal Experience

As a pharmacy technician in Canada for the past five years, I can attest to the importance of this role in the healthcare industry. I have had the opportunity to work in a variety of settings, including hospital pharmacies and retail pharmacies, and have found the work to be both challenging and rewarding.

While the starting salary for pharmacy technicians may not be as high as some other healthcare professions, the job security and opportunities for growth make it a great career choice. Plus, knowing that I am making a difference in the lives of patients by ensuring they receive the correct medications and dosages is incredibly fulfilling.

Factors That Influence Salary

As previously mentioned, there are several factors that can influence the salary of a pharmacy technician in Canada. The region in which they work is a significant factor, as wages can vary depending on the cost of living in a particular area. In general, wages tend to be higher in larger cities such as Toronto and Vancouver.

Experience is another factor that can impact salary. Those with more years of experience in the field may earn higher wages or be offered more opportunities for advancement. Additionally, the type of pharmacy can also play a role in determining salary. For example, those who work in hospital pharmacies may earn more than those who work in retail pharmacies.

Education and Training

In order to become a pharmacy technician in Canada, individuals must complete a recognized pharmacy technician program and become licensed by their provincial regulatory body. These programs typically take 1-2 years to complete and include both classroom and practical training.

Continuing education and training is also important for pharmacy technicians in order to keep up with the latest developments in the field and stay competitive in the job market. Many employers offer opportunities for on-the-job training or may provide financial assistance for employees to attend relevant courses or workshops.

Tips for Maximizing Earnings

If you are a pharmacy technician looking to maximize your earnings, there are a few things you can do. First, consider pursuing additional training or certifications in specialized areas such as compounding or sterile products. This can make you more valuable to employers and increase your earning potential.

Networking and building relationships with other healthcare professionals can also be beneficial. This can lead to new job opportunities or referrals for additional training or education.

Conclusion

Overall, pharmacy technicians in Canada can expect to earn a competitive salary with opportunities for growth and job security. By pursuing additional education and training, building relationships with other healthcare professionals, and seeking out opportunities for advancement, pharmacy technicians can maximize their earning potential and make a difference in the lives of patients.
