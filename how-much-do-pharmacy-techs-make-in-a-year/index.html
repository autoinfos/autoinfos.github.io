How Much Do Pharmacy Techs Make In A Year

Written by Robby Jun 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a pharmacy technician but wondering about the financial rewards? The salary of a pharmacy technician is a common question asked by many aspiring technicians. Knowing how much you can earn as a pharmacy tech can help you decide whether it is worth pursuing this career or not. In this article, we will explore the salary of a pharmacy technician and provide you with all the information you need to know.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), the median annual salary for a pharmacy technician in the United States is $34,020. However, the salary range for pharmacy technicians varies depending on several factors, such as location, level of experience, and type of employer. For instance, pharmacy technicians working in metropolitan areas and hospitals tend to earn more than those working in rural areas or retail stores.

Overall, pharmacy technician is a well-paying job that offers decent compensation and benefits. With the demand for pharmacy technicians projected to grow by 4% between 2019 and 2029, the job outlook for pharmacy technicians is promising.

What Affects the Salary of a Pharmacy Technician?

Several factors influence the salary of a pharmacy technician. Here are some of the most significant:

Location

The salary of a pharmacy technician varies depending on the state and city where they work. For example, the average annual salary of a pharmacy technician in California is $43,150, while the average salary of a pharmacy technician in Alabama is $29,640.

Experience

The more experience you have as a pharmacy technician, the higher your salary is likely to be. Experienced pharmacy technicians can earn up to $50,000 per year.

My Personal Experience

I have been working as a pharmacy technician for three years now, and I can attest that the salary is decent. I started with an annual salary of $28,000, but as I gained more experience and proved my worth, my salary increased to $38,000. The salary increase was a motivation for me to keep improving my skills and knowledge in the field.

Tips to Increase Your Salary as a Pharmacy Technician

If you are a pharmacy technician and want to increase your salary, here are some tips:

Get Certified

Pharmacy technicians who have a certification tend to earn more than those who do not. Getting certified demonstrates your competence and expertise in the field.

Gain More Experience

As mentioned earlier, experience is a significant factor that affects the salary of a pharmacy technician. The more you work in the field, the more you will learn and develop your skills, which can lead to a higher salary.

Question and Answer

Q: Is the salary of a pharmacy technician enough to live on?

A: Yes, the salary of a pharmacy technician is decent and can provide a comfortable living. However, it depends on your lifestyle and location.


Q: Can pharmacy technicians earn overtime pay?

A: Yes, pharmacy technicians can earn overtime pay if they work more than 40 hours per week.


Q: Do pharmacy technicians get health benefits?

A: Yes, many employers offer health benefits, such as medical, dental, and vision insurance, to their pharmacy technicians.


Q: What is the job outlook for pharmacy technicians?

A: The job outlook for pharmacy technicians is promising, with a projected growth rate of 4% between 2019 and 2029.

Conclusion of How Much Do Pharmacy Techs Make in a Year

In conclusion, the salary of a pharmacy technician is decent and provides a comfortable living. The median annual salary of a pharmacy technician in the United States is $34,020, but it varies depending on several factors. To increase your salary as a pharmacy technician, you can get certified, gain more experience, and consider working in metropolitan areas or hospitals. With the projected growth rate of 4% for pharmacy technicians, the job outlook for this profession is promising.
