How Much Do Pharmacy Techs Make In Tampa Florida

Written by Petter Nov 08, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a pharmacy technician in Tampa, Florida? If so, you may be wondering about the salary outlook for this profession. While it's important to consider factors beyond salary when choosing a career, it's understandable that you want to know what kind of income you can expect.

Pharmacy technicians are an essential part of the healthcare system. They work alongside pharmacists to prepare and dispense medications, answer patient questions, and perform administrative duties. However, like many healthcare professions, pharmacy techs are often underpaid for the important work they do.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for pharmacy technicians in the United States is $33,950. However, this figure can vary widely depending on factors such as location, experience, and level of education.

In Tampa, Florida, the average annual salary for pharmacy technicians is $34,290, slightly above the national median. However, entry-level pharmacy techs in Tampa may start out earning less than this amount, while those with more experience and education may earn more.

Personal Experience

As a pharmacy technician in Tampa, I can attest to the fact that the salary for this profession can vary widely depending on a variety of factors. When I first started out as a pharmacy tech, I earned around $26,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and education, I was able to earn more.

One thing to keep in mind is that pharmacy technicians may be able to earn more by specializing in certain areas, such as sterile compounding or oncology. These specialties often require additional education and training, but can lead to higher salaries in the long run.

Educational Requirements

In order to become a pharmacy technician in Tampa, you'll need to complete a training program and pass a certification exam. Many community colleges and vocational schools offer pharmacy technician programs, which can take anywhere from several months to two years to complete.

While certification is not required by law in Florida, many employers prefer to hire pharmacy techs who have earned certification through organizations such as the Pharmacy Technician Certification Board or the National Healthcareer Association. Certification can also lead to higher salaries and more job opportunities.

Job Outlook

The job outlook for pharmacy technicians in Tampa and beyond is generally positive. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of pharmacy technicians is projected to grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029, about as fast as the average for all occupations.

This growth is due in part to an aging population and an increased demand for prescription medications. As more people seek medical care and require medications, the need for pharmacy technicians to assist pharmacists will continue to grow.

Career Advancement

If you're interested in advancing your career as a pharmacy technician, there are several options available. One is to pursue additional education and training in a specialized area, as mentioned earlier.

You could also consider becoming a pharmacy technician supervisor, overseeing other pharmacy techs in a hospital or retail setting. With additional experience and education, you may also be able to become a pharmacist.

Conclusion of How Much Do Pharmacy Techs Make in Tampa Florida

While salary is an important consideration when choosing a career, it's also important to consider other factors such as job satisfaction, work-life balance, and opportunities for advancement. However, if you're considering a career as a pharmacy technician in Tampa, you can expect to earn an average annual salary of around $34,290, with the potential for more as you gain experience and education.
