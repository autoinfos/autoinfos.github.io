How Much Do Service Technicians Make An Hour

Written by Daniel Sep 29, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you considering a career as a service technician? Or perhaps you're already working in the field and wondering if your hourly rate is competitive. Whatever your situation, it's important to understand how much service technicians typically make per hour. In this post, we'll explore the average hourly rate for service technicians and factors that can impact their earnings.

Service technicians often face a variety of challenges in their work, from tight deadlines to complex repair tasks. These challenges can be compounded by concerns about pay, as service technicians may feel undervalued for their expertise and hard work. Additionally, the hourly rate for service technicians can vary significantly depending on factors such as location, industry, and experience level. All of these factors can make it difficult to know what to expect in terms of pay as a service technician.

So, how much do service technicians make an hour? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for service technicians was $21.52 as of May 2020. This means that half of all service technicians earned more than this amount and half earned less. However, it's important to note that this figure can vary widely depending on the specific area of service and industry in which a technician works. For example, service technicians working in the telecommunications industry may earn more than those working in the automotive industry.

In summary, service technicians can expect to make an average of $21.52 per hour, though this amount can vary based on a number of factors. Some of the key factors that can impact a service technician's hourly rate include their location, industry, and level of experience. By understanding these factors and doing research on typical pay rates in their specific field, service technicians can better negotiate for fair compensation for their valuable skills and expertise.

How Much Do Entry-Level Service Technicians Make an Hour?

As an entry-level service technician, you may be wondering what kind of hourly rate you can expect. While pay rates can vary depending on many factors, entry-level service technicians can typically expect to make around $15-$17 per hour. This rate may increase as technicians gain more experience and develop specialized skills.

How Much Do Experienced Service Technicians Make an Hour?

Experienced service technicians can expect to make significantly more than entry-level technicians. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the top 10% of service technicians earned more than $34.34 per hour as of May 2020. However, it's important to note that this figure can vary widely depending on the specific industry and location in which a technician works.

Factors That Can Impact a Service Technician's Hourly Rate

As mentioned earlier, a service technician's hourly rate can be impacted by a number of factors. Some of the most significant factors include:

  • Location - Service technicians in areas with a high cost of living may earn more than those in areas with a lower cost of living.
  • Industry - Service technicians in certain industries, such as telecommunications or healthcare, may earn more than those in other industries.
  • Experience - Service technicians with more experience and specialized skills may command higher pay rates.
  • Education and Training - Service technicians with advanced degrees or specialized training may earn more than those with only a high school diploma or equivalent.

Tips for Negotiating a Higher Hourly Rate as a Service Technician

If you're a service technician looking to negotiate a higher hourly rate, there are a few steps you can take. These include:

  • Do your research - Research typical pay rates for service technicians in your specific industry and location.
  • Highlight your skills and experience - Be sure to emphasize any specialized skills or experience you have that make you a valuable asset to your employer.
  • Be confident - Approach salary negotiations with confidence and a clear understanding of your worth as a service technician.
  • Consider additional benefits - In addition to salary, consider other benefits that may be negotiable, such as vacation time or health insurance.

Question and Answer

Q: How can I increase my hourly rate as a service technician?

A: To increase your hourly rate as a service technician, consider developing specialized skills or pursuing additional education or certifications. You can also negotiate with your employer for a higher pay rate based on your skills and experience.

Q: What industries pay service technicians the most?

A: Service technicians in industries such as healthcare, telecommunications, and information technology tend to earn higher pay rates than those in other industries.

Q: Is it possible to negotiate my hourly rate as an entry-level service technician?

A: While entry-level service technicians may have less room for negotiation than experienced technicians, it's still possible to negotiate for a higher hourly rate based on your skills and experience.

Q: How often do service technicians typically receive raises?

A: The frequency of raises for service technicians can vary depending on the employer and industry. Some service technicians may receive annual raises, while others may have to negotiate for a higher pay rate on their own.

Conclusion of How Much Do Service Technicians Make an Hour

Overall, service technicians can expect to make an average of $21.52 per hour, though pay rates can vary widely depending on a number of factors. By understanding the factors that impact their hourly rate and negotiating with their employer based on their skills and experience, service technicians can work towards fair compensation for their valuable work.
