How Much Do Starting Lineman Make

Written by Robby Jun 13, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you interested in the world of football but not so much in being a player? How about becoming a lineman? These players are crucial in protecting the quarterback, and their salaries are nothing to scoff at. In this article, we'll explore just how much starting linemen make.

It's no secret that football is a lucrative industry, but not all positions are created equal. Linemen have one of the most physically demanding positions on the field, and their salaries should reflect that. Many starting linemen make a solid six-figure salary, but there are a few factors that come into play when determining their earnings.

The average salary for a starting lineman in the NFL is around $750,000 per year. However, this number can vary based on a few factors. Experience, skill level, and team location can all impact a lineman's salary. Some starting linemen can earn up to $1 million or more per year, while others may make less than the average.

In summary, starting linemen can make a solid six-figure salary, with an average of $750,000 per year. This number can vary based on experience, skill level, and team location.

How do starting linemen negotiate their salaries?

When it comes to negotiating salaries, starting linemen have a few options. One common way is to hire an agent. Agents can help players negotiate better contracts and ensure they are being paid fairly based on their skills and experience. In addition, agents can help players navigate the complicated world of contract negotiations and ensure they are receiving the best possible deal.

Another way starting linemen can negotiate their salaries is by building a strong reputation in the league. The more respected and well-known a player is, the more leverage they have when it comes to salary negotiations. This can come from performing well on the field, being involved in community outreach, and having a strong social media presence.

How does a lineman's position affect their salary?

The position a lineman plays can also have an impact on their salary. For example, left tackles are typically the highest-paid linemen because they are responsible for protecting the quarterback's blind side. This is a crucial role on the field, and teams are willing to pay top dollar for the right player.

On the other hand, right guards and right tackles typically make less than left tackles. This is because they are responsible for protecting the quarterback's non-throwing side, which is considered less important than the blind side. However, this doesn't mean that right guards and right tackles are not important – they still play a crucial role in protecting the quarterback and keeping the offense moving forward.

What skills do starting linemen need to have?

Starting linemen need to have a variety of skills to be successful on the field. One of the most important skills is strength. Linemen need to be able to push and pull opposing players, as well as hold their ground against strong defensive players. In addition, linemen need to have good hand-eye coordination and footwork to be able to quickly react to the movements of other players on the field.

How can someone become a starting lineman?

Becoming a starting lineman requires a lot of hard work and dedication. Most linemen start playing football in high school and continue playing in college. From there, they may be scouted by NFL teams and offered a contract. However, even if someone is not scouted by NFL teams, they can still pursue a career in football by playing in lower-level leagues and working their way up the ladder.

Conclusion of how much do starting lineman make

In conclusion, starting linemen can make a solid six-figure salary, with an average of $750,000 per year. This number can vary based on experience, skill level, and team location. Becoming a starting lineman requires a lot of hard work and dedication, but for those who are passionate about football, it can be a rewarding career.
