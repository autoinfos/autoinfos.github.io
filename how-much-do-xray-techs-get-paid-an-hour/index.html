How Much Do Xray Techs Get Paid An Hour

Written by William Sep 28, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an xray tech but wondering about the potential earnings? It's a common concern. After all, you want to ensure that you can make a comfortable living doing something you enjoy. Understanding the salary of an xray tech can help you determine if it's the right path for you.

The average xray tech salary varies depending on several factors, including experience, location, and industry. However, one of the biggest pain points for xray techs is the potential for low pay. Many xray techs feel that they're not compensated fairly for the work they do.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for an xray tech is $62,280, which equates to roughly $29.95 per hour. However, the lowest 10% of earners make less than $41,480 per year, while the top 10% earn over $90,760 per year.

In summary, xray techs can earn a decent living, but potential earnings can vary widely depending on experience, location, and industry. It's essential to research and understand the salary expectations in your area before committing to a career as an xray tech.

What Factors Affect an Xray Tech's Salary?

When it comes to determining an xray tech's salary, several factors come into play. One of the most significant factors is experience. Xray techs with more experience tend to earn higher salaries than those just starting in the field.

Location is another crucial factor that affects an xray tech's salary. Salaries can vary widely depending on where you live and work. For example, xray techs in California tend to earn more than those in other states, while xray techs in rural areas may earn less than those in urban areas.

The industry you work in can also affect your salary as an xray tech. Xray techs working in hospitals tend to earn more than those working in physician's offices or diagnostic laboratories.

The Importance of Negotiating Your Salary

When starting a new job as an xray tech, it's important to negotiate your salary. Many xray techs fail to negotiate and end up accepting lower pay than they deserve. Negotiating your salary can help ensure that you're compensated fairly for your skills and experience.

How to Negotiate Your Salary as an Xray Tech

Before negotiating your salary, it's essential to research the average xray tech salary in your area. You can use websites like Glassdoor or Salary.com to get an idea of what you should be earning. Once you have an idea of the average salary, you can use this information to negotiate a fair wage.

When negotiating your salary, be sure to emphasize your skills and experience. You can also mention any certifications or licenses you hold that make you more valuable to the employer. Finally, be confident and assertive in your negotiations. Remember that you deserve to be compensated fairly for your work.

Questions and Answers

Q: Can xray techs earn overtime pay?

A: Yes, xray techs are eligible for overtime pay if they work more than 40 hours per week.

Q: What are the job prospects for xray techs?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of radiologic and MRI technologists is projected to grow 7% from 2019 to 2029, faster than the average for all occupations.

Q: Do xray techs receive benefits?

A: Yes, xray techs typically receive benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off.

Q: Can xray techs work part-time?

A: Yes, many xray techs work part-time or on a per-diem basis.

Conclusion of How Much Do Xray Techs Get Paid an Hour

While the salary of an xray tech may not be as high as some other healthcare professions, it can still provide a comfortable living. Understanding the factors that affect xray tech salaries can help you negotiate a fair wage and ensure that you're compensated fairly for your skills and experience. If you're considering a career as an xray tech, it's essential to research the salary expectations in your area to determine if it's the right path for you.
