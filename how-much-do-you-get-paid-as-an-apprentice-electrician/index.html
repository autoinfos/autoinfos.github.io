How Much Do You Get Paid As An Apprentice Electrician

Written by Luffy Jul 22, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an apprentice electrician but wondering about the pay? It’s important to know what to expect, especially if you’re taking on an apprenticeship as a means to support yourself or your family. In this blog post, we’ll explore how much you can expect to get paid as an apprentice electrician.

One of the biggest pain points for those considering an apprenticeship is the fact that pay is often lower than in other professions. As an apprentice electrician, you’ll be working under a licensed electrician and learning on the job. This means that you won’t be earning as much as a fully licensed electrician, and the pay may not be enough to cover all your expenses.

So, how much do you get paid as an apprentice electrician? The answer varies depending on your location, the company you work for, and your experience level. According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, the average hourly wage for an electrician apprentice is $16.97. However, this can range from $11 to $25 per hour depending on the factors mentioned above.

In summary, apprentice electricians can expect to earn an average of $16.97 per hour, although this can vary depending on location, company, and experience level. It’s important to keep in mind that as an apprentice, you’ll be learning valuable skills that can lead to higher-paying jobs in the future.

What to Expect as an Apprentice Electrician

As an apprentice electrician, you’ll be working with a licensed electrician and learning on the job. This means that you’ll be starting with the basics, such as running wires and installing outlets, and gradually working your way up to more complex tasks like diagnosing electrical problems and designing electrical systems.

During your apprenticeship, you can expect to work long hours and may be required to work weekends or evenings. You’ll also need to attend classes to learn the theory behind electrical work and prepare for your licensing exams.

My Experience as an Apprentice Electrician

When I started my apprenticeship as an electrician, I was excited to learn new skills and start a career in a field I was passionate about. However, I was also concerned about the pay and whether it would be enough to cover my bills.

During my first year as an apprentice, I earned around $12 per hour. While this wasn’t enough to cover all my expenses, I was able to make it work by cutting back on unnecessary expenses and living frugally. As I gained more experience and moved up to higher-level tasks, my pay increased to around $20 per hour.

Advancing Your Career as an Electrician

If you’re considering a career as an electrician, there are several paths you can take to advance your career and increase your earning potential. One option is to become a licensed electrician, which requires several years of experience and passing a licensing exam.

You can also specialize in a specific area of electrical work, such as industrial or commercial electrical systems, which can lead to higher-paying jobs. Another option is to start your own electrical contracting business, which can be a lucrative career path if you have the skills and experience to manage a business.

Tips for Maximizing Your Pay as an Apprentice Electrician

If you’re worried about making ends meet as an apprentice electrician, there are a few things you can do to maximize your pay:

  1. Look for apprenticeships with higher pay rates.
  2. Take on extra work or overtime when it’s available.
  3. Attend classes and study hard to prepare for your licensing exams.
  4. Consider specializing in a high-demand area of electrical work.

FAQs: How Much Do You Get Paid as an Apprentice Electrician?

Q: Is it worth becoming an apprentice electrician?

A: Yes, becoming an apprentice electrician can be a great career path for those who enjoy working with their hands and solving problems. While the pay may be lower than in other professions, the skills you learn can lead to higher-paying jobs in the future.

Q: How long does an apprenticeship last?

A: The length of an apprenticeship can vary depending on the program and location. In general, apprenticeships last around four years and include both on-the-job training and classroom instruction.

Q: What qualifications do I need to become an apprentice electrician?

A: To become an apprentice electrician, you’ll typically need a high school diploma or equivalent. Some programs may also require you to pass an aptitude test and have a valid driver’s license.

Q: How much can I expect to earn as a licensed electrician?

A: The average hourly wage for a licensed electrician is $26.01, according to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics. However, this can vary depending on your location, experience, and area of specialization.

Conclusion of How Much Do You Get Paid as an Apprentice Electrician

While the pay for apprentice electricians may be lower than in other professions, it’s important to keep in mind the valuable skills you’ll be learning on the job. As you gain more experience and advance in your career, you can expect to earn higher wages and enjoy a fulfilling career in a growing field.
