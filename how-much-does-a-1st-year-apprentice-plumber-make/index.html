How Much Does A 1St Year Apprentice Plumber Make

Written by Bardi Jul 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career in plumbing? If so, you may be wondering about the earning potential in your first year as an apprentice plumber. Understanding how much you can expect to make is an important factor in determining whether this career path is right for you.

As a first year apprentice plumber, it's important to understand that your pay may be lower than experienced plumbers. This can make it challenging to cover expenses while you're still learning the trade. However, the good news is that as you gain experience and complete more training, your earning potential will increase.

The average hourly pay for a first year apprentice plumber ranges from $14 to $20 per hour. This can vary depending on the employer, location, and the specific tasks you are assigned. As you gain experience and complete more training, your pay will increase. By your fourth year as an apprentice, you can expect to earn between $25 and $35 per hour.

In summary, a first year apprentice plumber can expect to earn between $14 and $20 per hour. While this may not seem like a lot, it's important to remember that as you gain experience and complete more training, your earning potential will increase significantly.

Personal Experience: How Much Does a 1st Year Apprentice Plumber Make

When I started my career as a plumber, I was earning around $15 per hour as a first year apprentice. While the pay was lower than what I had hoped for, I was excited to learn the trade and gain experience. As I completed more training and gained more experience, my pay increased significantly. By my fourth year as an apprentice, I was earning close to $30 per hour.

Training and Education

One of the most important factors in determining your earning potential as a first year apprentice plumber is the level of training and education you receive. To become a plumber, you will need to complete an apprenticeship program, which typically lasts between four and five years. During this time, you will receive both on-the-job training and classroom instruction. The more training you receive, the higher your earning potential will be.

Certifications and Specializations

To increase your earning potential as a plumber, it's important to consider obtaining certifications and specializations. These can include certifications in specific areas such as backflow prevention or medical gas installation. By obtaining these certifications, you can set yourself apart from other plumbers and increase your earning potential.

Negotiating Your Salary

If you're not satisfied with your pay as a first year apprentice plumber, it's important to remember that you can negotiate your salary. Talk to your employer and explain why you believe you deserve a higher rate of pay. Be prepared to back up your request with specific examples of your skills and experience.

Conclusion: How Much Does a 1st Year Apprentice Plumber Make

While the pay for a first year apprentice plumber may be lower than experienced plumbers, it's important to remember that as you gain experience and complete more training, your earning potential will increase significantly. By your fourth year as an apprentice, you can expect to earn between $25 and $35 per hour. To increase your earning potential, consider obtaining certifications and specializations, and don't be afraid to negotiate your salary.
