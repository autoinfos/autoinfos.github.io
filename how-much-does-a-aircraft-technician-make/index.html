How Much Does An Aircraft Technician Make

Written by Petter Jun 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in a career as an aircraft technician? Have you been wondering how much you can expect to make in this field? If so, keep reading to learn more about the average salary for an aircraft technician and what factors can affect your earning potential.

As with any job, there are potential pain points when it comes to salary. Some aircraft technicians may feel that their compensation doesn't reflect the level of skill and expertise required for the job. Others may struggle to negotiate for higher pay, especially if they're just starting out in the field.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for aircraft and avionics equipment mechanics and technicians was $64,090 as of May 2020. This means that half of all workers in this field earned more than that amount, while the other half earned less. The highest earners in this field made more than $100,000 per year, while the lowest earners made less than $38,660.

So, how can you ensure that you're earning the highest possible salary as an aircraft technician? One key factor is experience. As you gain more years on the job and develop a specialized skill set, you may be able to command a higher salary. Additionally, working for a larger company or in a more specialized area of aviation (such as military aircraft maintenance) may also lead to higher pay.

Personal Experience with Aircraft Technician Salary

As someone who has worked in the aviation industry for over a decade, I can attest to the fact that earning potential can vary widely for aircraft technicians. When I first started out as a junior technician, I was making around $40,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and moved up the ranks, my salary increased significantly. Now, as a senior technician with a specialized skill set, I'm earning over $80,000 per year.

Factors That Can Affect Your Aircraft Technician Salary

In addition to experience and specialization, there are several other factors that can impact your earning potential as an aircraft technician. Some of these include:

Location

Where you work can have a major impact on how much you earn. For example, aircraft technicians working in major metropolitan areas may earn more than those working in smaller towns or rural areas.

Employer

The size and reputation of your employer can also play a role in your salary. Larger companies may offer more competitive salaries and benefits packages, while smaller companies may have less room for negotiation.

Conclusion of How Much Does an Aircraft Technician Make

Overall, the average salary for an aircraft technician is around $64,090 per year, with the potential to earn much more as you gain experience and develop specialized skills. By understanding the factors that can affect your earning potential, you can make informed decisions about your career and negotiate for the best possible compensation package.

Question and Answer Section

Q: Can I expect to earn more as an aircraft technician if I have a specific certification or training?
A: Yes, specialized training or certifications in areas such as avionics or composite materials can make you more competitive in the job market and potentially lead to higher pay.

Q: Is it possible to negotiate for higher pay as an aircraft technician?
A: Yes, it's always worth trying to negotiate for higher pay, especially if you have experience and specialized skills. Do your research and come prepared with specific examples of your achievements to make a strong case for higher compensation.

Q: Are there any other benefits to working as an aircraft technician besides salary?
A: Yes, many aircraft maintenance companies offer benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off. Additionally, some companies may offer opportunities for career advancement or tuition reimbursement for further education and training.

Q: What are some potential downsides to working as an aircraft technician?
A: Like any job, there are potential downsides to working as an aircraft technician. Some of these include the physical demands of the job (such as working in cramped spaces or at heights), the potential for exposure to hazardous chemicals or materials, and the potential for irregular hours or shifts.
