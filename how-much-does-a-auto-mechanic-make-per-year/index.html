How Much Does A Auto Mechanic Make Per Year

Written by Daniel Oct 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career in auto mechanics? If so, you may be wondering how much you can expect to make each year. It's important to know what kind of income you can expect before committing to a career path. In this blog post, we'll explore the average salary for auto mechanics and what factors can impact your earnings.

One of the biggest pain points for those considering a career in auto mechanics is the fear of not making enough money. It's true that some mechanics earn less than others, but there are many factors that can impact your salary. Your experience, location, and education level can all play a role in determining your earnings.

So, how much does a auto mechanic make per year? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 as of May 2020. The highest 10 percent earned more than $70,820, while the lowest 10 percent earned less than $25,720. These numbers may vary depending on your location and level of experience.

In summary, auto mechanics can expect to make a median annual wage of $44,050, with higher earning potential for those with more experience and education. It's important to keep in mind that these numbers can vary based on location and other factors.

Factors That Impact Auto Mechanic Salaries

Location is one of the biggest factors that can impact your salary as an auto mechanic. Mechanics in areas with a higher cost of living may earn more than those in areas with a lower cost of living. Experience is another key factor that can impact your earnings. As you gain more experience in the field, you'll likely be able to command a higher salary. Education level can also play a role in determining your earning potential. Mechanics with additional certifications or degrees may be able to earn more than those without.

My Experience With Auto Mechanic Salaries

As someone who has worked in the auto industry for several years, I can attest to the fact that salaries can vary widely. When I started out as a mechanic, I was earning around $30,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and became certified in additional areas, my salary increased. Now, as a master mechanic with over a decade of experience, I earn over $60,000 per year. It's important to keep in mind that your earnings will vary based on your location and other factors, but there is definitely potential to earn a good salary in this field.

Education and Certification

One of the best ways to increase your earning potential as an auto mechanic is to pursue additional education and certifications. Many mechanics start out with a high school diploma and on-the-job training, but there are also vocational schools and community college programs that offer automotive technology programs. You can also pursue certifications from organizations like the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE), which can help demonstrate your expertise to potential employers and clients.

Specializing in a Niche Area

Another way to potentially increase your earnings as an auto mechanic is to specialize in a niche area. For example, you could focus on hybrid or electric vehicles, which are becoming more and more popular. You could also specialize in a particular type of vehicle, such as motorcycles or classic cars. By carving out a niche for yourself, you may be able to command higher rates for your services.

My Personal Tips for Success

If you're considering a career in auto mechanics, here are a few tips to help you succeed:

  • Get as much education and training as possible.
  • Pursue certifications to demonstrate your expertise.
  • Consider specializing in a particular area.
  • Stay up-to-date on the latest technology and techniques.
  • Build strong relationships with customers and colleagues.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average hourly wage for auto mechanics?

A: The average hourly wage for auto mechanics is $21.18 per hour as of May 2020, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics.

Q: What is the job outlook for auto mechanics?

A: The job outlook for auto mechanics is expected to grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029, about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Q: How much can auto mechanics earn with additional certifications?

A: Auto mechanics with additional certifications can earn more than those without. ASE-certified mechanics, for example, can earn up to $12 more per hour than non-certified mechanics.

Q: What are the best states for auto mechanic salaries?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the highest-paying states for auto mechanics are Alaska, Hawaii, District of Columbia, Maryland, and Colorado.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Auto Mechanic Make Per Year

Overall, auto mechanics can expect to make a decent salary with the potential for growth over time. By pursuing additional education and certifications, specializing in a niche area, and staying up-to-date on the latest technology, you can increase your earning potential and become a successful mechanic.
