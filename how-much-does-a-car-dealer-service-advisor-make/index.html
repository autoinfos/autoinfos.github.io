How Much Does A Car Dealer Service Advisor Make

Written by Christine Nov 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Working in the automotive industry can be a rewarding career choice, but it's important to know what kind of salary you can expect. If you're considering becoming a car dealer service advisor, you may be wondering how much you can expect to earn. In this blog post, we'll explore the average salary for a car dealer service advisor and what factors can impact your earnings.

As with any job, there are certain aspects of being a car dealer service advisor that can be challenging. For example, you may have to deal with difficult customers or work long hours. However, one of the biggest pain points for many service advisors is the uncertainty around their earnings. Without a clear understanding of what you can expect to make, it can be tough to plan for the future and make informed decisions about your career.

The average salary for a car dealer service advisor is around $50,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on a number of factors. For example, your location, experience level, and the type of dealership you work for can all impact your earnings. In addition, some dealerships may offer bonuses or other incentives that can increase your overall salary.

In summary, if you're considering becoming a car dealer service advisor, it's important to have a clear understanding of what you can expect to make. While the average salary is around $50,000 per year, there are many factors that can impact your earnings. By doing your research and exploring your options, you can make an informed decision about whether this career path is right for you.

Factors That Affect a Car Dealer Service Advisor's Salary

When it comes to determining how much a car dealer service advisor makes, there are several key factors to consider. One of the most important is location. In general, service advisors who work in larger cities or more affluent areas tend to earn more than those in rural or less affluent areas.

Another factor to consider is experience level. Like many jobs, car dealer service advisors tend to earn more as they gain experience and develop new skills. In addition, working for a larger dealership or one that specializes in luxury vehicles can also impact your earnings.

My Personal Experience as a Car Dealer Service Advisor

As someone who has worked as a car dealer service advisor for several years, I can attest to the fact that the job can be both challenging and rewarding. While there are certainly aspects of the job that can be frustrating, such as dealing with difficult customers, it can also be incredibly satisfying to help people solve their car-related problems and get back on the road.

When it comes to salary, I've found that it's important to be proactive in negotiating your earnings. Don't be afraid to ask for a raise or discuss your compensation package with your employer. In addition, staying up-to-date on industry trends and best practices can help you stay competitive in terms of salary.

How to Become a Car Dealer Service Advisor

If you're interested in becoming a car dealer service advisor, there are several steps you can take to get started. First, consider getting a degree or certification in automotive technology or a related field. This can help you develop the skills and knowledge you need to succeed in this role.

In addition, gaining experience in the automotive industry through internships or entry-level positions can be a valuable way to get your foot in the door. Finally, consider networking with industry professionals and attending job fairs or other events to learn more about potential job opportunities.

Question and Answer

Q: How much does a car dealer service advisor typically make per hour?

A: The average hourly rate for a car dealer service advisor is around $18 per hour.

Q: What is the job outlook for car dealer service advisors?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of automotive service technicians and mechanics, which includes service advisors, is projected to grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029.

Q: Do car dealer service advisors typically receive benefits?

A: Yes, many car dealer service advisors receive benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off.

Q: Are there opportunities for advancement as a car dealer service advisor?

A: Yes, many car dealer service advisors can advance to management positions within their dealership or even move into other areas of the automotive industry.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Car Dealer Service Advisor Make

Overall, working as a car dealer service advisor can be a great career choice for those who are passionate about cars and enjoy helping others. While the salary can vary depending on a number of factors, the average earnings are around $50,000 per year. By gaining experience, staying up-to-date on industry trends, and proactively negotiating your earnings, you can set yourself up for long-term success in this role.
