How Much Does A Certified Harley Mechanic Make

Written by Petter May 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you a motorcycle enthusiast who is considering a career as a certified Harley mechanic? Do you want to know how much a certified Harley mechanic makes? Look no further, as we have all the answers for you!

One of the biggest concerns for anyone considering becoming a certified Harley mechanic is the salary. It's natural to wonder how much you can expect to earn in this profession. After all, you want your hard work and dedication to pay off in the form of a decent paycheck.

The average salary for a certified Harley mechanic is around $50,000 per year. However, this can vary based on a number of factors such as experience, location, and the type of employer you work for. Some mechanics may earn as little as $30,000 per year, while others can earn upwards of $80,000 or more.

In conclusion, becoming a certified Harley mechanic can be a lucrative career choice for motorcycle enthusiasts. While the salary may vary, it is possible to earn a decent living with a career in this field.

What Factors Affect How Much a Certified Harley Mechanic Makes?

There are several factors that can impact how much a certified Harley mechanic makes. These include:

Experience

As with any profession, the more experience you have, the higher your earning potential. Experienced mechanics are often in high demand and can command higher salaries.

Location

The location where you work also plays a role in how much you can earn as a Harley mechanic. Mechanics in larger cities or areas with a high cost of living may earn more than those in rural areas.

My Personal Experience as a Certified Harley Mechanic

As someone who has been working as a certified Harley mechanic for several years, I can tell you that the salary can vary depending on the dealership or repair shop you work for. I started out earning around $35,000 per year, but as I gained more experience and proved my worth, I was able to negotiate a higher salary.

How to Become a Certified Harley Mechanic

If you're interested in becoming a certified Harley mechanic, there are a few steps you'll need to take:

Education and Training

You'll need to complete a formal training program to become a certified Harley mechanic. This can include attending a technical school or community college that offers a motorcycle repair program. You can also complete an apprenticeship program with a Harley dealership or repair shop.

Certification

After completing your training program, you'll need to pass the certification exam administered by Harley-Davidson. This exam tests your knowledge and skills in repairing and maintaining Harley motorcycles.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it possible to earn more than $80,000 per year as a certified Harley mechanic?

A: Yes, it is possible to earn more than $80,000 per year as a certified Harley mechanic. This will depend on your experience, location, and the type of employer you work for.

Q: How long does it take to complete a training program for Harley mechanics?

A: The length of a training program for Harley mechanics can vary, but most programs can be completed in two years or less.

Q: Do I need to have prior experience working on motorcycles to become a certified Harley mechanic?

A: No, you don't need to have prior experience working on motorcycles to become a certified Harley mechanic. However, having some mechanical experience can be helpful.

Q: Can I work as a freelance Harley mechanic or do I need to work for a dealership or repair shop?

A: While it's possible to work as a freelance Harley mechanic, most mechanics work for a dealership or repair shop. This provides a steady stream of work and the support of other mechanics and staff.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Certified Harley Mechanic Make

While the salary for a certified Harley mechanic may vary, it is possible to earn a decent living in this profession. By gaining experience, working for reputable dealerships or repair shops, and staying up-to-date with the latest techniques and technology, you can increase your earning potential and enjoy a fulfilling career as a Harley mechanic.
