How Much Does A Cvs Pharmacy Tech Make An Hour

Written by Daniel Jul 20, 2023 · 4 min read

When it comes to pursuing a career as a pharmacy technician, one of the most common concerns is how much does a CVS pharmacy tech make an hour? It's an important question to ask, as it can help you determine if this career path is right for you.

Many people are interested in becoming a pharmacy technician, but are unsure about the earning potential. Some may worry that it is not a lucrative enough career, while others may be concerned about the cost of pharmacy technician training programs. Whatever the case may be, it's important to understand the financial aspects of this career.

The average hourly wage for a CVS pharmacy tech is around $14-$15 per hour, according to Glassdoor. However, this can vary depending on a number of factors, such as location, experience, and education level. It's important to note that this wage is just an average, and some pharmacy technicians may earn more or less than this amount.

In summary, becoming a CVS pharmacy tech can be a rewarding career choice, but it's important to understand the financial aspects of the job. While the average hourly wage is around $14-$15 per hour, this can vary depending on a number of factors.

What Factors Affect the Hourly Wage of a CVS Pharmacy Tech?

When it comes to the hourly wage of a CVS pharmacy tech, there are several factors that can impact how much you can expect to earn. Here are a few key factors to keep in mind:

Experience

As with many jobs, experience can play a big role in determining your hourly wage as a pharmacy technician. Those with more experience may be able to negotiate a higher wage than those who are just starting out.

Education Level

While a formal education is not always required to become a pharmacy technician, having a degree or certification in the field can help you earn a higher wage. Many pharmacy technician programs offer training in areas such as medical terminology, pharmacology, and pharmacy law, which can make you a more valuable employee.

My Personal Experience as a CVS Pharmacy Tech

As a former CVS pharmacy tech, I can say that the hourly wage is just one aspect of the job. While it's important to be paid fairly for the work you do, there are other factors that make this job rewarding. For example, I enjoyed working directly with patients and helping them manage their medications. I also appreciated the opportunity to learn about new medications and medical conditions.

How to Become a CVS Pharmacy Tech

If you're interested in becoming a CVS pharmacy tech, there are a few steps you'll need to take:

Complete a Pharmacy Technician Training Program

While a formal education is not always required, completing a pharmacy technician training program can help you gain the skills and knowledge you need to succeed in this field. Many programs offer both classroom and hands-on training, which can be valuable when it comes to finding a job.

Get Certified

While certification is not required to become a pharmacy technician, it can help you stand out to potential employers. There are several organizations that offer certification exams, including the Pharmacy Technician Certification Board and the National Healthcare Association.

Question and Answer

Q: How much does a CVS pharmacy tech make per year?

A: The average annual salary for a CVS pharmacy tech is around $30,000-$35,000, although this can vary depending on a number of factors.

Q: Is it difficult to become a pharmacy technician?

A: While becoming a pharmacy technician does require some training and certification, it is generally considered to be a relatively easy field to enter. Many programs can be completed in less than a year, and the job outlook for pharmacy techs is positive.

Q: Can pharmacy technicians work part-time?

A: Yes, many pharmacy technicians work part-time hours. This can be a good option for those who are looking for a flexible schedule or who are still in school.

Q: What kind of tasks does a pharmacy technician perform?

A: Pharmacy technicians perform a variety of tasks, including filling prescriptions, managing inventory, and interacting with patients. They may also be responsible for answering phones, processing insurance claims, and providing customer service.

Conclusion

When it comes to the hourly wage of a CVS pharmacy tech, there are several factors that can impact how much you can expect to earn. While the average hourly wage is around $14-$15 per hour, this can vary depending on factors such as experience and education level. Overall, becoming a pharmacy technician can be a rewarding career choice for those who are interested in healthcare and helping others.
