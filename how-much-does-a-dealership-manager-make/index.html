How Much Does A Dealership Manager Make

Written by Aprilia Oct 31, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Working in the automobile industry is not only exciting but also rewarding. Dealership managers have a significant role in the automobile industry. They are responsible for overseeing the daily operations of the dealership, ensuring customer satisfaction, and managing the sales team. Many people wonder how much does a dealership manager make. In this post, we will talk about the salary range of a dealership manager and the factors that affect their pay.

Dealership managers have a challenging job, and they are expected to perform well. Their salary range varies depending on several factors. Some of these factors include the size of the dealership, location, experience, and education. These factors play a significant role in determining the dealership manager's pay.

The average salary of a dealership manager in the United States is $97,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on the dealership's size and location. A dealership manager in a smaller dealership may earn less than one in a larger dealership in a metropolitan area. Experience and education also play a significant role in determining a dealership manager's salary.

In summary, the salary of a dealership manager varies depending on several factors. The dealership's size and location, experience, and education all affect the dealership manager's pay.

How Much Does a Dealership Manager Make: Personal Experience

As someone who has worked in the automobile industry for over a decade, I have seen dealership managers earn a wide range of salaries. I have worked with managers who earn over $150,000 per year, while others earn less than $80,000 per year. The difference comes down to the dealership's size and location, as well as the manager's experience and education.

Factors That Affect a Dealership Manager's Salary

The size and location of the dealership are significant factors that affect a dealership manager's salary. A manager in a large dealership in a metropolitan area may earn more than a manager in a smaller dealership in a rural area. Experience and education are also factors that affect a dealership manager's pay. A manager with several years of experience and a higher level of education may earn more than a manager who is new to the job.

The Importance of Experience

Experience is a significant factor that affects a dealership manager's salary. A manager with several years of experience has a better understanding of the automobile industry and how to manage a dealership. Experienced managers are more likely to make better decisions, which can lead to increased sales and profitability for the dealership. As a result, experienced managers are often paid more than those who are new to the job.

The Importance of Education

Education is another factor that affects a dealership manager's salary. A manager with a higher level of education, such as a bachelor's or master's degree, may earn more than a manager with only a high school diploma. A higher level of education demonstrates that the manager has a greater understanding of business and management principles, which can be valuable in the automobile industry.

How Much Does a Dealership Manager Make: Personal Experience

One of my colleagues started as a sales consultant and worked his way up to become a dealership manager. He earned a salary of $95,000 per year, which was on the lower end of the salary range for dealership managers in our area. However, he had only been in the role for a year and was still learning the ropes. He was confident that his salary would increase as he gained more experience and proved his worth to the dealership.

Question and Answer

A: The average salary of a dealership manager in the United States is $97,000 per year.

Q: Do the size and location of the dealership affect a dealership manager's salary?

A: Yes, the size and location of the dealership are significant factors that affect a dealership manager's salary.

Q: Does education affect a dealership manager's salary?

Conclusion of How Much Does a Dealership Manager Make

The salary range of a dealership manager varies depending on several factors, including the size and location of the dealership, experience, and education. The average salary of a dealership manager in the United States is $97,000 per year. However, this can vary significantly depending on the manager's qualifications and the dealership's needs. It is essential for anyone interested in becoming a dealership manager to understand these factors and how they can affect their salary.
