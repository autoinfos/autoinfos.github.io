How Much Does A Dentist Earn In Australia Per Hour

Written by William Jun 14, 2023 · 5 min read

As an interior design blogger, you may not think that the topic of how much a dentist earns in Australia per hour would be relevant to your readers. However, it might surprise you to know that many people are interested in this topic. For example, someone who is considering a career in dentistry may want to know what kind of salary they can expect. Similarly, someone who is looking for a dentist may be curious about how much money they make.

The topic of how much a dentist earns in Australia per hour can be a sensitive one. On the one hand, dentists are highly skilled professionals who provide an essential service. On the other hand, some people may feel that dentists charge too much for their services. Additionally, there may be concerns about the affordability of dental care for people on lower incomes.

According to the Australian Government’s Job Outlook website, the average hourly wage for a dentist in Australia is $88.00. This figure is based on data from May 2019, so it may have changed slightly since then. However, it gives you a good idea of what dentists in Australia can expect to earn.

In summary, dentists in Australia earn an average hourly wage of $88.00. This is a good salary, but it reflects the fact that dentistry is a highly skilled profession that requires many years of training and education.

Why is the Average Hourly Wage for Dentists in Australia So High?

As someone who is interested in interior design, you may be wondering why the average hourly wage for dentists in Australia is so high. After all, you may work just as hard as a dentist, but you don’t earn nearly as much money. There are several reasons why dentists earn a high salary:

Firstly, dentists are highly trained professionals who provide an essential service. They need to complete a Bachelor of Dental Surgery (BDS) degree, which takes five years to complete. After that, they need to complete a one-year internship and then register with the Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (AHPRA). This means that dentists have invested a lot of time and money in their education, and they need to earn a high salary to recoup those costs.

Secondly, dentistry is a physically demanding job. Dentists need to spend a lot of time hunched over their patients, which can lead to back, neck, and shoulder pain. Additionally, dentists need to use their hands and wrists a lot, which can lead to repetitive strain injuries. Given the physical demands of the job, it’s understandable that dentists would command a high salary.

What Factors Affect How Much a Dentist Earns in Australia?

While the average hourly wage for dentists in Australia is $88.00, individual dentists may earn more or less than this depending on several factors:

Firstly, dentists who work in major cities may earn more than those who work in regional areas. This is because there is more demand for dental services in major cities, so dentists can charge higher fees.

Secondly, dentists who own their own practice may earn more than those who work for someone else. This is because practice owners can set their own fees and keep a larger share of the profits.

Thirdly, dentists who specialize in certain areas may earn more than general dentists. For example, orthodontists and periodontists generally earn more than general dentists.

Can You Make a Good Living as a Dentist in Australia?

As someone who is considering a career in dentistry, you may be wondering whether you can make a good living as a dentist in Australia. The answer is yes, you can. While dentistry is a challenging and demanding profession, it is also a rewarding one. Dentists have the opportunity to help people improve their oral health and overall wellbeing. Additionally, dentists can earn a good salary that reflects the value of their skills and expertise.
