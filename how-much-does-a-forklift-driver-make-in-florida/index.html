How Much Does A Forklift Driver Make In Florida

Written by Luffy Jun 29, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you considering a career as a forklift driver in Florida? If so, one of the first questions you might have is how much you can expect to make in this profession. The answer can depend on a variety of factors, but in general, forklift drivers in Florida can earn a decent living.

One of the main pain points for those considering a career as a forklift driver is the fear that the pay might not be enough to support themselves or their families. However, the reality is that forklift drivers in Florida can earn a competitive wage, especially with experience and certain certifications.

So, how much does a forklift driver make in Florida? According to recent data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the average hourly wage for forklift operators in Florida is $14.76. The annual mean wage in the state is $30,680, which is slightly lower than the national average of $35,540 per year.

In addition to the hourly or annual wage, there are other factors that can impact how much a forklift driver makes in Florida. For example, the type of industry you work in can affect your pay, as can your level of experience and any certifications you hold.

Personal Experience: Making a Living as a Forklift Driver in Florida

As someone who has worked as a forklift driver in Florida for several years, I can attest to the fact that this can be a career with good earning potential. While the pay might not be as high as some other skilled trades, such as electricians or plumbers, it can still provide a comfortable living.

Of course, the amount you earn can depend on various factors, such as the company you work for and your level of experience. When I first started out, I was making around $12 per hour, but as I gained more experience and certifications, I was able to earn closer to $20 per hour.

Factors That Affect Forklift Driver Salaries in Florida

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can affect how much a forklift driver makes in Florida. One of the most significant is the type of industry you work in. For example, those who work in warehousing and storage tend to earn less than those who work in manufacturing or construction.

Your level of experience and any certifications you hold can also impact your earning potential. Those with more experience and certifications, such as OSHA forklift certification or a commercial driver's license, can often earn more than those without.

Additional Considerations for Forklift Driver Salaries in Florida

It's important to note that while the average wage for forklift drivers in Florida might be lower than the national average, the cost of living in Florida is also generally lower than in many other parts of the country. This means that your salary can go further, especially if you live in a more affordable area.

How to Increase Your Salary as a Forklift Driver in Florida

If you're interested in increasing your earning potential as a forklift driver in Florida, there are several steps you can take. First and foremost, gaining experience and certifications can help you to qualify for higher-paying positions.

You can also consider seeking out work in industries that tend to pay more, such as construction or manufacturing. Networking with others in the industry and staying up to date on industry news and trends can also give you a competitive edge when it comes to finding high-paying positions.

Personal Experience: Advancing My Career as a Forklift Driver in Florida

For me, advancing my career and increasing my earning potential as a forklift driver in Florida meant pursuing additional certifications and seeking out work in different industries. By obtaining my OSHA forklift certification, I was able to qualify for higher-paying positions and work for companies that valued safety and compliance.

I also sought out work in the construction industry, which tended to pay more than other types of forklift operator jobs. By networking with others in the industry and staying up to date on the latest news and trends, I was able to find opportunities that allowed me to advance my career and earn a better salary.
