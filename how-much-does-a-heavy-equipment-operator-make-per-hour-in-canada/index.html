How Much Does A Heavy Equipment Operator Make Per Hour In Canada

Written by Christine Nov 20, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're considering a career in heavy equipment operation in Canada, one of the first questions you might have is how much you can expect to earn. It's an important question, and one that can have a big impact on your future. In this post, we'll take a deep dive into how much heavy equipment operators make per hour in Canada, and what factors can influence their earnings.

For many people, money is a major factor when choosing a career. You want to know that you'll be able to earn a good living doing the work you love. Heavy equipment operation can be a rewarding and challenging career, but it's important to know what you can expect to earn before you dive in.

According to the latest data from the Government of Canada, the average hourly wage for heavy equipment operators in Canada is $29.14 per hour. This can vary depending on a number of factors, including your level of experience, your location, and the type of equipment you operate.

So, what factors can impact how much you can expect to earn as a heavy equipment operator in Canada? Let's take a closer look.

Experience

One of the biggest factors that can influence your earning potential as a heavy equipment operator is your level of experience. As with many careers, the more experience you have, the more you can expect to earn. For example, a heavy equipment operator with less than a year of experience can expect to earn an average hourly wage of $21.48, while someone with 10 or more years of experience can earn an average of $33.38 per hour.

Location

The province or territory where you work can also have a big impact on your earning potential. For example, heavy equipment operators in Alberta tend to earn the highest wages, with an average hourly rate of $36.29. Meanwhile, those in the Northwest Territories earn an average of just $22.25 per hour.

Type of Equipment

The type of equipment you operate can also impact your earnings. For example, crane operators tend to earn more than excavator operators, with an average hourly wage of $36.81 compared to $30.40. Similarly, those who operate large equipment like bulldozers and graders tend to earn more than those who operate smaller equipment like skid steers.

Unionization

Joining a union can also impact your earning potential as a heavy equipment operator. Unionized workers tend to earn higher wages than non-unionized workers, and they may also have access to additional benefits like health insurance and retirement plans.

Personal Experience with Heavy Equipment Operation in Canada

As a heavy equipment operator in Canada, I can tell you that the earning potential in this field is quite good. While your hourly wage will depend on a number of factors, there are plenty of opportunities to earn a good living doing this work. It can be challenging and demanding, but also very rewarding.

How to Become a Heavy Equipment Operator in Canada

If you're interested in becoming a heavy equipment operator in Canada, there are a few things you'll need to do. First, you'll need to complete a training program that covers the skills and knowledge you'll need to operate heavy equipment safely and effectively. You can find training programs at trade schools, community colleges, and other institutions across the country.

Once you've completed your training, you'll need to obtain any necessary certifications and licenses. This can vary depending on the province or territory where you work, so be sure to check the specific requirements in your area.

Finally, you'll need to start building your experience in the field. This may involve working as an apprentice or junior operator, or taking on entry-level positions in the industry. Over time, you can work your way up to more advanced positions and higher earning potential.

Question and Answer

Q: Is heavy equipment operation a good career choice in Canada?

A: Absolutely. Heavy equipment operation is a growing field in Canada, with plenty of opportunities for skilled workers. If you enjoy working with your hands and are looking for a challenging and rewarding career, this could be a great choice for you.

Q: How long does it take to become a heavy equipment operator in Canada?

A: The length of time it takes to become a heavy equipment operator can vary depending on your training program and other factors. Generally, you can expect to spend anywhere from a few months to a year completing your training and obtaining any necessary certifications.

Q: What kind of equipment do heavy equipment operators in Canada typically work with?

A: Heavy equipment operators in Canada work with a wide range of equipment, including excavators, bulldozers, graders, skid steers, and more. The specific equipment you work with may depend on the type of work you specialize in and the needs of your employer.

Q: Is heavy equipment operation a dangerous job?

A: Like any job that involves heavy machinery, there are certain risks associated with heavy equipment operation. However, with proper training and safety protocols in place, it is possible to minimize these risks and ensure a safe working environment.

Conclusion

If you're considering a career in heavy equipment operation in Canada, it's important to understand how much you can expect to earn. While there are a number of factors that can influence your earning potential, the average hourly wage for heavy equipment operators in Canada is $29.14 per hour. With the right training, certifications, and experience, this can be a rewarding and lucrative career choice for those who enjoy working with their hands and operating heavy machinery.
