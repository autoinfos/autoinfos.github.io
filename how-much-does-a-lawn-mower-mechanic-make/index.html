How Much Does A Lawn Mower Mechanic Make

Written by Oliver Sep 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a lawn mower mechanic? If so, you may be wondering how much money you can expect to make in this field. As with any profession, the answer depends on a variety of factors, including your level of experience, location, and the type of employer you work for. In this post, we'll explore the average salary for a lawn mower mechanic and what you can do to increase your earning potential.

One of the biggest pain points for those considering a career as a lawn mower mechanic is the fear that the pay will not be enough to support themselves and their families. While it's true that some entry-level positions may not pay as well as other trades, the good news is that there is room for growth in this field.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for small engine mechanics, which includes lawn mower mechanics, is $38,200 as of May 2020. This means that half of all mechanics in this field earn more than $38,200, while the other half earn less.

In addition to experience and location, there are several other factors that can impact your earning potential as a lawn mower mechanic. For example, working for a large dealership or equipment rental company may offer more opportunities for advancement and higher pay. You may also be able to increase your earnings by obtaining specialized certifications or training in areas such as diesel engine repair or hydraulic systems.

Personal Experience

As a former lawn mower mechanic myself, I can speak to the earning potential in this field. When I first started out, my starting salary was around $25,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and took on additional responsibilities, my salary increased to around $40,000 per year.

One of the keys to success in this field is to always be willing to learn and take on new challenges. By doing so, you can position yourself as a valuable asset to your employer and increase your earning potential over time.

Opportunities for Growth

While the median salary for lawn mower mechanics may not seem particularly high, it's important to remember that there are many opportunities for growth in this field. For example, you may be able to move into a management position or start your own repair business, which could significantly increase your earnings.

Certifications and Training

Another way to increase your earning potential as a lawn mower mechanic is to obtain specialized certifications or training in areas such as diesel engine repair, welding, or hydraulics. By doing so, you can position yourself as an expert in your field and potentially command a higher salary.

Networking and Job Search Strategies

If you're just starting out in the field, it's important to be proactive in your job search and networking efforts. Consider joining professional organizations, attending trade shows and conferences, and reaching out to industry professionals on LinkedIn. By doing so, you may be able to uncover job opportunities that you wouldn't have otherwise known about.

Personal Experience

Another way to increase your earning potential as a lawn mower mechanic is to specialize in a particular brand of equipment. For example, if you become an expert in John Deere equipment, you may be able to command a higher salary than someone who works on a variety of different brands.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average hourly rate for a lawn mower mechanic?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for small engine mechanics, including lawn mower mechanics, is $18.35 as of May 2020.

Q: What other factors can impact a lawn mower mechanic's earning potential?

A: In addition to experience and location, working for a larger company, obtaining specialized certifications or training, and specializing in a particular brand of equipment can all impact a lawn mower mechanic's earning potential.

Q: Is there room for growth in the field of lawn mower mechanics?

A: Yes, there are many opportunities for growth in this field. Lawn mower mechanics can move into management positions or start their own repair businesses, both of which can significantly increase their earnings.

Q: How can I increase my chances of finding a job as a lawn mower mechanic?

A: Consider joining professional organizations, attending trade shows and conferences, and reaching out to industry professionals on LinkedIn. By doing so, you may be able to uncover job opportunities that you wouldn't have otherwise known about.

Conclusion

While the median salary for a lawn mower mechanic may not be particularly high, there are many opportunities for growth in this field. By obtaining specialized certifications or training, working for a larger company, and specializing in a particular brand of equipment, you can position yourself for success and potentially increase your earnings over time.
