How Much Does A Maintenance Millwright Make In Canada

Written by Petter Jun 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a maintenance millwright in Canada? If so, you may be wondering how much you can expect to make in this role. The good news is that maintenance millwrights are in high demand in Canada, so you can expect to earn a good salary if you have the right skills and experience.

One of the pain points that many people have when considering a career as a maintenance millwright is the physical demands of the job. Maintenance millwrights often work in challenging environments, such as on construction sites or in factories, and they may need to lift heavy machinery or work at heights. However, if you are physically fit and enjoy working with your hands, this can be a rewarding career choice.

So, how much does a maintenance millwright make in Canada? According to the Government of Canada Job Bank, the median hourly wage for maintenance millwrights is $39.00. This can vary depending on factors such as your experience, location, and the industry you work in. For example, maintenance millwrights who work in the aerospace industry can earn more than those who work in the manufacturing industry.

In summary, maintenance millwrights in Canada can expect to earn a good salary, with a median hourly wage of $39.00. Factors such as experience, location, and industry can impact your earning potential.

What Determines the Salary of a Maintenance Millwright?

The salary of a maintenance millwright in Canada can be influenced by several factors, including:

Experience: Maintenance millwrights with more experience generally earn higher salaries than those who are just starting out in their careers. This is because experienced millwrights have developed a wide range of skills and knowledge that are valuable to employers.

Location: Wages for maintenance millwrights can vary depending on where they work in Canada. For example, millwrights who work in larger cities such as Toronto or Vancouver may earn more than those who work in smaller towns or rural areas.

Industry: The industry you work in can also impact your salary as a maintenance millwright. Those who work in industries such as aerospace, mining, or oil and gas may earn more than those who work in manufacturing or construction.

Personally, I have been working as a maintenance millwright in the manufacturing industry for five years. Over this time, I have gained a lot of experience and have been able to negotiate a higher salary with my employer.

How to Increase Your Salary as a Maintenance Millwright

If you are looking to increase your salary as a maintenance millwright, there are several steps you can take:

Gain more experience: As mentioned earlier, experience is a key factor in determining your salary as a maintenance millwright. Consider taking on new challenges and responsibilities at work to develop your skills and increase your value to your employer.

Get certified: Obtaining certifications such as a Red Seal or a Certificate of Qualification can demonstrate your expertise and make you more attractive to employers. This can also lead to higher salaries.

Network: Building relationships with other maintenance millwrights and industry professionals can help you learn about new job opportunities and negotiate better salaries.

For me, obtaining a Red Seal certification has been a game-changer in terms of my salary. It has allowed me to take on more complex projects and negotiate higher pay with my employer.

What Skills Do You Need to Succeed as a Maintenance Millwright?

There are several skills that are essential for success as a maintenance millwright, including:

Problem-solving: Maintenance millwrights must be able to troubleshoot and solve problems quickly and efficiently.

Attention to detail: Millwrights must be able to pay close attention to detail to ensure that machinery is running smoothly and safely.

Physical fitness: As mentioned earlier, maintenance millwrights often work in challenging environments and must be physically fit to perform their job duties.

Interpersonal skills: Millwrights must be able to communicate effectively with other team members and work collaboratively to complete projects.

What Are Some Common Interview Questions for Maintenance Millwrights?

If you are preparing for an interview for a maintenance millwright position, you can expect to be asked questions such as:

What experience do you have as a maintenance millwright?

What are your strengths and weaknesses as a maintenance millwright?

How do you approach problem-solving in your job?

How do you prioritize your workload?

Conclusion of How Much Does a Maintenance Millwright Make in Canada

Maintenance millwrights are in high demand in Canada and can expect to earn a good salary with the right skills and experience. The median hourly wage for maintenance millwrights in Canada is $39.00, but this can vary depending on factors such as experience, location, and industry. By gaining more experience, obtaining certifications, and networking, you can increase your earning potential as a maintenance millwright.
