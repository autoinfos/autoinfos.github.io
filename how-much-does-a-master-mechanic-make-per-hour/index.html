How Much Does A Master Mechanic Make Per Hour

Written by Eveline Jun 07, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you interested in becoming a master mechanic? If so, you might be wondering how much money you can make in this field. Well, the answer isn't straightforward. There are many factors that can affect how much a master mechanic makes per hour, and it's essential to understand them before making any decisions.

One of the biggest pain points when it comes to how much does a master mechanic make per hour is the level of experience. As with any profession, the more experience you have, the more money you can expect to make. But it's not just about the number of years you've been working as a mechanic. Your skillset, certifications, and the type of vehicles you work on also play a significant role in determining your hourly rate.

According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $20.24 as of May 2020. However, the top 10% of earners in this field make more than $32.19 per hour. This means that if you're a highly skilled master mechanic with years of experience, you can expect to make a comfortable living in this profession.

In conclusion, how much does a master mechanic make per hour varies depending on several factors. These factors include the level of experience, skillset, certifications, and the type of vehicles you work on. However, if you're a highly skilled master mechanic with years of experience, you can make more than the median hourly wage of $20.24.

How Experience Affects Master Mechanic's Hourly Rate

When I started my career as a mechanic, I was making around $15 per hour. However, as I gained more experience and became a master mechanic, my hourly rate increased to $25 per hour. I've been in this profession for over 10 years now, and I can tell you that experience is the key to making more money as a master mechanic.

As you gain more experience, you become more proficient in diagnosing and repairing vehicles. You also become more familiar with the different types of vehicles and the parts and tools needed to fix them. This makes you more valuable to employers, and they'll be willing to pay you more for your expertise.

How Certification Affects Master Mechanic's Hourly Rate

Getting certified as a master mechanic can also significantly impact your hourly rate. Certifications demonstrate your expertise and knowledge in specific areas of automotive repair. They also show that you're committed to improving your skills and staying up-to-date with the latest technology and techniques.

When I got my ASE (Automotive Service Excellence) Master Technician certification, my hourly rate increased by $5 per hour. This certification is widely recognized in the industry and shows that you have a high level of expertise in several areas of automotive repair.

The Type of Vehicles You Work On

Another factor that can affect how much a master mechanic makes per hour is the type of vehicles they work on. Some vehicles are more complex and require specialized knowledge and tools to repair. For example, working on luxury cars or high-performance vehicles can pay more than working on standard vehicles.

If you're interested in making more money as a master mechanic, consider specializing in a particular type of vehicle. This will require additional training and certifications, but it can be worth it in terms of your earning potential.

Negotiating Your Hourly Rate

If you're looking to increase your hourly rate as a master mechanic, you may need to negotiate with your employer. Start by doing research on the average hourly rate for master mechanics in your area. This will give you an idea of what you should be earning based on your experience and certifications.

When negotiating your hourly rate, be prepared to demonstrate your expertise and value to the company. Highlight your certifications, experience, and skills that make you an asset to the team. You can also ask for additional benefits like health insurance, vacation time, or retirement plans.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Master Mechanic Make Per Hour

While how much a master mechanic makes per hour varies depending on several factors, they can expect to make a comfortable living if they're highly skilled with years of experience. With additional certifications and specializing in a particular type of vehicle, a master mechanic can increase their earning potential even further. Remember to negotiate your hourly rate based on your experience and expertise, and don't be afraid to ask for additional benefits.
