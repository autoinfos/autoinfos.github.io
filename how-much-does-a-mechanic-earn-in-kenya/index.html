How Much Does A Mechanic Earn In Kenya

Written by Frank Aug 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a mechanic in Kenya but wondering how much you can expect to earn? Look no further as we explore the average salary for mechanics in Kenya and what factors can affect your earnings.

Being a mechanic can be a rewarding career path, but it's important to have a good understanding of the earning potential in this field. Many factors can affect a mechanic's salary, such as their level of experience, education, and the location of their workplace.

The average salary for a mechanic in Kenya is KSh 30,000 to KSh 70,000 per month, depending on their level of experience and the type of work they specialize in. Entry-level mechanics can expect to earn around KSh 30,000, while experienced mechanics can earn up to KSh 70,000 per month. However, this salary can also be affected by the location of the workshop or garage, as those in urban areas tend to pay more than those in rural areas.

In summary, being a mechanic in Kenya can be a lucrative career path with the potential to earn a good salary. However, the amount you can earn can vary depending on your level of experience, education, and location.

What Affects a Mechanic's Salary in Kenya?

Speaking from personal experience, the level of experience and education can affect a mechanic's salary in Kenya. Those who have completed formal training and have certification tend to earn more than those who are self-taught. Additionally, mechanics who specialize in a particular field, such as diesel engines or air conditioning, can earn more than general mechanics.

Location of Workshop or Garage

Another factor that can affect a mechanic's salary in Kenya is the location of their workshop or garage. Those in urban areas tend to pay more than those in rural areas. This is because urban areas have a higher cost of living, and mechanics need to earn more to sustain their lifestyle.

Experience and Education

Experience and education play a significant role in determining a mechanic's salary in Kenya. As with many other professions, the more experience and education you have, the more you can earn. Mechanics who have completed formal training and have certification tend to earn more than those who are self-taught. Additionally, those with several years of experience can expect to earn more than entry-level mechanics.

Negotiation Skills

Finally, negotiation skills also play a role in determining a mechanic's salary in Kenya. Mechanics who are good at negotiating with their employers can earn more than those who are not. It's essential to understand your worth and be confident in your abilities when negotiating your salary.
