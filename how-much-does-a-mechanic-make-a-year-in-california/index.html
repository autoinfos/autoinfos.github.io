Are you interested in pursuing a career as a mechanic in California? Before you do, you might be wondering how much money you can expect to make in this field. The good news is that mechanics can earn a decent living in California, but the exact salary you can expect will depend on a variety of factors.

One of the main pain points related to how much does a mechanic make a year in California is the fact that salaries can vary widely depending on where you work and what type of mechanic you are. For example, mechanics who work for large dealerships or manufacturers may earn more than those who work for smaller independent shops. Additionally, specialized mechanics who work on high-end or exotic vehicles may earn more than those who work on more common vehicles.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for automotive service technicians and mechanics in California was $46,310 as of May 2020. However, this figure can range from around $28,000 to over $77,000 depending on experience, location, and other factors.

In conclusion, how much does a mechanic make a year in California can be a difficult question to answer definitively. However, mechanics in California can generally expect to earn a decent living, with salaries ranging from around $28,000 to over $77,000 depending on a variety of factors.
