How Much Does A Millwright Make An Hour

Written by Aprilia Jun 26, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you interested in becoming a millwright but unsure about the potential earnings? Or are you already in the trade and wondering if you're being paid fairly? Either way, understanding how much a millwright makes per hour is an important factor to consider.

The hourly rate for millwrights can vary depending on factors such as location, industry, and level of experience. However, the average hourly rate for a millwright in the United States is $26.91 per hour, according to data from PayScale.

When it comes to industry, millwrights in the manufacturing and industrial sectors tend to earn higher wages than those in other industries such as construction or automotive. Additionally, millwrights with more experience and specialized skills can command higher wages.

In summary, the average hourly rate for a millwright in the United States is $26.91 per hour, with higher wages typically found in the manufacturing and industrial sectors and for those with more experience and specialized skills.

Factors that Affect Millwright Hourly Rates

Location can play a significant role in determining a millwright's hourly rate. For example, millwrights working in urban areas or in states with higher costs of living may earn higher wages than those working in more rural areas or states with lower costs of living.

Industry can also impact millwright hourly rates. Manufacturing and industrial sectors tend to have higher wages due to the specialized skills and knowledge required for these positions. Additionally, millwrights working in unionized workplaces may also have higher wages and benefits compared to non-unionized workplaces.

My Personal Experience as a Millwright

As a millwright with over 10 years of experience, I can attest to the fact that specialized skills and knowledge can lead to higher wages. Early on in my career, I worked in various industries such as construction and automotive, earning around $20-25 per hour.

However, once I gained experience in the manufacturing sector and obtained certifications in welding and rigging, my hourly rate increased to around $30-35 per hour. Additionally, I have found that working in unionized workplaces has led to higher wages and benefits compared to non-unionized workplaces.

How to Become a Millwright

Becoming a millwright typically requires completing a formal apprenticeship program, which can take anywhere from three to five years. During this time, apprentices receive on-the-job training and classroom instruction to learn the skills and knowledge necessary for the trade.

Additionally, obtaining certifications in specialized areas such as welding or rigging can also increase job opportunities and earning potential for millwrights.

Salary Comparison: Millwright vs. Other Trades

According to data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the average hourly rate for a millwright in the United States is comparable to other skilled trades such as electricians and plumbers. However, millwrights in the manufacturing and industrial sectors can earn significantly higher wages than these other trades.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Millwright Make an Hour

While the hourly rate for millwrights can vary based on factors such as location, industry, and experience level, the average hourly rate in the United States is $26.91 per hour. Specialized skills and knowledge, as well as working in unionized workplaces or the manufacturing and industrial sectors, can lead to higher wages for millwrights. Becoming a millwright requires completing a formal apprenticeship program and obtaining certifications in specialized areas can increase earning potential.

Question and Answer

Q: Can millwrights earn overtime pay?
A: Yes, millwrights are typically eligible for overtime pay when they work more than 40 hours in a week.

Q: How do millwright hourly rates compare to other skilled trades?
A: According to data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the average hourly rate for a millwright is comparable to other skilled trades such as electricians and plumbers.

Q: What is the job outlook for millwrights?
A: The job outlook for millwrights is positive, with the Bureau of Labor Statistics projecting a 5% increase in employment from 2019-2029.

Q: Do millwrights typically receive benefits such as health insurance and retirement plans?
A: Yes, millwrights working in unionized workplaces or for companies that offer benefits typically receive health insurance and retirement plans.
