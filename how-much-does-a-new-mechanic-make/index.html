How Much Does A New Mechanic Make Exploring Salaries In The Automotive Industry

Written by Bardi Aug 09, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you interested in becoming a mechanic but wondering how much you can expect to make as a new professional in the field? While salaries can vary depending on a number of factors, such as location and level of experience, there are some general trends that can give you an idea of what to expect. In this post, we'll explore how much a new mechanic typically makes and what factors can impact their earning potential.

For many new mechanics, one of the biggest pain points when entering the field is uncertainty around their earning potential. Without a clear idea of what they can expect to make, it can be difficult to plan for the future or feel confident in their career choice. Additionally, some may have concerns about the overall job market and whether there will be enough demand for mechanics in the coming years.

So, how much does a new mechanic make on average? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 as of May 2020. This means that half of professionals in this field earned more than that amount, while the other half earned less. However, it's important to note that this figure can vary widely depending on a number of factors, such as geographic location, type of employer, and level of experience.

In summary, while there is no one-size-fits-all answer to the question of how much a new mechanic can expect to make, there are some general trends to be aware of. On average, new mechanics can expect to earn around $44,000 per year, but this figure can vary depending on a number of factors.

Factors Impacting Mechanic Salaries

Now that we know the average salary for new mechanics, let's dive into some of the factors that can impact earning potential in this field.

Location

One of the biggest factors impacting mechanic salaries is location. In general, mechanics working in areas with a higher cost of living can expect to earn more than those in areas with a lower cost of living. For example, a mechanic working in New York City may earn more than someone in a smaller town in the Midwest, even if they have the same level of experience and work for the same type of employer.

Type of Employer

The type of employer a mechanic works for can also impact their earning potential. For example, mechanics working for dealerships may earn more than those working for independent repair shops or franchises. Additionally, those working for government agencies or transportation companies may have higher salaries than those working in other industries.

Level of Experience

As with many professions, level of experience can be a major factor in mechanic salaries. New mechanics can expect to earn less than those with several years of experience under their belts. However, it's important to note that experience is not the only factor impacting earning potential.

Specializations and Certifications

Finally, mechanics with specializations or certifications may be able to command higher salaries than those without. For example, a mechanic who specializes in high-performance vehicles or hybrid engines may be able to earn more than a generalist mechanic. Additionally, those who hold certifications from organizations like the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) may be more competitive in the job market and able to command higher salaries.

Personal Experience: Starting Out in the Automotive Industry

When I first started out as a mechanic, I was making around $30,000 per year working for an independent repair shop in a small town. While this was a decent starting salary, I quickly realized that if I wanted to earn more, I would need to gain more experience and potentially specialize in a certain area of the field.

After a few years of working as a generalist mechanic, I decided to pursue ASE certifications in several areas, including engine repair and brake systems. This allowed me to become more competitive in the job market and eventually led to a job at a dealership, where I was able to earn a higher salary and work on more advanced vehicles.

The Future of Mechanic Salaries

While it's impossible to predict the future with complete accuracy, there are some trends that suggest that mechanic salaries may continue to rise in the coming years. For example, as vehicles become more complex and require more specialized knowledge to repair, mechanics with certifications and specializations may become more in demand.

Additionally, as the overall job market continues to improve, there may be more opportunities for mechanics to negotiate higher salaries or move to higher-paying employers. However, it's important to note that these trends may not hold true in all areas or for all mechanics.

Question and Answer

Q: Can new mechanics expect to earn more in certain geographic locations?

A: Yes, mechanics working in areas with a higher cost of living, such as major cities, may be able to earn more than those in smaller towns or rural areas.

Q: Are there any certifications that can help mechanics earn more?

A: Yes, certifications from organizations like the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) can help mechanics become more competitive in the job market and potentially command higher salaries.

Q: Is experience the only factor impacting mechanic salaries?

A: No, while experience is certainly a major factor, other factors such as location, type of employer, and specializations/certifications can also impact earning potential.

Q: What is the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics?

A: As of May 2020, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics.

Conclusion of How Much Does a New Mechanic Make

While there is no one-size-fits-all answer to the question of how much a new mechanic can expect to make, it's clear that salaries can vary widely depending on a number of factors. By understanding these factors and working to gain experience and certifications, new mechanics can position themselves for higher salaries and a successful career in the automotive industry.
