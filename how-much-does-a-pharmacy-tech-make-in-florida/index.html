How Much Does A Pharmacy Tech Make In Florida

Written by Petter Jul 28, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering becoming a pharmacy technician in Florida but wondering how much you can expect to make in this career? You're not alone. Many people are curious about pharmacy tech salaries and whether this job pays enough to support them.

The average pharmacy technician salary in Florida is around $33,000 per year. While this is lower than the national average for pharmacy tech salaries, it's important to remember that Florida has a lower cost of living than many other states.

Pharmacy techs in Florida can expect to make anywhere from $22,000 to $45,000 per year, depending on factors such as experience, location, and certification. Those who work in larger cities tend to earn more than those in smaller towns, and those with advanced certifications may earn higher salaries.

In summary, a pharmacy tech in Florida can expect to make a decent living, with the potential to earn more with experience and certification. It's important to consider the cost of living in Florida when evaluating salaries.

How to Get a Higher Salary as a Pharmacy Tech

One way to increase your salary as a pharmacy tech in Florida is to obtain additional certifications. The Pharmacy Technician Certification Board (PTCB) offers several certifications that can demonstrate your expertise in specialized areas of pharmacy, such as sterile compounding or hazardous drug management.

Another way to boost your salary is to gain experience in a specialized area of pharmacy. For example, working in a hospital or long-term care facility may offer higher salaries than working in a retail pharmacy.

Benefits of Being a Pharmacy Tech in Florida

Working as a pharmacy tech in Florida offers several benefits. For one, this career is in high demand, which means that you're likely to have job security and plenty of opportunities for advancement. Additionally, pharmacy techs in Florida are often eligible for benefits such as health insurance and retirement plans.

How to Become a Pharmacy Tech in Florida

To become a pharmacy technician in Florida, you'll need to complete a training program and pass the Pharmacy Technician Certification Exam (PTCE). You may also need to obtain additional certifications depending on your area of specialty.

How to Find Pharmacy Tech Jobs in Florida

If you're looking for pharmacy tech jobs in Florida, there are several resources available to you. Online job boards such as Indeed and Glassdoor can be helpful, as can professional organizations such as the Florida Pharmacy Association.

Personal Experience as a Pharmacy Tech in Florida

As a pharmacy tech in Florida, I've found this career to be both rewarding and challenging. The work can be fast-paced and stressful at times, but it's also incredibly fulfilling to help patients get the medications they need to feel better.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the job outlook for pharmacy techs in Florida?
A: The job outlook for pharmacy techs in Florida is positive, with a projected growth rate of 7% through 2029.

Q: Do pharmacy techs in Florida need to be certified?
A: While certification is not required by law in Florida, many employers prefer to hire certified pharmacy techs.

Q: Can pharmacy techs in Florida work from home?
A: It's possible for pharmacy techs in Florida to work from home, but this is not common in the industry.

Q: How do pharmacy tech salaries in Florida compare to other states?
A: Pharmacy tech salaries in Florida are generally lower than the national average, but this is offset by the state's lower cost of living.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Pharmacy Tech Make in Florida

While pharmacy tech salaries in Florida may not be as high as in other states, this career still offers a decent living and plenty of opportunities for growth and advancement. With additional certifications and experience, pharmacy techs in Florida can increase their earning potential and enjoy a fulfilling career in healthcare.
