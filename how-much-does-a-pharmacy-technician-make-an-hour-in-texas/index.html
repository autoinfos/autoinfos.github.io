How Much Does A Pharmacy Technician Make An Hour In Texas

Written by Eveline Sep 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in pursuing a career as a pharmacy technician in Texas but wondering about the salary prospects? The average hourly wage for pharmacy technicians in Texas is a common query that many aspiring pharmacy technicians have. In this blog post, we will delve deeper into this topic and explore the various factors that can impact the salary of pharmacy technicians in Texas.

As a pharmacy technician, you play a crucial role in ensuring the smooth functioning of a pharmacy. However, this job can also come with its fair share of challenges. One of the most common concerns among pharmacy technicians is the salary they can expect to earn. This is especially true in a state like Texas, where the cost of living can vary significantly depending on the location.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the average hourly wage for pharmacy technicians in Texas is $16.93, which is slightly lower than the national average of $18.56. However, it is essential to note that this figure can vary depending on the location, experience, and qualifications of the pharmacy technician.

In summary, the salary prospects for pharmacy technicians in Texas are relatively good, with an average hourly wage of $16.93. However, several factors can impact this figure, and it is essential to conduct proper research and evaluate your options before pursuing a career in this field.

The Importance of Qualifications and Experience

When it comes to determining the hourly wage of a pharmacy technician in Texas, qualifications and experience play a crucial role. Generally, pharmacy technicians with a higher level of education and more experience tend to earn higher salaries than those who are just starting.

For instance, a pharmacy technician with a certification from the Pharmacy Technician Certification Board (PTCB) can expect to earn a higher salary than someone without this qualification. Similarly, individuals with more experience working in a pharmacy can command a higher wage than those who are just starting.

The Impact of Location

Another critical factor that can impact the hourly wage of a pharmacy technician in Texas is the location. As mentioned earlier, the cost of living can vary significantly depending on where you live in Texas. Therefore, it is essential to consider the location when evaluating the salary prospects for pharmacy technicians.

For example, pharmacy technicians working in urban areas like Austin, Houston, or Dallas can expect to earn a higher wage than those working in rural areas. This is primarily because the cost of living in urban areas is generally higher than in rural areas.

The Benefits of a Career as a Pharmacy Technician in Texas

Despite the various factors that can impact the hourly wage of a pharmacy technician in Texas, there are several benefits to pursuing a career in this field. For one, pharmacy technicians are in high demand, with the job outlook projected to grow by 4% from 2019 to 2029.

Additionally, pharmacy technicians can work in a variety of settings, including retail pharmacies, hospitals, and long-term care facilities. This means that you can choose a work environment that suits your needs and preferences.

How to Increase Your Hourly Wage as a Pharmacy Technician in Texas

If you're looking to increase your hourly wage as a pharmacy technician in Texas, there are several steps you can take. First, consider pursuing additional qualifications, such as a certification from the PTCB. This can help you stand out from other candidates and increase your earning potential.

Additionally, gaining more experience working in a pharmacy can also help you command a higher wage. Finally, consider exploring job opportunities in higher-paying locations or negotiating your salary with your current employer.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the hourly wage for pharmacy technicians in Texas can vary depending on several factors, including qualifications, experience, and location. However, with an average hourly wage of $16.93 and a growing demand for pharmacy technicians, pursuing a career in this field can be a lucrative and rewarding choice.
