How Much Does A Receptionist Make At A Car Dealership

Written by Frank Jun 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a receptionist at a car dealership, but wondering how much you can expect to make? Look no further! In this post, we'll dive into the average salary for a dealership receptionist, as well as provide some insights into the factors that can impact your earning potential.

Working as a receptionist at a car dealership can come with its own unique set of challenges. From managing a high volume of calls and visitors to juggling various administrative tasks, it can be a demanding role. On top of that, receptionists often have to navigate complex dealership software and systems.

So, how much does a receptionist make at a car dealership? According to Glassdoor, the national average salary for a dealership receptionist is around $28,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on several factors, such as the size and location of the dealership, your level of experience, and any additional skills or certifications you may have.

In summary, if you're considering a career as a receptionist at a car dealership, you can expect to make an average salary of around $28,000 per year. However, keep in mind that this can vary depending on several factors.

Factors That Can Impact Your Salary

Now that we've discussed the average salary for a dealership receptionist, let's dive into some of the factors that can impact your earning potential.

Dealership Size and Location

The size and location of the dealership can play a big role in how much you can expect to make as a receptionist. For example, a larger dealership in a major city may offer higher salaries than a smaller dealership in a rural area.

Experience and Additional Skills

Your level of experience and any additional skills or certifications you have can also impact your salary. If you have several years of experience working as a receptionist in a dealership or a related field, you may be able to negotiate a higher salary. Similarly, if you have experience with specific software or systems used in dealerships, this can make you a more valuable candidate.

My Experience as a Dealership Receptionist

When I first started working as a receptionist at a car dealership, I was making around $25,000 per year. However, after a few years of gaining experience and taking on additional responsibilities, I was able to negotiate a higher salary of $32,000 per year. One thing that really helped was my experience with the dealership's software and systems, which made me a more valuable candidate.

Tips for Increasing Your Salary as a Dealership Receptionist

If you're looking to increase your earning potential as a dealership receptionist, here are a few tips to keep in mind:

1. Gain Experience

The more experience you have working as a receptionist in a dealership or a related field, the more valuable you will be to potential employers.

2. Develop Additional Skills

Consider taking courses or certifications to develop skills in areas such as customer service, software systems, and administrative tasks.

Questions and Answers

Q: Is it possible to negotiate a higher salary as a dealership receptionist?

A: Yes, it's definitely possible to negotiate a higher salary based on your level of experience and any additional skills or certifications you have.

Q: Are there any benefits that come with working as a dealership receptionist?

A: Yes, many dealerships offer benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off.

Q: What is the job outlook for dealership receptionists?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of receptionists is projected to grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029, which is about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Q: Are there any downsides to working as a dealership receptionist?

A: Some of the challenges of working as a dealership receptionist can include managing a high volume of calls and visitors, navigating complex dealership software and systems, and dealing with difficult or upset customers.

Conclusion

In conclusion, working as a receptionist at a car dealership can be a rewarding career path with opportunities for growth and development. While the average salary for a dealership receptionist is around $28,000 per year, this can vary based on several factors such as dealership size and location, experience, and additional skills. By gaining experience and developing additional skills, you can increase your earning potential and take your career to the next level.
