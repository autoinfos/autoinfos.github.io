How Much Does A Service Technician Make At A Dealership

Written by Robby Oct 31, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're considering becoming a service technician at a dealership, you might be wondering about the salary. After all, you want to make sure the job is worth the time and effort you'll be putting in. In this blog post, we'll explore how much a service technician can expect to make at a dealership, and what factors might affect their earning potential.

Service technicians at dealerships often face unique challenges compared to those at independent repair shops. While they may have access to more resources and training, they may also be held to higher standards and pressured to meet strict deadlines. Additionally, dealerships may have more rigid pay structures, which can limit a technician's earning potential.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 as of May 2020. However, this figure can vary widely depending on a number of factors, such as the dealership's location, the technician's level of experience and certification, and the type of vehicles being serviced. Some technicians may also receive bonuses or commissions based on their performance.

In summary, service technicians at dealerships can expect to earn a median salary of around $44,050 per year, although this figure can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and certification. It's also worth noting that dealerships may have more rigid pay structures than independent repair shops, which can limit earning potential.

How Certification Can Affect a Service Technician's Salary

When I first started working as a service technician at a dealership, I quickly realized that certification was key to earning a higher salary. While I had some experience working on cars, I didn't have any formal training or certification, which meant I was starting at the bottom of the pay scale.

However, I soon discovered that the dealership offered a number of certifications that I could pursue. By taking courses and passing exams, I was able to earn certifications in areas such as engine repair and emissions systems. As I earned more certifications, I was able to move up the pay scale and earn a higher salary.

Location and Type of Vehicles Can Impact Earning Potential

Another factor that can impact a service technician's salary is the dealership's location and the types of vehicles being serviced. For example, a dealership located in a wealthier area may be able to charge more for services, which can translate to higher salaries for technicians. Additionally, dealerships that specialize in high-end vehicles may pay more than those that service more affordable models.

Experience and Performance Can Also Affect Earnings

Of course, a service technician's level of experience and performance can also play a role in their earning potential. Technicians who have been working in the industry for a longer time and have a proven track record of success may be able to command higher salaries. Additionally, those who consistently exceed performance standards and meet targets may be eligible for bonuses or commissions.

How to Negotiate a Higher Salary as a Service Technician

If you're looking to earn a higher salary as a service technician at a dealership, there are a few strategies you can use. First, make sure you have the necessary certifications and experience to command a higher salary. Additionally, consider negotiating with your employer for a higher starting salary or bonuses based on performance. Finally, keep an eye out for opportunities to move up within the company, such as becoming a lead technician or service manager.

Question and Answer

Q: Can service technicians at dealerships earn commissions or bonuses?

A: Yes, some technicians may be eligible for bonuses or commissions based on their performance.

Q: Does a dealership's location affect a service technician's salary?

A: Yes, dealerships located in wealthier areas may be able to charge more for services, which can translate to higher salaries for technicians.

Q: What certifications can service technicians pursue at a dealership?

A: Dealerships may offer certifications in areas such as engine repair, emissions systems, and electrical systems.

Q: How can service technicians negotiate for a higher salary?

A: Technicians can negotiate for a higher starting salary or bonuses based on performance, and can also look for opportunities to move up within the company.

Conclusion of How Much Does a Service Technician Make at a Dealership

Overall, service technicians at dealerships can earn a median salary of around $44,050 per year, although this figure can vary widely depending on factors such as location, experience, and certification. By pursuing certifications, negotiating for higher salaries, and keeping an eye out for advancement opportunities, technicians can increase their earning potential and build rewarding careers in the automotive industry.
