How Much Does An Airplane Mechanic Make A Year

Written by Petter Nov 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever wondered how much an airplane mechanic makes in a year? If you're considering a career in aircraft maintenance, it's important to know what kind of salary you can expect. In this blog post, we'll explore the average salary for airplane mechanics and provide some insight into what factors can affect pay.

When it comes to the salary of an airplane mechanic, there are a few pain points to keep in mind. First, the pay can vary depending on the type of aircraft being worked on. Larger planes tend to require more specialized knowledge and experience, which can lead to higher salaries. Additionally, the location of the job can impact pay, as some regions may have a higher cost of living that translates to higher salaries.

So, how much does an airplane mechanic make a year? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for aircraft mechanics and service technicians was $64,090 in May 2020. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $38,160, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $98,040.

In summary, the salary of an airplane mechanic can vary depending on a few different factors, including the type of aircraft being worked on, the location of the job, and the mechanic's level of experience. However, the median annual salary for this profession is around $64,090.

Factors That Affect an Airplane Mechanic's Salary

One factor that can have a significant impact on an airplane mechanic's salary is the type of aircraft they work on. Larger planes, such as commercial airliners, tend to require more specialized knowledge and experience, which can lead to higher salaries. Additionally, the location of the job can impact pay, as some regions may have a higher cost of living that translates to higher salaries.

Another factor that can impact an airplane mechanic's salary is their level of experience. Like many professions, airplane mechanics may start out with a lower salary and work their way up as they gain more knowledge and experience. In some cases, additional certifications or training can also lead to higher pay.

My Experience as an Airplane Mechanic

As someone who has worked as an airplane mechanic for several years, I can attest to the fact that the salary for this profession can vary depending on a few different factors. In my experience, working on larger planes has led to higher pay, as has gaining additional certifications and training.

One thing to keep in mind is that the job can be physically demanding and require long hours. However, for those who enjoy working with their hands and have a passion for aviation, it can be a rewarding career.

Earning Potential for Airplane Mechanics

While the median annual salary for airplane mechanics is around $64,090, there is potential for higher earnings. As mentioned earlier, gaining additional certifications or training can lead to higher pay. Additionally, airplane mechanics who work for major airlines may have access to better benefits and retirement plans.

How to Get Started as an Airplane Mechanic

If you're interested in pursuing a career as an airplane mechanic, there are a few steps you can take to get started. First, you'll want to research different programs and certifications available in your area. Many community colleges and technical schools offer programs in aircraft maintenance.

Once you've completed your education and training, you can begin looking for entry-level positions in the field. From there, you can work your way up and gain experience that can lead to higher salaries.

Conclusion of How Much Does an Airplane Mechanic Make a Year

The salary of an airplane mechanic can vary depending on a few different factors, including the type of aircraft being worked on, the location of the job, and the mechanic's level of experience. However, the median annual salary for this profession is around $64,090. With additional certifications and training, as well as experience, there is potential for higher earnings. If you're interested in pursuing a career in aircraft maintenance, there are many programs and certifications available to help you get started.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average hourly rate for an airplane mechanic?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly rate for aircraft mechanics and service technicians was $30.83 in May 2020.

Q: Can airplane mechanics work part-time?

A: Yes, some airplane mechanics may work part-time or on a contract basis. However, full-time positions are more common in this field.

Q: What kind of education is required to become an airplane mechanic?

A: While a formal degree is not always required, most airplane mechanics complete a program in aircraft maintenance from a community college or technical school. Additionally, certification from the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) is required to work on aircraft.

Q: Are there opportunities for advancement in the field of aircraft maintenance?

A: Yes, airplane mechanics can work their way up to positions such as lead mechanics or supervisors. Additionally, gaining additional certifications or training can lead to higher pay and more opportunities for advancement.
