How Much Does An Airplane Mechanic Make Per Year

Written by Oliver Oct 24, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you interested in pursuing a career as an airplane mechanic? One of the most common questions people have about this career path is how much money they can expect to make. After all, salary is an important factor to consider when choosing a profession. In this blog post, we'll explore how much airplane mechanics typically earn and what factors can impact their income.

It's no secret that working in aviation can be challenging, and this is reflected in the salaries of airplane mechanics. While the exact amount of money you can expect to make can vary depending on several factors, the pay for this profession tends to be above average. However, the job can be physically demanding and may require working long hours, which can be a drawback for some people.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for aircraft mechanics and service technicians was $64,090 in May 2020. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $38,350, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $98,040. Factors that can impact an airplane mechanic's salary include their level of experience, the type of aircraft they work on, and the location of their employer.

In summary, airplane mechanics can earn a good salary, but the job can be physically demanding and may require working long hours. The median annual salary for this profession is $64,090, but this can vary depending on several factors.

What Influences an Airplane Mechanic's Salary?

One of the biggest factors that can influence an airplane mechanic's salary is their level of experience. Like many professions, airplane mechanics typically earn more money as they gain more experience and become more skilled at their job. Additionally, the type of aircraft an airplane mechanic works on can impact their salary. Some mechanics specialize in certain types of aircraft, such as commercial airliners, while others work on smaller planes. The location of an airplane mechanic's employer can also impact their salary, as some areas of the country pay more than others.

My Personal Experience as an Airplane Mechanic

As a former airplane mechanic, I can attest to the fact that this job can be physically demanding and requires long hours. However, the pay was good and I found the work to be rewarding. One thing to keep in mind is that the job can be stressful, as you are responsible for ensuring the safety of passengers and crew members. Overall, I found the job to be a good fit for me.

How to Become an Airplane Mechanic

If you're interested in becoming an airplane mechanic, there are several paths you can take. One option is to attend a Federal Aviation Administration (FAA)-approved aviation maintenance technician school. These programs typically take between 18 months and two years to complete and can prepare you for the FAA certification exams. Another option is to gain experience through an apprenticeship program or on-the-job training. Regardless of the path you choose, it's important to have a strong understanding of aviation mechanics and safety procedures.

Salary Expectations for Airplane Mechanics

As we mentioned earlier, the median annual salary for airplane mechanics and service technicians is $64,090. However, this can vary depending on several factors. For example, airplane mechanics who work on commercial airliners may earn more than those who work on smaller planes. Additionally, mechanics who work for larger airlines or companies may earn more than those who work for smaller ones. The location of an airplane mechanic's employer can also impact their salary.

Question and Answer

Q: How much do entry-level airplane mechanics typically earn?

A: Entry-level airplane mechanics typically earn less than more experienced mechanics. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the lowest 10 percent of airplane mechanics earned less than $38,350 in May 2020.

Q: Do airplane mechanics have opportunities for advancement?

A: Yes, there are opportunities for advancement in this field. For example, airplane mechanics can become supervisors or managers, or they can specialize in certain types of aircraft. Additionally, some mechanics may choose to become pilots or pursue other careers in aviation.

Q: Is it necessary to have a college degree to become an airplane mechanic?

A: No, a college degree is not required to become an airplane mechanic. However, attending an FAA-approved aviation maintenance technician school or completing an apprenticeship program can be helpful in gaining the necessary skills and knowledge for the job.

Q: Are there any certifications or licenses required to become an airplane mechanic?

A: Yes, airplane mechanics must be certified by the FAA. To become certified, mechanics must have a certain amount of experience or complete an FAA-approved aviation maintenance technician school program. Additionally, mechanics must pass a series of exams to demonstrate their knowledge and skills.

Conclusion of How Much Does an Airplane Mechanic Make Per Year

In conclusion, airplane mechanics can earn a good salary, but the job can be physically demanding and may require working long hours. The median annual salary for this profession is $64,090, but this can vary depending on several factors, including experience level, the type of aircraft worked on, and the location of the employer. If you're interested in pursuing a career as an airplane mechanic, there are several paths you can take, including attending an FAA-approved aviation maintenance technician school or gaining experience through an apprenticeship program or on-the-job training.
