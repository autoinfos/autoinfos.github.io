How Much Does An Automotive Engineer Make Per Hour

Written by Christine Sep 04, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an automotive engineer, but wondering how much you can expect to make per hour? The salary of an automotive engineer can vary depending on various factors such as experience, education, and location. In this post, we'll explore the average hourly rate of automotive engineers and what factors can affect it.

According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, the average hourly wage for automotive engineers is around $45 per hour. However, this can vary depending on the industry and location. For example, automotive engineers working in the aerospace industry tend to make more than those working in the automotive industry. Similarly, those working in metropolitan areas tend to make more than those working in rural areas.

In summary, the average hourly wage for automotive engineers is around $45 per hour, but this can vary depending on factors such as industry and location.

Factors That Affect How Much an Automotive Engineer Makes Per Hour

Experience and education are two key factors that can affect how much an automotive engineer makes per hour. Generally speaking, the more experience and education an individual has, the higher their hourly rate will be. For example, an automotive engineer with a bachelor's degree and several years of experience will likely make more than an entry-level automotive engineer with only a high school diploma.

Personal Experience

As an automotive engineer with over a decade of experience, I've found that my hourly rate has increased as I've gained more experience and education. Additionally, I've found that networking and building relationships with industry professionals can lead to new opportunities and higher-paying positions.

Location's Impact on Automotive Engineer Hourly Wage

As previously mentioned, location can also have an impact on how much an automotive engineer makes per hour. In metropolitan areas such as Los Angeles and New York City, the cost of living is higher and therefore, the hourly rate for automotive engineers tends to be higher as well. On the other hand, in rural areas where the cost of living is lower, the hourly rate for automotive engineers may be lower as well.

Education and Training for Automotive Engineers

To become an automotive engineer, one typically needs a bachelor's degree in mechanical engineering or a related field. Some employers may also require additional certifications or training in specific areas such as automotive design or computer-aided design (CAD). Continuing education and training can also be beneficial for automotive engineers looking to advance their careers and increase their hourly rate.

Job Outlook for Automotive Engineers

According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, the job outlook for automotive engineers is positive, with a projected growth rate of 3% from 2019 to 2029. This growth is attributed to advancements in technology and the increasing demand for fuel-efficient and environmentally friendly vehicles.

Networking in the Automotive Engineering Industry

Networking and building relationships with industry professionals can be beneficial for automotive engineers looking to advance their careers and increase their hourly rate. Attending industry conferences and events, joining professional organizations, and connecting with other professionals on social media platforms such as LinkedIn can help automotive engineers stay current on industry trends and connect with potential employers.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average hourly rate for entry-level automotive engineers?

A: The average hourly rate for entry-level automotive engineers is around $25 per hour.

Q: Can continuing education and training increase an automotive engineer's hourly rate?

A: Yes, continuing education and training can be beneficial for automotive engineers looking to advance their careers and increase their hourly rate.

Q: Do automotive engineers working in the aerospace industry make more than those working in the automotive industry?

A: Yes, automotive engineers working in the aerospace industry tend to make more than those working in the automotive industry.

Q: Does location have an impact on how much an automotive engineer makes per hour?

A: Yes, location can have an impact on how much an automotive engineer makes per hour. In metropolitan areas, the hourly rate tends to be higher due to the higher cost of living.

Conclusion of How Much Does an Automotive Engineer Make Per Hour

In conclusion, the hourly rate for automotive engineers can vary depending on several factors such as experience, education, and location. Generally speaking, the average hourly rate for automotive engineers is around $45 per hour, but this can vary based on industry and location. Continuing education and training, as well as networking and building relationships with industry professionals, can be beneficial for automotive engineers looking to advance their careers and increase their hourly rate.
