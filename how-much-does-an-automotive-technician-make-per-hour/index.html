How Much Does An Automotive Technician Make Per Hour

Written by Petter Oct 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an automotive technician? One of the most important factors to consider when choosing a career is how much money you can make. It's important to know the earning potential of your desired profession before investing time and money into education and training.

When it comes to how much an automotive technician makes per hour, there are several factors that can impact their salary. Location, experience, and education are all important factors that can affect earning potential. In addition, the type of employer, such as a dealership or independent repair shop, can also impact pay.

The average hourly wage for an automotive technician in the United States is $20.65 per hour, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. However, experienced technicians with specialized skills and certifications can earn significantly more.

In summary, the average hourly wage for an automotive technician is $20.65 per hour, but this can vary based on location, experience, education, and type of employer. Specialized skills and certifications can also increase earning potential.

What Factors Affect an Automotive Technician's Hourly Wage?

During my time working as an automotive technician, I've learned that there are several factors that can impact an automotive technician's hourly wage. One of the most important factors is location. Technicians who work in metropolitan areas or high-cost-of-living states may earn more than those in rural areas.

Experience and education are also important factors. Technicians with more experience and advanced education, such as an associate's degree in automotive technology, may earn more than those with less experience and education.

The type of employer can also impact an automotive technician's hourly wage. Technicians who work for dealerships may earn more than those who work for independent repair shops.

How Can an Automotive Technician Increase Their Hourly Wage?

If you're an automotive technician looking to increase your hourly wage, there are several steps you can take. First, consider obtaining specialized certifications, such as those offered by the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE). These certifications can demonstrate your expertise in specific areas of automotive repair and can increase your earning potential.

Continuing education is also important. Staying up-to-date on the latest automotive technologies and repair techniques can make you a more valuable employee and increase your earning potential.

Finally, consider seeking employment with a larger dealership or chain. These employers may offer higher salaries and better benefits than smaller independent repair shops.

What is the Job Outlook for Automotive Technicians?

The job outlook for automotive technicians is positive. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of automotive service technicians and mechanics is projected to grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029, about as fast as the average for all occupations.

This growth is due in part to the increasing complexity of automotive systems, which requires more specialized skills and training. In addition, the aging of the vehicle fleet in the United States is expected to increase demand for automotive technicians.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average starting hourly wage for an automotive technician?

A: The average starting hourly wage for an automotive technician is around $12 to $15 per hour.

Q: How much can an experienced automotive technician earn per hour?

A: Experienced automotive technicians can earn upwards of $30 per hour, depending on location and employer.

Q: What is the highest-paying type of employer for an automotive technician?

A: Dealerships generally offer higher salaries and better benefits than independent repair shops.

Q: What is the job outlook for automotive technicians?

A: The job outlook for automotive technicians is positive, with projected growth of 4 percent from 2019 to 2029.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the average hourly wage for an automotive technician is $20.65 per hour, but this can vary based on location, experience, education, and type of employer. Specialized skills and certifications can also increase earning potential. Automotive technicians can increase their hourly wage by obtaining specialized certifications, continuing education, and seeking employment with larger dealerships or chains.
