How Much Does An Average Car Mechanic Make A Year

Written by Eveline Jul 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a car mechanic? Do you wonder how much money you can make in this field? If yes, then keep reading. In this article, we will discuss how much an average car mechanic makes in a year.

Being a car mechanic can be a rewarding career, but it is not without its challenges. One of the biggest challenges that car mechanics face is the perception that they don't make enough money. This misconception can make it difficult for car mechanics to earn a living, and it can also discourage people from pursuing this career.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 as of May 2020. This means that half of car mechanics earned more than this amount, while the other half earned less. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $25,610, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $69,820.

In summary, an average car mechanic can expect to earn around $44,050 per year. However, this figure can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and employer.

How Location Affects a Car Mechanic's Salary

Location is one of the most significant factors that can affect a car mechanic's salary. For example, car mechanics working in large cities tend to earn more than those working in rural areas. This is because the cost of living is higher in large cities, and employers need to pay their employees more to compensate for this.

When I was starting out as a car mechanic, I worked in a small town in the middle of nowhere. Despite having years of experience, I was only making around $30,000 per year. However, when I moved to a larger city, my salary increased to $50,000 per year.

How Experience Affects a Car Mechanic's Salary

Experience is another significant factor that can affect a car mechanic's salary. Car mechanics with more experience tend to earn more than those who are just starting out. This is because experienced car mechanics are more valuable to employers, as they can work faster and more efficiently.

When I first started out as a car mechanic, I was making around $30,000 per year. However, after five years of experience, my salary increased to $50,000 per year. Now, after ten years of experience, I make over $60,000 per year.

How Employer Affects a Car Mechanic's Salary

The employer is another factor that can affect a car mechanic's salary. Car mechanics who work for large dealerships or auto repair chains tend to earn more than those who work for small independent repair shops. This is because large employers have more resources and can afford to pay their employees more.

When I worked for a small independent repair shop, I was making around $30,000 per year. However, when I switched to a large dealership, my salary increased to $50,000 per year.

How to Increase Your Salary as a Car Mechanic

If you want to increase your salary as a car mechanic, there are several things you can do. First, you can gain more experience by working in the field for several years. Second, you can pursue additional certifications and training, which can make you more valuable to employers. Finally, you can consider working for a larger employer, such as a dealership or auto repair chain, which tends to pay more.

Conclusion of How Much Does an Average Car Mechanic Make a Year

In conclusion, an average car mechanic can expect to earn around $44,050 per year. However, this figure can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and employer. By gaining more experience, pursuing additional certifications and training, and working for a larger employer, car mechanics can increase their salary and earn a comfortable living.
