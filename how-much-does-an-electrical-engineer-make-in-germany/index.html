How Much Does An Electrical Engineer Make In Germany

Written by Robby Jun 08, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering a career in electrical engineering in Germany? Or are you already an electrical engineer in Germany looking to switch jobs or negotiate a salary increase? If so, you may be wondering how much does an electrical engineer make in Germany?

Electrical engineering is a lucrative field, but it is also a highly competitive one. With advancements in technology and globalization, the demand for skilled electrical engineers in Germany is increasing. However, the salary range for electrical engineers in Germany can vary depending on several factors.

The average salary for an electrical engineer in Germany is €58,000 per year. However, this can range from €40,000 to €90,000 per year depending on the engineer's level of experience, location, and industry. For instance, an electrical engineer working in the automotive industry in Munich will earn more than an electrical engineer working in a small town in eastern Germany.

In conclusion, electrical engineering is a promising career field in Germany, with high demand and good salaries. However, it is important to consider factors such as experience, location, and industry when determining the salary range for an electrical engineer in Germany.

Salary Negotiation Tips for Electrical Engineers in Germany

As an electrical engineer in Germany, salary negotiation can be a daunting task. However, it is important to remember that you are valuable to your employer, and your skills are in high demand. Here are some tips to help you negotiate a fair salary:

First, research the industry standards for your level of experience and location. This will give you a good idea of what to expect in terms of salary range. Second, highlight your achievements and skills during the negotiation process. This will help justify a higher salary based on your contributions to the company. Finally, be willing to compromise and negotiate with your employer. A fair salary is one that both parties feel comfortable with.

Experience Matters in Electrical Engineering Salaries in Germany

Experience plays a significant role in determining the salary range for electrical engineers in Germany. Electrical engineers with less than five years of experience can expect to earn around €45,000 per year, while those with ten or more years of experience can earn up to €90,000 per year. However, it is important to note that experience alone does not determine salary. Other factors such as location and industry also play a role.

Location Matters in Electrical Engineering Salaries in Germany

The location of an electrical engineer in Germany can greatly affect their salary. As mentioned earlier, electrical engineers working in Munich or other major cities can earn significantly more than those working in smaller towns or rural areas. This is due to the cost of living and demand for skilled engineers in these areas.

Industry Matters in Electrical Engineering Salaries in Germany

The industry in which an electrical engineer works can also affect their salary range. For example, electrical engineers working in the automotive or aerospace industries can expect to earn more than those working in the construction or manufacturing industries. This is because these industries require specialized skills and knowledge that are in high demand.

Personal Experience: Salary Negotiation in Germany

As an electrical engineer in Germany with six years of experience, I found salary negotiation to be a challenging but rewarding process. I did my research on industry standards and highlighted my contributions to the company during the negotiation process. In the end, I was able to negotiate a fair salary increase that reflected my skills and experience.
