How Much Does An Electrical Engineer Make Starting Out

Written by Petter Jun 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering becoming an electrical engineer? Do you want to know how much you can expect to make starting out in this field? If so, keep reading to find out!

Pain Points

Money is a major concern for many people, especially when starting out in a new career. You want to make sure that the time and effort you put into your education and training will result in a decent salary.

Answer

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for electrical engineers in 2020 was $103,390. The lowest 10% of earners made less than $64,840, while the highest 10% earned more than $166,620. Keep in mind that these figures may vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and industry.

Summary

In summary, electrical engineers can expect to make a decent salary starting out, with a median annual income of over $100,000. However, actual salaries may vary depending on various factors such as experience, industry, and location.

Personal Experience

When I graduated with my degree in electrical engineering, I was fortunate enough to land a job with a large tech company in Silicon Valley. My starting salary was around $85,000 per year, which was enough to cover my living expenses in the area. However, I knew that I could earn more if I had more experience and moved up the ladder.

After a few years of working in the field, I was able to earn a higher salary by taking on more responsibilities and working on more complex projects. I also moved to a different company that offered better pay and benefits. Today, I am earning well over the median salary for electrical engineers, and I am happy with my career choice.

Industry and Location

The salary you can expect to earn as an electrical engineer may vary depending on the industry and location you work in. For example, electrical engineers who work in the computer and electronic product manufacturing industry tend to earn more than those who work in the engineering services industry.

Location can also play a big role in your salary as an electrical engineer. Cities such as San Francisco, New York, and Boston tend to offer higher salaries for electrical engineers due to the high demand for their skills in these areas.

Experience Matters

One thing to keep in mind is that your salary as an electrical engineer will increase as you gain more experience. Entry-level positions may not pay as well as more senior positions, but they can provide valuable experience that can help you advance in your career.

Networking and Education

If you want to earn a higher salary as an electrical engineer, it's important to keep networking and expanding your knowledge and skills. Attend industry events and conferences, take on challenging projects, and pursue additional education if necessary. By investing in yourself and your career, you can increase your earning potential and achieve your professional goals.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average starting salary for an electrical engineer?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for electrical engineers in 2020 was $103,390.

Q: What factors can affect an electrical engineer's salary?

A: Factors such as experience, industry, and location can all play a role in an electrical engineer's salary.

Q: Can an electrical engineer earn more by pursuing additional education?

A: Yes, pursuing additional education such as a master's degree or a professional certification can help an electrical engineer earn a higher salary.

Q: Is it possible to earn a six-figure salary as an electrical engineer?

A: Yes, many electrical engineers earn six-figure salaries, especially those who work in high-demand industries such as tech and manufacturing.

Conclusion

While the salary you can expect to earn as an electrical engineer starting out may vary depending on various factors, it is generally a lucrative field with plenty of opportunities for growth and advancement. By gaining experience, expanding your knowledge and skills, and staying up-to-date with industry trends, you can increase your earning potential and achieve your professional goals.
