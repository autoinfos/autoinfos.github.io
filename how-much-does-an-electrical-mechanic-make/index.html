How Much Does An Electrical Mechanic Make

Written by Frank Aug 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an electrical mechanic, but wondering about the potential salary? You're not alone. Many people want to know how much they can expect to earn before committing to a new profession. In this article, we'll explore the average salary for electrical mechanics and what factors can impact your earning potential.

As with any job, there are both positives and negatives to being an electrical mechanic. On one hand, it can be a fulfilling career that involves problem-solving and hands-on work. However, it can also be physically demanding, and the pay may not always be commensurate with the level of skill required. This is why it's important to carefully consider your options and do your research before deciding on this career path.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for electrical mechanics was $63,340 as of May 2020. This means that half of all electrical mechanics earned more than this amount, while the other half earned less. Additionally, the lowest 10 percent of earners in this field made less than $38,110 per year, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $100,690.

In order to maximize your earning potential as an electrical mechanic, there are several things you can do. First, consider getting certified by a professional organization like the National Institute for Certification in Engineering Technologies (NICET). This can demonstrate to potential employers that you have the necessary skills and knowledge to excel in this field. Additionally, gaining experience in a specific niche or specialization, such as industrial automation or renewable energy, can make you a more valuable employee.

Personal Experience

When I first started working as an electrical mechanic, I was earning around $50,000 per year. However, I quickly realized that I needed to expand my skill set in order to increase my earning potential. I decided to get certified by NICET, which helped me land a job at a larger company with higher pay. Now, I'm earning over $80,000 per year and have opportunities for advancement within the company.

Factors That Affect Salary

There are several factors that can impact how much an electrical mechanic makes. One of the most significant is location. Electrical mechanics in areas with high costs of living, such as California or New York, can expect to earn more than those in lower-cost regions. Additionally, the industry in which you work can affect your earning potential. For example, electrical mechanics who work in manufacturing or construction tend to make more than those in residential or commercial settings.

Certifications and Experience

Another important factor is your level of experience and any certifications you hold. As mentioned earlier, getting certified by a professional organization can significantly increase your earning potential. Additionally, having several years of experience in the field can make you a more attractive candidate to potential employers.

Negotiation Skills

Your negotiation skills can also impact your salary as an electrical mechanic. When discussing pay with a potential employer, be sure to do your research on industry standards and come prepared with specific examples of your accomplishments and contributions to previous companies. This can help you make a compelling case for a higher salary.

Personal Experience

When I was first offered a job as an electrical mechanic, the salary they offered was lower than what I was hoping for. However, I was able to negotiate a higher salary by coming prepared with data on industry standards and highlighting my previous work experience. It's important to remember that negotiation is a normal part of the hiring process, and you shouldn't be afraid to ask for what you're worth.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average hourly wage for electrical mechanics?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the average hourly wage for electrical mechanics is $30.46 as of May 2020.

Q: How much do electrical mechanics with certification earn compared to those without?

A: According to a survey by the National Society of Professional Engineers, electrical mechanics with certification earn an average of $8,000 more per year than those without.

Q: Are there any benefits to working as an electrical mechanic?

A: Yes, many electrical mechanics enjoy the problem-solving aspect of their job and the satisfaction of completing complex projects. Additionally, there is often room for advancement within the industry.

Q: What kind of education is required to become an electrical mechanic?

A: Typically, electrical mechanics need to have a high school diploma or equivalent, as well as some postsecondary education in electrical systems or a related field.

Conclusion

Overall, the salary for electrical mechanics can vary widely depending on several factors, including location, industry, certifications, and negotiation skills. By doing your research, gaining experience, and pursuing certification, you can increase your earning potential in this field. However, it's important to remember that this is a physically demanding job that requires a high level of skill and expertise. If you're passionate about electrical systems and enjoy hands-on work, then a career as an electrical mechanic could be a great choice for you.
