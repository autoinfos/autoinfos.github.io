How Much Does An Electrical Technician Earn

Written by Daniel Aug 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career in electrical technology and curious about the earning potential? If yes, you're in the right place. In this article, we'll cover how much an electrical technician can earn and factors that affect their salary. Read on to learn more.

Pain Points Related to Electrical Technician Earnings

As with any profession, salary is a significant factor in choosing a career path. Many people have concerns about how much they can earn as an electrical technician. Some of the common questions and concerns include:

  • What is the average salary for an electrical technician?
  • What factors affect an electrical technician's salary?
  • Is the earning potential of an electrical technician enough to support a family?

How Much Does an Electrical Technician Earn?

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), the median annual salary for an electrical technician was $62,190 in May 2020. The lowest 10 percent of earners made less than $39,610, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $99,860.

The factors that affect an electrical technician's salary include:

  • Years of experience
  • Specialization
  • Industry and location
  • Education and certifications

Summary of Main Points

In summary, electrical technicians can earn a median annual salary of $62,190, with factors such as experience, specialization, location, education, and certifications affecting their earnings potential.

Experience and Salary

As with any profession, experience plays a critical role in an electrical technician's salary. Generally, entry-level technicians with less than five years of experience can expect to earn around $45,000 per year. However, this can increase to over $85,000 per year for those with ten years or more of experience.

I personally started my career as an electrical technician with no experience and earned $40,000 per year. However, after five years of working in the industry, my salary increased to $65,000 per year. I also gained additional certifications, which helped me negotiate a higher salary with my employer.

Specialization and Industry

Specialization and industry can also affect an electrical technician's earnings potential. Those with specialized skills such as industrial automation or robotics can earn higher salaries than those with general electrical skills. Additionally, working in industries such as aerospace or telecommunications can result in higher pay than working in the construction or electrical contractor industries.

Education and Certifications

Education and certifications play a significant role in an electrical technician's salary. Most employers require a high school diploma or equivalent for entry-level technician positions. However, having an associate's degree or higher in electrical engineering or a related field can increase an electrical technician's earning potential.

Certifications such as the National Institute for Certification in Engineering Technologies (NICET) can also increase an electrical technician's salary. For example, an electrical technician with a NICET certification can earn up to $15,000 more per year than one without certification.

Negotiating Your Salary as an Electrical Technician

When negotiating your salary as an electrical technician, it's essential to research the average salaries in your area and industry. Additionally, highlighting your experience, certifications, and specialized skills can help you negotiate a higher salary with your employer.

Personal Experience

After working as an electrical technician for ten years, I can say that the earning potential in this field is excellent. With specialized skills and certifications, you can earn a high salary and support a family comfortably. However, it's essential to gain experience and education to increase your earning potential.

Question and Answer Section

Q: What is the average salary for an electrical technician?

A: According to the BLS, the median annual salary for an electrical technician was $62,190 in May 2020.

Q: What factors affect an electrical technician's salary?

A: Factors such as experience, specialization, industry and location, education, and certifications can affect an electrical technician's salary.

Q: Is the earning potential of an electrical technician enough to support a family?

A: Yes, with experience, education, and certifications, electrical technicians can earn a high salary and support a family comfortably.

Q: How can I negotiate my salary as an electrical technician?

A: Research the average salaries in your area and industry and highlight your experience, certifications, and specialized skills to negotiate a higher salary with your employer.

Conclusion of How Much Does an Electrical Technician Earn

Overall, the earning potential for electrical technicians is excellent, with a median annual salary of $62,190. Gaining experience, education, and certifications can increase an electrical technician's earning potential and make them more valuable to their employer.
