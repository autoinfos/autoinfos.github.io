How Much Does An Electrical Technician Make

Written by Daniel Jun 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you thinking about becoming an electrical technician but wondering how much you can make in this field? If so, you're not alone. Many people are interested in the salary potential for this type of job.

As an electrical technician, you may face some pain points related to your salary. It can be frustrating to work hard every day and still struggle to make ends meet. You may feel like you're not being paid what you're worth, or that you're not getting the recognition you deserve for your hard work.

So, how much does an electrical technician make? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for electrical and electronics installers and repairers was $57,890 as of May 2020. This means that half of all workers in this field earned more than that amount, and half earned less.

In summary, becoming an electrical technician can be a lucrative career choice. With a median annual wage of $57,890, you can earn a decent living in this field. But there are also some factors that can affect your salary, such as your experience level, location, and industry.

Factors That Affect an Electrical Technician's Salary

One of the biggest factors that can affect an electrical technician's salary is their experience level. If you're just starting out in this field, you may earn less than someone who has been working as an electrical technician for several years. However, as you gain more experience and knowledge, you can expect your salary to increase.

Another factor that can affect your salary is your location. Electrical technicians in certain parts of the country may earn more than those in other areas, depending on the demand for their services. For example, if you live in a large city with a high cost of living, you may be able to earn more money than if you live in a smaller town.

My Personal Experience as an Electrical Technician

When I first started working as an electrical technician, I was earning around $40,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and became more skilled in my job, my salary increased to around $60,000 per year.

One thing that I found helpful was to stay up-to-date with the latest technology and trends in my field. This allowed me to take on more complex projects and offer more specialized services, which in turn helped me to earn a higher salary.

Job Outlook for Electrical Technicians

The job outlook for electrical technicians is fairly positive. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of electrical and electronics installers and repairers is projected to grow 2 percent from 2019 to 2029, which is about as fast as the average for all occupations.

One reason for this growth is the increasing demand for renewable energy sources, such as solar and wind power. As more homes and businesses switch to these types of energy sources, there will be a greater need for electrical technicians to install and maintain the necessary equipment.

Education and Training for Electrical Technicians

In order to become an electrical technician, you typically need a high school diploma or equivalent. You may also need to complete a formal training program or apprenticeship, depending on the requirements in your state or employer.

During these programs, you'll learn about electrical systems, safety procedures, and other important skills for your job. You may also have the opportunity to gain hands-on experience through internships or on-the-job training.

How to Find a Job as an Electrical Technician

If you're interested in becoming an electrical technician, there are several ways to find job opportunities in this field. One option is to search online job boards, such as Indeed or Monster, for openings in your area.

You may also want to consider networking with other professionals in your field, such as through industry events or online forums. This can help you learn about job opportunities and get tips on how to succeed in your job search.

Conclusion of How Much Does an Electrical Technician Make

Overall, becoming an electrical technician can be a rewarding and lucrative career choice. With a median annual wage of $57,890 and positive job growth projected in the coming years, this field offers many opportunities for those who are willing to put in the hard work and dedication.
