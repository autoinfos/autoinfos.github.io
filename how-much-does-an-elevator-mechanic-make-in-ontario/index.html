How Much Does An Elevator Mechanic Make In Ontario

Written by William Oct 31, 2023 · 4 min read

Have you ever wondered how much an elevator mechanic makes in Ontario? If you are interested in pursuing a career in this field or are simply curious about the earning potential, this post is for you.

Elevator mechanics are responsible for installing, maintaining, and repairing elevators, escalators, and other types of lifts. While this job can be rewarding, it can also be physically demanding and requires a high level of technical knowledge and skill. As such, it's important to know what kind of salary you can expect if you're considering becoming an elevator mechanic in Ontario.

The median hourly wage for elevator mechanics in Ontario is around $39 per hour. This translates to an annual salary of approximately $80,000. However, salaries can vary depending on a number of factors, including experience, location, and type of employer.

In general, elevator mechanics who work for larger companies or on more complex projects tend to earn higher salaries. Additionally, those who have been in the field for several years and have obtained additional certifications or training may also command higher wages.

To summarize, elevator mechanics in Ontario can expect to earn a median salary of around $80,000 per year, although this can vary depending on factors such as experience, location, and type of employer.

Personal Experience

When I first started working as an elevator mechanic in Ontario, I was making around $35 per hour. However, as I gained more experience and took on more complex projects, my salary increased to around $45 per hour. I have also found that working for larger companies tends to come with higher salaries and more opportunities for advancement.

Certifications and Training

If you're interested in becoming an elevator mechanic in Ontario, there are a number of certifications and training programs you can pursue to increase your earning potential. For example, the Elevating Devices Mechanic (EDM) apprenticeship program is a four-year program that provides both on-the-job training and classroom instruction. Additionally, obtaining certifications from industry associations such as the National Elevator Industry Education Program (NEIEP) can also help to increase your earning potential.

Job Outlook

The job outlook for elevator mechanics in Ontario is positive, with a steady demand for qualified professionals in this field. As buildings become taller and more complex, the need for skilled elevator mechanics will only continue to grow. Additionally, advancements in elevator technology are creating new opportunities for those with specialized skills and knowledge.

Salary Negotiation Tips

If you're considering a job as an elevator mechanic in Ontario, it's important to know how to negotiate your salary. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

1. Research salaries for similar positions in your area and use this information to negotiate a fair wage.

2. Highlight your experience and any certifications or training you have obtained.

3. Be prepared to discuss your goals and how you plan to contribute to the company.

4. Don't be afraid to negotiate - employers expect it and may even respect you more for it.

Conclusion

In conclusion, elevator mechanics in Ontario can expect to earn a median salary of around $80,000 per year, although this can vary depending on a number of factors. By obtaining certifications and training, negotiating your salary, and gaining experience in the field, you can increase your earning potential as an elevator mechanic in Ontario.
