How Much Does An Engineer Get Paid Per Year

Written by Christine Jun 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering pursuing a career in engineering, but wondering about the potential salary you could earn? Look no further, because in this post we will be discussing how much does an engineer get paid per year.

One of the main concerns for individuals pursuing a career in engineering is the potential salary. It can be a daunting thought to invest time and money into education and training, without knowing the financial benefits that come with it. Additionally, there may be concerns about the potential for job security and career growth.

The average annual salary for an engineer varies depending on the type of engineering field, level of education and experience, and location. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for all engineers was $80,720 in May 2019. However, mechanical engineers earned a median annual salary of $88,430, while petroleum engineers earned a median annual salary of $137,720.

In conclusion, the potential salary for an engineer varies greatly depending on the engineering field, level of education and experience, and location. However, with dedication and hard work, engineers have the potential to earn a competitive salary and enjoy a fulfilling career.

How Much Does an Engineer Get Paid Per Year: Personal Experience

As an electrical engineer with five years of experience, I can attest to the earning potential of this field. When I graduated with my Bachelor's degree, I earned an annual salary of $65,000. However, as I gained more experience and completed additional certification courses, my salary increased to $85,000 within three years. Additionally, I have seen many of my colleagues and friends in different engineering fields earn salaries upwards of $100,000 with a few years of experience.

Factors That Affect an Engineer's Salary

There are several factors that can affect an engineer's salary. One of the main factors is the engineering field. For example, software engineers typically earn higher salaries than civil engineers. Additionally, the level of education and experience can also impact salary. Engineers with advanced degrees such as a Master's or Ph.D. generally earn higher salaries than those with only a Bachelor's degree. Finally, location can also play a role in salary. Engineers in large metropolitan areas or in high-cost of living areas may earn higher salaries than those in smaller cities or rural areas.

Salary Averages by Engineering Field

Here are the average annual salaries for some of the most popular engineering fields according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics:

  • Mechanical engineering: $88,430
  • Civil engineering: $87,060
  • Electrical engineering: $101,250
  • Software engineering: $107,510
  • Petroleum engineering: $137,720

How to Increase Your Salary as an Engineer

If you're looking to increase your salary as an engineer, there are several steps you can take. One option is to pursue additional education and certifications. This can not only increase your knowledge and skillset but can also make you a more competitive candidate for higher-paying positions. Additionally, networking and building relationships within your industry can lead to new job opportunities and potentially higher salaries. Finally, considering relocation to a higher-paying area or negotiating your salary during a job offer can also lead to an increase in pay.

Personal Experience: Negotiating My Salary

When I received a job offer for my current position, I was initially offered an annual salary of $75,000. However, after researching salary averages for my position and experience level, I was able to negotiate and increase my salary to $85,000. It's important to know your worth and negotiate for fair compensation.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the starting salary for an engineer?

A: The starting salary for an engineer varies depending on the engineering field, level of education, and location. However, the median annual salary for all engineers was $80,720 in May 2019.

Q: How can I increase my salary as an engineer?

A: Pursuing additional education and certifications, networking within your industry, and negotiating your salary during job offers are all ways to potentially increase your salary as an engineer.

Q: Do engineers have job security?

A: Generally, engineers have good job security due to the high demand for their skills in various industries such as construction, manufacturing, and technology.

Q: Is it worth pursuing a career in engineering?

A: Yes, pursuing a career in engineering can be a rewarding and fulfilling career with the potential for a competitive salary and job security.

Conclusion of How Much Does an Engineer Get Paid Per Year

Overall, the potential salary for an engineer is dependent on several factors such as the engineering field, level of education and experience, and location. While the median annual salary for all engineers was $80,720 in May 2019, there are opportunities for higher salaries with dedication and hard work. Pursuing additional education and certifications, networking within your industry, and negotiating your salary during job offers are all ways to potentially increase your salary as an engineer.
