How Much Does An Entry Level Pharmacy Technician Make

Written by Luffy Oct 18, 2023 · 4 min read

If you are considering a career as a pharmacy technician, you may be wondering how much an entry level pharmacy technician makes. Salary is an important factor when choosing a career, and it's important to have a clear idea of what you can expect to earn when starting out in this field.

Many people are attracted to the pharmacy technician profession because of the potential for good pay and job security. However, it's important to keep in mind that entry level pharmacy technician jobs may not pay as much as more experienced positions.

The average salary for an entry level pharmacy technician in the United States is around $30,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on factors such as location, employer, and level of experience. Some entry level pharmacy technicians may earn as little as $20,000 per year, while others may earn closer to $40,000.

In conclusion, an entry level pharmacy technician can expect to make around $30,000 per year on average. However, this can vary depending on a number of factors. It's important to research potential employers and locations to get a better idea of what you can expect to earn in this field.

What Determines an Entry Level Pharmacy Technician's Salary?

When it comes to determining an entry level pharmacy technician's salary, there are several factors that come into play. One of the most important factors is location. Pharmacy technicians who work in large cities or high cost of living areas may earn more than those who work in smaller towns or rural areas.

Another factor that can impact an entry level pharmacy technician's salary is their employer. Pharmacy technicians who work for large retail pharmacies or hospitals may earn more than those who work for smaller independent pharmacies.

In addition, level of experience can also play a role in an entry level pharmacy technician's salary. Those who have completed training programs or have previous pharmacy experience may be able to command higher salaries than those who are just starting out in the field.

The Importance of Continuing Education for Pharmacy Technicians

While an entry level pharmacy technician may not earn as much as more experienced technicians, there is still room for growth in this field. One way to increase earning potential is by pursuing additional education and certifications.

Many pharmacy technicians choose to pursue certification through organizations such as the Pharmacy Technician Certification Board or the National Healthcareer Association. These certifications can demonstrate a higher level of knowledge and expertise, which can lead to higher pay and more job opportunities.

Negotiating Salary as an Entry Level Pharmacy Technician

When starting out as an entry level pharmacy technician, it's important to research potential employers and understand the average salary for the position in your area. It's also important to be prepared to negotiate salary and benefits during the hiring process.

One way to negotiate salary is by highlighting any relevant education or certifications you have obtained. You can also negotiate for additional benefits such as paid time off, health insurance, or retirement savings plans.

Personal Experience: Earning Potential as an Entry Level Pharmacy Technician

When I started out as an entry level pharmacy technician, I was earning around $28,000 per year. However, after completing a certification program and gaining experience in the field, I was able to negotiate a higher salary and now earn closer to $35,000 per year.

Question and Answer

Q: Is there room for growth in the pharmacy technician field?

A: Yes, there is definitely room for growth in the pharmacy technician field. Pursuing additional education and certifications can lead to higher paying jobs and more opportunities for advancement.

Q: Do entry level pharmacy technicians receive benefits?

A: It depends on the employer. Many employers offer benefits such as health insurance, paid time off, and retirement savings plans to their employees.

Q: Can entry level pharmacy technicians negotiate their salary?

A: Yes, entry level pharmacy technicians can negotiate their salary during the hiring process. It's important to research potential employers and understand the average salary for the position in your area before negotiating.

Q: How can pharmacy technicians increase their earning potential?

A: Pharmacy technicians can increase their earning potential by pursuing additional education and certifications, gaining experience in the field, and negotiating salary and benefits during the hiring process.

Conclusion of How Much Does an Entry Level Pharmacy Technician Make

In conclusion, an entry level pharmacy technician can expect to make around $30,000 per year on average. However, this can vary depending on a number of factors such as location, employer, and level of experience. Pursuing additional education and certifications can lead to higher paying jobs and more opportunities for advancement in this field.
