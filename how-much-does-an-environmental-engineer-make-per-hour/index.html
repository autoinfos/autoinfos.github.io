How Much Does An Environmental Engineer Make Per Hour

Written by Christine Oct 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in a career as an environmental engineer, but curious about how much you can expect to make per hour? Look no further, because we've got the answer for you.

Many people are drawn to careers in environmental engineering because of their passion for sustainability and protecting the environment. However, it's important to also consider the financial aspects of the job. Knowing how much you can expect to make per hour can help you plan for your future and make informed decisions about your career path.

So, how much does an environmental engineer make per hour? According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for environmental engineers is $43.87. However, this can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and education level.

In addition to the median hourly wage, environmental engineers can also receive benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off. It's important to consider these factors in addition to the hourly wage when evaluating the overall compensation package for a job.

Personal Experience with Environmental Engineering Salaries

As someone who has worked in the environmental engineering field for several years, I can attest to the fact that salaries can vary widely depending on location and experience. In my first job as an environmental engineer in a rural area, I started out making around $25 per hour. However, after several years of experience and obtaining my Professional Engineer license, I was able to negotiate a salary of over $60 per hour at my next job in a major city.

One thing to note is that environmental engineering is a field that requires continuous learning and education. Obtaining advanced degrees or certifications can often lead to higher salaries and more opportunities for advancement.

Factors Affecting Environmental Engineering Salaries

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can affect how much an environmental engineer makes per hour. One of the biggest factors is location. Environmental engineers who work in major cities or in areas with a high cost of living can often command higher salaries than those in rural areas.

Experience is another factor that can greatly impact an environmental engineer's salary. Those who are just starting out in their careers may make less than those with several years of experience and a proven track record of success.

Finally, education level can also play a role in salary. Environmental engineers with advanced degrees or professional certifications may be able to command higher salaries and more opportunities for career advancement.

How to Negotiate a Higher Environmental Engineering Salary

If you're looking to negotiate a higher salary in your environmental engineering job, there are several steps you can take. First, do your research and find out what the average salary is for someone in your position and with your level of experience. This will give you a baseline for what you can expect to make.

Next, come prepared to the negotiation with a list of your accomplishments and successes in your current role. This can help demonstrate your value to the company and justify a higher salary.

Finally, be willing to negotiate and compromise. While it's important to advocate for your worth, it's also important to be realistic and consider the company's financial constraints.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the median hourly wage for environmental engineers?

A: The median hourly wage for environmental engineers is $43.87.

Q: What factors can impact an environmental engineer's salary?

A: Location, experience, and education level can all impact an environmental engineer's salary.

Q: How can I negotiate a higher environmental engineering salary?

A: Research the average salary for your position, come prepared with a list of accomplishments, and be willing to negotiate and compromise.

Q: Is there room for advancement in environmental engineering?

A: Yes, obtaining advanced degrees or certifications can lead to higher salaries and more opportunities for advancement in the field.

Conclusion of How Much Does an Environmental Engineer Make per Hour

As you can see, the hourly wage for environmental engineers can vary depending on a variety of factors. However, the median hourly wage of $43.87 is a good baseline to keep in mind when considering a career in this field. By researching salaries, negotiating effectively, and continuing to learn and grow in your role, you can set yourself up for success in the exciting and rewarding field of environmental engineering.
