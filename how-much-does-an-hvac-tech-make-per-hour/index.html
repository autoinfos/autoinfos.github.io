How Much Does An Hvac Tech Make Per Hour

Written by Oliver Sep 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering becoming an HVAC technician? Or are you already working in the industry and wondering if you're being paid fairly? Either way, you're probably curious about how much an HVAC tech makes per hour. It's an important question to ask, as your salary can greatly impact your quality of life and financial stability.

Without a doubt, HVAC technicians play an essential role in our society. They install, repair, and maintain heating, ventilation, and air conditioning systems in homes, buildings, and other structures. However, this job can come with its fair share of challenges and risks, including working in extreme temperatures, dealing with hazardous materials, and encountering dangerous situations. As such, HVAC technicians deserve to be compensated fairly for their hard work and expertise.

So, how much does an HVAC tech make per hour? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for HVAC technicians as of May 2020 was $24.43. This means that half of HVAC techs earned more than this amount, while the other half earned less. The lowest 10 percent of earners made less than $15.96 per hour, while the highest 10 percent made more than $38.78 per hour.

In addition to hourly wages, HVAC technicians may also receive benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off. The amount of these benefits can vary depending on the employer and the technician's level of experience.

The Importance of Experience in Determining HVAC Tech Salaries

While the median hourly wage for HVAC technicians is a helpful benchmark, it's important to note that salaries can vary widely based on factors such as experience, location, and industry. HVAC technicians with more experience and expertise are likely to earn higher wages than those who are just starting out.

For example, an entry-level HVAC technician may earn around $15 to $20 per hour, while a technician with several years of experience may earn $25 to $30 per hour or more. Additionally, HVAC technicians who work in specialized industries such as commercial refrigeration or industrial HVAC may earn higher salaries than those who work in residential settings.

The Benefits of Pursuing a Career as an HVAC Tech

Despite the challenges and risks that come with working as an HVAC technician, this career path can be highly rewarding for those who are interested in the field. HVAC technicians are in high demand, and the job market is expected to grow by 4 percent between 2019 and 2029. Additionally, HVAC technicians can enjoy job security, competitive salaries, and opportunities for career advancement.

How to Increase Your Earnings as an HVAC Tech

If you're currently working as an HVAC technician and you're looking to increase your earnings, there are several steps you can take. One option is to pursue additional training and certifications in specialized areas such as refrigeration, ductwork, or electrical work. This can help you become more knowledgeable and skilled in your field, which can lead to higher-paying job opportunities.

Another option is to seek out HVAC companies that offer competitive salaries and benefits packages. Do your research and compare salaries and benefits across different companies to ensure that you're being paid fairly for your skills and experience.

Conclusion of How Much Does an HVAC Tech Make Per Hour

Overall, HVAC technicians play a vital role in keeping our homes and buildings comfortable and safe. While salaries can vary based on various factors, the median hourly wage for HVAC technicians is $24.43 per hour. With the right training, experience, and employer, HVAC technicians can enjoy competitive salaries, job security, and opportunities for career advancement.
