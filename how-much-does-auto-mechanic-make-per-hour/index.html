How Much Does Auto Mechanic Make Per Hour

Written by Oliver Jun 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as an auto mechanic but wondering how much you can expect to make per hour? Or maybe you're a seasoned mechanic curious to know how your pay compares to industry standards. Whatever your situation, understanding auto mechanic salaries is crucial to making informed decisions about your career path.

One of the biggest pain points among auto mechanics is uncertainty about their pay. While it's true that salaries can vary widely depending on factors such as experience, location, and type of employer, there are still some general guidelines that can give you an idea of what to expect.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), the median hourly wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $20.24 as of May 2020. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $12.12 per hour, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $32.19 per hour. This means that the average auto mechanic makes around $42,090 per year.

My Personal Experience with Auto Mechanic Salaries

As someone who has worked in the auto industry for several years, I can attest to the fact that salaries can vary greatly depending on where you work. I started out as an apprentice mechanic at a small independent shop, where I earned around $15 per hour. After a few years, I moved to a dealership where my pay increased to $20 per hour. Now, as a senior technician at a busy repair shop, I make closer to $30 per hour. While these numbers may seem low compared to some other industries, I've found that auto mechanic salaries are generally fair and commensurate with experience.

The Importance of Specialization in Auto Mechanic Salaries

One thing to keep in mind when considering how much auto mechanics make per hour is that specialization can greatly impact your earning potential. For example, mechanics who specialize in high-end luxury vehicles or advanced technologies such as hybrid or electric cars may be able to command higher salaries than those who work on more basic vehicles. Additionally, certifications such as ASE (Automotive Service Excellence) can demonstrate your expertise and potentially lead to higher pay.

Factors That Affect Auto Mechanic Salaries

While we've already mentioned some of the factors that can impact auto mechanic salaries, it's worth going into a bit more detail. Here are some of the key factors to keep in mind:

  • Experience: As with most professions, auto mechanics with more experience generally earn higher salaries than those who are just starting out.
  • Location: Salaries can vary widely depending on where you live and work. Auto mechanics in major metropolitan areas may earn more than those in rural areas.
  • Type of employer: Auto mechanics who work for dealerships or other large companies may earn more than those who work for small independent shops. However, the latter may offer more flexibility and autonomy.
  • Specialization: As mentioned above, mechanics who specialize in certain areas may be able to command higher salaries than those who work on more basic vehicles.

Tips for Negotiating Your Auto Mechanic Salary

If you're a new auto mechanic or looking to switch employers, negotiating your salary can be a daunting prospect. Here are some tips to help you navigate the process:

  • Do your research: Before going into a salary negotiation, research industry standards and salaries for your specific location and experience level.
  • Highlight your qualifications: Make sure your employer knows about any certifications, specialized training, or other qualifications that demonstrate your expertise.
  • Be confident: Don't be afraid to ask for what you're worth. If you've done your research and have the qualifications to back it up, you should be able to negotiate a fair salary.

Conclusion of How Much Does Auto Mechanic Make per Hour

While there is no one-size-fits-all answer to how much auto mechanics make per hour, understanding the factors that impact salaries can help you make informed decisions about your career path. Whether you're just starting out or have been in the industry for years, there are opportunities to increase your earning potential through specialization, certifications, and negotiation. With the right skills and experience, a career as an auto mechanic can be both rewarding and financially stable.

Question and Answer

A: Yes, auto mechanics can make a good living, especially with experience and specialization. According to the BLS, the median hourly wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $20.24 as of May 2020.

Q: What factors impact auto mechanic salaries?

A: Factors that can impact auto mechanic salaries include experience, location, type of employer, and specialization.

Q: How can I increase my earning potential as an auto mechanic?

A: You can increase your earning potential as an auto mechanic by specializing in certain areas such as high-end luxury vehicles or advanced technologies, obtaining certifications such as ASE, and negotiating your salary.

Q: Is negotiation important when it comes to auto mechanic salaries?
