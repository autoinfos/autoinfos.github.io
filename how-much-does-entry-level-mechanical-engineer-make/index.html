How Much Does Entry Level Mechanical Engineer Make

Written by Aprilia Oct 24, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you interested in becoming a mechanical engineer but unsure about the salary of an entry-level position? It's important to know what you can expect to make as a starting salary, especially if you have student loans or other financial obligations.

When it comes to entry-level mechanical engineering salaries, there are several factors that can affect how much you make. These factors include your level of education, the industry you work in, and the location of your job.

On average, an entry-level mechanical engineer can expect to make around $62,000 per year. However, this can vary depending on the factors mentioned above. For example, mechanical engineers working in the aerospace industry tend to make more than those in other industries. Similarly, mechanical engineers in cities with a high cost of living may make more than those in smaller towns.

To sum up, entry-level mechanical engineers can expect to make around $62,000 per year, with variations depending on factors such as education level, industry, and location.

Education Level and Salary

When it comes to mechanical engineering salaries, your level of education can have a significant impact. Generally, those with more education tend to make more money. For example, a mechanical engineer with a bachelor's degree can expect to make around $62,000 per year, while a mechanical engineer with a master's degree can expect to make around $72,000 per year.

Personally, I pursued a master's degree in mechanical engineering and found that it helped me land a higher-paying job right out of school. Additionally, having a higher level of education can also lead to more opportunities for advancement and higher salaries in the long run.

Industry and Salary

The industry you work in can also have a significant impact on your salary as a mechanical engineer. Generally, those working in industries such as aerospace and defense tend to make more money than those in other industries. This is because these industries often require specialized knowledge and skills that are in high demand.

Personally, I worked in the aerospace industry as an entry-level mechanical engineer and found that the starting salary was higher than what I would have made in other industries. However, keep in mind that these industries can also be highly competitive and may require additional skills or experience beyond what is required for other industries.

Location and Salary

The location of your job can also have a significant impact on your salary as a mechanical engineer. Generally, those working in cities with a high cost of living can expect to make more money than those in smaller towns. This is because the cost of living is generally higher in these cities, and salaries are adjusted accordingly.

Personally, I found that moving to a city with a higher cost of living led to a higher salary as a mechanical engineer. However, keep in mind that the cost of living in these cities can also be higher, so it's important to factor in things like rent and other expenses when considering a move.

Experience and Salary

Finally, your level of experience can also have a significant impact on your salary as a mechanical engineer. Generally, those with more experience tend to make more money. This is because experience often leads to a higher level of expertise and a better understanding of the industry.

Personally, I found that gaining more experience as a mechanical engineer led to higher salaries and more opportunities for advancement. However, keep in mind that gaining experience can take time, and it's important to be patient and continue learning and growing in your field.

Conclusion of How Much Does Entry Level Mechanical Engineer Make

In conclusion, entry-level mechanical engineers can expect to make around $62,000 per year, with variations depending on factors such as education level, industry, location, and experience. It's important to consider these factors when deciding on a career path and when negotiating your salary. Remember to continue learning and growing in your field to increase your opportunities for advancement and higher salaries in the long run.

Question and Answer

Q: Can an entry-level mechanical engineer negotiate their salary?

A: Yes, it is possible to negotiate your salary as an entry-level mechanical engineer. However, it's important to do your research on industry standards and be prepared to make a case for why you deserve a higher salary.

Q: Is it better to work for a large or small company as an entry-level mechanical engineer?

A: It depends on your personal preferences and career goals. Large companies may offer more opportunities for advancement and higher salaries, but small companies may offer more hands-on experience and a closer-knit work environment.

Q: What skills are important for an entry-level mechanical engineer?

A: Important skills for an entry-level mechanical engineer include problem-solving, attention to detail, communication, and a strong understanding of math and science.

Q: What are some ways to increase your salary as a mechanical engineer?

A: Some ways to increase your salary as a mechanical engineer include gaining more experience, pursuing additional education or certifications, and seeking out opportunities for advancement within your company.
