How Much Does Mechanics Make An Hour

Written by Aprilia Jul 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a mechanic? Or are you already a mechanic and wondering if you are being paid fairly? One of the biggest questions people have is how much does mechanics make an hour?

It can be frustrating to not know if you are being paid what you are worth, especially when you are working hard every day. Mechanics have unique skills and knowledge that require training and experience, but unfortunately, they are not always compensated appropriately.

The average hourly wage for a mechanic in the United States is $19.45 per hour, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. However, this can vary depending on your experience, location, and the type of work you are doing. Some mechanics can make as little as $12 per hour, while others can make upwards of $30 per hour.

My Personal Experience

As a mechanic for over 10 years, I have seen the range of hourly wages firsthand. When I first started out, I was making around $14 per hour at a small auto shop. However, as I gained more experience and became certified in certain areas, my hourly wage increased to $25 per hour at a larger dealership.

Factors That Affect Hourly Wage

There are several factors that can impact how much a mechanic makes per hour. The first is experience. Entry-level mechanics will typically make less than those with several years of experience under their belt. Additionally, certifications and specialized training can also increase hourly wages.

Location is another important factor. Mechanics working in rural areas or small towns may make less than those in larger cities with higher costs of living. The type of work you are doing can also impact your hourly wage. For example, a mechanic working on diesel engines may make more than one working on small cars.

How To Negotiate Your Hourly Wage

If you feel like you are not being paid fairly for your skills and experience, it may be time to negotiate your hourly wage. The first step is to do your research and find out what other mechanics in your area are making. You can use online resources like Glassdoor or the Bureau of Labor Statistics to get an idea of the average hourly wage.

Next, approach your employer and present your research. Highlight your skills and experience and explain why you deserve a higher hourly wage. Be prepared to negotiate and have a number in mind that you feel is reasonable based on your research.

Tips For Increasing Your Hourly Wage

If you are looking to increase your hourly wage, there are several steps you can take. First, consider getting certified in a specialized area like diesel engines or hybrid vehicles. This can make you more valuable to your employer and increase your hourly wage.

Additionally, consider gaining experience in a variety of areas. The more skills you have, the more valuable you are to your employer. Finally, consider taking on additional responsibilities like training new hires or managing projects. This can show your employer that you are a valuable asset to the company and deserve a higher hourly wage.

Conclusion of How Much Does Mechanics Make An Hour

As you can see, there are several factors that can impact how much a mechanic makes per hour. While the average hourly wage is around $19.45 per hour, this can vary based on experience, location, and the type of work being done. If you feel like you are not being paid fairly, it may be time to negotiate your hourly wage or consider increasing your skills and responsibilities to become more valuable to your employer.
