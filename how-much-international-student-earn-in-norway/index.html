How Much International Students Earn In Norway

Written by Eveline Nov 14, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you considering studying in Norway as an international student? Have you been wondering how much you can earn while studying and living in this country? Look no further, because this blog post will provide you with all the information you need to know about how much international students earn in Norway.

Studying abroad can be an exciting and life-changing experience, but it can also be expensive. As an international student, you may face additional financial challenges, such as high tuition fees, accommodation costs, and living expenses. It is essential to know how much you can earn to support yourself while studying in Norway.

The answer to this question varies depending on different factors such as the type of work you do, the number of hours you work, and your employer. On average, international students in Norway can earn between 120 and 150 NOK per hour, which is equivalent to 12-15 euros per hour.

In summary, international students in Norway can earn a decent amount of money to support themselves while studying. It is essential to keep in mind that working part-time should not interfere with your studies, as your primary focus should be on your education.

How to Find Part-Time Jobs in Norway?

One of the best ways to find part-time jobs in Norway is through online job portals such as finn.no, nav.no, and jobbnorge.no. You can also check with your university's career center, as they may have job listings specifically for international students.

Working Hours for International Students in Norway

International students in Norway are allowed to work for up to 20 hours per week during the academic year and full-time during the summer break. However, it is essential to check your visa requirements as some visas may have different restrictions.

Benefits of Working as an International Student in Norway

Working as an international student in Norway has many benefits. Firstly, it can help you cover your living expenses and reduce your financial burden. Secondly, it can improve your language skills and provide you with valuable work experience. Finally, it can help you build your network and make new connections in Norway.

Taxation for International Students in Norway

International students in Norway are subject to pay taxes on their income. However, they may be eligible for tax deductions and refunds, depending on their income and expenses. It is essential to keep track of your earnings and expenses and consult with a tax professional to ensure you are paying the correct amount of taxes.

Conclusion of How Much International Students Earn in Norway

In conclusion, international students in Norway can earn a decent amount of money to support themselves while studying. It is crucial to find a balance between work and study and ensure that working part-time does not interfere with your education. Working as an international student in Norway has many benefits, including improving your language skills, gaining valuable work experience, and building your network.

Question and Answer

Q: Can international students work full-time during the academic year?

A: No, international students in Norway are only allowed to work up to 20 hours per week during the academic year.

Q: Are international students in Norway eligible for tax deductions?

A: Yes, international students in Norway may be eligible for tax deductions and refunds, depending on their income and expenses.

Q: How can international students find part-time jobs in Norway?

A: International students can find part-time jobs in Norway through online job portals and their university's career center.

Q: What is the average hourly wage for international students in Norway?

A: The average hourly wage for international students in Norway is between 120 and 150 NOK per hour, which is equivalent to 12-15 euros per hour.
