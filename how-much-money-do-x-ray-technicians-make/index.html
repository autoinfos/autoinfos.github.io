How Much Money Do X Ray Technicians Make

Written by Robby Oct 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in pursuing a career as an x-ray technician? One question that often comes up is how much money do x-ray technicians make? The answer to this question can vary based on a number of factors, but in general, x-ray technicians can earn a good salary. Read on to learn more about x-ray technician salaries and what you can expect if you decide to pursue this career path.

Working in healthcare can be a rewarding career, but it can also come with its own unique set of challenges. One of the biggest challenges x-ray technicians face is the potential for exposure to radiation. This can be a concern for some people, but with proper safety measures in place, the risk can be minimized. Another challenge is the unpredictable nature of the job. X-ray technicians may be called upon to work long hours or be on-call at all hours of the day or night.

In terms of salary, the median annual wage for x-ray technicians was $62,280 in May 2020, according to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics. However, this can vary based on a number of factors including the industry in which you work, your level of experience, and your geographic location. For example, x-ray technicians who work in hospitals tend to earn more than those who work in physician's offices or diagnostic imaging centers.

The Importance of Experience

When it comes to determining how much money do x-ray technicians make, experience is a key factor. X-ray technicians who have been in the field for a number of years can earn significantly more than those who are just starting out. This is because experienced technicians have had the opportunity to develop a wide range of skills and knowledge that can be applied in a variety of settings. Additionally, experienced technicians may have had the opportunity to specialize in a particular area of radiology, which can lead to higher pay.

Geographic Location Matters

Another factor that can impact x-ray technician salaries is geographic location. In areas with higher costs of living, salaries tend to be higher as well. For example, x-ray technicians who work in California or New York tend to earn more than those who work in states with a lower cost of living. Additionally, some states may have a higher demand for x-ray technicians, which can also impact salaries.

Education and Certification Requirements

In order to become an x-ray technician, you typically need to complete a postsecondary program in radiologic technology. These programs typically take two years to complete and can result in an associate degree. Additionally, x-ray technicians need to be licensed or certified in order to work in most states. The specific requirements vary by state, but typically involve passing a certification exam administered by the American Registry of Radiologic Technologists (ARRT).

Continuing Education and Advancement Opportunities

Once you have completed your initial education and certification, it is important to continue to develop your skills and knowledge. X-ray technicians can do this by pursuing continuing education opportunities or seeking out advanced certifications. Additionally, some x-ray technicians may have the opportunity to advance to supervisory or management positions, which can come with higher salaries.

Conclusion

In general, x-ray technicians can earn a good salary, but much depends on factors such as experience, geographic location, and industry. If you are considering a career as an x-ray technician, it is important to do your research and understand the potential opportunities and challenges. With the right education and training, x-ray technicians can find a rewarding career in healthcare.

Question and Answer

Q: How much do entry-level x-ray technicians make?

A: Entry-level x-ray technicians can expect to earn around $45,000 per year, but this can vary based on factors such as location and industry.

Q: Is it possible to earn more than the median salary as an x-ray technician?

A: Yes, experienced x-ray technicians who have specialized skills or work in high-demand areas can earn significantly more than the median salary.

Q: What industries employ the most x-ray technicians?

A: The healthcare industry is by far the largest employer of x-ray technicians, with hospitals and physician's offices being the most common settings.

Q: What are some of the benefits of working as an x-ray technician?

A: X-ray technicians have the opportunity to work in a fast-paced, dynamic environment where they can make a real difference in patients' lives. Additionally, the field offers good job security and opportunities for advancement.
