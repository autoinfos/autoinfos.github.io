How Much Money Does An Ase Certified Mechanic Make

Written by William Jul 30, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering becoming an ASE certified mechanic? One of the top questions on your mind is likely how much you can expect to make in this profession. The good news is that ASE certified mechanics can earn a good living, but the amount can vary depending on several factors.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $42,090 as of May 2020. However, ASE certified mechanics typically earn more than those without certification due to their advanced skills and knowledge.

There are several factors that can affect an ASE certified mechanic's salary, including their level of experience, location, and the type of employer. Mechanics who work for dealerships and specialty repair shops tend to earn more than those who work for general repair shops.

In summary, becoming an ASE certified mechanic can lead to a good salary, but the amount can vary depending on several factors such as experience, location, and employer. It is important to do your research and consider all factors before pursuing this career path.

Location:

Location can have a big impact on an ASE certified mechanic's salary. Mechanics who work in urban areas tend to earn more than those in rural areas due to the higher cost of living. Additionally, some states have higher demand for automotive repair services, which can lead to higher salaries for mechanics.

Experience:

Experience is another important factor that can affect an ASE certified mechanic's salary. Mechanics with more years of experience tend to earn more than those who are just starting out. Additionally, mechanics who specialize in certain areas, such as diesel engines or transmissions, can earn even more due to their advanced skills.

How to Increase Your Salary:

If you're an ASE certified mechanic looking to increase your salary, there are several steps you can take. First, consider specializing in a certain area of automotive repair. This can help you earn more due to your advanced skills and knowledge. Additionally, consider working for a dealership or specialty repair shop, as these tend to pay higher salaries than general repair shops.
