How Much Money Does A Radiologist Technician Make Per Hour

Written by Bardi Oct 05, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you interested in pursuing a career as a radiologist technician but wondering about the potential earnings? The salary of a radiologist technician is one of the major considerations before making a decision. In this post, we will explore the average hourly wage of a radiologist technician and what factors determine their earnings.

As with any profession, there are a few pain points that radiologist technicians may face when it comes to their salary. One of the most common concerns is the potential for burnout due to long hours and high-stress situations. Additionally, some radiologist technicians may feel undervalued for their work, given the complex and important role they play in patient care.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median hourly wage for radiologic technologists was $29.60 as of May 2020. This means that half of all radiologic technologists earned more than this amount, while the other half earned less. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $20.80 per hour, while the highest 10 percent earned more than $42.10 per hour.

In summary, the hourly wage of a radiologist technician varies greatly depending on a variety of factors, including location, experience, and education level. However, the median hourly wage for radiologic technologists is $29.60 as of May 2020.

Factors That Affect Radiologist Technician Salary

Location is one of the biggest factors that can impact a radiologist technician's salary. Technologists working in high-cost-of-living areas, such as major cities or coastal regions, may earn higher wages. Additionally, experience level and education level can play a role in determining salary. Technologists with more experience or higher levels of education may be able to command higher wages.

Personally, my experience as a radiologic technologist has taught me a lot about the importance of advocating for fair compensation. I have seen firsthand how hard radiologic technologists work to provide the best possible care to patients, and it is important that we are paid fairly for our efforts.

The Importance of Continuing Education for Radiologic Technologists

Continuing education is crucial for radiologic technologists who want to stay up-to-date with the latest advancements in their field. Technologists who pursue additional certifications or degrees may be able to earn higher wages and take on more responsibilities in their workplace.

Personally, I have found that continuing education has helped me to stay engaged and passionate about my work as a radiologic technologist. It has also opened up new opportunities for me within the field.

How Can Radiologic Technologists Negotiate for Higher Pay?

One way for radiologic technologists to negotiate for higher pay is to gather information about salary trends in their area. Technologists can research job postings to see what other employers are offering for similar positions. They can also join professional organizations and attend networking events to connect with other technologists and learn more about current salary trends.

What Are Some Other Careers in the Medical Field That Offer Similar Pay to Radiologic Technologists?

Other careers in the medical field that offer similar pay to radiologic technologists include respiratory therapists, nuclear medicine technologists, and diagnostic medical sonographers. These careers require specialized training and education, but can be rewarding and lucrative for those who pursue them.

My Personal Experience as a Radiologic Technologist

As a radiologic technologist, I have found this career to be both challenging and rewarding. I have the opportunity to work closely with patients and provide them with high-quality care. Additionally, I have been able to pursue continuing education opportunities that have helped me to grow within my field.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the job outlook for radiologic technologists?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of radiologic technologists is projected to grow 7 percent from 2019 to 2029, faster than the average for all occupations.

Q: What education is required to become a radiologic technologist?

A: Radiologic technologists typically need an associate's degree in radiologic technology. Some technologists may also pursue a bachelor's degree in the field.

Q: What skills are important for radiologic technologists to possess?

A: Radiologic technologists should have strong communication skills, technical skills, and attention to detail. They should also possess the ability to work well under pressure and provide compassionate care to patients.

Q: What is the difference between a radiologic technologist and a radiologist?

A: Radiologic technologists are responsible for operating the equipment used to create images of the body. Radiologists are medical doctors who specialize in the interpretation of these images and the diagnosis and treatment of diseases.

Conclusion of How Much Money Does a Radiologist Technician Make Per Hour

As we have explored in this post, the hourly wage of a radiologic technologist can vary greatly depending on a variety of factors. However, the median hourly wage for radiologic technologists is $29.60 as of May 2020. By pursuing continuing education opportunities and staying up-to-date with current salary trends, radiologic technologists can advocate for fair compensation and build rewarding careers in the medical field.
