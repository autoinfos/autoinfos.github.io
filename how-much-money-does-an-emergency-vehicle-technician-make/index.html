How Much Money Does An Emergency Vehicle Technician Make

Written by Eveline Oct 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Emergency vehicle technicians play a critical role in ensuring that emergency vehicles are in top working condition. These professionals are responsible for servicing and repairing emergency vehicles, including ambulances, fire trucks, and police cars. If you're interested in pursuing a career as an emergency vehicle technician, one of the most important questions you may have is: how much money does an emergency vehicle technician make?

Many individuals who are considering a career in emergency vehicle technology may worry about the potential earning potential. However, the good news is that emergency vehicle technicians can earn a good salary, especially with experience and additional certifications.

According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics, which includes emergency vehicle technicians, was $44,050 as of May 2020. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $25,110, and the highest 10 percent earned more than $70,820.

Overall, the earning potential for emergency vehicle technicians can vary depending on factors such as location, level of experience, and additional certifications. However, with dedication and hard work, it is possible to earn a good salary in this field.

Personal Experience with Emergency Vehicle Technician Salary

As someone who has worked in the emergency vehicle technology field for several years, I can attest to the fact that there are many opportunities to earn a good salary. When I first started out, I was earning around $30,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and worked towards additional certifications, my salary increased significantly.

One of the ways that I was able to earn a higher salary was by obtaining my Emergency Vehicle Technician (EVT) certification. This certification allowed me to work on more complex emergency vehicles, which in turn made me more valuable to my employer. With this certification, I was able to earn a salary of over $60,000 per year.

Factors That Can Affect Emergency Vehicle Technician Salary

As mentioned earlier, there are many factors that can affect the earning potential of emergency vehicle technicians. One of the most important factors is location. Emergency vehicle technicians who work in areas with a higher cost of living may earn a higher salary to compensate for the higher cost of living expenses.

Additionally, experience and certifications can also play a role in determining salary. Emergency vehicle technicians who have several years of experience and additional certifications may be able to command a higher salary than those who are just starting out.

Earning More as an Emergency Vehicle Technician

If you're interested in earning a higher salary as an emergency vehicle technician, there are several steps you can take. One of the most important things you can do is to obtain additional certifications. These certifications can demonstrate to your employer that you are committed to your profession and have the skills necessary to work on more complex emergency vehicles.

Another way to earn a higher salary is to seek out employment opportunities in areas with a high demand for emergency vehicle technicians. For example, emergency vehicle technicians who work in large cities may earn a higher salary than those who work in smaller towns or rural areas.

Final Thoughts on Emergency Vehicle Technician Salary

Overall, the earning potential for emergency vehicle technicians can be quite good, especially with experience and additional certifications. If you're interested in pursuing a career in this field, it's important to do your research and consider all of the factors that can affect your earning potential.
