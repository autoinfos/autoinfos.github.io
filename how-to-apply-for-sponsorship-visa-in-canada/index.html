How To Apply For Sponsorship Visa In Canada

Written by Robby Jul 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to move to Canada to start a new life or join your family? Applying for a sponsorship visa can be a daunting task, but it doesn't have to be. In this blog post, we will guide you through the process of how to apply for sponsorship visa in Canada in a relaxed English language.

One of the biggest pain points when it comes to applying for a sponsorship visa is the amount of paperwork and documentation required. It can be overwhelming, especially if you are not familiar with the process. Additionally, the process can be time-consuming, and mistakes can lead to delays or even rejection of your application.

The first step in applying for a sponsorship visa in Canada is to determine your eligibility. You must be sponsored by a Canadian citizen or permanent resident, and you must meet certain requirements, such as being in a genuine relationship or being a dependent child. Once you have determined your eligibility, you must complete and submit the appropriate forms and documentation.

In summary, to apply for sponsorship visa in Canada, you need to:

  • Determine your eligibility
  • Complete the appropriate forms and documentation
  • Submit your application

My Personal Experience Applying for Sponsorship Visa in Canada

When I decided to move to Canada to be with my partner, I was unaware of the process of applying for a sponsorship visa. However, with the help of online resources and the guidance of immigration lawyers, I was able to complete the process with ease. The key is to stay organized, double-check all documentation, and be patient throughout the process.

What Are the Required Documents for a Sponsorship Visa in Canada?

The required documents for a sponsorship visa in Canada may vary depending on your specific situation, but generally, you will need:

  • Proof of identity
  • Proof of relationship with your sponsor
  • Police certificates
  • Medical exams
  • Financial support documents

Proof of Identity

You will need to submit a copy of your passport or other government-issued identification to prove your identity. This will also be used to verify your age and nationality.

Proof of Relationship with Your Sponsor

You will need to provide evidence of your relationship with your sponsor, such as marriage certificates, birth certificates, or other official documents. This is to prove that your relationship is genuine and not solely for the purpose of obtaining a visa.

How Long Does it Take to Process a Sponsorship Visa Application?

The processing time for a sponsorship visa application can vary depending on several factors, including the type of application, the completeness of the application, and the volume of applications being processed. Generally, it can take anywhere from several months to a year or more to process a sponsorship visa application.

What Happens After My Sponsorship Visa Application is Approved?

If your sponsorship visa application is approved, you will be issued a visa and be able to travel to Canada. Once you arrive in Canada, you will be granted permanent resident status and will be able to live and work in Canada indefinitely.

Conclusion of How to Apply for Sponsorship Visa in Canada

Applying for a sponsorship visa in Canada can be a complex and lengthy process, but with the right guidance and preparation, it can be a smooth and successful experience. Remember to stay organized, double-check all documentation, and be patient throughout the process. We hope that this blog post has provided you with valuable information on how to apply for sponsorship visa in Canada.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I apply for a sponsorship visa if I am not married to my sponsor?

A: Yes, you can still apply for a sponsorship visa if you are in a common-law or conjugal relationship with your sponsor. You will need to provide evidence of your relationship, such as joint bank accounts or shared living arrangements.

Q: Do I need to provide police certificates for every country I have lived in?

A: No, you only need to provide police certificates for countries where you have lived for six months or more since the age of 18.

Q: Can I work in Canada while my sponsorship visa application is being processed?

A: If you are applying for an inland sponsorship visa, you may be eligible for an open work permit that allows you to work while your application is being processed. However, if you are applying for an overseas sponsorship visa, you will need to wait until your application is approved before you can work in Canada.

Q: Can I appeal a rejected sponsorship visa application?

A: Yes, you can appeal a rejected sponsorship visa application. You will need to provide new evidence to support your application and submit it to the Immigration and Refugee Board of Canada.
