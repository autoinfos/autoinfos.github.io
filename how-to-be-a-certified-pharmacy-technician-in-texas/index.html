How To Become A Certified Pharmacy Technician In Texas A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Bardi Nov 22, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you interested in becoming a certified pharmacy technician in Texas? If so, you're in the right place. Many people find that working in a pharmacy is a rewarding and fulfilling career path. However, the process of becoming a certified pharmacy technician in Texas can be confusing and overwhelming. In this post, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to become a certified pharmacy technician in Texas.

Working in a pharmacy can be a great career choice, but it does come with some challenges. One of the biggest challenges is the certification process. Becoming a certified pharmacy technician in Texas requires a significant amount of time, effort, and dedication. It's not an easy process, but it is definitely worth it in the end.

So, how do you become a certified pharmacy technician in Texas? The first step is to complete a pharmacy technician training program. There are many different programs available, both online and in-person. You should choose a program that is accredited by the American Society of Health-System Pharmacists (ASHP) or the Accreditation Council for Pharmacy Education (ACPE).

Once you have completed your training program, you will need to pass the Pharmacy Technician Certification Exam (PTCE). This exam is administered by the Pharmacy Technician Certification Board (PTCB). The exam covers a wide range of topics, including medication safety, pharmacy law, and pharmacology. You will need to pass the exam with a score of 1,400 or higher to become certified.

In summary, becoming a certified pharmacy technician in Texas requires completing a training program and passing the PTCE. It's a challenging process, but it's worth it in the end. Below, we'll go into more detail about each step of the process.

Step 1: Choose a Pharmacy Technician Training Program

Before you can become a certified pharmacy technician in Texas, you will need to complete a training program. There are many different programs available, both online and in-person. It's important to choose a program that is accredited by the ASHP or ACPE. These accrediting organizations ensure that the program meets certain standards of quality and rigor.

When choosing a program, you should consider factors such as cost, location, and schedule. Some programs are more expensive than others, so you will need to factor in the cost of tuition, books, and other materials. You should also consider the location of the program and whether it's convenient for you to attend. Finally, you should look at the schedule of the program and make sure it fits with your other commitments.

Step 2: Pass the Pharmacy Technician Certification Exam (PTCE)

Once you have completed your training program, you will need to pass the PTCE. This exam is administered by the PTCB and covers a wide range of topics related to pharmacy practice. The exam consists of 90 multiple-choice questions and you will have two hours to complete it.

To prepare for the exam, you should review the content outline provided by the PTCB. This outline will give you an idea of what topics will be covered on the exam. You can also purchase study materials or take a review course to help you prepare.

On the day of the exam, you should arrive at the testing center at least 30 minutes before your scheduled appointment. You will need to bring two forms of identification, including one government-issued ID. You will also need to pay a fee of $129 to take the exam.

Step 2.1: Maintaining Your Certification

Once you have become a certified pharmacy technician in Texas, you will need to maintain your certification. This requires completing 20 hours of continuing education every two years. You will also need to renew your certification every two years by paying a fee and submitting a renewal application.

Step 2.2: Working as a Certified Pharmacy Technician

After you have become a certified pharmacy technician in Texas, you will be eligible to work in a variety of settings, including retail pharmacies, hospitals, and long-term care facilities. Your job duties may include preparing medications, counting pills, and assisting pharmacists with patient counseling.

Step 3: Gain Work Experience

While it's not strictly necessary to gain work experience before becoming a certified pharmacy technician in Texas, it can be helpful. Many pharmacy technician training programs offer internships or externships that allow you to gain hands-on experience in a pharmacy setting. You can also look for entry-level pharmacy technician positions that don't require certification.

Step 4: Apply for Certification

Once you have completed your training program and gained any necessary work experience, you can apply for certification through the PTCB. You will need to provide proof of your education and work experience, as well as pay a fee of $129.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average salary for a certified pharmacy technician in Texas?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for pharmacy technicians in Texas is $33,840.

Q: How long does it take to become a certified pharmacy technician in Texas?

A: The process of becoming a certified pharmacy technician in Texas typically takes between 6 months and 2 years, depending on the training program you choose.

Q: Is certification required to work as a pharmacy technician in Texas?

A: Certification is not required by law to work as a pharmacy technician in Texas, but many employers prefer to hire certified technicians.

Q: What are some common job duties of a certified pharmacy technician in Texas?

A: Some common job duties of a certified pharmacy technician in Texas include preparing medications, counting pills, and assisting pharmacists with patient counseling.

Conclusion of How to Become a Certified Pharmacy Technician in Texas

Becoming a certified pharmacy technician in Texas is not an easy process, but it's definitely worth it in the end. By completing a training program and passing the PTCE, you can gain the knowledge and skills necessary to work in the pharmacy field. Whether you're interested in retail pharmacy or hospital pharmacy, becoming a certified pharmacy technician in Texas can open up a world of career opportunities.
