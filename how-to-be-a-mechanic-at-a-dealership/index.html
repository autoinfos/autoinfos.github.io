How To Be A Mechanic At A Dealership A Beginner s Guide

Written by Petter Sep 27, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you interested in becoming a mechanic at a dealership but don't know where to start? Do you want to learn how to fix cars and get paid for it? If so, you've come to the right place. In this blog post, we'll cover everything you need to know about how to be a mechanic at a dealership.

Being a mechanic at a dealership can be a challenging but rewarding career. You'll be responsible for diagnosing and repairing vehicles, working with customers, and keeping up with the latest automotive technology. However, it can also be a stressful job with long hours and tight deadlines.

If you're passionate about cars and enjoy working with your hands, becoming a mechanic at a dealership may be the perfect career for you. But where do you start? Here are some steps you can take to become a successful dealership mechanic:

Get the Right Education and Training

The first step to becoming a mechanic at a dealership is to get the right education and training. You'll need a high school diploma or equivalent, and most dealerships require a certification from the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE).

You can attend a technical school or community college to learn the basics of automotive repair. Many dealerships also offer on-the-job training programs for new mechanics. It's important to keep up with the latest technology and techniques in the industry, so continuing education and training is essential.

Gain Experience and Build Your Skills

Once you have the education and training, it's time to gain experience and build your skills. Start by working as an entry-level mechanic at a small repair shop or dealership. This will give you the opportunity to learn from experienced mechanics and get hands-on experience.

As you gain more experience, you can work your way up to more advanced positions. You can also specialize in a specific area of automotive repair, such as transmissions or brakes. The more skills and experience you have, the more valuable you'll be to a dealership.

Develop Excellent Customer Service Skills

Being a dealership mechanic isn't just about fixing cars – it's also about interacting with customers. You'll need to have excellent customer service skills to build relationships with customers and keep them coming back to the dealership for their automotive needs.

Be friendly, patient, and professional when working with customers. Listen to their concerns and explain repairs in simple terms they can understand. Provide accurate estimates and keep customers updated on the status of their repairs. Good customer service skills can go a long way in building a successful career as a dealership mechanic.

Stay Up-to-Date with the Latest Technology

The automotive industry is constantly evolving, with new technology and techniques emerging all the time. As a dealership mechanic, it's important to stay up-to-date with the latest trends and advancements in the industry.

Attend training seminars and workshops, read industry publications, and network with other professionals in the field. This will help you stay on top of the latest technology and techniques, and make you a more valuable asset to the dealership.

Question and Answer

Q: How much does a dealership mechanic make?

A: The average salary for a dealership mechanic is around $50,000 per year, but this can vary depending on experience and location.

Q: What kind of tools do I need to be a dealership mechanic?

A: You'll need a basic set of hand tools, as well as diagnostic equipment and specialized tools for specific repairs. Most dealerships will provide the necessary tools, but many mechanics prefer to have their own.

Q: Is it hard to become a dealership mechanic?

A: Becoming a dealership mechanic requires education, training, and experience. It can be a challenging career, but with dedication and hard work, it's definitely achievable.

Q: What are the benefits of working as a dealership mechanic?

A: Some of the benefits of working as a dealership mechanic include job stability, good pay, and opportunities for advancement. You'll also have access to the latest technology and equipment in the industry.

Conclusion

Becoming a mechanic at a dealership can be a rewarding career for those with a passion for cars and a desire to work with their hands. By getting the right education and training, gaining experience, developing excellent customer service skills, staying up-to-date with the latest technology, and continuously learning and growing in the field, you can become a successful dealership mechanic. Good luck on your journey!
