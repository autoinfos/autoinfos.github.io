How To Become A Race Car Instructor A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 22, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Have you ever dreamed of becoming a race car instructor? Do you have a passion for speed and a love for teaching others? If so, you are in the right place. In this article, we will provide you with a comprehensive guide on how to become a race car instructor. So, buckle up and let's get started!

Many people dream of becoming a race car instructor, but they don't know where to start or what steps to take. It can be a daunting task, but it doesn't have to be. Some of the common pain points include lack of proper guidance, not knowing what qualifications are needed, and not having the right connections to get started. But fear not, we have got you covered.

The first step to becoming a race car instructor is to gain experience in the field. This can be achieved by participating in racing events, attending driving schools, and obtaining proper certifications. It is also essential to have excellent communication skills, patience, and a willingness to teach others. Networking with professionals in the industry can also help to open doors and land your first opportunity.

In summary, to become a race car instructor, you need to gain experience in the field, attend driving schools, obtain certifications, have excellent communication skills, patience, and network with professionals in the industry. Let's dive a little deeper into each of these steps.

Step 1: Gain Experience in the Field

One of the most crucial steps in becoming a race car instructor is gaining experience in the field. This can be achieved by participating in racing events, attending track days, and practicing on your own. It is essential to have a strong understanding of racing techniques, safety protocols, and the mechanics of a race car. The more experience you have, the more opportunities you will have to teach others.

When I first started, I participated in local racing events and attended track days to gain experience. This allowed me to learn from experienced drivers and improve my skills on the track. I also spent time practicing on my own to perfect my technique.

Step 2: Attend Driving Schools and Obtain Certifications

Attending driving schools and obtaining proper certifications is another essential step in becoming a race car instructor. These schools offer courses on racing techniques, safety procedures, and the mechanics of a race car. They also provide hands-on experience and help to build your network in the industry.

There are several driving schools to choose from, including the Skip Barber Racing School and the Bondurant Racing School. It is also important to obtain certifications, such as the Sports Car Club of America (SCCA) Instructor Certification and the National Auto Sport Association (NASA) Instructor Certification. These certifications demonstrate your expertise and credibility as a race car instructor.

Step 3: Develop Excellent Communication Skills and Patience

As a race car instructor, you will be teaching others how to drive at high speeds and navigate complex tracks. Therefore, it is essential to have excellent communication skills and patience. You must be able to explain racing techniques and safety procedures effectively and calmly under pressure. It is also crucial to be patient with your students and understand that everyone learns at their own pace.

When I first started as a race car instructor, I struggled with communicating effectively under pressure. However, I worked on developing my skills and learned to stay calm and collected in all situations. This helped me to become a more effective and successful instructor.

Step 4: Network with Professionals in the Industry

Networking with professionals in the industry can help open doors and land your first opportunity as a race car instructor. Attend racing events, join local clubs, and engage with others in the industry. Building relationships with other race car drivers, instructors, and track owners can help to establish your credibility and reputation.

When I first started, I attended as many racing events as possible and made connections with other drivers and instructors. This helped me to land my first opportunity as a race car instructor, and I have been teaching ever since.

FAQs About How to Become a Race Car Instructor

Q: What qualifications do I need to become a race car instructor?

A: To become a race car instructor, you need to gain experience in the field, attend driving schools, obtain certifications, have excellent communication skills, patience, and network with professionals in the industry.

Q: How long does it take to become a race car instructor?

A: The time it takes to become a race car instructor varies depending on your level of experience, dedication, and network. However, on average, it can take 2-5 years to establish yourself as a credible instructor.

Q: Is it necessary to have a college degree to become a race car instructor?

A: No, a college degree is not necessary to become a race car instructor. However, having a degree in a related field, such as mechanical engineering or motorsports management, can be helpful.

Q: How much can I expect to earn as a race car instructor?

A: The salary of a race car instructor varies depending on their level of experience, location, and the type of courses they teach. On average, race car instructors earn between $30,000-$70,000 per year.

Conclusion of How to Become a Race Car Instructor

Becoming a race car instructor takes time, dedication, and hard work. By following the steps outlined in this article, you can establish yourself as a credible and successful instructor in the industry. Remember to gain experience in the field, attend driving schools, obtain certifications, develop excellent communication skills and patience, and network with professionals in the industry. With these key steps, you can achieve your dream of becoming a race car instructor.
