How To Get A Full Auto License In Oklahoma A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Nov 20, 2023 · 4 min read

Driving is a valuable life skill that gives you independence and freedom to travel wherever and whenever you want. However, obtaining a driver’s license can be intimidating and overwhelming, especially if you are a first-time driver. If you’re looking to get a full auto license in Oklahoma, you might be feeling confused and unsure about where to start.

Learning how to drive can be a challenging experience, but it doesn’t have to be a stressful one. In this article, we’ll guide you through the process of getting a full auto license in Oklahoma, step-by-step. We’ll cover everything from the application process to the driving test, so you can feel confident and prepared on your journey to becoming a licensed driver.

Step 1: Gather Required Documents

Before you can apply for a full auto license in Oklahoma, you’ll need to gather a few important documents. These documents include:

• Proof of identity (such as a birth certificate or passport)

• A Social Security card

• Proof of residency (such as a utility bill or rental agreement)

• A completed driver’s license application

It’s important to gather all of these documents before you start the application process to ensure a smooth and efficient process. Once you have all of these documents, you can move on to the next step.

Step 2: Complete Driver’s Education

In Oklahoma, all first-time drivers under the age of 18 are required to complete a driver’s education course before they can obtain a license. This course covers topics such as traffic laws, safe driving practices, and defensive driving techniques. You can complete this course through a private driving school or through your high school.

Step 3: Apply for a Learner’s Permit

Once you’ve completed driver’s education, you can apply for a learner’s permit. To apply, you’ll need to visit your local Department of Public Safety (DPS) office and bring all of the required documents with you. You’ll also need to pass a written knowledge test and a vision test.

Step 4: Practice Driving

With your learner’s permit in hand, you can start practicing your driving skills. You’ll need to complete at least 50 hours of supervised driving, including 10 hours of nighttime driving, before you can take the driving test. Be sure to practice driving in a variety of conditions, such as in heavy traffic, on the highway, and in adverse weather conditions.

Step 5: Schedule and Pass the Driving Test

When you feel confident in your driving skills, you can schedule your driving test at your local DPS office. During the test, you’ll need to demonstrate your ability to drive safely and follow all traffic laws. If you pass the test, you’ll receive your full auto license!

Question and Answer

Q: How much does it cost to get a full auto license in Oklahoma?

A: The fee for a full auto license in Oklahoma is $38.50.

Q: Can I take the driving test in my own car?

A: Yes, you can take the driving test in your own car as long as it meets certain requirements, such as having a valid license plate, proof of insurance, and functioning safety features.

Q: How long is a full auto license valid in Oklahoma?

A: A full auto license in Oklahoma is valid for four years.

Q: What happens if I fail the driving test?

A: If you fail the driving test, you can retake it after a certain amount of time has passed. You may be required to complete additional driver’s education or practice driving hours before retaking the test.

Conclusion of How to Get a Full Auto License in Oklahoma

Getting a full auto license in Oklahoma might seem like a daunting task, but it’s a manageable process if you follow these steps. By gathering the necessary documents, completing driver’s education, applying for a learner’s permit, practicing your driving skills, and passing the driving test, you’ll be on your way to becoming a licensed driver. Remember to always prioritize safety while driving and enjoy the freedom and independence that comes with having a driver’s license.
