How To Get A Car Dealer License In Oklahoma

Written by Bardi Jul 21, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you interested in starting your own car dealership in Oklahoma? Whether you're a seasoned business owner or a first-time entrepreneur, getting a car dealer license in Oklahoma can be a daunting task. Fortunately, the process can be broken down into a few simple steps. In this blog post, we'll guide you through everything you need to know to get your car dealer license in Oklahoma.

Before we dive into the details, let's address some common pain points that aspiring car dealers in Oklahoma may face. For example, you may be concerned about the cost and time involved in obtaining a car dealer license. Additionally, you may be unsure about the specific requirements and regulations that apply to car dealerships in Oklahoma.

First and foremost, in order to get a car dealer license in Oklahoma, you will need to apply for and obtain a Dealer License from the Oklahoma Used Motor Vehicles and Parts Commission. This license is required for anyone who wants to sell or lease motor vehicles in Oklahoma. The first step in this process is to complete the necessary application forms and submit them to the Commission, along with all required supporting documentation.

Once your application has been received and reviewed, you will need to pass a background check and provide proof of liability insurance. You will also need to provide a sales tax permit and a surety bond, which is essentially a type of insurance that protects customers in case you fail to deliver on a sale. Finally, you will need to complete a dealer training course, which covers topics such as vehicle title and registration, sales tax collection, and advertising regulations.

Personal Experience

As someone who has gone through the process of getting a car dealer license in Oklahoma, I can tell you that it's not as difficult as it may seem. While there are certainly some bureaucratic hoops to jump through, the application process is straightforward and the Commission is generally helpful and responsive. One tip I would offer is to make sure you have all of your paperwork in order before you submit your application. This will help streamline the process and minimize any delays.

Costs and Fees

It's important to be aware of the costs and fees associated with getting a car dealer license in Oklahoma. The application fee is $125, and the surety bond can range from $25,000 to $100,000 depending on the volume of sales you anticipate. Additionally, you will need to pay for liability insurance, which can vary depending on your business's size and location. Finally, there may be additional fees for things like dealer plates and advertising permits.

Dealer Training Course

The dealer training course is a mandatory requirement for obtaining a car dealer license in Oklahoma. This course is designed to ensure that dealers have a basic understanding of the laws and regulations that govern their business. The course covers topics such as vehicle title and registration, sales tax collection, and advertising regulations. The cost of the course is $200, and it can be completed online or in-person.

Advertising Regulations

One area that is often overlooked by aspiring car dealers is advertising regulations. In Oklahoma, there are specific rules governing how car dealers can advertise their vehicles. For example, all advertising must include the dealer's name and address, and any claims made in the advertising must be truthful and verifiable. Additionally, there are strict rules around the use of certain terms, such as "new" and "used." Violating these rules can result in fines and other penalties.

Final Thoughts

Getting a car dealer license in Oklahoma may seem overwhelming at first, but with the right guidance and preparation, it can be a relatively straightforward process. Remember to take your time, gather all of the necessary paperwork, and complete the dealer training course. With a little bit of hard work and dedication, you can be on your way to running a successful car dealership in Oklahoma.

Question and Answer

Q: How long does it take to get a car dealer license in Oklahoma?

A: The amount of time it takes to get a car dealer license in Oklahoma can vary depending on the completeness of your application and the volume of applications being processed by the Commission. Generally speaking, you should expect the process to take several weeks.

Q: What is a surety bond?

A: A surety bond is a type of insurance that protects customers in case you fail to deliver on a sale. The bond is essentially a contract between you, the customer, and a third-party bonding company.

Q: Do I need a physical location to get a car dealer license in Oklahoma?

A: Yes, you will need to have a physical location in Oklahoma in order to get a car dealer license. This location must meet certain requirements, such as having a sign with your business name and address and having a designated area for vehicle displays.

Q: Can I sell motorcycles or other types of vehicles with a car dealer license in Oklahoma?

A: No, a car dealer license in Oklahoma only allows you to sell motor vehicles. If you want to sell motorcycles, boats, or other types of vehicles, you will need to obtain additional licenses and permits.

Conclusion of How to Get Car Dealer License in Oklahoma

In conclusion, getting a car dealer license in Oklahoma requires some effort, but it's a relatively straightforward process. By following the steps outlined in this blog post, you can be well on your way to starting your own successful car dealership in Oklahoma. Remember to stay organized, be diligent in your paperwork, and take advantage of the resources available to you.
