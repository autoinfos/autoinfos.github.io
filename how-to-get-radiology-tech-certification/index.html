How To Get Radiology Tech Certification

Written by William Nov 04, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you interested in becoming a radiology technician but unsure of how to get started? Look no further as we dive into the steps you need to take in order to obtain radiology tech certification.

One of the biggest pain points for individuals seeking radiology tech certification is the overwhelming amount of information available online. It can be difficult to sort through the various requirements and certifications needed to become a radiology technician. Additionally, the process can seem daunting and confusing without proper guidance.

The first step in obtaining radiology tech certification is to earn a high school diploma or equivalent. From there, you will need to complete a radiology technology program that is accredited by the Joint Review Committee on Education in Radiologic Technology (JRCERT). Upon completion of the program, you must pass the American Registry of Radiologic Technologists (ARRT) certification exam to become certified.

In summary, the steps to obtaining radiology tech certification include earning a high school diploma or equivalent, completing an accredited radiology technology program, and passing the ARRT certification exam.

Accredited Radiology Technology Programs

Choosing an accredited radiology technology program is crucial in obtaining radiology tech certification. Not only will an accredited program provide you with the necessary knowledge and skills, but it will also ensure that you are eligible to take the ARRT certification exam. During my own personal experience, I found that researching and comparing different programs helped me make an informed decision on which program was the best fit for me.

Passing the ARRT Certification Exam

The ARRT certification exam is a comprehensive exam that tests your knowledge and skills in radiology technology. While it can seem intimidating, proper preparation and studying can help increase your chances of passing. I personally found it helpful to utilize study materials and practice exams in order to prepare for the exam.

Continuing Education Requirements

After obtaining radiology tech certification, it is important to stay up-to-date on the latest advancements and technologies in the field. This is where continuing education comes in. In order to maintain certification, individuals must complete a certain amount of continuing education credits every two years. These credits can be earned through various educational activities such as attending conferences or completing online courses.

Career Opportunities

Obtaining radiology tech certification can open up a wide range of career opportunities in the healthcare industry. Radiology technicians can work in hospitals, clinics, and imaging centers, just to name a few. Additionally, with experience and further education, individuals can advance into supervisory or managerial positions.

Question and Answer

Q: How long does it take to complete an accredited radiology technology program?

A: The length of accredited radiology technology programs can vary, but typically take 2-4 years to complete.

Q: Is it necessary to obtain certification in order to work as a radiology technician?

A: While certification is not required in all states, it is highly recommended as it can increase job opportunities and earning potential.

Q: What is the passing score for the ARRT certification exam?

A: The passing score for the ARRT certification exam is 75.

Q: Can continuing education credits be earned through on-the-job training?

A: Yes, continuing education credits can be earned through a variety of educational activities including on-the-job training and attending conferences.

Conclusion of How to Get Radiology Tech Certification

Obtaining radiology tech certification can seem like a daunting process, but with proper guidance and preparation, it can be a rewarding career path. Remember to research and choose an accredited radiology technology program, prepare for the ARRT certification exam, and continue your education to stay up-to-date in the field. With dedication and hard work, you can become a certified radiology technician and excel in the healthcare industry.
