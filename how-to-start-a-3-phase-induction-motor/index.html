How To Start A 3 Phase Induction Motor A Beginner s Guide

Written by Daniel Nov 07, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you new to the world of industrial machines and are wondering how to start a 3 phase induction motor? Starting an induction motor might seem daunting at first, but with the right knowledge and guidance, it can be a simple process. In this post, we'll provide you with a beginner's guide on how to start a 3 phase induction motor, and help you understand the basics of motor starting.

Starting a 3 phase induction motor can be a challenging task, especially for those who are not familiar with electrical machinery. Some common pain points that people experience while starting an induction motor are not knowing the correct sequence of switching, not using the right starter, or not understanding the motor's specifications. These can lead to problems like motor failure, damaged electrical components, and even injury in some cases.

The first and foremost step in starting a 3 phase induction motor is to check the motor's specifications and requirements. You need to know the motor's rated voltage, rated current, and rated frequency to determine the type of starter required. The starter is an essential component that regulates the voltage and current during motor starting, and choosing the wrong starter can cause damage to the motor.

In summary, to start a 3 phase induction motor, you need to know its rated voltage, current, and frequency, and choose the right starter. Once you have the correct starter, you need to follow the correct sequence of switching to start the motor. Don't forget to ensure that the motor is properly grounded and the supply voltage is within the motor's specifications.

Understanding the Types of Starters for 3 Phase Induction Motors

There are two types of starters for 3 phase induction motors: Direct-On-Line (DOL) starter and Star-Delta starter. A DOL starter is a simple and cost-effective starter that provides full voltage to the motor during starting. However, it can cause high inrush currents, which can damage the motor and other electrical components. A Star-Delta starter, on the other hand, is a more efficient starter that reduces the inrush current by starting the motor in a 'star' configuration and then switching to a 'delta' configuration.

Personally, I prefer using a Star-Delta starter as it reduces the stress on the motor during starting and extends its life. However, it's essential to choose the right starter based on your motor's specifications and requirements.

The Importance of Motor Starting in Industrial Applications

Motor starting is a critical process in industrial applications as it determines the motor's performance, efficiency, and life. Starting a motor improperly can cause damage to the motor and other electrical components, leading to costly repairs and downtime. Therefore, it's essential to follow the correct sequence of switching and use the right starter to ensure that the motor starts smoothly and operates efficiently.

Correct Sequence of Switching for 3 Phase Induction Motors

The correct sequence of switching for 3 phase induction motors is as follows:

1. Turn off the power supply to the motor

2. Close the main switch or circuit breaker

3. Turn on the control circuit

4. Check the direction of rotation of the motor

5. Turn on the power supply to the motor

It's essential to follow this sequence of switching to ensure that the motor starts smoothly and operates efficiently.

Common Problems While Starting a 3 Phase Induction Motor

Some common problems that people face while starting a 3 phase induction motor are:

1. High inrush current

2. Overheating of the motor

3. Motor failure

4. Damaged electrical components

To avoid these problems, it's crucial to choose the right starter and follow the correct sequence of switching. Additionally, you should ensure that the motor is properly grounded and the supply voltage is within the motor's specifications.
