How To Write A Cover Letter For A Graduate Program In Relaxed English Language

Written by Bardi Jun 19, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you struggling to write a cover letter for your graduate program application? Do you find it hard to express yourself in a formal tone? Writing a cover letter can be a daunting task, but it doesn't have to be. In this post, we will guide you on how to write a cover letter for a graduate program in relaxed English language.

The Pain Points of Writing a Cover Letter for a Graduate Program

When it comes to writing a cover letter for a graduate program, there are a few pain points that most students face. Firstly, it can be challenging to strike a balance between sounding professional and expressing your personality. Secondly, you may not know what to include in your cover letter and what to leave out. Thirdly, you may not be confident in your writing skills and worry about making mistakes.

Answering the Target of Writing a Cover Letter for a Graduate Program

A cover letter is an essential part of your graduate program application. It is your chance to introduce yourself to the admissions committee and make a lasting impression. A well-written cover letter can help you stand out from other applicants. Here are some tips on how to write a cover letter for a graduate program:

1. Research the Program

Before you start writing your cover letter, research the graduate program you are applying to. Look at their website, mission statement, and values. This will help you tailor your cover letter to the program and show that you are genuinely interested in studying there.

2. Start with a Strong Opening

Your opening sentence should grab the reader's attention and make them want to keep reading. Avoid generic openings such as "I am writing to apply for the graduate program." Instead, start with a personal anecdote or a thought-provoking statement.

3. Highlight Your Skills and Achievements

Your cover letter should highlight your skills, achievements, and experiences that are relevant to the graduate program. Use specific examples to illustrate your points and show how you will contribute to the program.

4. Keep it Concise and Clear

Your cover letter should be no more than one page long. Use short paragraphs and bullet points to make it easy to read. Avoid using jargon or technical language that the admissions committee may not understand.

Personal Experience and Explanation of Writing a Cover Letter for a Graduate Program

When I was applying for my graduate program, I found it challenging to write a cover letter that expressed my personality while sounding professional. I struggled to find the right balance between being too formal and too casual. However, after researching the program and tailoring my cover letter to it, I was able to write a compelling letter that showcased my skills and experiences.

To write a cover letter for a graduate program, it's essential to understand what the program is looking for and how you can contribute to it. Use specific examples to illustrate your points and be concise and clear in your writing.

Mistakes to Avoid When Writing a Cover Letter for a Graduate Program

When writing a cover letter for a graduate program, there are a few mistakes you should avoid:

1. Using a Generic Cover Letter

A generic cover letter that you send to multiple programs will not be effective. Tailor your cover letter to each program, highlighting how you fit their specific requirements.

2. Being Too Casual

A cover letter is a formal document that should be written in a professional tone. Avoid being too casual or using slang.

3. Making Grammatical Errors

Grammatical errors can make your cover letter look unprofessional. Proofread your letter carefully and ask someone else to read it before you submit it.

4. Focusing on Yourself Only

Your cover letter should not only focus on your achievements but also on how you can contribute to the program. Show that you have researched the program and understand what they are looking for.

Question and Answer Section

Q: What Should I Include in My Cover Letter?

A: Your cover letter should include an introduction, a brief summary of your skills and achievements, and a conclusion. You should also tailor your letter to the specific program you are applying to.

Q: How Long Should My Cover Letter Be?

A: Your cover letter should be no more than one page long. Use short paragraphs and bullet points to make it easy to read.

Q: Should I Use Technical Language in My Cover Letter?

A: Avoid using jargon or technical language that the admissions committee may not understand. Instead, use clear and concise language to explain your skills and experiences.

Q: Can I Use a Template for My Cover Letter?

A: While templates can be helpful, avoid using a generic template. Instead, use a template as a guide and tailor your letter to the specific program you are applying to.

Conclusion of How to Write a Cover Letter for a Graduate Program

Writing a cover letter for a graduate program can be challenging, but with the right approach, you can make it a compelling document that showcases your skills and experiences. Remember to research the program, tailor your letter to it, and be clear and concise in your writing. Avoid common mistakes such as using a generic cover letter or being too casual. By following these tips, you can increase your chances of getting accepted into your dream graduate program.
