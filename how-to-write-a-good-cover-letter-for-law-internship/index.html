How To Write A Good Cover Letter For Law Internship Tips And Tricks

Written by William Jun 24, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you a law student looking for an internship but struggling with writing a cover letter? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many students find it challenging to write a cover letter that stands out and showcases their skills and experience. In this post, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to write a good cover letter for law internship.

Understanding the Pain Points

Writing a cover letter can be a daunting task, especially when you're competing with other candidates for the same position. Some common pain points that students face while writing a cover letter include:

  • Not knowing what to include in the letter
  • Not being able to showcase their skills and experience effectively
  • Not understanding the tone and language to use in a cover letter

How to Write a Good Cover Letter for Law Internship

Now that we've covered the pain points, let's dive into how to write a good cover letter for law internship:

  • Research the company or organization you're applying to and tailor your letter accordingly
  • Start with a strong opening that grabs the reader's attention
  • Showcase your skills and experience that align with the position
  • Use a professional tone and language throughout the letter
  • End with a strong closing and a call to action

Personal Experience and Further Explanation

When I was applying for my law internship, I found it challenging to write a cover letter that stood out. However, after doing some research and seeking feedback, I was able to write a compelling letter that showcased my skills and experience. One tip that helped me was to use strong action verbs and quantify my achievements wherever possible. For example, instead of saying "I helped with legal research," I wrote "I conducted legal research on a complex case which resulted in a successful outcome."

Additional Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to keep in mind while writing your cover letter:

  1. Proofread your letter multiple times to avoid any spelling or grammatical errors
  2. Keep your letter concise and to the point
  3. Show enthusiasm for the position and the company/organization
  4. Get feedback from a mentor, professor, or career advisor

Using Keywords in Your Cover Letter

Using relevant keywords in your cover letter can help you stand out and showcase your knowledge of the industry. Some keywords that you can include in your letter are:

  • Legal research
  • Legal writing
  • Courtroom experience
  • Client communication
  • Teamwork

Showcasing Your Skills and Experience

When showcasing your skills and experience, make sure to provide specific examples and quantify your achievements wherever possible. For example, instead of saying "I have excellent communication skills," you can write "I have experience communicating with clients, colleagues, and stakeholders in a clear and concise manner, resulting in positive feedback."

Personal Experience and Further Explanation

During my law internship, I was able to apply the skills and knowledge that I had gained in law school. One thing that helped me stand out was my experience with legal writing and research, which I had highlighted in my cover letter. I was able to work on complex legal cases and provide valuable input to my team. Additionally, my communication skills helped me build strong relationships with clients and stakeholders, which was crucial to the success of the cases I worked on.

Question and Answer

Q: Should I include my GPA in my cover letter?

A: It's not necessary to include your GPA in your cover letter. However, if you have a high GPA and it's relevant to the position, you can mention it briefly.

Q: How long should my cover letter be?

A: Your cover letter should be one page or less. Keep it concise and to the point.

Q: Is it okay to use a template for my cover letter?

A: Using a template can be helpful, but make sure to tailor it to the position and company/organization you're applying to.

Q: What should I include in the closing of my cover letter?

A: In the closing of your cover letter, you should thank the reader for considering your application and express your enthusiasm for the position. Also, include your contact information and a call to action, such as requesting an interview.

Conclusion of How to Write a Good Cover Letter for Law Internship

Writing a good cover letter can be a challenging task, but by following these tips and tricks, you can create a compelling letter that showcases your skills and experience. Remember to tailor your letter to the position and company/organization, use relevant keywords, and show enthusiasm for the position. Good luck with your law internship search!
