How To Write A Professional Cover Letter For Resume In Relaxed English Language

Written by Christine Jun 26, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you struggling to write a cover letter for your resume? Do you find it challenging to communicate your skills and achievements in a professional yet relaxed tone? A cover letter is your chance to make a great first impression on potential employers, and it's essential to get it right. In this post, we'll guide you through the process of writing a professional cover letter for your resume in relaxed English language.

The Pain Points of Writing a Cover Letter

For many job seekers, writing a cover letter can be daunting. It's challenging to strike the right balance between professionalism and personality. Moreover, it's crucial to customize the cover letter for each job application, which can be time-consuming. Even for native English speakers, writing a cover letter can be intimidating, let alone for non-native speakers.

How to Write a Professional Cover Letter for Resume

Here are some tips for writing a professional cover letter for your resume:

  • Start with a strong opening that grabs the reader's attention.
  • Introduce yourself and explain why you're interested in the job.
  • Showcase your skills and achievements that match the job requirements.
  • Explain why you're the best fit for the job and what you can bring to the company.
  • Close with a call to action and thank the employer for considering your application.

Summary of How to Write a Professional Cover Letter for Resume

In summary, writing a professional cover letter for your resume doesn't have to be daunting. By following the tips above, you can craft a cover letter that showcases your skills and personality in a relaxed and professional tone. Remember to customize your cover letter for each job application and proofread it for any errors before submitting.

Understand Your Target: How to Write a Professional Cover Letter for Resume

As a non-native English speaker, I understand the challenges of writing a cover letter that sounds professional yet reflects my personality. When I was applying for my first job in the US, I struggled to express my skills and achievements in a way that resonated with potential employers. However, by following some simple tips, I was able to write a cover letter that got me noticed and ultimately landed me the job.

First, I made sure to research the company and the job requirements thoroughly. This allowed me to tailor my cover letter to the specific job and show how my skills and experience matched the requirements. Second, I used a clear and concise writing style, avoiding jargon and complex sentences. Finally, I asked a native English speaker to proofread my cover letter to ensure it was error-free and sounded natural.

The Importance of Personalization in a Cover Letter

One of the most critical aspects of writing a cover letter is personalization. Employers receive hundreds of applications for each job opening, and a generic cover letter is unlikely to stand out. Instead, take the time to research the company and the job requirements and customize your cover letter accordingly. Use specific examples of your skills and experience that match the job description, and explain how you can contribute to the company's success.

The Power of a Great Opening in a Cover Letter

Your cover letter's opening is your chance to make a great first impression on potential employers. A strong opening should grab the reader's attention and make them want to keep reading. Here are some tips for writing a great opening:

  • Start with an attention-grabbing statement or question.
  • Introduce yourself and explain why you're interested in the job.
  • Show your enthusiasm for the company and the industry.

How to Showcase Your Skills and Achievements in a Cover Letter

One of the most challenging aspects of writing a cover letter is showcasing your skills and achievements. Here are some tips for doing so effectively:

  • Use specific examples of how you've demonstrated the skills required for the job.
  • Quantify your achievements with numbers and statistics.
  • Show how your skills and experience match the job requirements.

Why a Call to Action Matters in a Cover Letter

A call to action is essential in a cover letter as it encourages the employer to take the next step. Here are some tips for writing a strong call to action:

  • Express your enthusiasm for the job and the company.
  • Reiterate your interest in the position and your qualifications.
  • Thank the employer for considering your application.

Question and Answer

Q: Should I include my salary expectations in my cover letter?

A: No, it's not necessary to include your salary expectations in your cover letter. Save that discussion for the interview stage.

Q: How long should my cover letter be?

A: Your cover letter should be no longer than one page. Keep it concise and to the point.

Q: Should I follow up after submitting my cover letter?

A: Yes, it's a good idea to follow up after submitting your cover letter. Send a polite email or make a phone call to inquire about the status of your application.

Q: Can I use a template for my cover letter?

A: Yes, you can use a template for your cover letter, but make sure to customize it for each job application. Avoid using generic templates that don't reflect your personality or match the job requirements.

Conclusion of How to Write a Professional Cover Letter for Resume

Writing a professional cover letter for your resume is an essential part of the job application process. By following the tips above and personalizing your cover letter for each job application, you can showcase your skills and personality in a relaxed and professional tone. Remember to proofread your cover letter for any errors and follow up after submitting your application.
