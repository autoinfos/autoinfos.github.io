What is an Instrument Supervisor?

Instrument supervisors are responsible for overseeing the installation, maintenance, and repair of various types of instruments and control systems. This can include everything from pressure gauges and temperature sensors to programmable logic controllers (PLCs) and distributed control systems (DCS).

Instrument supervisors work in a variety of industries, including oil and gas, petrochemicals, power generation, and manufacturing. They are typically responsible for managing a team of technicians and ensuring that all work is completed safely, efficiently, and in compliance with industry standards.

Qualifications and Requirements

Most instrument supervisor jobs in Abu Dhabi require a degree in electrical or instrumentation engineering, along with several years of experience in the field. Additionally, candidates should have strong leadership skills and be able to work effectively in a team environment.

Other qualifications that may be required include specific certifications or training in areas such as safety, project management, or quality control. It's important to carefully review the job description and ensure that you meet all of the requirements before applying.

Salary and Career Growth

The salary for an instrument supervisor in Abu Dhabi can vary depending on the industry and level of experience. According to Payscale, the average salary for an instrument supervisor in Abu Dhabi is around AED 195,000 per year.
