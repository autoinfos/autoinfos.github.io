Is A Dental Assistant A Good Job In Australia

Written by Christine Aug 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a dental assistant in Australia? If so, you may be wondering if it's a good job choice. While every job has its pros and cons, there are many reasons why becoming a dental assistant can be a smart move.

One pain point for many job seekers is the uncertainty of job security. However, the demand for dental assistants is high and expected to grow over the next decade. According to the Australian Government's Job Outlook website, there are currently over 25,000 dental assistants employed in Australia, and employment growth in this field is projected to increase by 10.7% over the next five years.

So, is a dental assistant a good job in Australia? The answer is yes! Dental assistants play an essential role in dental offices, and their responsibilities include assisting with patient care, sterilizing equipment, and performing administrative tasks.

In summary, becoming a dental assistant in Australia can be a smart career choice. With high demand and job security, it's a field that offers stability and the opportunity to make a difference in patients' lives.

Dental Assistant Training and Certification

Before becoming a dental assistant in Australia, you'll need to complete a dental assisting program that is accredited by the Dental Board of Australia (DBA). These programs typically take one to two years to complete and include both classroom and clinical training.

After completing your training, you'll need to obtain certification from the DBA to legally work as a dental assistant. This involves passing the National Examination for Dental Assistants, which tests your knowledge in areas such as infection control, dental materials, and oral health education.

Personally, I found my dental assistant training to be both challenging and rewarding. The hands-on clinical experience gave me the confidence I needed to excel in my role, and the classroom training provided me with a strong foundation in dental theory and practice.

The Benefits of Being a Dental Assistant

Aside from job security, there are many benefits to working as a dental assistant in Australia. These include:

1. A flexible schedule

Many dental offices offer flexible working hours, which can be a significant benefit for those who require a work-life balance.

2. The opportunity to work in a professional environment

Dental offices are typically clean, modern, and professional environments, which can be an attractive feature for many job seekers.

3. The chance to help others

As a dental assistant, you'll be working directly with patients, and you'll have the opportunity to make a difference in their lives by improving their oral health and helping to alleviate their dental fears.

4. Career advancement opportunities

With experience, dental assistants can advance to roles such as dental practice manager or even dental hygienist, which typically offer higher salaries and more responsibilities.

FAQs About Becoming a Dental Assistant in Australia

1. How much does a dental assistant make in Australia?

According to the Australian Government's Job Outlook website, the average salary for a dental assistant in Australia is $55,000 per year.

2. What skills do I need to become a dental assistant?

Some essential skills for dental assistants include attention to detail, good communication skills, and the ability to work well in a team.

3. What is the job outlook for dental assistants in Australia?

The job outlook for dental assistants in Australia is strong, with employment growth projected to increase by 10.7% over the next five years.

4. How long does it take to become a dental assistant in Australia?

Most dental assisting programs in Australia take one to two years to complete, depending on the level of qualification you're pursuing.

Conclusion of Is a Dental Assistant a Good Job in Australia

Overall, becoming a dental assistant in Australia can be a smart career choice. With high demand, job security, and the opportunity to make a difference in patients' lives, it's a field that offers stability and professional growth.
