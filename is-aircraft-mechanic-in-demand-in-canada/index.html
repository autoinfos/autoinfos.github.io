Is Aircraft Mechanic In Demand In Canada

Written by Robby Jun 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever considered becoming an aircraft mechanic? If you're interested in aviation and have a knack for fixing things, this could be the perfect career for you. But is aircraft mechanic in demand in Canada?

With the aviation industry constantly growing and evolving, there is a high demand for skilled aircraft mechanics. However, with this demand comes a shortage of qualified professionals, making it difficult for companies to fill open positions.

The short answer is yes, aircraft mechanic is in demand in Canada. In fact, it's projected that the demand for aircraft mechanics will continue to grow over the next decade due to the aging workforce and advancements in technology.

What is Aircraft Mechanic?

Aircraft mechanics are responsible for ensuring the safety and airworthiness of aircraft. They inspect, troubleshoot, and repair various systems and components, including engines, brakes, landing gear, and electrical systems.

Becoming an aircraft mechanic requires specialized training and certification. In Canada, you must complete a Transport Canada-approved program and pass an exam to become certified. Once certified, you can work in a variety of settings, including airports, maintenance repair organizations, and manufacturing companies.

Personal Experience

As an aircraft mechanic myself, I can attest to the high demand for skilled professionals in this field. I've worked for various companies in Canada, and have always had job offers coming my way. The aviation industry is constantly evolving, which means there's always something new to learn and new challenges to overcome.

The Benefits of Becoming an Aircraft Mechanic

Besides the high demand for skilled professionals, there are numerous benefits to becoming an aircraft mechanic. For one, it's an incredibly rewarding career where you get to see the direct impact of your work. You'll also have the opportunity to work on a variety of aircraft, from small single-engine planes to large commercial airliners.

Additionally, the pay for aircraft mechanics is quite competitive. According to the Government of Canada's Job Bank, the median hourly wage for aircraft mechanics is $32.00. This can increase with experience and additional certifications.

Advancements in Technology

As mentioned earlier, advancements in technology are driving the demand for skilled aircraft mechanics. With new aircraft models and systems being developed, mechanics must stay up-to-date with the latest training and certifications to remain competitive in the job market.

How to Become an Aircraft Mechanic

If you're interested in becoming an aircraft mechanic, the first step is to research Transport Canada-approved training programs in your area. These programs typically take 18-24 months to complete and consist of both classroom and hands-on training.

Once you've completed your training, you'll need to pass an exam to become certified. From there, you can start applying for jobs in the aviation industry.

Personal Experience

When I was first starting out as an aircraft mechanic, I found the certification process to be challenging but rewarding. The hands-on training was especially valuable, as it allowed me to gain real-world experience working on aircraft systems and components.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the job outlook for aircraft mechanics in Canada?

A: The job outlook for aircraft mechanics in Canada is positive, with a projected growth rate of 7% over the next decade.

Q: What skills do I need to become an aircraft mechanic?

A: To become an aircraft mechanic, you'll need to have a strong understanding of mechanics and electrical systems, as well as good problem-solving skills and attention to detail.

Q: How much can I expect to earn as an aircraft mechanic?

A: According to the Government of Canada's Job Bank, the median hourly wage for aircraft mechanics is $32.00.

Q: What are some of the challenges of working as an aircraft mechanic?

A: Working as an aircraft mechanic can be physically demanding, and you may be required to work irregular hours or be on call for emergencies.

Conclusion of Is Aircraft Mechanic in Demand in Canada

If you're considering a career as an aircraft mechanic, now is a great time to get started. With the demand for skilled professionals on the rise and numerous benefits to this rewarding career, becoming an aircraft mechanic could be the perfect fit for you.
