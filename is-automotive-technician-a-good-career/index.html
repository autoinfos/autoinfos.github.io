Is Automotive Technician A Good Career Everything You Need To Know

Written by Frank Oct 11, 2023 · 4 min read

Do you love cars? Are you interested in fixing them and making them run smoothly? Then a career as an automotive technician might be perfect for you. But the question is, is automotive technician a good career choice? In this post, we’ll explore everything you need to know about pursuing a career in automotive technology.

One of the biggest concerns for those considering this career is job security. With the rise of electric cars and self-driving technology, many worry that traditional automotive technicians will become obsolete. Additionally, the physical demands of the job can be a challenge for some individuals.

However, despite these concerns, the demand for skilled automotive technicians is expected to remain steady in the coming years. As long as there are cars on the road, there will be a need for mechanics to repair and maintain them. And while the job can be physically demanding, advancements in technology have made it easier to diagnose and fix issues.

In summary, yes, automotive technician is a good career choice for those who are passionate about cars and interested in pursuing a hands-on career. While there are challenges to the job, the demand for skilled technicians is expected to remain strong in the coming years.

Personal Experience with Pursuing Automotive Technology

When I was in high school, I knew I loved cars but wasn’t sure what career path to pursue. After doing some research and talking to professionals in the field, I decided to pursue a career as an automotive technician. I attended a technical school for two years and learned everything from basic maintenance to complex electrical systems.

After graduation, I landed a job at a local dealership and have been working there for the past five years. While the job can be physically demanding at times, I love the satisfaction of diagnosing and fixing issues and seeing a customer drive away happy. Plus, the demand for skilled technicians means there is always room for growth and advancement in my career.

Salary and Job Outlook for Automotive Technicians

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 in May 2020. The top 10 percent earned more than $70,820, while the bottom 10 percent earned less than $25,000.

Additionally, the job outlook for automotive technicians is positive. The Bureau of Labor Statistics projects employment of automotive service technicians and mechanics to grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029, about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Education and Training for Automotive Technicians

While some individuals may learn the necessary skills on the job, most employers prefer to hire those with formal training. Technical schools and community colleges offer programs in automotive technology, and some manufacturers even offer specialized training for their vehicles.

Additionally, certification from the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) can increase job prospects and earning potential for automotive technicians.

How to Succeed as an Automotive Technician

Success in this field requires a combination of technical skill and customer service. Automotive technicians must have a deep understanding of how cars work and be able to diagnose and fix issues efficiently. Additionally, strong communication skills and the ability to explain complex issues to customers is essential.

Continuing education and staying up-to-date on the latest advancements in automotive technology is also crucial for success in this field.

Conclusion of Is Automotive Technician a Good Career
