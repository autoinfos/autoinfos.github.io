Is Electrical Trade A Good Career Exploring The Pros And Cons

Written by Daniel Aug 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you been considering a career in the electrical trade but aren't sure if it's the right path for you? It's important to weigh the pros and cons before making any decisions. In this blog post, we'll explore the benefits and drawbacks of pursuing a career in the electrical trade.

One of the main pain points associated with the electrical trade is the physical demands of the job. Electricians often have to work in cramped spaces, climb ladders and work on their feet for long periods of time. Additionally, the job can be dangerous if proper safety measures aren't taken.

However, if you have a passion for problem-solving and enjoy working with your hands, a career in the electrical trade can be incredibly rewarding. Electricians play a vital role in ensuring that homes and businesses have safe and reliable electrical systems. The job also offers a high level of job security and the potential for a lucrative income.

In summary, the electrical trade can be a good career choice for individuals who are physically fit, enjoy hands-on work and have a strong interest in electrical systems. However, it's important to be aware of the physical demands and safety risks associated with the job.

What is the Electrical Trade?

The electrical trade encompasses a wide range of professions, including electricians, electrical engineers, and electrical contractors. These professionals work with electrical systems in a variety of settings, including residential, commercial, and industrial environments.

As an electrician, you would be responsible for installing, repairing and maintaining electrical systems. This could include everything from replacing light switches to rewiring an entire building. Electrical engineers, on the other hand, design and develop electrical systems, while electrical contractors oversee the installation and maintenance of electrical systems.

My Personal Experience

As someone who has worked in the electrical trade for several years, I can attest to the fact that it can be a highly rewarding career. I enjoy the problem-solving aspect of the job and the sense of satisfaction that comes from knowing that I'm helping to keep people safe.

That being said, the job can be physically demanding, and it's important to take proper safety precautions. It's also crucial to stay up-to-date on the latest technology and techniques in order to provide the best possible service to clients.

The Pros and Cons of Pursuing a Career in the Electrical Trade

There are several pros and cons to consider when deciding whether or not to pursue a career in the electrical trade.

Pros

One of the biggest pros of the electrical trade is the potential for a high income. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for electricians was $56,180 in May 2020. Additionally, the job offers a high level of job security, as there is always a need for skilled electricians.

Another advantage of the electrical trade is the opportunity for hands-on work. If you enjoy working with your hands and solving problems, this could be an ideal career path for you. Additionally, the job offers opportunities for growth and advancement, whether that be through additional training or moving up the ranks to a supervisory role.

Cons

One of the biggest cons of the electrical trade is the potential for injury. Electricians work with high voltage electrical systems, which can be dangerous if proper safety measures aren't taken. Additionally, the job can be physically demanding, requiring electricians to work in cramped spaces and on their feet for long periods of time.

Another potential drawback of the job is the need for continuing education. In order to stay up-to-date on the latest technology and techniques, electricians need to be constantly learning and improving their skills. This can be time-consuming and expensive.

FAQs About Pursuing a Career in the Electrical Trade

Q: What education do I need to become an electrician?

A: In order to become an electrician, you typically need to complete an apprenticeship program or attend a trade school. Some states also require electricians to be licensed.

Q: What skills do I need to be successful in the electrical trade?

A: In addition to technical skills, successful electricians need to have strong problem-solving abilities, attention to detail, and good communication skills.

Q: What are the job prospects for electricians?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of electricians is projected to grow 8 percent from 2020 to 2030, faster than the average for all occupations.

Q: How much can I expect to earn as an electrician?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for electricians was $56,180 in May 2020.

Conclusion

So, is electrical trade a good career? The answer ultimately depends on your personal interests and goals. If you enjoy hands-on work and have a passion for electrical systems, it can be a highly rewarding career with opportunities for growth and advancement. However, it's important to be aware of the physical demands and safety risks associated with the job, as well as the need for continuing education. With the right training and mindset, a career in the electrical trade can be a fulfilling and lucrative choice.
