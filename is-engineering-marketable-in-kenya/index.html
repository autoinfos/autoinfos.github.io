Are you considering pursuing a career in engineering in Kenya but unsure if it's a marketable field? You're not alone. Many students and young professionals are wondering if engineering is a viable career choice in Kenya. In this post, we'll explore the current state of the engineering job market in Kenya and provide insights into whether engineering is a marketable field.

With the rise of technology and the need for infrastructure development, engineering has been a popular field in Kenya for many years. However, the current job market for engineers in Kenya is highly competitive. Employers are looking for candidates with not only technical skills but also soft skills such as communication, teamwork, and problem-solving abilities.

Despite the competitive job market, engineering is still a highly marketable field in Kenya. According to a report by the World Bank, the demand for engineers in Kenya is expected to grow by 10% annually. This is due to the country's focus on infrastructure development, including the construction of roads, bridges, and buildings. Additionally, the growth of the technology sector in Kenya has increased the demand for software and hardware engineers.

In summary, engineering is a marketable field in Kenya, but it's important to have the right skills and qualifications to stand out in a highly competitive job market.

Skills and Qualifications Needed for Engineering Jobs in Kenya

To be successful in the engineering job market in Kenya, it's important to have not only technical skills but also soft skills such as communication, teamwork, and problem-solving abilities. Employers are looking for candidates who can work well with others and effectively communicate their ideas.

Additionally, a degree in engineering is typically required for most engineering jobs in Kenya. Some popular engineering fields in Kenya include civil engineering, electrical engineering, and mechanical engineering. It's also important to stay up-to-date with the latest technologies and developments in your field to remain competitive.
