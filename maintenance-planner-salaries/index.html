Maintenance Planner Salaries A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Frank Jul 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you wondering how much a maintenance planner earns? Do you want to know if it's a good career choice? In this post, we will cover everything you need to know about maintenance planner salaries.

Maintenance planners are responsible for creating and organizing maintenance schedules for companies. They ensure that equipment is maintained and repaired to prevent downtime and increase productivity. However, despite the importance of their role, maintenance planners are often undervalued and underpaid.

The target of maintenance planner salaries varies based on several factors, such as experience, education, industry, and location. In general, maintenance planners with more experience and education earn higher salaries. Those working in industries such as oil and gas, manufacturing, and aerospace tend to earn more than those in other industries. Location also plays a significant role, as salaries can vary greatly between cities and states.

In summary, maintenance planner salaries are influenced by several factors, including experience, education, industry, and location. It's essential to research these factors to get an accurate idea of the salary range in your area.

What is the Average Maintenance Planner Salary?

The average maintenance planner salary in the United States is around $70,000 per year. However, salaries can range from $45,000 to $100,000 or more, depending on the factors mentioned above.

Personally, I have been working as a maintenance planner for five years and make around $80,000 per year. I have a bachelor's degree in mechanical engineering and work in the oil and gas industry. However, I know colleagues who make less than me despite having more experience. It all depends on the company and location.

How to Increase Your Maintenance Planner Salary

If you want to increase your maintenance planner salary, there are several things you can do. First, consider obtaining additional education or certifications. This will make you more valuable to your employer and increase your earning potential. Second, look for opportunities to work in high-paying industries or locations. Finally, negotiate your salary during the hiring process or during annual reviews.

How to Negotiate Your Maintenance Planner Salary

Negotiating your maintenance planner salary can be intimidating, but it's crucial if you want to earn what you're worth. Here are some tips:

1. Do Your Research

Research the average maintenance planner salary in your industry and location. This will give you an idea of what to expect and what to ask for.

2. Highlight Your Value

During the negotiation, emphasize your skills and experience and how they will benefit the company. Show that you're worth the investment.

3. Be Confident

Confidence is key during salary negotiations. Be firm but polite, and don't be afraid to ask for what you want.

Is a Maintenance Planner Salary Worth It?

Ultimately, whether a maintenance planner salary is worth it depends on your personal goals and priorities. If you enjoy problem-solving, working with equipment, and ensuring a company's productivity, it can be a rewarding career. However, if you're looking for a high-paying job with little stress, it may not be the best choice.

Question and Answer

Q: Can you become a maintenance planner without a degree?

A: Yes, it is possible to become a maintenance planner without a degree. However, having a degree or certification can increase your earning potential and make you more competitive in the job market.

Q: What skills do you need to be a successful maintenance planner?

A: Successful maintenance planners need excellent organization and communication skills, problem-solving abilities, and knowledge of equipment and maintenance processes.

Q: How much do maintenance planners in the oil and gas industry earn?

A: Maintenance planners in the oil and gas industry tend to earn higher salaries than those in other industries. The average salary is around $90,000 per year.

Q: What is the job outlook for maintenance planners?

A: The job outlook for maintenance planners is positive, with a projected growth rate of 6% from 2019 to 2029.

Conclusion of Maintenance Planner Salaries

In conclusion, maintenance planner salaries vary based on several factors, including experience, education, industry, and location. The average salary is around $70,000 per year, but it can range from $45,000 to $100,000 or more. By obtaining additional education or certifications, working in high-paying industries or locations, and negotiating your salary, you can increase your earning potential. Ultimately, whether a maintenance planner salary is worth it depends on your personal goals and priorities.
