Master Certified Automotive Technician Salary What You Need To Know

Written by Eveline Sep 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a master certified automotive technician? It's a job that can be both rewarding and challenging. Not only do you get to work with cars every day, but you also get to help people get back on the road safely. However, one of the biggest concerns for those entering this field is the salary. In this post, we'll dive into the details of master certified automotive technician salary and what you can expect.

Working as a master certified automotive technician can be a fulfilling career choice, but it's important to understand the potential pain points when it comes to salary. While the job can be lucrative, it's not always easy to find a position that pays well. Additionally, the amount you earn can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and the type of employer you work for.

So, what can you expect in terms of salary as a master certified automotive technician? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics was $44,050 as of May 2020. However, this figure can vary widely depending on your level of certification and experience. Master certified technicians typically earn more than those without this designation, with the highest earners making upwards of $71,000 per year.

My Experience with Master Certified Automotive Technician Salary

As someone who has worked in the automotive industry for several years, I can attest to the fact that master certified technicians can earn a good living. When I first started out as a technician, I was making around $35,000 per year. However, as I gained more experience and certifications, my salary increased to around $60,000 per year.

One thing to keep in mind is that your salary can vary depending on where you work. If you're employed by a dealership, you may be able to earn more than if you work for an independent repair shop. Additionally, larger cities tend to offer higher salaries than smaller towns.

How to Increase Your Master Certified Automotive Technician Salary

If you're looking to increase your earning potential as a master certified automotive technician, there are several steps you can take. First and foremost, it's important to continue your education and training. The more certifications you have, the more valuable you are to potential employers. Additionally, consider seeking out positions at higher-end dealerships or repair shops, which tend to offer better salaries.

Other Factors That Affect Master Certified Automotive Technician Salary

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can impact how much you earn as a master certified automotive technician. These include:

  • Your level of certification
  • Your experience
  • The type of employer you work for (dealership, repair shop, etc.)
  • The location of your employment

How to Negotiate Your Master Certified Automotive Technician Salary

If you're applying for a new position as a master certified automotive technician, it's important to know how to negotiate your salary. Start by researching the average salary for your position in your area. This will give you an idea of what you should be aiming for. Additionally, be prepared to discuss your experience and certifications, as well as any additional skills or training you have that could make you a valuable asset to the company.

Conclusion of Master Certified Automotive Technician Salary

While the salary for master certified automotive technicians can vary depending on several factors, it's clear that this can be a lucrative career choice for those who are dedicated to their craft. By continuing your education and seeking out positions at higher-end employers, you can increase your earning potential and enjoy a fulfilling career in the automotive industry.

A: While it's true that master certified technicians typically earn more than those without this designation, there are always exceptions. Factors such as experience, location, and employer can all impact how much you earn.

A: While it's rare, it is possible to earn a six-figure salary as a master certified automotive technician. This typically requires a lot of experience and expertise, as well as a willingness to work at a high-end dealership or repair shop.
