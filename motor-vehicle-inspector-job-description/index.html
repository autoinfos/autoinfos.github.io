What Does A Motor Vehicle Inspector Do A Detailed Job Description

Written by Aprilia Nov 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever wondered what a motor vehicle inspector does? Do you know the important role they play in ensuring the safety of our roads? Well, in this post we will dive into the details of a motor vehicle inspector job description and explain everything you need to know.

As a motor vehicle inspector, you are responsible for inspecting vehicles to ensure they meet safety and emissions standards set by the government. This job can be challenging and requires a keen eye for detail, as well as knowledge of state laws and regulations.

The primary target of a motor vehicle inspector job description is to ensure that all vehicles on the road meet safety and emissions standards. This is important because it helps reduce the number of accidents caused by faulty vehicles, and it also helps protect the environment by reducing harmful emissions.

In summary, a motor vehicle inspector is responsible for inspecting vehicles to ensure they meet safety and emissions standards set by the government. This job requires knowledge of state laws and regulations, as well as a keen eye for detail. The primary goal of this job is to ensure the safety of our roads and protect the environment by reducing harmful emissions.

What are the Duties of a Motor Vehicle Inspector?

During my time as a motor vehicle inspector, I had several duties that were crucial to my job. One of my main responsibilities was to inspect vehicles for safety and emissions standards. This involved checking the brakes, steering, suspension, tires, and exhaust system, among other things.

Another duty was to keep up-to-date with state laws and regulations regarding vehicle inspections. This meant attending training sessions and workshops to stay current with any changes in the law.

What are the Requirements to Become a Motor Vehicle Inspector?

To become a motor vehicle inspector, you must have a high school diploma or equivalent. You must also pass a written exam and a hands-on inspection test to obtain a certification.

Additionally, you must have a valid driver's license and a clean driving record. You must also be able to lift heavy objects, as some inspections require the use of equipment such as a hydraulic lift.

What Skills are Needed to be a Successful Motor Vehicle Inspector?

There are several skills that are required to be a successful motor vehicle inspector. These include attention to detail, critical thinking, problem-solving, and good communication skills. It is also important to have knowledge of state laws and regulations regarding vehicle inspections.

How to Prepare for a Motor Vehicle Inspector Exam?

If you are interested in becoming a motor vehicle inspector, there are several things you can do to prepare for the exam. First, study the state laws and regulations regarding vehicle inspections. You can find this information on your state's DMV website.

Next, enroll in a training program that will help you prepare for the exam. These programs are usually offered by community colleges or vocational schools.

What is the Salary of a Motor Vehicle Inspector?

The salary of a motor vehicle inspector can vary depending on the state and the level of experience. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive service technicians and mechanics, which includes motor vehicle inspectors, was $44,050 in May 2020.

Conclusion of Motor Vehicle Inspector Job Description

In conclusion, a motor vehicle inspector plays an important role in ensuring the safety of our roads and protecting the environment. To become a motor vehicle inspector, you must have a high school diploma or equivalent, pass a written exam and a hands-on inspection test, and have a valid driver's license and clean driving record. The job requires attention to detail, critical thinking, problem-solving, and good communication skills, as well as knowledge of state laws and regulations.

Question and Answer

Q: What are some common reasons for failing a vehicle inspection?
A: Some common reasons for failing a vehicle inspection include faulty brakes, worn tires, malfunctioning lights, and emissions that exceed the legal limit.

Q: How often do vehicles need to be inspected?
A: The frequency of vehicle inspections varies by state. Some states require annual inspections, while others require bi-annual inspections.

Q: Can a vehicle pass inspection with a check engine light on?
A: No, a vehicle cannot pass inspection with a check engine light on. This is because the check engine light indicates that there is a problem with the vehicle's emissions system, which is a critical component of the inspection process.

Q: What happens if a vehicle fails inspection?
A: If a vehicle fails inspection, the owner must have the necessary repairs made and then have the vehicle re-inspected. Depending on the state, there may be a time limit for completing the repairs and re-inspection.
