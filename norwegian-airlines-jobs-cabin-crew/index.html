Why Norwegian Airlines Jobs Cabin Crew Could Be Your Dream Career

Written by Christine Nov 11, 2023 · 4 min read

Being a cabin crew member for Norwegian Airlines can be a highly rewarding and exciting career. However, like any job, it comes with its own unique set of challenges and experiences. If you are considering a career as a Norwegian Airlines cabin crew member, it is important to understand what you may encounter on the job.

One of the biggest challenges of being a cabin crew member is dealing with difficult passengers. Whether it’s a passenger who is angry about a delay or a passenger who is unruly during the flight, cabin crew members are trained to handle these situations in a professional and calm manner. Additionally, cabin crew members must be able to work long hours and be away from home for extended periods of time.

However, the benefits of working as a Norwegian Airlines cabin crew member are numerous. Cabin crew members have the opportunity to travel to exciting destinations all over the world, meet new people, and gain valuable customer service and communication skills.

In this article, we will go over the requirements for becoming a Norwegian Airlines cabin crew member, the training process, and the day-to-day responsibilities of the job.

Requirements for Norwegian Airlines Jobs Cabin Crew

To become a Norwegian Airlines cabin crew member, candidates must meet several requirements. These include:

- Being at least 18 years old

- Being able to swim

- Being able to pass a medical examination

- Having a high school diploma or equivalent

- Being fluent in English and another language (preferably Norwegian or a Scandinavian language)

In addition to these requirements, candidates must also have excellent customer service skills, be able to work well in a team, and have a positive attitude.

Training for Norwegian Airlines Jobs Cabin Crew

The training process for Norwegian Airlines cabin crew members is extensive and covers a wide range of topics. Some of the topics covered during training include:

- Safety and emergency procedures

- First aid and medical emergencies

- Customer service and communication skills

- Food and beverage service

Training is conducted both in the classroom and in a simulated aircraft environment. Cabin crew members must pass all aspects of the training program in order to be certified to work as a Norwegian Airlines cabin crew member.

Day-to-Day Responsibilities of Norwegian Airlines Jobs Cabin Crew

The day-to-day responsibilities of a Norwegian Airlines cabin crew member can vary depending on the flight and destination. However, some of the common responsibilities include:

- Greeting passengers as they board the aircraft

- Conducting safety demonstrations and providing safety information to passengers

- Assisting passengers with their luggage and seating

- Serving food and beverages to passengers

- Monitoring the cabin during the flight for any safety or security issues

How to Apply for Norwegian Airlines Jobs Cabin Crew

If you are interested in becoming a Norwegian Airlines cabin crew member, you can apply through the Norwegian Airlines careers website. The application process includes submitting a resume and cover letter, completing an online application form, and attending an assessment day.

During the assessment day, candidates will be evaluated on their customer service skills, communication skills, and ability to work in a team. Successful candidates will then be invited to participate in the training program.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the salary for a Norwegian Airlines cabin crew member?

A: The salary for a Norwegian Airlines cabin crew member varies based on experience and other factors. However, according to Glassdoor, the average salary for a Norwegian Airlines cabin crew member is around $43,000 per year.

Q: What is the uniform for Norwegian Airlines cabin crew members?

A: The uniform for Norwegian Airlines cabin crew members includes a navy blue suit for men and a navy blue dress for women. The uniform also includes a scarf, tie, or ascot, depending on the season.

Q: How long is the training program for Norwegian Airlines cabin crew members?

A: The training program for Norwegian Airlines cabin crew members is approximately six weeks long.

Q: What is the work schedule like for Norwegian Airlines cabin crew members?

A: The work schedule for Norwegian Airlines cabin crew members can vary significantly. Cabin crew members may work early morning or late night flights, and may be away from home for several days at a time.

Conclusion of Norwegian Airlines Jobs Cabin Crew

Becoming a Norwegian Airlines cabin crew member can be a challenging but rewarding career. By meeting the requirements, completing the training program, and demonstrating excellent customer service skills, you can be on your way to a fulfilling career as a cabin crew member for Norwegian Airlines.
