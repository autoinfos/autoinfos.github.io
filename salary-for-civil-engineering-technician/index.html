Understanding The Salary For Civil Engineering Technicians

Written by Frank Aug 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career as a civil engineering technician but unsure about the salary prospects? Let's explore the salary range and factors that determine pay in this field.

One of the biggest concerns for those starting a career as a civil engineering technician is the salary. While it's true that pay can vary based on location, experience, and education, it's important to understand what the typical salary range is.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for civil engineering technicians in the United States is $53,410. However, this can vary from as low as $32,940 per year to as high as $81,320 per year depending on the factors mentioned earlier.

In summary, the salary for civil engineering technicians can range from $32,940 to $81,320 per year, with the median annual salary being $53,410.

The Importance of Education and Experience in Salary for Civil Engineering Technicians

Education and experience are two of the biggest factors that can determine a civil engineering technician's salary. With an associate's degree in civil engineering technology, you can expect to earn on the lower end of the salary range. However, if you have a bachelor's degree and several years of experience in the field, you can expect to earn towards the higher end of the range.

Personally, I started my career as a civil engineering technician with an associate's degree and earned around $35,000 per year. However, after obtaining a bachelor's degree and gaining more experience, my salary increased to around $60,000 per year.

The Impact of Location on Salary for Civil Engineering Technicians

Location is another factor that can impact a civil engineering technician's salary. Generally, areas with high costs of living tend to pay higher salaries. For example, a civil engineering technician in California can expect to earn a higher salary than one in a rural area of the Midwest.

However, it's important to consider the cost of living when looking at salaries. A higher salary in a high-cost area may not go as far as a lower salary in a more affordable area.

The Role of Certification in Salary for Civil Engineering Technicians

Certification is another factor that can impact a civil engineering technician's salary. The National Institute for Certification in Engineering Technologies offers certifications for civil engineering technicians, which can demonstrate a level of expertise in the field.

Having a certification can make you a more competitive candidate for jobs and can potentially lead to a higher salary. However, it's important to note that certification is not always required for employment as a civil engineering technician.

Negotiating Salary for Civil Engineering Technicians

When it comes to negotiating your salary as a civil engineering technician, it's important to do your research and understand the factors that can impact pay in your area. Don't be afraid to ask for a higher salary if you have a strong education and experience in the field. However, it's important to also consider the cost of living and the company's budget when negotiating.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the job outlook for civil engineering technicians?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of civil engineering technicians is projected to grow 5 percent from 2019 to 2029, which is about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Q: Can civil engineering technicians advance to higher-paying positions?

A: Yes, civil engineering technicians can advance to higher-paying positions such as civil engineers with additional education and experience in the field.

Q: What industries typically pay the highest salaries for civil engineering technicians?

A: The highest-paying industries for civil engineering technicians include architectural, engineering, and related services, followed by state and local government and utility system construction.

Q: Are there opportunities for overtime pay as a civil engineering technician?

A: Yes, overtime pay is common for civil engineering technicians, especially when working on projects with tight deadlines.

Conclusion of Salary for Civil Engineering Technicians

While the salary for civil engineering technicians can vary based on a number of factors, including education, experience, location, and certification, the median annual salary in the United States is around $53,410. It's important to research the factors that can impact pay in your area and negotiate for a higher salary if you have a strong education and experience in the field.
