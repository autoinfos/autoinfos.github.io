Are you a science enthusiast looking to make a difference in the education sector? Have you considered a career as a school laboratory technician in Kenya? If you're interested in a fulfilling job that allows you to work closely with students and teachers, while also utilizing your scientific knowledge, then a school laboratory technician job might be the perfect fit for you. In this post, we will explore everything you need to know about school laboratory technician jobs in Kenya in 2020.

Working as a school laboratory technician can be a challenging role. You will need to have a passion for science and education, as well as excellent communication and organizational skills. Additionally, you will be responsible for ensuring that laboratory equipment is properly maintained, that experiments are set up correctly, and that students are safe while conducting experiments. As such, this role requires a high level of responsibility and attention to detail.

If you're interested in pursuing a career as a school laboratory technician in Kenya, there are a few key requirements that you will need to meet. Firstly, you will need to have a relevant degree or diploma in a scientific field, such as chemistry or biology. Additionally, you will need to have experience working in a laboratory environment, as well as a good understanding of laboratory safety and procedures.

The Target of School Laboratory Technician Jobs in Kenya 2020

The target of school laboratory technician jobs in Kenya in 2020 is to provide students with a safe and engaging laboratory experience that enhances their understanding of science. School laboratory technicians are responsible for maintaining laboratory equipment, setting up experiments, and ensuring that students are safe while conducting experiments. Additionally, they work closely with teachers to develop lesson plans and create engaging experiments that help students to learn about science in a practical and hands-on way.

Personal Experience with School Laboratory Technician Jobs in Kenya 2020

As a former school laboratory technician in Kenya, I can attest to the challenging yet rewarding nature of this role. It was incredibly fulfilling to work with students and teachers to develop engaging experiments that helped students to understand complex scientific concepts in a practical way. Additionally, I enjoyed working with a variety of laboratory equipment and ensuring that everything was properly maintained and safe for student use.

Key Responsibilities of School Laboratory Technician Jobs in Kenya 2020

Some of the key responsibilities of school laboratory technician jobs in Kenya in 2020 include:

1. Maintaining laboratory equipment

School laboratory technicians are responsible for ensuring that laboratory equipment is properly maintained and in good working order. This includes regular cleaning and calibration of equipment, as well as identifying and addressing any issues that arise.

2. Setting up experiments

School laboratory technicians work closely with teachers to set up experiments that align with the curriculum and help students to learn about science in a practical way. This involves preparing materials, setting up equipment, and ensuring that everything is safe for student use.

3. Ensuring laboratory safety

Safety is a top priority in any laboratory environment, and school laboratory technicians are responsible for ensuring that students are safe while conducting experiments. This involves identifying any potential hazards and taking steps to mitigate them, as well as ensuring that students are aware of laboratory safety procedures.

FAQs about School Laboratory Technician Jobs in Kenya 2020

Q: What qualifications do I need to become a school laboratory technician in Kenya?

A: To become a school laboratory technician in Kenya, you will need to have a relevant degree or diploma in a scientific field, as well as experience working in a laboratory environment.

Q: What skills do I need to succeed as a school laboratory technician in Kenya?

A: To succeed as a school laboratory technician in Kenya, you will need to have excellent communication and organizational skills, as well as a passion for science and education. Additionally, you will need to be detail-oriented and safety-conscious.

Q: What is the salary range for school laboratory technicians in Kenya?

A: The salary range for school laboratory technicians in Kenya varies depending on the level of experience and qualifications. However, on average, school laboratory technicians in Kenya can expect to earn between KES 30,000 and KES 60,000 per month.

A: Some of the challenges of working as a school laboratory technician in Kenya include maintaining laboratory equipment, ensuring laboratory safety, and working with a variety of students and teachers with different levels of scientific knowledge.

Conclusion of School Laboratory Technician Jobs in Kenya 2020

School laboratory technician jobs in Kenya in 2020 are a great opportunity for science enthusiasts looking to make a difference in the education sector.
