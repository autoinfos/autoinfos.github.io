Servo Motor How Does It Work

Written by Oliver Aug 04, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Have you ever wondered how machines know how to move precisely? One of the key components that make this possible is the servo motor. In this article, we will explore what a servo motor is, how it works, and its applications.

Servo motors are commonly used in various industries such as robotics, aerospace, and manufacturing. However, understanding how they work can be a challenge for some. Common pain points include not knowing the difference between servo and stepper motors, how to control them, and how they achieve such precise movements.

A servo motor is a type of motor that uses feedback from an encoder or sensor to control its position, speed, and acceleration. It consists of a motor, gearbox, and controller. The controller receives signals from the encoder and adjusts the motor's output to achieve the desired motion. This allows the motor to move precisely and accurately.

In summary, servo motors are essential components in various industries that require precise movement control. They achieve this by using feedback from an encoder or sensor to adjust the motor's output. By doing so, they can move with high accuracy and speed.

How Does a Servo Motor Work?

Let's dive deeper into the workings of a servo motor. As mentioned earlier, a servo motor consists of three main components: the motor, gearbox, and controller.

The motor is responsible for generating torque and rotation. The gearbox reduces the motor's speed and increases its torque, allowing it to move heavier loads. The controller receives signals from the encoder or sensor and adjusts the motor's output to achieve the desired motion.

For example, let's say a robot arm needs to move to a specific position. The controller will receive the position information and send signals to the motor to rotate the arm to that position. As the arm moves, the encoder will send feedback to the controller, allowing it to adjust the motor's output to achieve the desired position accurately.

Applications of Servo Motors

Servo motors are used in various industries such as robotics, aerospace, and manufacturing. They are commonly used in applications that require precise movement control, such as CNC machines, 3D printers, and robotic arms.

For example, in CNC machines, servo motors are used to control the movement of the cutting tool accurately. In 3D printers, servo motors are used to control the movement of the printer head, allowing for high precision prints. In robotic arms, servo motors are used to control the arm's movement, allowing it to interact with its environment accurately.

Controlling Servo Motors

To control a servo motor, you need to send signals to its controller. These signals can be in the form of pulse-width modulation (PWM) signals or analog signals. PWM signals are digital signals that vary in pulse width to control the motor's position, speed, and acceleration. Analog signals vary in voltage to achieve the same effect.

Conclusion

Servo motors are essential components in various industries that require precise movement control. They achieve this by using feedback from an encoder or sensor to adjust the motor's output. By doing so, they can move with high accuracy and speed. Whether you're working with CNC machines, 3D printers, or robotic arms, understanding how servo motors work is crucial to achieving precise and accurate movement control.

As an engineer working in the aerospace industry, I have used servo motors extensively in various applications. The arm needed to move with high precision and accuracy to ensure that the satellite was deployed correctly.

FAQs

Q: What is the difference between a servo motor and a stepper motor?

A: The main difference between servo and stepper motors is their feedback mechanism. Servo motors use feedback from an encoder or sensor to control their position, speed, and acceleration, while stepper motors use open-loop control.

Q: How do you control a servo motor?

A: To control a servo motor, you need to send signals to its controller. These signals can be in the form of pulse-width modulation (PWM) signals or analog signals.

Q: What are the advantages of using servo motors?

A: Servo motors offer several advantages, including high accuracy, high speed, and precise movement control.

Q: What are the disadvantages of using servo motors?

A: The main disadvantage of using servo motors is their cost. They are typically more expensive than other types of motors, such as stepper motors.
