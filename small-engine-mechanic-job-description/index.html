Small Engine Mechanic Job Description: Everything You Need to Know

If you love working with your hands and are passionate about fixing things, then becoming a small engine mechanic might be the perfect career for you. However, before you jump in, it's essential to understand what the job entails and whether it's the right fit for you.

Small engine mechanics are responsible for repairing and maintaining small engines, such as those found in lawnmowers, generators, and chainsaws. They diagnose problems, replace defective parts, and perform routine maintenance to ensure that equipment operates efficiently.

In this post, we'll take a closer look at the small engine mechanic job description, including the skills required, job duties, and career outlook.

What Does a Small Engine Mechanic Do?

As we mentioned earlier, small engine mechanics are responsible for repairing and maintaining small engines. However, their job duties go beyond just fixing broken equipment. Here are some of the tasks that small engine mechanics perform on a regular basis:

Diagnosing Problems

Small engine mechanics are tasked with identifying the root cause of a problem. They use a variety of diagnostic tools to troubleshoot issues and determine the best course of action to fix the problem.

Repairing Equipment

Once they've identified the problem, small engine mechanics must repair the equipment. This may involve replacing defective parts, repairing damaged components, or performing a full engine overhaul.

Performing Maintenance

Small engines require regular maintenance to keep them running efficiently. Small engine mechanics are responsible for performing routine maintenance tasks such as oil changes, filter replacements, and tune-ups.

Customer Service

Small engine mechanics often work directly with customers, both in-person and over the phone. They must be able to communicate effectively and explain complex technical issues in a way that is easy for customers to understand.

Skills Required for Small Engine Mechanics

To be a successful small engine mechanic, you need to have a combination of technical skills and personal qualities. Here are some of the skills and traits that are essential for this career:

Technical Skills

  • Ability to diagnose and repair small engines
  • Proficiency with diagnostic tools and equipment
  • Strong mechanical aptitude

Personal Qualities

  • Attention to detail
  • Strong problem-solving skills
  • Excellent communication skills
  • Ability to work independently
  • Physical dexterity

Career Outlook for Small Engine Mechanics

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for small engine mechanics was $38,630 in May 2020. The job outlook for small engine mechanics is also positive, with a projected job growth rate of 6 percent from 2019 to 2029.

Conclusion of Small Engine Mechanic Job Description

In conclusion, becoming a small engine mechanic can be a rewarding career choice for those who enjoy working with their hands and solving complex problems. With the right combination of technical skills and personal qualities, you can build a successful career in this field.
