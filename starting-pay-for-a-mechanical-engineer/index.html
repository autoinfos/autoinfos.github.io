Starting Pay For A Mechanical Engineer What You Need To Know

Written by William Jul 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career in mechanical engineering? One of the biggest concerns for many prospective engineers is the starting pay. After all, you want to ensure that your career choice will provide you with financial stability and the ability to support yourself and your family. In this post, we'll take a closer look at starting pay for a mechanical engineer and what you can expect in your first job.

Starting salaries for mechanical engineers can vary depending on a number of factors, including your location, level of education, and industry. It's important to understand that your first job may not offer the highest salary, but it's an opportunity to gain experience and build your skill set. Additionally, the field of mechanical engineering is constantly evolving, which means that the demand for engineers with specialized skills is growing.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for mechanical engineers was $88,430 in May 2019. The lowest 10 percent earned less than $57,130, and the highest 10 percent earned more than $138,020. These numbers can vary depending on your location and industry. For example, mechanical engineers working in the oil and gas industry tend to earn higher salaries than those in the manufacturing industry.

My Personal Experience

When I graduated with my bachelor's degree in mechanical engineering, I was offered a starting salary of $60,000. While it wasn't the highest salary, I knew that it was an opportunity to gain experience and build my skill set. After a few years of working in the industry, I was able to negotiate a higher salary and take on more responsibilities. Today, I work as a senior mechanical engineer and earn a comfortable salary that allows me to support my family and save for the future.

Factors That Affect Starting Pay for Mechanical Engineers

As mentioned earlier, there are a number of factors that can affect your starting pay as a mechanical engineer. Here are a few to keep in mind:

Level of Education

Engineers with advanced degrees, such as a master's or PhD, tend to earn higher salaries than those with just a bachelor's degree. Additionally, engineers with specialized certifications or licenses may be able to command higher salaries.

Location

Your location can also affect your starting pay as a mechanical engineer. Generally, engineers working in urban areas or in areas with a high cost of living tend to earn higher salaries.

Conclusion of Starting Pay for a Mechanical Engineer

While starting salaries for mechanical engineers may not be the highest, it's important to remember that this is just the beginning of your career. With experience and skill building, you can work your way up the ladder and earn a comfortable salary. Additionally, the demand for mechanical engineers is growing, which means that there will be plenty of job opportunities in the coming years.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average starting salary for a mechanical engineer?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for mechanical engineers was $88,430 in May 2019. However, your starting salary may be lower depending on your location and industry.

Q: Can I negotiate my starting salary as a mechanical engineer?

A: Yes! It's always worth asking if there is room for negotiation. However, it's important to be realistic and do your research before going into a salary negotiation.

Q: What industries tend to offer higher starting salaries for mechanical engineers?

A: Mechanical engineers working in the oil and gas industry tend to earn higher salaries than those in the manufacturing industry.

Q: Is it worth getting a master's degree in mechanical engineering to increase my starting salary?

A: While a master's degree can lead to higher salaries, it's important to consider the cost of tuition and the time it will take to complete the degree. Additionally, gaining experience and building your skill set in the field can also lead to higher salaries.
