Typical Automotive Technician Interview Questions What To Expect

Written by Frank Aug 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you preparing for an interview as an automotive technician? It can be nerve-wracking to think about the questions that might be asked, but don't worry - we've got you covered. In this article, we'll go over some typical automotive technician interview questions and how to answer them.

Interviews can be a stressful experience, especially if you're not sure what to expect. But by knowing the pain points that are often touched on during an interview, you can better prepare yourself and increase your chances of landing the job.

The target of typical automotive technician interview questions is to gauge your knowledge and experience in the field. The interviewer will likely ask about your education, certifications, and previous work experience. They may also ask about your problem-solving skills, ability to work in a team, and customer service experience.

In summary, be prepared to answer questions related to your education, certifications, work experience, problem-solving skills, ability to work in a team, and customer service experience.

What education and certifications do you have?

When I was interviewed for an automotive technician position, the interviewer asked about my education and certifications. I shared that I had completed a two-year automotive technology program at a technical college and had obtained ASE certification in brakes, suspension, and steering.

It's important to be honest about your education and certifications. If you don't have any formal education or certifications, emphasize your hands-on experience and any on-the-job training you may have received.

Can you give an example of a difficult problem you solved?

During the interview, the interviewer asked about a difficult problem I had solved in the past. I shared an experience where a customer's car was experiencing a misfire and I had to diagnose and repair the issue. I explained my process for diagnosing the problem and how I ultimately fixed the issue.

When answering this type of question, be sure to explain your thought process and be as specific as possible. The interviewer is looking for your problem-solving abilities and how you handle difficult situations.

What experience do you have working on [specific type of vehicle]?

If the company you're interviewing with specializes in a certain type of vehicle, the interviewer may ask about your experience working on that type of vehicle. For example, if the company focuses on European cars, they may ask about your experience working on BMWs or Audis.

Be honest about your experience, but emphasize your willingness to learn and adapt to new challenges. If you don't have experience working on a certain type of vehicle, explain how you would go about learning and gaining experience.

How do you handle difficult customers?

The interviewer may ask about your experience with difficult customers and how you handle those situations. During my interview, I shared an experience where a customer was upset about the cost of a repair. I explained how I calmly and respectfully explained the situation to the customer and worked to find a solution that would satisfy both parties.

When answering this question, emphasize your customer service skills and ability to handle difficult situations with grace and professionalism.

Question and Answer:

Q: What experience do you have working on diesel engines?

A: I have experience working on diesel engines from my previous job at a truck repair shop. I have experience with diagnosing and repairing issues with diesel engines.

Q: Can you explain your experience with air conditioning systems?

A: I have experience repairing and replacing air conditioning systems in vehicles. I am knowledgeable about the different components of an air conditioning system and how to diagnose and repair issues.

Q: How do you stay up to date with the latest automotive technology?

A: I stay up to date with the latest technology by attending training sessions and seminars. I also read industry publications and keep up with new developments in the field.

Q: Can you explain your experience with electrical systems?

A: I have a thorough understanding of electrical systems in vehicles and have experience diagnosing and repairing issues with electrical systems.

Conclusion of Typical Automotive Technician Interview Questions

By knowing what to expect during an automotive technician interview, you can better prepare yourself and increase your chances of landing the job. Be honest about your experience, emphasize your problem-solving and customer service skills, and show a willingness to learn and adapt to new challenges. With these tips in mind, you'll be well on your way to acing your next interview.
