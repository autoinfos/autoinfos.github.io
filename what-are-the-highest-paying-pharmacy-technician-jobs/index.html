What Are The Highest Paying Pharmacy Technician Jobs

Written by Frank Jul 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking for a high-paying job in the field of pharmacy? Do you want to know what are the highest paying pharmacy technician jobs? If you answered yes, then you're in the right place. In this article, we'll discuss the top-paying pharmacy technician jobs and how you can land one of these positions.

Working as a pharmacy technician can be a challenging job, but it can also be very rewarding. Pharmacy technicians are responsible for dispensing medication and providing assistance to pharmacists. They work in various settings, including hospitals, retail pharmacies, and long-term care facilities. However, not all pharmacy technician jobs pay the same amount.

The highest paying pharmacy technician jobs are typically found in specialty pharmacies, such as nuclear pharmacies, compounding pharmacies, and mail-order pharmacies. These types of pharmacies require specialized training and certifications, which can result in higher salaries for pharmacy technicians. Additionally, pharmacy technicians who work in hospitals, especially in intensive care units, can also earn higher salaries due to the critical nature of their work.

In summary, the highest paying pharmacy technician jobs are typically found in specialty pharmacies and hospitals. However, these jobs require additional training and certifications, so it's important to invest in your education if you want to land one of these positions.

Specialty Pharmacies

When I first started my career as a pharmacy technician, I worked in a retail pharmacy. While I enjoyed the work, I knew that I wanted to earn a higher salary. That's when I decided to pursue a job in a specialty pharmacy. I took additional courses and earned certifications in nuclear pharmacy and sterile compounding, which helped me land a job in a high-paying specialty pharmacy.

Specialty pharmacies require pharmacy technicians to have specialized training and certifications. For example, nuclear pharmacies require technicians to be certified by the Nuclear Pharmacy Technician Certification Board. Additionally, sterile compounding pharmacies require technicians to have completed a course in sterile compounding and to be certified by the Pharmacy Technician Certification Board.

Hospital Pharmacies

After working in a specialty pharmacy for several years, I decided to switch to a hospital pharmacy. I found that hospital pharmacies offer unique challenges and opportunities for growth, and they also pay higher salaries. In particular, working in an intensive care unit can be very rewarding and can pay even higher salaries.

Hospital pharmacies require pharmacy technicians to have a solid foundation in medication safety and pharmacology. Additionally, many hospitals prefer to hire pharmacy technicians who have completed a pharmacy technician training program and are certified by the Pharmacy Technician Certification Board.

Additional Certifications

If you're looking to increase your earning potential as a pharmacy technician, you may want to consider earning additional certifications. For example, you can become certified in chemotherapy, which can be a valuable skill in oncology pharmacies. Additionally, you can become certified as a pharmacy technician educator, which can lead to higher salaries in training and education roles.

Networking

Finally, one of the best ways to land a high-paying pharmacy technician job is to network with other pharmacy professionals. Attend conferences and events in your area, join professional organizations, and connect with other pharmacy technicians and pharmacists on social media. By building your network, you'll increase your chances of hearing about job openings and making valuable connections.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average salary for a pharmacy technician?
A: The average salary for a pharmacy technician is around $35,000 per year, but this can vary depending on experience and location.

Q: Do pharmacy technicians need to be certified?
A: While certification is not required in all states, it is recommended by many employers and can lead to higher salaries.

Q: What types of pharmacies pay the most?
A: Specialty pharmacies, such as nuclear pharmacies, compounding pharmacies, and mail-order pharmacies, typically pay the most.

Q: How can I increase my earning potential as a pharmacy technician?
A: You can increase your earning potential by earning additional certifications, networking with other pharmacy professionals, and pursuing jobs in specialty pharmacies and hospitals.

Conclusion

In conclusion, if you're looking for a high-paying job in the field of pharmacy, you may want to consider working in a specialty pharmacy or hospital. These types of pharmacies require additional training and certifications, but they offer unique challenges and opportunities for growth. Additionally, by earning additional certifications and networking with other pharmacy professionals, you can increase your earning potential as a pharmacy technician.
