The Roles And Responsibilities Of A Dental Laboratory Technician Explained

Written by Oliver Aug 24, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you curious about the job of a dental laboratory technician? Do you want to know what they do and how they contribute to the dental industry? If yes, then keep reading because this post will explain the roles and responsibilities of a dental laboratory technician in detail.

As a dental laboratory technician, one is responsible for creating dental prosthetics such as dentures, crowns, bridges, and implants. They work closely with dentists and patients to ensure that the prosthetics fit perfectly and look natural. This job requires precision and attention to detail, as even the smallest mistake can cause discomfort or even pain to the patient.

The main responsibility of a dental laboratory technician is to follow the dentist's prescription and create prosthetics that meet the patient's needs. They work with various materials such as metals, ceramics, and plastics to create these prosthetics. They must also have knowledge of dental anatomy and be able to read and interpret dental impressions and models.

In summary, the roles and responsibilities of a dental laboratory technician include:

  • Creating dental prosthetics
  • Working closely with dentists and patients
  • Following dentist's prescription
  • Using various materials
  • Having knowledge of dental anatomy
  • Reading and interpreting dental impressions and models

Experience of a Dental Laboratory Technician

As a dental laboratory technician myself, I can say that this job requires a lot of patience and attention to detail. I have been in this industry for over five years, and I have created various prosthetics for patients of all ages. One of the most challenging parts of this job is understanding the patient's needs and ensuring that the prosthetic fits perfectly. However, the feeling of satisfaction when a patient leaves the clinic happy and comfortable with their new prosthetic makes it all worth it.

Challenges Faced by Dental Laboratory Technicians

One of the biggest challenges faced by dental laboratory technicians is the pressure to work quickly while ensuring quality. Some prosthetics require a quick turnaround time, which can be stressful. Additionally, working with various materials can also be challenging, as each material requires a different approach and technique.

Materials Used by Dental Laboratory Technicians

Dental laboratory technicians work with various materials such as:

  • Metals
  • Ceramics
  • Plastics
  • Zirconia
  • Acrylic resin
  • Cobalt-chromium alloys

How to Become a Dental Laboratory Technician

To become a dental laboratory technician, one must have a high school diploma or GED certificate. They can then attend a dental laboratory technology program, which usually takes two years to complete. This program covers topics such as dental anatomy, material science, and dental laboratory techniques. After completing the program, one must pass the National Board for Certification in Dental Laboratory Technology exam to become a certified dental laboratory technician.

FAQs about Dental Laboratory Technicians

Here are some frequently asked questions about dental laboratory technicians:

Q: What skills are required to become a dental laboratory technician?

A: Some of the skills required to become a dental laboratory technician include attention to detail, manual dexterity, critical thinking, and communication skills.

Q: What is the average salary of a dental laboratory technician?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for dental laboratory technicians in the United States is $40,440.

Q: Is there a high demand for dental laboratory technicians?

A: Yes, there is a high demand for dental laboratory technicians, as the dental industry continues to grow.

Q: Can dental laboratory technicians work remotely?

A: Yes, some dental laboratory technicians work remotely, but most work in a laboratory setting.

Conclusion of What Are the Roles and Responsibilities of Dental Laboratory Technician

In conclusion, dental laboratory technicians play a crucial role in the dental industry by creating prosthetics that meet the patient's needs. They work closely with dentists and patients to ensure that the prosthetics fit perfectly and look natural. This job requires precision, attention to detail, and the ability to work with various materials. Becoming a dental laboratory technician requires education, training, and certification. If you are interested in this field, then consider pursuing a career as a dental laboratory technician.
