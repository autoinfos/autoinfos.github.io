What Can I Do With A Pharmacy Technician License

Written by Daniel Jul 02, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you a pharmacy technician who is looking for ways to expand your career options? Do you want to know what you can do with a pharmacy technician license? If so, you've come to the right place. In this blog post, we will discuss the various career paths open to pharmacy technicians and how you can make the most of your license.

As a pharmacy technician, you may feel limited in your career options. You may feel that you are stuck in a dead-end job with no room for growth or advancement. However, this is not the case. There are many opportunities available to pharmacy technicians, and with the right skills and training, you can take your career to the next level.

So, what can you do with a pharmacy technician license? The answer is, quite a lot. Pharmacy technicians are in demand in a variety of settings, including hospitals, retail pharmacies, long-term care facilities, and more. Some of the career paths available to pharmacy technicians include:

Hospital Pharmacy Technician

As a hospital pharmacy technician, you will work in a fast-paced environment, preparing and dispensing medications to patients. You will work closely with pharmacists, nurses, and other healthcare professionals to ensure that patients receive the correct medications at the right time. This is a challenging and rewarding career path that requires attention to detail and strong communication skills.

Retail Pharmacy Technician

As a retail pharmacy technician, you will work in a community pharmacy, preparing and dispensing medications to customers. You will also provide customer service, answer questions about medications, and assist with insurance claims. This is a great career path for those who enjoy working with people and want to make a difference in their community.

Long-Term Care Pharmacy Technician

As a long-term care pharmacy technician, you will work in a facility that provides care to elderly or disabled patients. You will prepare and dispense medications, as well as manage medication orders and inventory. This is a great career path for those who want to work in a healthcare setting but prefer a more relaxed environment.

Specialty Pharmacy Technician

As a specialty pharmacy technician, you will work in a pharmacy that specializes in a particular type of medication or disease state. You may work with patients who have cancer, HIV/AIDS, or other complex conditions. This is a challenging and rewarding career path that requires specialized knowledge and training.

Now that you know what you can do with a pharmacy technician license, it's time to take the next step in your career. Whether you want to work in a hospital, retail pharmacy, long-term care facility, or specialty pharmacy, there are opportunities available to you.

Personal Experience

When I first became a pharmacy technician, I was unsure of what my career options were. However, I soon discovered that there were many opportunities available to me. I started my career in a retail pharmacy, where I gained valuable experience working with customers and managing inventory. After a few years, I decided to pursue a career in hospital pharmacy, where I could work in a more fast-paced environment and be part of a healthcare team. Today, I am a hospital pharmacy technician, and I love my job.

Skills Needed for a Pharmacy Technician Career

To be successful in a pharmacy technician career, you will need to have strong attention to detail, good communication skills, and a commitment to patient care. You will also need to be able to work well under pressure and be comfortable using technology.

Education and Training

To become a pharmacy technician, you will need to complete a training program and pass a certification exam. Many community colleges and vocational schools offer pharmacy technician programs, which can be completed in as little as six months. In addition, some employers offer on-the-job training for pharmacy technicians.

Certification and Licensing

Once you have completed your training, you will need to pass a certification exam to become a certified pharmacy technician (CPhT). This certification is offered by the Pharmacy Technician Certification Board (PTCB) and is recognized by most states. Some states also require pharmacy technicians to be licensed, so be sure to check the requirements in your state.

Conclusion of What Can I Do with a Pharmacy Technician License

As you can see, there are many career paths open to pharmacy technicians. Whether you want to work in a hospital, retail pharmacy, long-term care facility, or specialty pharmacy, there are opportunities available to you. With the right skills and training, you can take your career to the next level and make a difference in the lives of patients.

Question and Answer

Q: What kind of salary can I expect as a pharmacy technician?

A: The median annual salary for pharmacy technicians is around $34,000, but this can vary depending on location, experience, and other factors.

Q: Can pharmacy technicians work in hospitals?

A: Yes, hospital pharmacy technicians are in high demand and can work in a variety of settings, including emergency departments, intensive care units, and more.

Q: Do I need to be certified to work as a pharmacy technician?

A: While certification is not always required, it is strongly recommended. Many employers prefer to hire certified pharmacy technicians, and some states require certification for licensure.

Q: What skills do I need to be a successful pharmacy technician?

A: To be successful in a pharmacy technician career, you will need to have strong attention to detail, good communication skills, and a commitment to patient care. You will also need to be able to work well under pressure and be comfortable using technology.
