What Companies Hire Automotive Engineers

Written by Robby Jun 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you an automotive engineer looking for a job? It can be tough to figure out which companies are hiring and where to start your search. But don't worry, we've got you covered! In this post, we'll go over what companies hire automotive engineers and offer some tips to help you land your dream job.

If you're an automotive engineer, you know that your skills are in high demand. But finding the right job can be a challenge. Some common pain points for automotive engineers include a lack of job openings, stiff competition, and uncertainty about which companies are hiring.

So, which companies hire automotive engineers? The short answer is: a lot of them! Automotive engineers are needed in a wide range of industries, from automotive manufacturing to aerospace and defense. Some of the top companies that hire automotive engineers include Ford, General Motors, Tesla, Toyota, BMW, and Honda.

Overall, the automotive industry is a great place to look for jobs if you're an automotive engineer. But there are also plenty of opportunities in other industries, such as aerospace, defense, and even technology.

Automotive Manufacturing

One of the most obvious places to look for automotive engineering jobs is in the automotive manufacturing industry. This includes companies like Ford, General Motors, and Toyota, which are known for producing cars, trucks, and other vehicles.

When I was looking for my first job as an automotive engineer, I applied to several automotive manufacturing companies. I ended up getting a job at Ford, where I worked for several years before moving on to other opportunities.

Aerospace and Defense

Another industry that hires a lot of automotive engineers is aerospace and defense. This includes companies like Boeing, Lockheed Martin, and Northrop Grumman, which are known for producing airplanes, spacecraft, and other advanced technologies.

While aerospace and defense might not seem like an obvious fit for automotive engineers, there are actually a lot of transferable skills between the two industries. For example, both require expertise in materials science, aerodynamics, and systems integration.

Technology

Finally, there are plenty of opportunities for automotive engineers in the technology industry. Companies like Tesla, Waymo, and Uber are all working on developing self-driving cars and other advanced technologies that require the expertise of automotive engineers.

Personally, I've always been interested in the intersection between automotive engineering and technology. That's why I ended up working for Waymo, where I'm helping to develop the next generation of self-driving cars.

Tips for Landing a Job as an Automotive Engineer

So, now that you know which companies hire automotive engineers, how do you go about landing a job at one of these companies? Here are a few tips to help you get started:

  1. Build your network. Networking is key in any industry, but it's especially important in automotive engineering. Attend industry events, connect with other professionals on LinkedIn, and look for opportunities to meet people who work at the companies you're interested in.
  2. Stay up-to-date on industry trends. The automotive industry is constantly evolving, so it's important to stay on top of the latest trends and technologies. Read industry publications, follow thought leaders on social media, and attend conferences to stay informed.
  3. Show off your skills. When applying for jobs, make sure to highlight your skills and accomplishments. Use concrete examples to demonstrate how you've contributed to projects in the past, and emphasize your technical expertise.
  4. Be persistent. Landing a job as an automotive engineer can take time, so don't get discouraged if you don't hear back from companies right away. Keep applying to jobs, and don't be afraid to follow up with recruiters to express your interest.

Question and Answer

Q: What skills do I need to be an automotive engineer?

A: Automotive engineers need a strong background in mechanical engineering, as well as knowledge of materials science, electronics, and computer-aided design (CAD). They should also have strong problem-solving skills and the ability to work well in a team.

Q: Do I need a degree to be an automotive engineer?

A: While it's not strictly necessary to have a degree to work as an automotive engineer, most companies prefer candidates with a bachelor's or master's degree in mechanical engineering or a related field.

Q: How much do automotive engineers typically make?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for mechanical engineers (including automotive engineers) was $88,430 as of May 2020.

Q: What companies are currently hiring automotive engineers?

A: Some of the top companies currently hiring automotive engineers include Ford, General Motors, Tesla, Toyota, BMW, Waymo, and Uber.

Conclusion

If you're an automotive engineer looking for a job, there are plenty of opportunities out there. Whether you're interested in working in automotive manufacturing, aerospace and defense, or technology, there are companies hiring for automotive engineers across a range of industries. By building your network, staying up-to-date on industry trends, and showcasing your skills, you can increase your chances of landing your dream job.
