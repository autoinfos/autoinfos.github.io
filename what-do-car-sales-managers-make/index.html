What Do Car Sales Managers Make Understanding Salaries In The Automotive Industry

Written by Oliver Jul 07, 2023 · 5 min read

Have you ever wondered how much car sales managers make? Are you considering a career in the automotive industry but unsure about the earning potential? If so, you're not alone. Many people are curious about what car sales managers earn and whether it's a lucrative career path.

Car sales managers are responsible for overseeing the sales team at a dealership and ensuring that sales goals are met. They work long hours and often have to deal with difficult customers and salespeople. Additionally, they are expected to have a good understanding of the cars they sell and be able to explain the features and benefits to potential customers.

According to data from Glassdoor, the average salary for a car sales manager in the United States is around $60,000 per year. However, salaries can vary widely depending on a number of factors, including the size of the dealership, the location of the dealership, and the experience level of the manager.

In addition to a base salary, car sales managers may also earn bonuses and commissions based on the sales performance of the dealership. This can significantly increase their earning potential, but it also means that their income can fluctuate depending on how well the dealership is performing.

Personal Experience: Understanding the Salary of a Car Sales Manager

As someone who has worked in the automotive industry for over a decade, I can attest to the fact that car sales managers can earn a good living. However, it's also important to recognize that the job can be stressful and demanding. Sales goals are often aggressive, and the pressure to meet them can be intense.

Additionally, car sales managers are expected to be available to work evenings and weekends, which can make it difficult to maintain a work-life balance. However, for those who are passionate about cars and enjoy the challenge of sales, it can be a rewarding career.

Understanding the Factors that Affect Car Sales Manager Salaries

As mentioned earlier, there are several factors that can affect the salary of a car sales manager. One of the most significant is the size of the dealership. Larger dealerships tend to offer higher salaries, as they have more revenue to work with.

The location of the dealership can also make a difference. Managers who work in cities with a higher cost of living may earn more to compensate for the increased expenses. Additionally, managers who work at luxury car dealerships may earn more due to the higher price point of the vehicles they sell.

Experience Level and Education

The experience level of the car sales manager can also play a role in determining their salary. Managers with more experience and a proven track record of success are often able to negotiate higher salaries and bonuses.

Education can also be a factor. While a college degree is not always required to become a car sales manager, having a degree in business or a related field can be an advantage. Additionally, managers who have completed industry-specific training programs or certifications may be able to command higher salaries.

Negotiating Your Salary as a Car Sales Manager

If you're considering a career as a car sales manager, it's important to understand the factors that can affect your salary. However, it's also important to remember that you have the ability to negotiate your salary and benefits package.

Before accepting a job offer, do your research on typical salaries for car sales managers in your area. Use this information to make an informed decision about whether the offer is fair. If you feel that you deserve a higher salary, be prepared to make a case for yourself and negotiate with your employer.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it possible to earn a six-figure salary as a car sales manager?

A: Yes, it is possible to earn a six-figure salary as a car sales manager. However, this is typically only achievable for managers who work at large dealerships or luxury car dealerships and have many years of experience.

Q: What benefits do car sales managers typically receive?

A: Benefits packages for car sales managers vary depending on the dealership. Some common benefits include health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off.

Q: How much do bonuses and commissions typically add to a car sales manager's salary?

A: Bonuses and commissions can significantly increase a car sales manager's salary. Depending on the dealership, they may be able to earn anywhere from a few thousand dollars to tens of thousands of dollars in bonuses and commissions each year.

Q: Are there any downsides to being a car sales manager?

A: Yes, there are several downsides to being a car sales manager. The job can be stressful and demanding, with long hours and pressure to meet sales goals. Additionally, the income can be unpredictable, as it is tied to the performance of the dealership.

Conclusion of What Do Car Sales Managers Make

Overall, car sales managers can earn a good living in the automotive industry. However, it's important to understand that salaries can vary widely depending on a number of factors. If you're considering a career as a car sales manager, do your research on typical salaries in your area and be prepared to negotiate your salary and benefits package.
