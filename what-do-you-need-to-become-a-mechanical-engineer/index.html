What Do You Need To Become A Mechanical Engineer

Written by Petter Nov 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in designing machines, developing new technologies, and solving complex problems? If so, mechanical engineering may be the perfect career path for you. But before you can start pursuing this exciting field, you need to know what it takes to become a mechanical engineer. In this blog post, we'll explore the education, skills, and experience you need to succeed in this challenging but rewarding profession.

The Challenges of Becoming a Mechanical Engineer

Becoming a mechanical engineer requires a significant investment of time, money, and effort. You'll need to complete a rigorous academic program, gain practical experience through internships or co-op programs, and develop a range of technical and soft skills to succeed in this field.

Education Requirements for Mechanical Engineers

The first step to becoming a mechanical engineer is earning a bachelor's degree in mechanical engineering or a related field. Most employers require candidates to have at least a bachelor's degree from an accredited program, and many prefer candidates with a master's degree or higher.

In addition to core courses in math, science, and engineering, mechanical engineering programs typically include classes in mechanics, thermodynamics, materials science, and computer-aided design (CAD). You'll also have opportunities to specialize in areas like robotics, aerospace engineering, or automotive engineering.

Developing the Skills You Need to Succeed

In addition to academic coursework, you'll need to develop a range of skills to succeed as a mechanical engineer. These include:

  • Strong analytical and problem-solving skills
  • Excellent communication and teamwork skills
  • Proficiency in technical tools and software programs like CAD and MATLAB
  • An ability to manage multiple projects and deadlines
  • A commitment to lifelong learning and professional development

One way to develop these skills is through internships, co-op programs, or other hands-on learning experiences. You can also join professional organizations like the American Society of Mechanical Engineers (ASME) or attend industry conferences and workshops to stay up-to-date on the latest trends and technologies in the field.

My Personal Experience Becoming a Mechanical Engineer

As a mechanical engineer myself, I can attest to the challenges and rewards of this profession. I first became interested in mechanical engineering while working on a project in high school that involved designing and building a wind turbine. I loved the combination of creativity, problem-solving, and hands-on work that the project required.

After completing my bachelor's degree in mechanical engineering, I worked as a design engineer for a few years before going back to school for a master's degree. Since then, I've worked in a variety of roles in the aerospace and defense industries, including as a project manager and systems engineer.

What Do You Need to Become a Mechanical Engineer: Other Considerations

In addition to the education, skills, and experience required to become a mechanical engineer, there are a few other factors to consider:

Licensing and Certification Requirements

Depending on your area of specialization and the state where you work, you may need to obtain a professional engineering (PE) license to practice as a mechanical engineer. To obtain a PE license, you typically need to pass an exam and meet other requirements related to education and experience.

Career Outlook and Salary Expectations

The career outlook for mechanical engineers is strong, with the Bureau of Labor Statistics projecting 4% job growth between 2019 and 2029. The median annual salary for mechanical engineers was $88,430 as of May 2019, according to the BLS.

Conclusion of What Do You Need to Become a Mechanical Engineer

Becoming a mechanical engineer requires a significant investment of time, money, and effort, but it can also be a rewarding and fulfilling career path. By earning a bachelor's or master's degree, developing a range of technical and soft skills, and gaining practical experience through internships or co-op programs, you can position yourself for success in this exciting field.
