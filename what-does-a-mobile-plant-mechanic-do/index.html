What Does A Mobile Plant Mechanic Do

Written by Daniel Jul 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you curious about what a mobile plant mechanic does? If so, you're in the right place. In this article, we'll explain what a mobile plant mechanic does in simple terms so you can easily understand their role in the workforce.

Have you ever had a piece of heavy machinery break down in the middle of a job? It can be frustrating and costly, especially if you don't have someone to fix it quickly. That's where a mobile plant mechanic comes in. Their job is to travel to job sites and repair heavy machinery and equipment that has broken down. They are responsible for diagnosing the issue, repairing or replacing the broken parts, and conducting routine maintenance to prevent future breakdowns.

In short, a mobile plant mechanic is a skilled tradesperson who repairs and maintains heavy machinery and equipment on-site. They work in industries such as construction, mining, and agriculture, among others.

In summary, a mobile plant mechanic is a vital part of the workforce, ensuring that heavy machinery and equipment are functioning correctly and efficiently. They are responsible for diagnosing and repairing issues and conducting routine maintenance to prevent future breakdowns.

What Does a Mobile Plant Mechanic Do: An In-Depth Explanation

As mentioned, a mobile plant mechanic is responsible for repairing and maintaining heavy machinery and equipment on job sites. This includes diagnosing issues, repairing or replacing broken parts, and conducting routine maintenance. They typically work with machinery such as excavators, bulldozers, loaders, and dump trucks, among others.

One of the most critical tasks that a mobile plant mechanic does is to diagnose the issue with the machinery or equipment. They use their knowledge and expertise to identify what's causing the problem and find the best solution to fix it. This could involve running diagnostic tests, taking apart machinery and equipment, and inspecting individual components.

Once they've diagnosed the problem, a mobile plant mechanic will repair or replace the broken parts. This could involve welding, fabricating, or ordering new parts to replace the broken ones. They'll also conduct routine maintenance to ensure that machinery and equipment are working efficiently and prevent future breakdowns. This could involve changing oil, replacing filters, and inspecting components for signs of wear and tear.

The Skills Needed to Be a Mobile Plant Mechanic

Being a mobile plant mechanic requires a range of skills and knowledge. Some of the essential skills include:

Technical Skills

A mobile plant mechanic needs to have a deep understanding of how heavy machinery and equipment work. They need to be able to diagnose issues and repair or replace broken parts efficiently. They should also be comfortable using a range of tools and equipment, including welding machines, hoists, and diagnostic equipment.

Problem-Solving Skills

Mobile plant mechanics need to be quick thinkers and problem solvers. They need to be able to diagnose issues quickly and find the best solution to fix them. They should also be able to work independently and make decisions on their own.

Communication Skills

Mobile plant mechanics need to be able to communicate effectively with clients, supervisors, and other staff members. They should be able to explain complex issues in simple terms and provide recommendations on how to fix them. They should also be able to provide accurate estimates of repair costs and timelines.

Physical Fitness

Mobile plant mechanics need to be physically fit and able to work in challenging environments. They may need to climb onto heavy machinery or work in tight spaces.

Question and Answer

Q: What qualifications do you need to be a mobile plant mechanic?

A: To become a mobile plant mechanic, you typically need to complete an apprenticeship or a trade school program. You may also need to obtain a certification or license, depending on where you live and work.

Q: What industries do mobile plant mechanics work in?

A: Mobile plant mechanics can work in a range of industries, including construction, mining, agriculture, and forestry, among others.

Q: How much does a mobile plant mechanic earn?

A: The salary of a mobile plant mechanic varies based on experience, location, and industry. According to Payscale, the average salary for a mobile plant mechanic in the United States is $25.96 per hour.

Q: What are the job prospects for mobile plant mechanics?

A: The job prospects for mobile plant mechanics are strong, with the Bureau of Labor Statistics predicting a 4% job growth rate from 2019 to 2029. This is due to the continued demand for heavy machinery and equipment in industries such as construction and mining.

Conclusion of What Does a Mobile Plant Mechanic Do

In summary, a mobile plant mechanic is a skilled tradesperson who repairs and maintains heavy machinery and equipment on job sites. They are responsible for diagnosing issues, repairing or replacing broken parts, and conducting routine maintenance to prevent future breakdowns. Mobile plant mechanics require a range of skills, including technical knowledge, problem-solving, communication, and physical fitness. The job prospects for mobile plant mechanics are strong, with continued demand in industries such as construction and mining.
