What Does A Refrigeration Mechanic Do

Written by Oliver Oct 14, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Have you ever wondered about the person behind the scenes who keeps your fridge running smoothly? Refrigeration mechanics are the professionals who make sure that your refrigeration systems are always functioning properly. They are responsible for the installation, maintenance, and repair of refrigeration systems in various settings, including homes, restaurants, and grocery stores. In this article, we'll dive deeper into the world of refrigeration mechanics and find out what they do.

When it comes to refrigeration systems, there are plenty of things that can go wrong. From electrical faults to mechanical issues, refrigeration mechanics are responsible for identifying and fixing these problems. They work with various types of refrigeration systems, including commercial and industrial refrigeration units, air conditioning systems, and heat pumps. Refrigeration mechanics must have a strong understanding of electrical and mechanical systems, as well as refrigerant handling procedures.

Refrigeration mechanics are responsible for installing new refrigeration systems, ensuring that they meet safety and environmental standards. They also perform routine maintenance, such as cleaning coils and replacing filters. When refrigeration systems break down, refrigeration mechanics are called in to troubleshoot the problem and make the necessary repairs. They may also be responsible for replacing parts and components, such as compressors and condensers.

What Does a Refrigeration Mechanic Do: A Personal Experience

As a refrigeration mechanic, I have had the opportunity to work on a variety of refrigeration systems, from small residential units to large commercial walk-in coolers. One of the most challenging projects I worked on was the installation of a new refrigeration system for a grocery store. The project required me to work closely with other contractors, such as electricians and plumbers, to ensure that the system was installed correctly and met all safety and environmental standards.

On a day-to-day basis, my work involves routine maintenance, such as cleaning coils and replacing filters. I also receive calls from clients who have refrigeration systems that have broken down. I troubleshoot the problem, make the necessary repairs, and test the system to ensure that it is functioning properly.

What Are the Skills Required to Become a Refrigeration Mechanic?

To become a refrigeration mechanic, you must have a strong understanding of electrical and mechanical systems. You should also have knowledge of refrigeration systems and refrigerant handling procedures. In addition to technical skills, refrigeration mechanics must also have good problem-solving skills and be able to work independently. Strong communication skills are also essential, as refrigeration mechanics often work with clients to understand their needs and explain technical issues in a way that is easy to understand.

What Are the Educational Requirements for Becoming a Refrigeration Mechanic?

Most refrigeration mechanics have completed a high school diploma or equivalent. However, some employers may require additional training or education, such as an apprenticeship or vocational training program. These programs typically provide students with a combination of classroom instruction and hands-on training, allowing them to gain the skills and knowledge necessary to succeed in the field.

How Much Does a Refrigeration Mechanic Make?

The salary of a refrigeration mechanic can vary depending on their level of experience, education, and location. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for heating, air conditioning, and refrigeration mechanics and installers was $50,590 in May 2020.

Their walk-in cooler had broken down, and they were losing valuable inventory.
