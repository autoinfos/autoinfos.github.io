What Does An Aircraft Mechanic Helper Do

Written by Robby Oct 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever wondered about the behind-the-scenes work that goes into maintaining an aircraft? Or perhaps you have a passion for aviation and are considering a career in the field? Whatever your reason, understanding the role of an aircraft mechanic helper is a great place to start.

As with any job, there are both rewarding and challenging aspects to being an aircraft mechanic helper. On one hand, you have the opportunity to work with cutting-edge technology and play a crucial role in ensuring the safety of airline passengers. On the other hand, the work can be physically demanding and requires a high level of attention to detail.

An aircraft mechanic helper is responsible for assisting licensed aircraft mechanics in performing maintenance and repair work on aircraft. This can include tasks such as inspecting aircraft components for wear and tear, replacing faulty parts, and performing routine maintenance tasks such as oil changes and tire rotations.

In summary, an aircraft mechanic helper plays a vital role in keeping aircraft in safe and working order. While the work can be challenging at times, it is also rewarding and offers a unique opportunity to work in the exciting world of aviation.

What Are the Duties of an Aircraft Mechanic Helper?

My journey as an aircraft mechanic helper began with a passion for all things aviation. After completing my basic training, I was assigned to work alongside licensed aircraft mechanics in performing maintenance and repair work on commercial aircraft.

One of the key duties of an aircraft mechanic helper is to assist licensed mechanics in performing routine maintenance tasks such as oil changes and tire rotations. This involves working closely with the licensed mechanic to ensure that all work is carried out safely and in compliance with industry regulations.

What Are the Qualifications Required for an Aircraft Mechanic Helper?

As an aircraft mechanic helper, a high school diploma or equivalent is typically required. Additionally, many employers prefer candidates with some prior experience in a related field such as automotive or mechanical engineering.

What Are the Key Skills Required for an Aircraft Mechanic Helper?

One of the key skills required for an aircraft mechanic helper is attention to detail. This is essential for ensuring that all maintenance work is carried out to a high standard and that all safety protocols are followed.

How Can You Become an Aircraft Mechanic Helper?

To become an aircraft mechanic helper, you will need to complete a basic training program that covers the fundamentals of aircraft maintenance and repair. Additionally, many employers offer on-the-job training to help new hires gain practical experience in the field.

What Are the Career Prospects for an Aircraft Mechanic Helper?

As an aircraft mechanic helper, there are many opportunities for career advancement. With experience and additional training, you may be able to progress to a licensed aircraft mechanic or take on a supervisory role within your organization.

Conclusion of What Does an Aircraft Mechanic Helper Do

In conclusion, an aircraft mechanic helper plays an important role in ensuring the safety and reliability of commercial aircraft. While the work can be physically demanding and requires a high level of attention to detail, it is also rewarding and offers a unique opportunity to work in the exciting world of aviation.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average salary for an aircraft mechanic helper?

A: The average salary for an aircraft mechanic helper varies depending on your level of experience and the company you work for. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for aircraft mechanics and service technicians was $64,090 as of May 2020.

Q: What are the working hours like for an aircraft mechanic helper?

A: The working hours for an aircraft mechanic helper can vary depending on the company you work for and the type of aircraft you are working on. Some companies may require you to work evenings, weekends or holidays to accommodate flight schedules.

Q: What are some of the challenges of working as an aircraft mechanic helper?

A: Some of the challenges of working as an aircraft mechanic helper include working in confined spaces, dealing with tight schedules, and working in noisy environments.

Q: What is the demand for aircraft mechanic helpers?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of aircraft and avionics equipment mechanics and technicians is projected to grow 5 percent from 2019 to 2029, faster than the average for all occupations. This means that there will likely be a steady demand for skilled aircraft mechanic helpers in the coming years.
