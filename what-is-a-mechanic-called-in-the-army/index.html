What Is A Mechanic Called In The Army

Written by William Sep 22, 2023 · 4 min read

Have you ever wondered what a mechanic is called in the army? Are you curious about what they do and how they contribute to the army's success? If so, you've come to the right place.

Being a military mechanic is not an easy job. They have to work in extreme conditions, from scorching deserts to freezing temperatures in the mountains. They are responsible for maintaining and repairing army vehicles and equipment, which are essential to the success of military operations. A single malfunctioning vehicle or piece of equipment can put the lives of soldiers in danger.

A mechanic in the army is called a "91B Wheeled Vehicle Mechanic." They are responsible for performing maintenance and repairs on wheeled vehicles, including trucks, Humvees, and other support vehicles. They are also trained to operate and maintain various types of equipment, such as generators and air compressors. In addition to their technical skills, they must also be physically fit and mentally prepared to work in high-stress situations.

In summary, a mechanic in the army is called a "91B Wheeled Vehicle Mechanic." They are highly skilled individuals who play a crucial role in the army's success. They work under extreme conditions to ensure that army vehicles and equipment are in top condition, which is essential to the safety and success of military operations.

What Does a 91B Wheeled Vehicle Mechanic Do?

When I was serving in the army, I had the opportunity to work alongside 91B mechanics. One of the most important tasks they perform is regular maintenance on army vehicles. They check the oil levels, change the oil and filters, check the brakes, and inspect the tires. They also repair any minor issues before they become major problems.

Another critical task they perform is repairing vehicles and equipment that have broken down. They are trained to diagnose and fix mechanical issues quickly and efficiently to get vehicles back on the road as soon as possible. This is essential to the success of military operations, as vehicles are often needed in remote areas where there are no repair shops or spare parts available.

Training for 91B Wheeled Vehicle Mechanics

91B Wheeled Vehicle Mechanics go through extensive training before they are deployed to their units. They attend Basic Combat Training (BCT), followed by Advanced Individual Training (AIT) at Fort Jackson, South Carolina. The training covers a wide range of topics, including vehicle maintenance, diagnosis and repair of mechanical problems, and equipment operation and maintenance.

After completing their training, 91B mechanics are assigned to a unit, where they continue to receive on-the-job training and gain experience. They also have the opportunity to attend advanced training courses to enhance their skills.

How to Become a 91B Wheeled Vehicle Mechanic

If you're interested in becoming a 91B Wheeled Vehicle Mechanic in the army, you must first meet the eligibility requirements. You must be a U.S. citizen or permanent resident, be at least 17 years old, and meet the physical and mental standards required by the army.

The next step is to talk to a recruiter, who can provide you with more information about the job and the enlistment process. If you decide to enlist, you will attend Basic Combat Training, followed by Advanced Individual Training at Fort Jackson, South Carolina.

Why 91B Wheeled Vehicle Mechanics Are Important

During my time in the army, I saw firsthand how important 91B mechanics are to the success of military operations. Without them, vehicles and equipment would break down, and soldiers would be left stranded in dangerous situations. Their dedication and expertise are essential to keeping the army running smoothly.
