What Is A Pharmacy Tech Job

Written by Frank Jun 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in working in the healthcare industry but don't want to become a doctor or a nurse? A pharmacy tech job might be the perfect fit for you! Pharmacy tech jobs are in high demand and offer a great career path for those who want to help patients but don't want to pursue a degree in medicine.

One of the biggest pain points for those interested in pharmacy tech jobs is understanding what the job actually entails. Many people assume that pharmacy techs simply fill prescriptions and assist pharmacists, but the role is much more complex than that.

A pharmacy tech job involves working alongside a pharmacist to ensure that patients receive the correct medications in a timely and efficient manner. This includes tasks such as preparing medications, managing inventory, processing insurance claims, and communicating with patients and healthcare providers.

What Does a Pharmacy Tech Job Entail?

When I first started working as a pharmacy tech, I was surprised by the amount of responsibility I was given. I quickly learned that the role involved much more than just filling prescriptions.

As a pharmacy tech, my day-to-day tasks included preparing medications, managing inventory, communicating with patients and healthcare providers, and processing insurance claims. I also had the opportunity to work closely with pharmacists to ensure that patients were receiving the correct medications in a timely and efficient manner.

What Are the Requirements for a Pharmacy Tech Job?

In order to become a pharmacy tech, you'll need to complete a training program and obtain a certification. The training program can be completed in as little as six months, and there are a variety of programs available both online and in-person.

After completing the training program, you'll need to pass a certification exam to become a certified pharmacy technician (CPhT). Once you've obtained your certification, you'll be qualified to work in a variety of healthcare settings, including retail pharmacies, hospitals, and long-term care facilities.

What Are the Benefits of a Pharmacy Tech Job?

One of the biggest benefits of a pharmacy tech job is the flexibility it offers. Many pharmacy techs work part-time or on a flexible schedule, making it a great option for those who need to balance work with other responsibilities.

Additionally, pharmacy techs are in high demand, meaning that job security is high. The Bureau of Labor Statistics projects that employment of pharmacy technicians will grow 4 percent from 2019 to 2029, about as fast as the average for all occupations.

How Can You Excel in a Pharmacy Tech Job?

If you're interested in pursuing a career as a pharmacy tech, there are a few key skills that will help you excel in the role. These include strong attention to detail, excellent communication skills, and the ability to work well under pressure.

Additionally, staying up-to-date with the latest developments in the field is crucial. This can be achieved by attending continuing education courses and conferences, and by staying current with industry publications and news.

Conclusion of What is a Pharmacy Tech Job

A pharmacy tech job is an excellent option for those interested in working in the healthcare industry but don't want to become a doctor or a nurse. The role involves much more than simply filling prescriptions, and requires a range of skills including attention to detail, communication, and the ability to work well under pressure. With job growth projected to be 4 percent from 2019 to 2029, now is a great time to pursue a career as a pharmacy tech.
