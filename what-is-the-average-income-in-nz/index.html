What Determines the Average Income in NZ?

When I first moved to NZ, I was curious about what industries or regions paid higher salaries and how my qualifications and experience would be valued. After some research and networking, I learned that the average income in NZ is influenced by various factors.

Firstly, education plays a significant role in determining income in NZ. Individuals with higher levels of education, such as a bachelor's or master's degree, tend to earn more than those with only a high school diploma or some college experience. Secondly, occupation and industry can impact income, with some professions such as healthcare, engineering, and IT paying higher salaries than others.

Thirdly, location is another factor that affects the average income in NZ. Salaries tend to be higher in major cities such as Auckland, Wellington, and Christchurch, where the cost of living is also higher. Lastly, age and gender can also impact income, with older individuals and males tending to earn more than younger individuals and females.

Education and Income in NZ

In NZ, education is highly valued and can impact your income potential. According to Stats NZ, individuals with a bachelor's degree or higher earned a median income of $58,900 per year, while those with only a high school diploma earned a median income of $28,100 per year. Additionally, individuals with postgraduate degrees tend to earn even higher salaries, with a median income of $75,000 per year.

Location and Income in NZ

Location is another significant factor that affects the average income in NZ. Salaries tend to be higher in major cities such as Auckland, Wellington, and Christchurch, where demand for skilled workers is higher and the cost of living is more expensive. For example, the average income in Auckland is $65,000, while the average income in Northland is $45,000, according to Careers NZ.

Q: How does income tax work in NZ?

A: In NZ, income tax is based on a progressive tax system, which means that individuals who earn more pay a higher percentage of their income in taxes. The tax rate ranges from 10.5% for income up to $14,000 to 33% for income over $70,000.
