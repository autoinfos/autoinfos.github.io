What Is The Average Pay For An Auto Body Repair Technician

Written by Frank Aug 03, 2023 · 4 min read

Have you ever wondered how much an auto body repair technician makes? If you're considering a career in this field, or you're simply curious, keep reading to find out what the average pay is for an auto body repair technician.

Working as an auto body repair technician can be physically demanding and may require working in uncomfortable positions. Additionally, the work can be stressful, especially when dealing with tight deadlines or difficult customers. However, for those who enjoy working with their hands and taking on new challenges, this career can be rewarding and financially satisfying.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for automotive body and related repairers was $44,550 as of May 2020. However, this amount can vary depending on factors such as experience, location, and the type of employer.

In addition to the median annual wage, auto body repair technicians may also receive benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off. Some employers may also offer bonuses or commissions for meeting certain performance goals.

My Personal Experience

As someone who has worked in the auto body repair industry for several years, I can attest that the pay can vary greatly depending on the employer and location. In my experience, working for a dealership or larger corporation often comes with more benefits and higher pay, but may also require longer hours and more demanding work.

On the other hand, working for a smaller, family-owned auto body shop may come with a more relaxed work environment and a closer relationship with customers, but may also offer lower pay and fewer benefits.

Factors That Affect Pay

As previously mentioned, several factors can influence an auto body repair technician's pay. One significant factor is location, as wages can vary greatly depending on the cost of living in a particular area. Additionally, experience and skill level can also affect pay, as those with more experience or specialized skills may be able to demand higher wages.

The type of employer can also play a role in determining pay, as larger corporations or dealerships may offer higher salaries and more benefits than smaller, independent shops. Finally, the demand for auto body repair technicians in a particular area can also affect pay, as areas with higher demand may offer higher wages to attract skilled workers.

How to Increase Your Pay as an Auto Body Repair Technician

If you're looking to increase your pay as an auto body repair technician, there are several steps you can take. First, consider obtaining additional certifications or training in specialized areas, such as welding or paintless dent repair. These skills can make you a more valuable employee and may lead to higher pay.

You may also want to consider pursuing a management or supervisory role, which can come with higher salaries and more benefits. Additionally, networking with other professionals in the industry and staying up-to-date on the latest technologies and techniques can help you stay competitive and increase your earning potential.

Question and Answer

Q: Is the demand for auto body repair technicians expected to grow?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of automotive body and related repairers is projected to grow 2 percent from 2019 to 2029, which is about as fast as the average for all occupations.

Q: Can auto body repair technicians become self-employed?

A: Yes, many auto body repair technicians choose to become self-employed and open their own shops. This can come with additional responsibilities such as managing employees and finances, but can also offer the potential for higher earnings.

Q: Is there room for advancement in the auto body repair industry?

A: Yes, auto body repair technicians can advance to management or supervisory roles or even become trainers or instructors in the industry.

Q: Do auto body repair technicians need to have a college degree?

A: No, most auto body repair technicians only need a high school diploma or equivalent, although some employers may prefer applicants with postsecondary education or certification.

Conclusion of What is the Average Pay for an Auto Body Repair Technician

Overall, the average pay for an auto body repair technician is $44,550 per year, although this amount can vary based on several factors. By obtaining additional certifications or training, pursuing management roles, and staying up-to-date on industry trends, auto body repair technicians can increase their earning potential and advance their careers.
