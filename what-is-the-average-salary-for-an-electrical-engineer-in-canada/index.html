What Is The Average Salary For An Electrical Engineer In Canada

Written by Bardi May 31, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering a career in electrical engineering but wondering what the average salary is in Canada? Look no further, as we dive into the topic of what an electrical engineer can expect to earn in the Canadian job market.

As with any profession, there are various factors that can impact an electrical engineer's salary, such as years of experience, education level, and specialization. Additionally, location can also play a significant role in determining earnings, as salaries can vary between different provinces and cities.

According to recent data from the Government of Canada's Job Bank, the median salary for an electrical engineer in Canada is $85,008 per year. However, this can range from $51,000 to $133,000 depending on the above-mentioned factors.

In summary, electrical engineering is a lucrative field with competitive salaries in Canada. With a median salary of $85,008, this profession can provide a comfortable income for those who pursue it.

What Impacts an Electrical Engineer's Salary?

After completing a degree or diploma in electrical engineering, graduates can expect to enter the workforce with an entry-level salary of around $50,000 to $60,000 per year. However, as they gain experience and specialize in certain areas, their salary could increase significantly.

For example, an electrical engineer with five to ten years of experience can earn an average salary of $94,000 per year, while those with more than ten years of experience can expect to earn over $110,000 per year. Additionally, individuals with a master's degree or higher can also earn higher salaries compared to those with only a bachelor's degree.

Another factor that can impact an electrical engineer's salary is their specialization. For instance, those who specialize in power systems engineering can earn an average salary of $89,000 per year, while those who specialize in aerospace engineering can earn up to $99,000 per year.

Personal Experience of an Electrical Engineer

As an electrical engineer with six years of experience, I have seen firsthand how my salary has increased over time. Starting out at an entry-level salary, I was able to increase my earnings by specializing in power systems engineering and obtaining a master's degree in the field.

I have also found that location can play a significant role in determining salaries. While I was living in a smaller city, my salary was lower compared to when I moved to a larger metropolitan area.

How to Increase Your Salary as an Electrical Engineer

If you're looking to increase your salary as an electrical engineer, there are several steps you can take. First, consider specializing in a particular area of electrical engineering, such as power systems or aerospace engineering.

Additionally, obtaining a higher education degree, such as a master's or PhD, can also lead to higher salaries. Continuing education courses and obtaining professional certifications can also increase your earning potential.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average starting salary for an electrical engineer in Canada?

A: The average starting salary for an electrical engineer in Canada is around $50,000 to $60,000 per year.

Q: How much can an electrical engineer with ten years of experience expect to earn in Canada?

A: An electrical engineer with ten years of experience can expect to earn over $110,000 per year in Canada.

Q: Does location impact an electrical engineer's salary in Canada?

A: Yes, location can play a significant role in determining an electrical engineer's salary in Canada.

Q: Can obtaining a higher education degree lead to higher salaries for electrical engineers in Canada?

A: Yes, obtaining a higher education degree such as a master's or PhD can lead to higher salaries for electrical engineers in Canada.

Conclusion of What is the Average Salary for an Electrical Engineer in Canada

Overall, electrical engineering is a rewarding profession with competitive salaries in Canada. With experience, specialization, and higher education, an electrical engineer can expect to earn a comfortable income in the Canadian job market.
