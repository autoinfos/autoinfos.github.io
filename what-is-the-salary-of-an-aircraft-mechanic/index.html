What Is The Salary Of An Aircraft Mechanic

Written by Robby Jul 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you a plane enthusiast thinking of pursuing a career in aircraft maintenance? One of the most common questions that come to mind is, what is the salary of an aircraft mechanic? If you're considering a career in aviation maintenance, it's essential to know the earning potential of this profession.

Aircraft maintenance is a highly skilled and critical job that requires a lot of training and hard work. However, it's not a secret that the aviation industry is one of the most lucrative fields worldwide. Despite the high salary, it's also a job that comes with a lot of responsibility and risks.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), the median annual salary for aircraft and avionics equipment mechanics and technicians was $64,090 as of May 2020. The lowest 10% earned less than $39,860, while the highest 10% earned more than $98,040. The salary of an aircraft mechanic varies depending on the industry, location, experience, and credentials.

In conclusion, the salary of an aircraft mechanic is highly competitive, making it a popular career choice for many aviation enthusiasts. However, it requires a lot of hard work, dedication, and skill to succeed in this field. Let's dive deeper into what factors affect an aircraft mechanic's salary.

Factors that Affect the Salary of an Aircraft Mechanic

Several factors affect an aircraft mechanic's salary, including:

Industry

The aviation industry is vast, with many sectors employing aircraft mechanics. These include commercial airlines, the military, repair and maintenance facilities, and corporate aviation. The salary of an aircraft mechanic varies depending on the industry. Commercial airlines and the military tend to offer higher salaries than repair and maintenance facilities.

Location

The location also plays a significant role in determining an aircraft mechanic's salary. Salaries in urban areas are generally higher than those in rural areas. The cost of living in a particular area also affects the salary, with high-cost areas offering higher salaries.

Experience and Credentials

The more experience and credentials an aircraft mechanic has, the higher their earning potential. Aircraft mechanics with advanced certifications, such as an Airframe and Powerplant (A&P) license, tend to earn higher salaries. Additionally, aircraft mechanics with specialized skills or experience in a specific type of aircraft may earn more.

Personal Experience

As an aircraft mechanic, I can attest that the job comes with a lot of responsibilities and risks. However, it's also a highly rewarding career that offers many opportunities for growth and advancement. The salary of an aircraft mechanic is highly competitive, making it a popular career choice for many aviation enthusiasts.

The Future of Aircraft Mechanics

The future of aircraft mechanics is promising, with the BLS projecting a 5% growth rate from 2019 to 2029. This growth is due to the increasing demand for air travel and the need to maintain and repair the growing fleet of aircraft. The demand for skilled aircraft mechanics is expected to rise, leading to more job opportunities and higher salaries.

How to Become an Aircraft Mechanic

To become an aircraft mechanic, you need to have a high school diploma or equivalent and complete an FAA-approved aviation maintenance technician program. These programs typically take 18 to 24 months to complete and cover topics such as aviation electronics, composite materials, and turbine engines.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average hourly wage of an aircraft mechanic?

A: The average hourly wage of an aircraft mechanic is $30.83.

Q: How much does an entry-level aircraft mechanic make?

A: The entry-level aircraft mechanic makes an average of $44,000 per year.

Q: Can aircraft mechanics work remotely?

A: No, aircraft mechanics cannot work remotely as the job requires working with the aircraft physically.

Q: What benefits do aircraft mechanics receive?

A: Aircraft mechanics receive benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off.

Conclusion of What is the Salary of an Aircraft Mechanic

The salary of an aircraft mechanic depends on various factors such as industry, location, experience, and credentials. Despite the hard work and dedication required to succeed in this field, the salary of an aircraft mechanic is highly competitive, making it a popular career choice for many aviation enthusiasts. With the projected growth in the aviation industry, the demand for skilled aircraft mechanics is set to rise, leading to more job opportunities and higher salaries.
