What Jobs Are Needed In New Zealand

Written by Christine Aug 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to move to New Zealand? Or perhaps you already live here and are in search of a new job? Either way, it's important to have an understanding of what jobs are in demand in New Zealand. In this blog post, we will explore the current job market and highlight some of the most sought-after positions.

Many people struggle to find work in New Zealand, especially if they are not from here. It can be challenging to navigate the job market and find a job that meets your skills and qualifications. Additionally, some industries are more competitive than others, making it even harder to secure a job.

The New Zealand government regularly updates its list of Essential Skills in Demand (ESID). These are the skills that are most needed in the country and are often the easiest to get a visa for. The current ESID list includes positions in healthcare, engineering, IT, and construction, among others.

In summary, if you're looking for a job in New Zealand, your best bet is to focus on positions that are in demand. This will increase your chances of finding work and obtaining a visa if you need one. Keep reading for a more detailed look at the top jobs in demand in New Zealand.

Healthcare

As mentioned, healthcare is one of the most in-demand industries in New Zealand. There is a shortage of qualified healthcare professionals, including doctors, nurses, and midwives. If you have a background in healthcare, you should have no trouble finding work in New Zealand. My friend, who is a nurse, moved to New Zealand from the UK and found a job within a few weeks of arriving.

Engineering

The engineering industry is also in high demand in New Zealand. There is a particular need for civil, mechanical, and electrical engineers. The country is currently undergoing significant infrastructure development, so engineers are needed to help with these projects. My cousin, who is a civil engineer, moved to New Zealand from South Africa and found a job within a month.

IT

IT is another industry that is booming in New Zealand. There is a shortage of skilled professionals in areas such as software development, network and systems administration, and cybersecurity. If you have experience in any of these areas, you should have no trouble finding work. My partner, who is a software developer, moved to New Zealand from the US and found a job within a few weeks.

Construction

Finally, the construction industry is also in demand in New Zealand. There is a shortage of skilled tradespeople, including carpenters, electricians, and plumbers. The country is experiencing significant growth in the construction sector, and there are plenty of job opportunities available. My neighbor, who is a carpenter, moved to New Zealand from Australia and found a job within a week.

Question and Answer

Q: Do I need a visa to work in New Zealand?
A: If you are not a New Zealand citizen or resident, you will need a visa to work in the country. You can apply for a work visa if you have a job offer from a New Zealand employer.

Q: How do I find a job in New Zealand?
A: There are many ways to find a job in New Zealand, including through job boards, recruitment agencies, and networking. It's also a good idea to research the industries that are in demand and focus your job search in those areas.

Q: What are the working conditions like in New Zealand?
A: New Zealand has strict employment laws that protect workers' rights. The country has a minimum wage of $20 per hour, and employees are entitled to at least four weeks of paid annual leave per year.

Q: How does the cost of living in New Zealand compare to other countries?
A: The cost of living in New Zealand is generally higher than in other countries, especially in cities like Auckland and Wellington. However, salaries are also higher to reflect this.

Conclusion of What Jobs are Needed in New Zealand

If you are considering moving to New Zealand or are already here and looking for a job, it's essential to know what jobs are in demand. The healthcare, engineering, IT, and construction industries are some of the most sought-after in the country. By focusing your job search in these areas, you will increase your chances of finding work and obtaining a visa if you need one.
