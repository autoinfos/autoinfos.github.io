What Jobs Can I Get With An Automotive Degree

Written by Christine Nov 07, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're interested in cars and have pursued an automotive degree, you might be wondering what kind of jobs are available in this field. The good news is that there are plenty of career opportunities for those with an automotive background. From designing cars to repairing them, the possibilities are endless.

One of the most common pain points for those with an automotive degree is figuring out what kind of jobs are available. Many assume that their only option is to become a mechanic, but this is far from the truth.

So, what jobs can you get with an automotive degree? The answer really depends on your interests and skillset. However, here are some possibilities to consider:

Design Engineer

If you have a creative side and enjoy problem-solving, a career as a design engineer might be a good fit for you. As a design engineer, you would be responsible for coming up with new and innovative designs for cars, parts, and accessories. You would need to have a strong understanding of automotive engineering principles, as well as an eye for aesthetics.

Automotive Journalist

If you have a passion for writing and love cars, a career as an automotive journalist might be up your alley. As an automotive journalist, you would write articles and reviews about cars, test drive new models, and attend industry events. You would need to have strong writing skills and be able to convey technical information in an engaging way.

Vehicle Inspector

If you have an eye for detail and enjoy working independently, a career as a vehicle inspector might be a good fit for you. As a vehicle inspector, you would be responsible for inspecting cars and other vehicles to ensure they meet safety and quality standards. You would need to have a strong understanding of automotive technology and be able to identify potential issues.

Automotive Sales Representative

If you have strong interpersonal skills and enjoy working with people, a career as an automotive sales representative might be a good fit for you. As a sales representative, you would be responsible for selling cars and other vehicles to customers. You would need to have a strong understanding of automotive technology and be able to communicate technical information in a clear and concise way.

Conclusion of What Jobs Can I Get with an Automotive Degree

As you can see, there are many different career paths to explore with an automotive degree. Whether you're interested in design, writing, inspection, or sales, there is something for everyone in this field. With the right skills and experience, you can find a fulfilling and rewarding career in the automotive industry.

Question and Answer Section

Q: Do I need to be good at math to work in the automotive industry?

A: While a strong understanding of math is helpful, it's not always necessary for all roles in the automotive industry. However, if you're interested in pursuing a career as an engineer or mechanic, you will need to have a solid understanding of math and physics.

Q: Is it possible to work in the automotive industry without a degree?

A: Yes, it's possible to work in the automotive industry without a degree. However, having a degree can open up more opportunities and potentially lead to higher-paying jobs.

Q: What kind of skills do I need to work in the automotive industry?

A: The skills you need will depend on the specific role you're interested in. However, some common skills include problem-solving, critical thinking, communication, and technical expertise.

Q: How can I gain experience in the automotive industry?

A: There are many ways to gain experience in the automotive industry, such as internships, apprenticeships, and entry-level positions. You can also work on personal projects, such as restoring a car or building a custom vehicle.
