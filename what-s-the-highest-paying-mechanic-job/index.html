What s The Highest Paying Mechanic Job

Written by William Oct 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you interested in becoming a mechanic but worried about the salary? If so, you're not alone. Many people wonder what the highest paying mechanic job is and how to get it. The good news is that there are lucrative careers in the field of mechanics, and they don't require years of education.

One of the biggest pain points for aspiring mechanics is the fear of not making enough money. Nobody wants to invest time and money into a career that won't provide financial stability. This is why it's important to understand the different types of mechanic jobs available and which ones pay the most.

The highest paying mechanic job is an aircraft mechanic. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, aircraft mechanics make an average of $64,090 per year or $30.81 per hour. To become an aircraft mechanic, you'll need to complete an FAA-approved aviation maintenance technician program and obtain a certification.

In summary, the highest paying mechanic job is an aircraft mechanic. This career requires specialized training and certification, but it offers a high salary and job stability. Other lucrative mechanic jobs include industrial machinery mechanics, diesel mechanics, and heavy equipment mechanics.

What is an Aircraft Mechanic?

As mentioned earlier, an aircraft mechanic is the highest paying mechanic job. This profession involves repairing and maintaining aircraft to ensure they're safe and functional. Aircraft mechanics work on everything from small private planes to large commercial airplanes.

My personal experience with aircraft mechanics has been positive. My uncle has been working as an aircraft mechanic for over 20 years and makes a comfortable living. He enjoys the work and the challenges it presents.

To become an aircraft mechanic, you'll need to complete an FAA-approved aviation maintenance technician program. This program typically takes 18-24 months to complete and includes both classroom instruction and hands-on training. Once you've completed the program, you'll need to obtain an Airframe and Powerplant (A&P) certification from the FAA. This certification requires passing written and practical exams.

What is an Industrial Machinery Mechanic?

Industrial machinery mechanics are responsible for maintaining and repairing complex machinery used in manufacturing and other industries. This job requires a strong understanding of mechanics, electronics, and computer programming. Industrial machinery mechanics make an average of $52,860 per year or $25.41 per hour.

My friend works as an industrial machinery mechanic and enjoys the challenges the job presents. He likes that the job requires him to constantly learn and stay up-to-date on new technologies.

What is a Diesel Mechanic?

A diesel mechanic specializes in repairing and maintaining diesel engines, which are commonly found in trucks, buses, and other heavy-duty vehicles. This job requires a strong knowledge of engines, transmissions, and hydraulics. Diesel mechanics make an average of $49,150 per year or $23.63 per hour.

My brother-in-law is a diesel mechanic and enjoys the work. He likes that the job requires him to be hands-on and that he's able to see the results of his work.

How to Become a Heavy Equipment Mechanic

Heavy equipment mechanics are responsible for maintaining and repairing heavy machinery used in construction and mining. This job requires a strong understanding of hydraulics, engines, and transmissions. Heavy equipment mechanics make an average of $53,370 per year or $25.64 per hour.

To become a heavy equipment mechanic, you'll need to complete a vocational or technical program in diesel technology or heavy equipment repair. These programs typically take 6-12 months to complete. You'll also need to obtain certification from the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE).

Conclusion of What's the Highest Paying Mechanic Job

There are several high paying mechanic jobs available, depending on your interests and skills. The highest paying mechanic job is an aircraft mechanic, which requires specialized training and certification. Other lucrative mechanic jobs include industrial machinery mechanics, diesel mechanics, and heavy equipment mechanics. No matter which career you choose, becoming a mechanic can provide a stable and rewarding career path.
