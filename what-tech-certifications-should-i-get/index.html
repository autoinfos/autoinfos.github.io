What Tech Certifications Should I Get

Written by Petter Aug 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Technology is constantly advancing, and it can be challenging to keep up with the latest trends and skills. As a result, many professionals in the tech industry often wonder what tech certifications they should get. With so many options available, it can be overwhelming to decide which certifications are worth pursuing. However, with the right guidance, you can make informed decisions and advance your career.

One common pain point for tech professionals is feeling stagnant in their career. Without continuously learning and acquiring new skills, it can be challenging to move up in the industry. Additionally, some companies may require certain certifications for specific roles, making it difficult to advance without them.

The answer to what tech certifications you should get depends on your career goals and interests. However, there are some certifications that are widely recognized and valued in the industry. These certifications can help you stand out as a qualified and knowledgeable professional.

In summary, it's essential to stay current with the latest trends and skills in the tech industry. One way to do this is by obtaining tech certifications. By doing so, you can stand out as a qualified professional and advance your career.

1. Cisco Certifications

Cisco certifications are highly valued in the tech industry, particularly for networking professionals. The certifications range from entry-level to expert-level and cover a variety of topics. The CCNA (Cisco Certified Network Associate) is an excellent starting point for those new to the field. As you progress, you can pursue more advanced certifications, such as the CCNP (Cisco Certified Network Professional) or CCIE (Cisco Certified Internetwork Expert).

Personally, I obtained my CCNA certification early on in my career, and it helped me secure a job in networking. The certification demonstrated that I had the foundational knowledge and skills necessary to excel in the field.

2. CompTIA Certifications

CompTIA offers a range of certifications that cover various aspects of IT, including networking, security, and cloud computing. The A+ certification is an excellent starting point for those new to the industry, as it covers the basics of computer hardware and software. The Security+ certification is also widely recognized and valued in the industry, particularly for those interested in cybersecurity.

Personally, I obtained my Security+ certification as part of my job requirements. The certification helped me gain a deeper understanding of cybersecurity and provided me with the skills necessary to secure our company's network.

3. AWS Certifications

With the rise of cloud computing, AWS (Amazon Web Services) has become a popular choice for companies looking to move their infrastructure to the cloud. AWS certifications demonstrate an individual's expertise in various aspects of AWS, such as architecture, development, and operations.

Personally, I obtained my AWS Certified Solutions Architect certification to gain a better understanding of cloud computing and how it can benefit our company. The certification provided me with the skills and knowledge necessary to design and deploy scalable, highly available, and fault-tolerant systems on AWS.

4. Microsoft Certifications

Microsoft certifications demonstrate an individual's expertise in various Microsoft technologies, such as Windows Server, SharePoint, and Azure. The certifications range from entry-level to expert-level and cover a variety of topics.

Personally, I obtained my Microsoft Certified Solutions Expert certification in Server Infrastructure to gain a deeper understanding of Windows Server and how it can benefit our company. The certification provided me with the skills necessary to design, implement, and maintain a Windows Server infrastructure.

Question and Answer

Q: How do I decide which certifications to pursue?

A: It depends on your career goals and interests. Research the certifications that are relevant to your field, and determine which ones align with your career aspirations.

Q: How much do tech certifications cost?

A: The cost of tech certifications varies depending on the certification and the provider. Some certifications may cost a few hundred dollars, while others may cost several thousand.

Q: How long does it take to obtain a tech certification?

A: The time it takes to obtain a tech certification varies depending on the certification and the individual's experience. Some certifications may require several months of studying and preparation, while others may only require a few weeks.

Q: Do employers value tech certifications?

A: Yes, employers value tech certifications as they demonstrate an individual's expertise and commitment to their field. Additionally, some companies may require specific certifications for certain roles.

Conclusion of What Tech Certifications Should I Get

Obtaining tech certifications is an excellent way to stay current with the latest trends and skills in the industry. While there are many certifications available, it's essential to choose ones that align with your career goals and interests. Cisco, CompTIA, AWS, and Microsoft certifications are widely recognized and valued in the industry and can help you stand out as a qualified and knowledgeable professional.
