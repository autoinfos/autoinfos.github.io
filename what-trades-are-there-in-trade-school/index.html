Explore The Different Trades You Can Learn In Trade School

Written by Oliver Oct 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking for a career where you can work with your hands and see the tangible results of your hard work? Do you enjoy problem-solving and learning new skills? If so, trade school may be the perfect fit for you. But what trades are there in trade school? Let's dive in.

Many people may not realize that trade school offers a wide variety of trades to choose from. Whether you're interested in construction, automotive repair, or culinary arts, there's a trade that can fit your interests and career goals.

Some of the most popular trades in trade school include:

1. Construction Trades

This trade includes a range of fields, such as carpentry, electrical work, plumbing, and HVAC (heating, ventilation, and air conditioning). These trades involve building and maintaining structures, installing and repairing systems, and working on a variety of projects such as homes, commercial buildings, and infrastructure.

When I was in trade school, I studied carpentry and learned how to build structures from the ground up. It was incredibly rewarding to see a project come to life and know that I played a role in creating something that would last for years to come.

2. Automotive Trades

If you have a passion for cars and enjoy working with your hands, this trade may be for you. Automotive trades include fields such as auto mechanics, collision repair, and diesel technology. These trades involve diagnosing and repairing problems with vehicles, restoring damaged cars to their original condition, and maintaining fleet vehicles for large companies.

One of my friends from trade school went on to become an auto mechanic and now owns his own shop. He loves being able to solve problems for his clients and keep them safe on the road.

3. Culinary Arts

If you enjoy cooking and want to turn your passion into a career, culinary arts may be a great choice. This trade involves learning how to prepare and present food, manage a kitchen, and create menus for restaurants, hotels, and other food service operations.

During my time in trade school, I took a few culinary classes and learned how to make everything from classic French dishes to sushi. It was a lot of hard work, but it was so much fun to experiment with different flavors and techniques.

The Benefits of Trade School

One of the biggest benefits of trade school is that it offers a more hands-on approach to learning. Instead of sitting in a classroom all day, you'll have the opportunity to work with your hands and learn practical skills that you can apply in the real world. Additionally, trade school programs are often shorter and less expensive than traditional college programs, which means you can start your career sooner and with less debt.

How to Choose the Right Trade for You

When choosing a trade to pursue, it's important to consider your interests and career goals. Do you enjoy working outdoors or indoors? Do you want to work with your hands or with technology? Are you interested in a specific industry, such as healthcare or hospitality? By answering these questions, you can narrow down your options and find a trade that fits your unique needs.

Conclusion of What Trades are There in Trade School

Trade school offers a wide variety of trades to choose from, including construction, automotive repair, and culinary arts. By pursuing one of these trades, you can gain practical skills and start a rewarding career in a growing industry. So if you're ready to take the next step in your career, consider trade school as a viable option.

Question and Answer

Q: Is trade school a good option for someone who doesn't want to go to college?

A: Yes! Trade school is a great option for people who want to gain practical skills and start a career without attending a four-year college. In fact, many trade school programs can be completed in less than two years, which means you can start your career sooner and with less debt.

Q: How much does trade school cost?

A: The cost of trade school varies depending on the program and location. However, trade school programs are often less expensive than traditional college programs, and many schools offer financial aid and scholarships to help offset the cost.

Q: What kind of jobs can I get with a trade school degree?

A: There are many jobs you can get with a trade school degree, including carpenter, electrician, auto mechanic, HVAC technician, chef, and more. These trades offer a wide range of career opportunities and growth potential.

Q: Can I transfer credits from trade school to a four-year college?

A: In some cases, yes. Many trade school programs offer transferable credits that can be applied towards a degree at a four-year college. However, this varies by program and school, so it's important to check with the school you're interested in attending.
