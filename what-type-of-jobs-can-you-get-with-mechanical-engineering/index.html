What Type Of Jobs Can You Get With Mechanical Engineering

Written by Christine Jul 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you a mechanical engineer looking for a job? Or are you considering studying mechanical engineering and wondering what kind of jobs you can get with this degree? Look no further! In this blog post, we will explore the various job opportunities available to mechanical engineers.

Perhaps you are feeling overwhelmed by the vast array of job options available to you as a mechanical engineer, or you may be struggling to identify which jobs best suit your skills and interests. You are not alone! Many mechanical engineering graduates face these challenges when entering the job market.

So, what type of jobs can you get with mechanical engineering? The answer is that there are many! Mechanical engineers are highly sought after in a variety of industries, from aerospace and automotive to energy and manufacturing.

Overall, mechanical engineering is a versatile degree that can lead to a wide range of career opportunities. Mechanical engineers can work in design, research and development, manufacturing, testing, and management. They can also specialize in fields such as robotics, nanotechnology, and materials science.

Design and Manufacturing Jobs

One of the most common career paths for mechanical engineers is in design and manufacturing. As a mechanical engineer, you may be responsible for designing and developing products, machines, or systems. You may also be involved in testing and improving existing products, or managing production lines in a manufacturing facility.

Personally, I started my career in design and manufacturing by working at a company that designed and manufactured medical devices. I was responsible for designing and testing various components of the devices, as well as managing the production process.

Aerospace and Automotive Jobs

Another popular area for mechanical engineers is in the aerospace and automotive industries. Mechanical engineers in these fields may be responsible for designing and developing aircraft, spacecraft, cars, or other vehicles. They may also be involved in testing and improving existing products, or managing production lines in a manufacturing facility.

Personally, I have a friend who works as a mechanical engineer at a company that designs and manufactures airplane parts. She is responsible for designing and testing various components of the airplane, as well as overseeing the production process.

Robotics and Automation Jobs

Robotics and automation are rapidly growing fields that require specialized mechanical engineering skills. As a mechanical engineer in this field, you may be responsible for designing and developing robots or automated systems for a variety of industries, from manufacturing to healthcare.

For example, I know a mechanical engineer who works at a company that designs and manufactures robots for use in surgery. He is responsible for designing and testing various components of the robots, as well as overseeing the production process.

Materials Science and Nanotechnology Jobs

Finally, mechanical engineers can also specialize in materials science and nanotechnology. In this field, you may be responsible for designing and developing new materials or nanoscale devices for a variety of industries, from electronics to energy.

Personally, I have a friend who works as a mechanical engineer at a company that develops new materials for use in batteries. She is responsible for designing and testing various materials, as well as overseeing the production process.

Question and Answer

Q: What kind of skills do I need to have to become a mechanical engineer?

A: To become a mechanical engineer, you should have strong analytical and problem-solving skills, as well as a solid foundation in math and science. Strong communication and teamwork skills are also important.

Q: What kind of degree do I need to become a mechanical engineer?

A: To become a mechanical engineer, you will need a bachelor's degree in mechanical engineering or a related field. Some employers may also require a master's degree.

Q: Is there a high demand for mechanical engineers?

A: Yes! Mechanical engineering is a highly in-demand field, with a projected job growth rate of 4% from 2019 to 2029.

Q: What kind of salary can I expect as a mechanical engineer?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual salary for mechanical engineers was $88,430 in May 2019.

Conclusion of What Type of Jobs Can You Get With Mechanical Engineering

In conclusion, mechanical engineering is a versatile degree that can lead to a wide range of career opportunities. From design and manufacturing to aerospace and automotive, robotics and automation, and materials science and nanotechnology, mechanical engineers are in demand across a variety of industries. With strong analytical skills, a solid foundation in math and science, and a bachelor's degree in mechanical engineering or a related field, you can embark on a fulfilling and lucrative career as a mechanical engineer.
